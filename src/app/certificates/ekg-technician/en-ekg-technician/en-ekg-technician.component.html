Certificate of EKG Technician

An EKG Technician is a member of the health care team whose primary duty is the taking of EKGs on patients. An EKG measures the electrical activity of the heart and can be used to diagnose a range of many heart diseases.

The principal responsibilities of an EKG technician are as follows:

  • Set up and administer EKGs (electrocardiograms) and stress tests.
  • Prepare patients for Holter or ambulatory monitoring.
  • Edit and deliver final test results to physicians for analysis.
  • Perform Holter monitor processing and schedule appointments.
  • Ensure the patient is comfortable and informed about the procedure.
  • Monitor the patient's physical condition during the test.
  • Troubleshoot and maintain EKG equipment to ensure accurate readings.

EKG technicians work in hospitals, physicians' offices, and other health care facilities. As the population ages, the demand for EKG technicians is expected to grow.

More and more employers require a professional EKG technician certification.

Necessary requirements:

Graduation from High School (or equivalent), and graduation from an approved EKG program within the last 5 years.

Or

1-year verifiable work experience as an EKG Technician.

EKG Technician Blueprint

Domains

  • I. Safety, Compliance, and Coordinated Patient Care - 45 Hours
  • II. EKG Acquisition - 60 Hours
  • III. EKG Analysis and Interpretation - 35 Hours

I. Safety, Compliance, and Coordinated Patient Care

  • Competency 1. Adhering to HIPAA regulations and maintaining patient confidentiality
  • Competency 2. Practicing infection control including universal precautions and PPE usage
  • Competency 3. Complying with scope of practice and ethical standards in healthcare
  • Competency 4. Communicating effectively and appropriately with patients and healthcare team members
  • Competency 5. Measuring, recording, and monitoring patient vital signs
  • Competency 6. Instructing patients on preparation and expectations for cardiac testing
  • Competency 7. Utilizing electronic medical records (EMR/EHR) for patient data entry
  • Competency 8. Recognizing signs and symptoms of cardiopulmonary compromise or distress
  • Competency 9. Ensuring patient safety during all procedures and stress testing

II. EKG Acquisition

  • Competency 1. Maintaining EKG equipment (paper loading, cable maintenance, calibration)
  • Competency 2. Verifying and adjusting EKG machine settings (speed, gain, filters)
  • Competency 3. Preparing skin properly for electrode placement to ensure signal quality
  • Competency 4. Positioning patients correctly for 12-lead EKG, stress tests, and telemetry
  • Competency 5. Applying electrodes and attaching leads accurately for standard and special placements
  • Competency 6. Identifying and resolving artifacts (wandering baseline, somatic tremor, AC interference)
  • Competency 7. Verifying that all leads are recording clear and accurate waveforms
  • Competency 8. Mounting and labeling completed EKG tracings for the patient's medical record
  • Competency 9. Assisting in monitoring patient conditions throughout stress testing procedures
  • Competency 10. Instructing patients on the proper use and care of ambulatory monitors (Holter, event)

III. EKG Analysis and Interpretation

  • Competency 1. Calculating heart rate accurately from EKG tracings
  • Competency 2. Determining the regularity and rhythm of the patient's heart beat
  • Competency 3. Measuring EKG intervals and segments (PR interval, QRS duration, QT interval)
  • Competency 4. Inspecting waveform characteristics for symmetry, amplitude, and morphology
  • Competency 5. Identifying common arrhythmias (sinus, atrial, ventricular, junctional, blocks)
  • Competency 6. Recognizing pacemaker spikes and function on an EKG tracing
  • Competency 7. Identifying signs of ischemia, injury, and infarction (ST elevation/depression, T wave changes)
  • Competency 8. Recognizing life-threatening arrhythmias and initiating appropriate emergency response
