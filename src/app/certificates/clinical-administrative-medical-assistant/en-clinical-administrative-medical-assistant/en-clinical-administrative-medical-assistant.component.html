Certificate of Clinical & Administrative Medical Assistant

A Medical Assistant is a person who, under the direction of a physician, performs various routine administrative and clinical tasks in a hospital, clinic, or similar facility.

It is the responsibility of the Medical Assistant to know the laws and policies concerning a Medical Assistant’s scope of practice in the state and facility in which they work. The responsibilities of a medical assistant may include, but not limited to the following:

Administrative Responsibilities of a Medical Assistant

  • Scheduling patient appointments
  • Using computer for various applications
  • Answering telephone lines
  • Greeting patients and assisting them to exam rooms
  • Filing patient medical records
  • Entering patient information on records and charts
  • Helping with insurance forms
  • Setting up lab tests and procedures
  • Arranging for hospital admissions
  • Sorting mail

Clinical Responsibilities of a Medical Assistant

  • Taking of patient medical history
  • Explaining procedures to patients
  • Collecting blood and urine samples
  • Preparing exam room
  • Preparing patients for examination
  • Assisting the physician and or nurse during an exam
  • Informing patients about medication and diets
  • Preparing and administering medications as directed by a physician
  • Drawing blood by needle and collection tubes
  • Taking EKGs
  • Removing sutures

Patient Communication and Contact Responsibilities

Help patients relax in the physician’s office and often explain the physician’s instructions. The medical assistant may act as an interface in conveying messages from the physician to the patient.

More and more employers require a professional Medical Assistant certification.

Necessary requirements:

Graduation from High School (or equivalent), and graduation from an approved Medical Assistant program within the last 5 years.

Or

2-year verifiable work experience as a Medical Assistant professional.

Clinical & Administrative Medical Assistant Blueprint

Domains

  • I. Pharmacology - 20 Hours
  • II. Medical Procedures - 25 Hours
  • III. Patient Care - 50 Hours
  • IV. Phlebotomy - 35 Hours
  • V. EKG - 10 Hours
  • VI. Other Diagnostic Tests - 15 Hours
  • VII. Legal and Ethical Issues - 25 Hours
  • VIII. Administrative Duties - 20 Hours

I. Pharmacology

  • Competency 1. Handle drugs in a manner that complies with regulatory agency guidelines (i.e., DEA) in order to ensure proper ordering, documenting, and storing of drugs in a medical facility.
  • Competency 2. Complete prescriptions and authorizations of drug refills following legal requirements in order to ensure proper use of medication and patient confidentiality.
  • Competency 3. Identify commonly used medications by generic and brand names to facilitate communications with pharmacies and patients.
  • Competency 4. Perform basic medication-related calculations using appropriate references and tools in order to determine proper dosage.
  • Competency 5. Crosscheck medications for possible interactions using pharmaceutical references in order to prevent adverse drug reactions.
  • Competency 6. Recognize general indications for common therapeutic medications using knowledge of medical conditions and their treatments in order to properly respond to patient indications.

II. Medical Procedures

  • Competency 1. Use safety techniques (i.e., cleansing, disinfecting, sterilizing, medical asepsis) when handling and discarding chemicals and biohazardous waste using protective equipment.
  • Competency 2. Comply with regulations (i.e., OSHA and CDC) when handling patients and chemical and biohazardous waste in order to prevent transmission of diseases.
  • Competency 3. Use safety procedures when handling patients with infectious diseases using protective equipment and established procedures (i.e., isolation).
  • Competency 4. Handle emergency situations in office or by phone using appropriate standards and established procedures in order to protect personal, patient, and medical facility safety.

III. Patient Care

  • Competency 1. Obtain patient vital signs using appropriate instruments (i.e., scale, stethoscope, blood pressure cuff, thermometer, pulse oximetry) in order to establish baseline health information.
  • Competency 2. Record patient vital signs and patient medical/family history using the applicable EMR/EHR or paper-based system.
  • Competency 3. Identify the correct position for a patient given the exam or procedure in order to properly and safely prepare the patient.
  • Competency 4. Transfer patient using correct body mechanics in order to ensure patient and medical staff safety.
  • Competency 5. Perform patient vision screening using appropriate materials and procedures in order to evaluate patient condition.
  • Competency 6. Assist physician with patient procedures using appropriate tools and processes in order to provide patient care.
  • Competency 7. Assess patient pain level using a pain scale in order to determine patient’s current physical comfort level.
  • Competency 8. Perform ear irrigation on a patient using appropriate tools and solutions in order to remove wax buildup or foreign materials from the ear.
  • Competency 9. Recognize abnormal measures from screening results in order to inform appropriate medical staff for follow-up with the patient.
  • Competency 10. Maintain patient exam rooms including appropriate supplies and applying appropriate cleaning procedures.
  • Competency 11. Review basic instructions related to medications, treatments, devices, and equipment using appropriate procedures in order to ensure safe patient use.
  • Competency 12. Respond to patient with trouble breathing using oxygen-related equipment and appropriate procedures in order to alleviate patient condition.
  • Competency 13. Treat patient wounds using appropriate cleaning and bandaging techniques.
  • Competency 14. Perform necessary tests and screenings (i.e., EKG, blood cultures, CPR, vital signs) on a patient in critical condition using appropriate procedures.
  • Competency 15. Take precautions to protect the health and safety of medical staff and patients, including protection from airborne diseases and use of gloves.

IV. Phlebotomy

  • Competency 1. Verify patient identification and order verification using standards in order to ensure patient safety.
  • Competency 2. Verify patient followed correct procedures (i.e., fasting) before testing to evaluate pretest conditions prior to collection of specimen.
  • Competency 3. Select appropriate equipment (i.e., needle gauge, correct tube) given the blood test ordered and type/age of the patient.
  • Competency 4. Prepare the patient and site for blood collection using appropriate procedures in order to ensure safe collection.
  • Competency 5. Perform blood draw using appropriate procedures to ensure proper collection storing and accurate result of the sample.
  • Competency 6. Identify additives in evacuated blood collection tubes to check proper inversion and filling technique based on laboratory manual requirement.
  • Competency 7. Fill blood collection tube correctly following manufacturer recommendations in order to ensure proper protocol for filling each tube and the number of inversions for each tube.
  • Competency 8. Screen patient history before performing venipuncture to ensure patient does not develop any reaction.
  • Competency 9. Perform capillary punctures using finger stick method in order to obtain a specimen.
  • Competency 10. Respond to complications (i.e., hematoma, patient fainting) using appropriate methods in order to ensure patient safety.
  • Competency 11. Recognize pre-analytical errors and complications in order to obtain a sufficient quantity of specimen.
  • Competency 12. Process specimens (i.e., handle, label, transport, storing) according to appropriate methods in order to ensure accurate results.
  • Competency 13. Use butterfly needles to obtain a specimen for patients who have small or hard-to-reach veins.

V. EKG

  • Competency 1. Explain the EKG procedure to the patient using appropriate language in order to ensure patient understands procedure.
  • Competency 2. Prepare patient for EKG testing using appropriate procedures based on the specific patient in order to complete testing.
  • Competency 3. Place electrodes on the patient using appropriate procedures and placement in order to complete testing.
  • Competency 4. Record EKG tracings, vitals, and tolerance on a patient using appropriate resources and techniques depending on patient’s medical condition.

VI. Other Diagnostic Tests

  • Competency 1. Obtain specimen (i.e., urinalysis, hemoglobin, glucose, pregnancy) in correct form to complete the point of care test ordered by the physician.
  • Competency 2. Instruct patients in specimen collection or screening procedure using applicable pre-collection procedures and materials in order to ensure an accurate screening or collection.
  • Competency 3. Manage medical instrument/machinery maintenance and calibration by identifying and recording hazards or maintenance issues and implementing quality control procedures.

VII. Legal and Ethical Issues

  • Competency 1. Recognize legal responsibilities and scope of practice for medical assistants using standards in order to ensure patient fairness and safety.
  • Competency 2. Recognize applicable ethical standards in order to ensure patient and professional fairness and safety.
  • Competency 3. Comply with HIPAA laws to ensure patient confidentiality.
  • Competency 4. Follow proper procedures for handling reportable incidents (e.g., HIV, human bodily fluids) using OSHA standards in order to maintain patient confidentiality.
  • Competency 5. Flag and show respect for patient preferences in gender identification.

VIII. Administrative Duties

  • Competency 1. Schedule appointments using applicable office software in order to accurately manage patient care.
  • Competency 2. Handle patient records using procedures for updating, recording procedures, vaccinations, and test results, and filing in order to ensure accurate and confidential patient records.
  • Competency 3. Stock medical office using appropriate procedures and vendors in order to support the function of the medical facility.
  • Competency 4. Obtain medication prior authorizations, patient outreach for health care maintenance and wellness exams, referrals and authorizations for specialists or special screening using appropriate procedures in order to ensure patient care.
