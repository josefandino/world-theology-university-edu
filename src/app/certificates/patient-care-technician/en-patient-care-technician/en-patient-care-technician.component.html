Certificate of Patient Care Technician

A Patient Care Technician is a health technician working under the supervision of a registered nurse, physician, or other health professional to provide basic patient care. Duties may include taking vital signs, drawing blood and urine samples, performing basic diagnostic tests such as EKG, and assisting the physician as needed.

The principal responsibilities of a Patient Care Technician are as follows:

  • Provide basic patient care such as bathing, feeding, and assisting with personal hygiene.
  • Perform safety checks and ensure patient environment is clean and hazard-free.
  • Obtain, record, and monitor vital signs including blood pressure, pulse, respiration, and temperature.
  • Collect biological specimens including blood (phlebotomy) and urine samples.
  • Perform and monitor electrocardiograms (EKGs) and report abnormalities.
  • Assist patients with mobility, turning, positioning, and transfers.
  • Provide emotional support to patients and families during treatment.
  • Document patient care findings and report changes in patient condition to nursing staff.

Patient Care Technicians are vital members of the healthcare team, often serving as the primary point of contact for patients in hospitals, nursing homes, and long-term care facilities.

More and more employers require a professional patient care technician certification.

Necessary requirements:

Graduation from High School (or equivalent), and graduation from an approved Patient Care Technician program within the last 5 years.

Or

1-year verifiable work experience as a Patient Care Technician professional.

Patient Care Technician Blueprint

Domains

  • I. Patient Care - 65 Hours
  • II. Compliance, Safety, and Professional Responsibility - 30 Hours
  • III. Infection Control - 20 Hours
  • IV. Phlebotomy - 25 Hours
  • V. EKG - 20 Hours

I. Patient Care

  • Competency 1. Providing assistance with Activities of Daily Living (ADLs) including bathing, feeding, and toileting
  • Competency 2. Obtaining, recording, and monitoring vital signs accurately
  • Competency 3. Performing restorative skills such as range of motion exercises and patient positioning
  • Competency 4. Assisting with patient transfers, ambulation, and use of assistive devices
  • Competency 5. Measuring and recording intake and output (I&O)
  • Competency 6. Recognizing and reporting changes in patient condition (skin breakdown, pain, edema)
  • Competency 7. Administering basic first aid and performing CPR when necessary
  • Competency 8. Setting up and using oxygen delivery systems and suction equipment
  • Competency 9. Providing post-mortem care with dignity and respect
  • Competency 10. Supporting patients and families coping with grief and loss

II. Compliance, Safety, and Professional Responsibility

  • Competency 1. Adhering to HIPAA regulations and maintaining patient confidentiality
  • Competency 2. Following Joint Commission patient safety goals and guidelines
  • Competency 3. Recognizing and reporting signs of patient abuse or neglect
  • Competency 4. Practicing within the authorized scope of practice and ethical standards
  • Competency 5. Utilizing effective communication with patients and the healthcare team
  • Competency 6. Responding appropriately to emergency situations (fire, medical emergencies)
  • Competency 7. Documenting patient care accurately in electronic heath records (EHR)

III. Infection Control

  • Competency 1. Adhering to CDC guidelines and Standard Precautions
  • Competency 2. Utilizing appropriate Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)
  • Competency 3. Implementing transmission-based precautions (contact, droplet, airborne)
  • Competency 4. Performing aseptic techniques and maintaining a sterile field
  • Competency 5. Properly handling and disposing of biohazardous waste and sharps
  • Competency 6. Performing proper hand hygiene techniques

IV. Phlebotomy

  • Competency 1. Performing venipuncture using correct techniques and equipment
  • Competency 2. Selecting appropriate tubes and order of draw for blood collection
  • Competency 3. Properly labeling and processing blood specimens
  • Competency 4. collecting blood cultures and other special tests
  • Competency 5. Performing capillary punctures (finger/heel sticks)
  • Competency 6. Identifying and resolving pre-analytical errors in specimen collection

V. EKG

  • Competency 1. Preparing patient and skin for EKG electrode placement
  • Competency 2. Performing 12-lead EKG recording accurately
  • Competency 3. Recognizing and identifying common arrhythmias and waveforms
  • Competency 4. Identifying artifacts and troubleshooting technical issues
  • Competency 5. Recognizing life-threatening cardiac rhythms and responding immediately
  • Competency 6. Maintaining EKG equipment and verifying settings
