Certificate of Medical Administrative Assistant

This profession requires the individual to help manage a medical office. The medical administrative assistant oversees and manages the day-to-day medical office duties to ensure the smooth functioning of the medical office.

The principal responsibilities of a Medical Administrative Assistant are as follows:

  • Manage and maintain the front desk reception area.
  • Greet patients and visitors professionally and assist with check-in/check-out procedures.
  • Schedule and confirm patient appointments and medical procedures.
  • Update and maintain patient medical records (EMR/EHR) and ensure confidentiality.
  • Verify insurance eligibility and benefits for patients.
  • Process co-payments and handle basic billing inquiries.
  • Answer telephone calls, screen inquiries, and take accurate messages.
  • Coordinate referrals to other healthcare providers or specialists.
  • Manage incoming and outgoing correspondence and mail.
  • Ensure compliance with healthcare regulations such as HIPAA and OSHA.

Medical Administrative Assistants play a crucial role in the healthcare system by bridging the gap between patients and medical staff, ensuring that the practice runs efficiently and patients receive timely care.

More and more employers require a professional Medical Administrative Assistant certification.

Necessary requirements:

Graduation from High School (or equivalent), and graduation from an approved Medical Administrative Assistant program within the last 5 years.

Or

1-year verifiable work experience as a Medical Administrative Assistant professional.

Medical Administrative Assistant Blueprint

Domains

  • I. Foundational Knowledge - 15 Hours
  • II. Communication and Professionalism - 25 Hours
  • III. Medical Law, Ethics, and Compliance - 20 Hours
  • IV. Scheduling - 20 Hours
  • V. Patient Encounter - 25 Hours
  • VI. Billing and Revenue Cycle - 15 Hours
  • VII. Medical Practice Administrative Procedures and Logistics - 20 Hours

I. Foundational Knowledge

  • Competency 1. Understanding basic medical terminology, anatomy, and physiology
  • Competency 2. Identifying types of healthcare organizations and delivery models
  • Competency 3. Recognizing the components and types of medical records (paper and electronic)
  • Competency 4. Understanding the relationship between front office administration and clinical processes

II. Communication and Professionalism

  • Competency 1. Communicating effectively with patients, caregivers, and healthcare personnel
  • Competency 2. Demonstrating professionalism, teamwork, and appropriate workplace etiquette
  • Competency 3. Managing challenging customer service situations and conflict resolution
  • Competency 4. Adapting communication styles to diverse patient populations and audiences
  • Competency 5. Utilizing therapeutic communication techniques

III. Medical Law, Ethics, and Compliance

  • Competency 1. Complying with HIPAA regulations for patient privacy and confidentiality
  • Competency 2. Adhering to federal and state healthcare laws and regulations
  • Competency 3. Understanding patient rights and informed consent
  • Competency 4. Maintaining security of Protected Health Information (PHI)
  • Competency 5. Recognizing ethical considerations in healthcare administration

IV. Scheduling

  • Competency 1. Managing appointment schedules and templates efficiently
  • Competency 2. Scheduling and confirming diagnostic testing and procedures
  • Competency 3. Handling cancellations, no-shows, and rescheduling requests
  • Competency 4. Implementing urgency protocols (triage) for appointment scheduling
  • Competency 5. Coordinating referrals and pre-authorizations for specialist care

V. Patient Encounter

  • Competency 1. Performing patient intake procedures and verifying demographic information
  • Competency 2. Ensuring completion of required patient forms and consent documents
  • Competency 3. Verifying insurance coverage and eligibility
  • Competency 4. Calculating and collecting patient financial responsibility (co-pays, deductibles)
  • Competency 5. Facilitating patient check-out and follow-up planning

VI. Billing and Revenue Cycle

  • Competency 1. Understanding the phases of the revenue cycle management
  • Competency 2. Preparing accurate documentation for billing purposes
  • Competency 3. Performing basic charge reconciliation and payment collection
  • Competency 4. Understanding ICD-10 and CPT coding basics for administrative purposes
  • Competency 5. Handling billing inquiries and explaining financial policies to patients

VII. Medical Practice Administrative Procedures and Logistics

  • Competency 1. Managing and maintaining medical records (filing, archiving, auditing)
  • Competency 2. Performing financial procedures such as establishing petty cash and reconciling daysheets
  • Competency 3. Opening and closing the medical office according to protocols
  • Competency 4. Managing administrative supplies and inventory
  • Competency 5. Verifying deliveries and processing invoices
