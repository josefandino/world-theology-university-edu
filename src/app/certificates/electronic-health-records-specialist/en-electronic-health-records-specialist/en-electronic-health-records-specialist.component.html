Certificate of Electronic Health Records Specialist

An Electronic Health Records Specialist provides support to the rest of the team in doctorsâ€™ offices, hospitals, outpatient facilities, nursing homes and other health care settings. He or she has the knowledge required to efficiently manage and maintain medical records in those settings. Although an EHR specialist has no direct contact with patients, he or she plays a vital role in patient care. As a certified EHR Specialist, you will have the ability to process health information data, navigate EHR software and manage applications of EHR technology in a medical site. In addition to maintaining electronic patient records, the EHR specialist is responsible for protecting the privacy and security of patient information.

The principal responsibilities of an Electronic Health Records Specialist are as follows:

  • Audit patient records for compliance with legal and regulatory requirements.
  • Abstract clinical information for reports and quality improvement.
  • Perform basic coding to submit claims for reimbursement.
  • Process Release of Information (ROI) requests for medical records.
  • Review patient records to ensure completion and accuracy.
  • Collect patient demographic and insurance information.
  • Discuss patient information with physicians and insurance companies.
  • Maintain strict confidentiality of patient information (HIPAA).
  • Provide technical support and training on EHR systems to staff.

Electronic Health Record Specialists are critical to the modern healthcare environment, ensuring the integrity, accessibility, and security of patient health information across various platforms.

More and more employers require a professional Electronic Health Records Specialist certification.

Necessary requirements:

Graduation from High School (or equivalent), and graduation from an approved EHR program within the last 5 years.

Or

1-year verifiable work experience as an EHR Specialist.

Electronic Health Records Specialist Blueprint

Domains

  • I. Non-Clinical Operations - 35 Hours
  • II. Clinical Operations - 40 Hours
  • III. Revenue Cycle/Finance - 20 Hours
  • IV. Regulatory Compliance - 20 Hours
  • V. Reporting - 15 Hours

I. Non-Clinical Operations

  • Competency 1. Verifying patient identifiers and registration information accurately.
  • Competency 2. Collecting and updating patient demographic and insurance data.
  • Competency 3. Managing appointment schedules and patient portals.
  • Competency 4. Ensuring secure access to the EHR system and maintaining passwords.
  • Competency 5. Processing release of information (ROI) requests according to protocols.

II. Clinical Operations

  • Competency 1. Navigating the EHR system to retrieve and review patient charts.
  • Competency 2. Documenting and auditing clinical encounters for completeness.
  • Competency 3. Reconciling medication lists and allergy information.
  • Competency 4. Managing electronic orders for lab tests and diagnostic imaging.
  • Competency 5. Scanning and indexing paper documents into the electronic record.

III. Revenue Cycle/Finance

  • Competency 1. Verifying procedure and diagnosis codes (CPT/ICD-10) for billing.
  • Competency 2. Generating patient statements and financial reports.
  • Competency 3. Posting payments and adjustments to patient accounts.
  • Competency 4. Managing insurance claims and understanding denial reasons.

IV. Regulatory Compliance

  • Competency 1. Adhering to HIPAA Privacy and Security Rules for PHI protection.
  • Competency 2. Understanding HITECH Act implications and meaningful use standards.
  • Competency 3. Maintaining compliance with fraud, waste, and abuse regulations.
  • Competency 4. Participating in internal audits to ensure regulatory adherence.

V. Reporting

  • Competency 1. Generating standard and ad-hoc reports for administrative decision-making.
  • Competency 2. Ensuring data integrity and accuracy in all generated reports.
  • Competency 3. Using data analytics to improved healthcare quality and efficiency.
