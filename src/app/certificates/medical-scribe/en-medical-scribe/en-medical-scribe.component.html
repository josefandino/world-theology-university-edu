Certificate of Medical Scribe

The primary duty of a scribe is to record the clinical, family and social history of the patient; physical examination, current complaints, diagnoses, procedures performed (either by the physician or other healthcare professionals), as well as lab results, radiographic impressions, medical orders given (including prescriptions and tests), as well as patient education and explanations of risk and benefits.

The principal responsibilities of a Medical Scribe are as follows:

  • Transcribe medical charts and physician dictations accurately and efficiently in real-time.
  • Document patient history, physical exams, and course of treatment into the Electronic Health Record (EHR).
  • Assist providers by locating information like lab and radiology results.
  • Ensure documentation compliance with regulatory coding and billing guidelines (ICD-10, CPT, Evaluation and Management).
  • Maintain comprehensive and up-to-date patient records.
  • Track patient flow and help manage provider efficiency during clinical shifts.
  • Proofread and edit medical documents for accuracy, spelling, and grammar.
  • Strictly adhere to HIPAA regulations and maintain patient confidentiality.

Medical Scribes are essential in modern healthcare, alleviating the documentation burden on physicians and allowing them to focus more on direct patient care.

More and more employers require a professional Medical Scribe certification.

Necessary requirements:

Graduation from High School (or equivalent), and graduation from an approved Medical Scribe program within the last 5 years.

Or

1-year verifiable work experience as a Medical Scribe Professional.

Medical Scribe Blueprint

Domains

  • I. Medical Knowledge and Terminology - 30 Hours
  • II. Clinical Documentation and Charting - 40 Hours
  • III. Electronic Health Records (EHR) Handling - 25 Hours
  • IV. Medicolegal and Ethical Aspects - 20 Hours
  • V. Scribe Role, Workflow, and Efficiency - 15 Hours

I. Medical Knowledge and Terminology

  • Competency 1. Utilizing correct medical prefixes, suffixes, and root words for accurate documentation.
  • Competency 2. Understanding anatomy, physiology, and pathophysiology of major body systems.
  • Competency 3. Identifying common medications, indications, and pharmacological terminology.
  • Competency 4. Recognizing diagnostic tests, imaging modalities, and laboratory values.
  • Competency 5. Applying knowledge of common diseases and clinical presentations.

II. Clinical Documentation and Charting

  • Competency 1. Documenting the History of Present Illness (HPI) and Chief Complaint accurately.
  • Competency 2. Recording Review of Systems (ROS) and Physical Exam (PE) findings as dictated.
  • Competency 3. Formatting clinical notes using standard structures (e.g., SOAP notes).
  • Competency 4. Documenting Assessment and Plan, including medical decision-making processes.
  • Competency 5. Capturing patient education, discharge instructions, and follow-up plans.

III. Electronic Health Records (EHR) Handling

  • Competency 1. Navigating various EHR platforms proficiently to input data.
  • Competency 2. Managing computerized physician order entry (CPOE) where permitted.
  • Competency 3. Retrieving past medical records, lab results, and imaging reports efficiently.
  • Competency 4. Understanding the principles of Meaningful Use and Merit-based Incentive Payment System (MIPS).

IV. Medicolegal and Ethical Aspects

  • Competency 1. Adhering strictly to HIPAA Privacy and Security Rules regarding PHI.
  • Competency 2. Understanding the legal limitations and scope of practice for a medical scribe.
  • Competency 3. Mitigating medico-legal risks through accurate and timely documentation.
  • Competency 4. Recognizing and navigating ethical dilemmas in the clinical environment.

V. Scribe Role, Workflow, and Efficiency

  • Competency 1. Facilitating efficient provider workflow in outpatient and emergency settings.
  • Competency 2. Practicing effective communication with the healthcare team.
  • Competency 3. Developing speed and accuracy in real-time transcription.
  • Competency 4. Prioritizing tasks during high-volume clinical shifts.
