Certificate of Pharmacy Technician

Pharmacy technicians primarily assist pharmacists in dispensing prescription medication to customers or health professionals. They work in a variety of settings, including retail pharmacies, hospitals, pharmaceutical manufacturers, and other medical facilities.

The principal responsibilities of a pharmacy technician are as follows:

  • Receive written prescription or refill requests and verify that information is complete and accurate.
  • Maintain proper storage and security conditions for drugs.
  • Answer telephones, responding to questions or requests.
  • Fill bottles with prescribed medications, and type and affix labels.
  • Assist customers by answering simple questions, locating items, or referring them to the pharmacist for medication information.
  • Price and file prescriptions that have been filled.
  • Clean and help maintain equipment or work areas and sterilize glassware according to prescribed methods.
  • Establish and maintain patient profiles, including lists of medications taken by individual patients.
  • Order, label, and count stock of medications, chemicals, or supplies and enter inventory data into computer.
  • Receive and store incoming supplies, verify quantities against invoices, and check for outdated or recalled medications.

As the healthcare industry expands, the role of the Pharmacy Technician is becoming increasingly vital. Professionalism, accuracy, and attention to detail are of the utmost importance to ensure patient safety and efficient pharmacy operations.

More and more employers require a professional pharmacy technician certification.

Necessary requirements:

Graduation from High School (or equivalent), and graduation from an approved Pharmacy Technician program within the last 5 years.

Or

1-year verifiable work experience as a Pharmacy Technician professional.

Pharmacy Technician Blueprint

Domains

  • I. Overview and Laws - 20 Hours
  • II. Drugs and Drug Therapy - 25 Hours
  • III. Dispensing Process - 55 Hours
  • IV. Medication Safety and Quality Assurance - 25 Hours

I. Overview and Laws

  • Task 1. Understanding the scope of practice and role of the Pharmacy Technician
  • Task 2. Federal and State laws regarding pharmacy practice
  • Task 3. Controlled substances regulations and DEA requirements
  • Task 4. HIPAA regulations and patient privacy
  • Task 5. Record keeping and documentation standards
  • Task 6. Ethics and professional conduct in pharmacy

II. Drugs and Drug Therapy

  • Task 1. Drug classifications and their indications
  • Task 2. Mechanisms of action for common medications
  • Task 3. Common side effects and contraindications
  • Task 4. Identifying and preventing drug interactions
  • Task 5. Basic anatomy and physiology related to pharmacotherapy
  • Task 6. Medical terminology, abbreviations, and symbols
  • Task 7. Generic vs. Brand name medications

III. Dispensing Process

  • Task 1. Receiving and screening prescriptions for completeness
  • Task 2. Interpreting prescription sig codes and instructions
  • Task 3. Calculating dosages, days supply, and quantity to dispense
  • Task 4. Pharmaceutical calculations and conversions
  • Task 5. Packaging and labeling prescriptions accurately
  • Task 6. Compounding non-sterile preparations
  • Task 7. Compounding sterile preparations and aseptic technique
  • Task 8. Unit dose systems and repackaging
  • Task 9. Inventory management and ordering procedures
  • Task 10. Handling insurance claims and adjudication

IV. Medication Safety and Quality Assurance

  • Task 1. Identifying high-alert medications and look-alike/sound-alike drugs
  • Task 2. Strategies for medication error prevention
  • Task 3. Root cause analysis and reporting errors
  • Task 4. Infection control procedures and PPE use
  • Task 5. Handling and disposal of hazardous drugs and waste
  • Task 6. Laminar flow hood maintenance and cleaning
  • Task 7. Recall procedures for medications
