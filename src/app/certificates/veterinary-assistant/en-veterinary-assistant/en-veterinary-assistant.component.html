Certificate of Veterinary Assistant

A veterinary assistant is someone who works under the direction of a veterinarian, laboratory animal technologist or scientist to help with administrative and non-medical care of pets and non-farm animals such as cats, rabbits, dogs, ferrets, and birds. The veterinary assistant is responsible for providing care such as feeding, cleaning, giving water and administering medication to animals in veterinary offices, zoos, laboratories, or animal hospitals.

The principal responsibilities of a Veterinary Assistant are as follows:

  • Sanitize and sterilize surgical equipment and examination rooms.
  • Feed, bathe, and restrain animals during examinations and procedures.
  • Administer medications and immunizations as directed by veterinarians.
  • Assist veterinarians with surgery, diagnostic imaging, and laboratory tests.
  • Monitor animals recovering from surgery or suffering from illnesses.
  • Educate pet owners on animal care, post-operative instructions, and medication administration.
  • Maintain accurate medical records and manage appointment schedules.
  • Collect biological samples (urine, stool, blood) for laboratory analysis.
  • Perform basic emergency first aid for animals when necessary.

Veterinary Assistants are the compassionate heart of animal healthcare, providing essential support that ensures the well-being of animals and the smooth operation of veterinary practices.

More and more employers require a professional Veterinary Assistant certification.

Necessary requirements:

Graduation from High School (or equivalent), and graduation from an approved Veterinary Assistant program within the last 5 years.

Or

1-year verifiable work experience as a Veterinary Assistant professional.

Veterinary Assistant Blueprint

Domains

  • I. Office and Hospital Procedures - 15 Hours
  • II. Communication and Client Relations - 20 Hours
  • III. Pharmacy and Pharmacology - 25 Hours
  • IV. Examination Room Procedures - 30 Hours
  • V. Small Animal Nursing - 35 Hours
  • VI. Surgical Preparation and Assisting - 25 Hours
  • VII. Laboratory Procedures - 20 Hours
  • VIII. Radiography and Ultrasound Imaging - 15 Hours

I. Office and Hospital Procedures

  • Competency 1. Managing patient records and filing systems accurately.
  • Competency 2. Scheduling appointments and handling invoices/billing.
  • Competency 3. Maintaining cleanliness and inventory in the reception and hospital areas.
  • Competency 4. Demonstrating professional telephone etiquette.

II. Communication and Client Relations

  • Competency 1. Communicating effectively with pet owners regarding care instructions.
  • Competency 2. Handling sensitive conversations (e.g., euthanasia, costs) with empathy.
  • Competency 3. Navigating client relationships and providing excellent service.
  • Competency 4. Understanding the professional hierarchy and roles within the animal hospital.

III. Pharmacy and Pharmacology

  • Competency 1. Understanding common drug categories and veterinary terminology.
  • Competency 2. Preparing and filling prescriptions accurately under supervision.
  • Competency 3. Properly labeling and storing medications and vaccines.
  • Competency 4. Complying with regulations regarding controlled substances.

IV. Examination Room Procedures

  • Competency 1. Safely restraining animals for examinations and procedures.
  • Competency 2. Obtaining and recording accurate patient history and vital signs.
  • Competency 3. Assisting veterinarians with physical exams and treatments.
  • Competency 4. Preparing examination rooms with necessary instruments and supplies.

V. Small Animal Nursing

  • Competency 1. Implementing infection control and zoonotic disease prevention protocols.
  • Competency 2. Administering oral and topical medications as directed.
  • Competency 3. Monitoring hospitalized patients (eating, drinking, elimination).
  • Competency 4. Providing basic husbandry including grooming, bathing, and nail trimming.

VI. Surgical Preparation and Assisting

  • Competency 1. Identifying and sterilizing surgical instruments and equipment.
  • Competency 2. Preparing the surgical site (shaving, scrubbing) aseptically.
  • Competency 3. Monitoring patients during pre-operative and post-operative recovery.
  • Competency 4. Understanding aseptic technique and operating room conduct.

VII. Laboratory Procedures

  • Competency 1. Collecting and handling biological samples (fecal, urine, blood) properly.
  • Competency 2. Using and maintaining laboratory equipment like centrifuges and microscopes.
  • Competency 3. Preparing slides and specimens for diagnostic analysis.
  • Competency 4. Understanding guidelines for disposal of biohazardous waste.

VIII. Radiography and Ultrasound Imaging

  • Competency 1. Applying radiation safety measures and using PPE correctly.
  • Competency 2. Assisting with patient positioning and restraint for imaging.
  • Competency 3. Processing and filing radiographic images.
