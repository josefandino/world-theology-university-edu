The principal responsibilities of a Medical Billing & Coding Specialist are as follows:

  • Review patient records for timeliness, completeness, accuracy, and appropriateness of data.
  • Organize and maintain data for clinical databases and registries.
  • Assign clinical codes for reimbursement and data analysis.
  • Electronically record data for collection, storage, analysis, retrieval, and reporting.
  • Protect patient health information confidentiality and security.
  • Prepare and submit insurance claims to third-party payers.
  • Follow up on unpaid claims and clarify billing discrepancies with insurers and patients.
  • Stay updated on ICD-10, CPT, and HCPCS coding guidelines and regulations.

Medical Billing and Coding specialists are the backbone of the healthcare revenue cycle, ensuring that healthcare providers are properly reimbursed for their services while complying with complex insurance regulations.

More and more employers require a professional Medical Billing & Coding certification.

Necessary requirements:

Graduation from High School (or equivalent), and graduation from an approved Medical Billing & Coding program within the last 5 years.

Or

1-year verifiable work experience as a Medical Billing and Coding professional.

Medical Billing & Coding Blueprint

Domains

  • I. The Revenue Cycle and Regulatory Compliance - 25 Hours
  • II. Insurance Eligibility and Other Payer Requirements - 30 Hours
  • III. Coding and Coding Guidelines - 50 Hours
  • IV. Billing and Reimbursement - 55 Hours

I. The Revenue Cycle and Regulatory Compliance

  • Competency 1. Integrating revenue cycle concepts with business and payer requirements for efficient processing.
  • Competency 2. Communicating effectively with specific stakeholders (patients, providers, payers) regarding billing issues.
  • Competency 3. Maintaining confidentiality of Protected Health Information (PHI) in accordance with HIPAA regulations.
  • Competency 4. Understanding the life cycle of a medical claim from patient registration to final payment.
  • Competency 5. Complying with federal laws (Stark Law, Anti-Kickback Statute, False Claims Act) to prevent fraud and abuse.

II. Insurance Eligibility and Other Payer Requirements

  • Competency 1. Verifying patient insurance coverage, benefits, and eligibility prior to services relationships.
  • Competency 2. Differentiating between types of insurance plans (Medicare, Medicaid, HMO, PPO, TRICARE).
  • Competency 3. Calculating patient financial responsibility including copayments, coinsurance, and deductibles.
  • Competency 4. Obtaining necessary pre-certifications and pre-authorizations for medical procedures.
  • Competency 5. Coordinating benefits for patients with primary and secondary insurance coverage.

III. Coding and Coding Guidelines

  • Competency 1. Applying ICD-10-CM guidelines to assign accurate diagnostic codes to the highest level of specificity.
  • Competency 2. Utilizing CPT coding principles for outpatient and physician service procedures.
  • Competency 3. Assigning HCPCS Level II codes for equipment, supplies, and services not covered by CPT.
  • Competency 4. Abstracting relevant clinical information from medical records to support code assignment.
  • Competency 5. Applying appropriate modifiers to CPT codes to describe specific circumstances of the procedure.
  • Competency 6. Understanding the importance of medical necessity in coding.

IV. Billing and Reimbursement

  • Competency 1. Preparing and submitting accurate CMS-1500 (paper) and 837P (electronic) claim forms.
  • Competency 2. Interpreting Explanation of Benefits (EOB) and Remittance Advice (RA) documents.
  • Competency 3. Managing Accounts Receivable (A/R) and analyzing aging reports.
  • Competency 4. Identifying and correcting claims errors (rejections vs. denials) for resubmission.
  • Competency 5. Determining the accurate assignment of benefits and payment posting.
  • Competency 6. Navigating the appeals process for denied claims.
