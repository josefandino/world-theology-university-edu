Certificate of Insurance Exam Technician

An Insurance Examination Technician performs duties such as drawing blood for medical laboratory testing, and EKG recordings for diagnostic evaluation. Phlebotomy refers to the drawing of blood for medical laboratory testing. EKG recordings measure the electrical activity of the heart which is used to help diagnose a range of heart diseases.

The principal responsibilities of an Insurance Exam Technician are as follows:

  • Travel to examinees' homes or workplaces to conduct paramedical examinations.
  • Verify applicant identity and complete insurance medical history questionnaires accurately.
  • Perform mobile phlebotomy collecting blood samples (venipuncture) appropriate for testing.
  • Collect and process urine or saliva specimens following strict chain of custody protocols.
  • Obtain accurate physical measurements including height, weight, pulse, and blood pressure.
  • Administer 12-lead EKG or other cardiac monitoring as required by insurance underwriting.
  • Ensure all biological specimens are centrifuged, labeled, and shipped according to lab standards.
  • Maintain professionalism and confidentiality (HIPAA) while interacting with insurance applicants.
  • Manage inventory of medical supplies and forms necessary for mobile exams.

Insurance Examination Technicians, often synonymous with Paramedical Examiners, are vital to the risk assessment process in the life and health insurance industry, ensuring that underwriting decisions are based on accurate medical data.

More and more employers require a professional Insurance Exam Technician certification.

Necessary requirements:

Graduation from High School (or equivalent), and graduation from an approved Phlebotomy and EKG Technician program within the last 5 years.

Or

1-year verifiable work experience as an Insurance Examination Technician.

Insurance Exam Technician Blueprint

Domains

  • I. Mobile Phlebotomy and Specimen Collection - 40 Hours
  • II. Mobile EKG Acquisition - 25 Hours
  • III. Paramedical Examination Protocols - 30 Hours
  • IV. Professionalism and Compliance - 15 Hours

I. Mobile Phlebotomy and Specimen Collection

  • Competency 1. Performing venipuncture in non-clinical settings (homes/offices) safely and effectively.
  • Competency 2. Collecting urine and oral fluid samples following chain of custody procedures.
  • Competency 3. Processing specimens on-site (centrifuging, splitting samples) for transport.
  • Competency 4. Proper labeling and packaging of biohazardous materials for shipping to reference labs.
  • Competency 5. Adhering to infection control and safety standards in a mobile environment.

II. Mobile EKG Acquisition

  • Competency 1. Setting up portable EKG equipment in various environments.
  • Competency 2. Ensuring patient privacy and comfort during electrode placement.
  • Competency 3. Recording clear and accurate 12-lead EKG tracings free of artifacts.
  • Competency 4. Troubleshooting equipment issues in the field.
  • Competency 5. Transmitting or attaching EKG results to insurance documentation.

III. Paramedical Examination Protocols

  • Competency 1. Accurately measuring and recording height, weight, and chest/waist circumference.
  • Competency 2. Taking accurate blood pressure and pulse readings.
  • Competency 3. conducting thorough medical history interviews and documenting findings.
  • Competency 4. Understanding specific requirements of different insurance carriers.
  • Competency 5. Completing paper or electronic exam forms with high attention to detail.

IV. Professionalism and Compliance

  • Competency 1. Verifying applicant identification to prevent fraud.
  • Competency 2. Maintaining strict confidentiality of applicant health information (HIPAA).
  • Competency 3. managing appointments and travel schedules efficiently.
  • Competency 4. providing excellent customer service to calm anxious applicants.
