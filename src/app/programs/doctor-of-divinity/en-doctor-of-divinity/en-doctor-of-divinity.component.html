The Doctor of Divinity Program at World Theology University offers a unique opportunity for those seeking to deepen their theological understanding and develop practical skills to serve religious and spiritual communities around the world. Through a fully online education, this program provides the flexibility students need to integrate their studies into their lives without compromising the quality of academic training or the practical experiences essential for their professional growth.

This program combines both intellectual and practical approaches. Students explore a broad spectrum of religious traditions, fostering an environment of dialogue and understanding among various faiths and perspectives. Simultaneously, the program is designed for students to apply their learning in real-world contexts, such as churches, mosques, hospitals, and community organizations, thereby developing the skills necessary to lead and serve effectively.

What sets the Doctor of Divinity Program at World Theology University apart is its inclusive and diversified approach. Our students come from diverse religious, cultural, and professional backgrounds, enriching the collective learning experience. Whether students feel called to ministry, academic teaching, social justice, or leadership in nonprofit organizations, this program offers the academic, ethical, and practical tools necessary to fulfill their purpose.

Throughout the program, students embark on a personal and academic journey that challenges them to critically reflect on life’s big questions, the meaning of spirituality, and the role of religion in modern society. As they progress, they are supported by a team of dedicated faculty who are not only renowned scholars but also committed mentors invested in each student's personal and professional success.

This program also serves as an invitation to those wishing to explore religion from a rigorous academic perspective, whether for personal development or to enhance their contribution to the global community. Upon completing the program, graduates will be equipped not only with profound knowledge but also with the ability to effectively apply that knowledge in the world, whether in religious, academic, or other contexts where ethics and theology play a crucial role.

The Doctor of Divinity Program at World Theology University is designed with a clear and transformative vision, focused on developing spiritual, academic, and professional leaders who can apply their theological knowledge to address contemporary challenges. Through this program, students not only gain a deep understanding of religious traditions but also develop the skills needed to lead, guide, and serve effectively in their communities and beyond.

This involves in-depth exploration of sacred texts, religious history, ethics, and philosophy, all within a framework that promotes critical analysis and reflection. Additionally, the program seeks to develop pastoral and public speaking skills. Students are trained to communicate effectively, both in religious contexts and in the public sphere. These skills are essential for those called to serve in religious leadership roles, as well as for those who aim to make an impact in social justice organizations, education, activism, or any field where interfaith dialogue and ethics are fundamental.

Through this comparative study, students develop the ability to engage in dialogue with individuals from diverse backgrounds and traditions—a crucial skill in an increasingly globalized and interconnected world.

Graduates of the program will be equipped to tackle issues such as social justice, environmental sustainability, gender equity, and conflict resolution, using the intellectual and spiritual tools gained during their studies. This focus makes the Doctor of Divinity Program an ideal choice for those who wish to make a significant impact in their communities and the world, contributing to dialogue and action on the most pressing issues of our time.

The Doctor of Divinity Program at World Theology University is designed to be accessible and flexible while maintaining high academic standards. The program requirements are carefully structured to ensure that students acquire deep knowledge and complete practical training without compromising the flexibility needed to integrate their studies with personal or professional responsibilities. Below are the essential requirements for completing the program.

Duration and Academic Load: The program is designed to be completed in three years of full-time study. During this time, students must complete and pass a total of 24 courses, equivalent to 96 credits. The courses cover a wide range of study areas, from theology and comparative religious studies to pastoral leadership and ethics, offering a comprehensive education that combines theoretical learning with practical experience.

Core Courses: To ensure a solid foundation in theological studies, all students must complete several fundamental courses. These include "Introduction to Ministerial Studies," which provides a general overview of diverse religious and ministerial traditions, and "Theories and Methods in the Study of Religion," a key course teaching students the methodological tools needed to approach the academic study of religion. Completing "Creating Meaning: Theological Reflection on Ministerial Experience" is also mandatory, guiding students in theological reflection on ministerial practice.

Practical Experience: One of the most valuable components of the program is field-based practical experience. Students must complete two placements in religious or community institutions such as churches, synagogues, mosques, hospitals, or shelters. These experiences allow students to apply their knowledge in real-world situations, developing pastoral, leadership, and community support skills. The placements are supervised by experienced mentors who guide students as they navigate the challenges of working in pastoral or social service settings.

Specialization in Histories, Theologies, and Practices: Students must complete a minimum of 12 courses in specific areas related to the histories, theologies, and practices of different religious traditions. At least six of these courses must focus on histories and theologies, while three must be dedicated to scriptural interpretation. This structure ensures that students develop a deep and comparative understanding of different religious traditions, as well as the skills to critically and reflectively interpret sacred texts.

Language Requirements: Since understanding religious and theological texts often involves knowledge of ancient or contemporary languages, the program requires proficiency in at least one language relevant to theological studies. Students must complete three semesters of study in a biblical or theological language, such as Hebrew, Greek, Arabic, Sanskrit, or Latin. This linguistic training enables students to conduct more precise academic research and access key texts in their original languages.

Ministry Arts: To complement the academic and practical components of the program, students must complete three courses focused on Ministry Arts. These courses provide specialized training in areas such as preaching, congregational leadership, liturgical planning, and pastoral counseling, preparing students for leadership roles within their religious communities.

Senior Seminar and Written Work: In the final phase of the program, students must participate in the Senior Seminar, an advanced course where they reflect on their accumulated experiences throughout the program and prepare a written project synthesizing their learnings and theological approaches. This final project is an opportunity for students to demonstrate their research, critical thinking, and knowledge application skills in the context of a specific theological issue or topic.

Residency Requirement: Although the program is fully online, students are required to complete at least three courses during each of their first three consecutive terms. This ensures a consistent pace of study and allows students to build closer relationships with peers and faculty throughout the program.

Academic Performance: To maintain the program's academic quality, students must achieve a minimum grade of "B" in all their courses throughout the program. This requirement ensures that students continue their training with a rigorous and committed approach.

Student Handbook and Additional Policies: All students must familiarize themselves with the Student Handbook and the Field Education Policies of the School of Divinity at World University. These documents provide additional information on academic requirements, field placement policies, and resources available to students.

In summary, the program requirements are designed to offer comprehensive theological and pastoral training, ensuring that students acquire advanced academic knowledge and develop practical skills to serve effectively in various religious and community contexts. The balance between academic rigor, practical training, and online flexibility makes this program ideal for those seeking a doctoral degree in theology with a global and contemporary focus.
