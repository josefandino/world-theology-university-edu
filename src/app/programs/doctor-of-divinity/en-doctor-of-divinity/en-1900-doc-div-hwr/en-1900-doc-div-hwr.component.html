This course offers an in-depth historical and social analysis of the world's major religions, examining their emergence, evolution, mutual influences, and impact on civilizations. Through a comparative and interdisciplinary approach, students will explore the historical, political, and cultural contexts that shaped the most influential religious traditions, gaining an understanding of their role in human history and contemporary challenges.

Course Objectives

  • Understand the origins and historical development of the major world religions.
  • Analyze the interactions between religions and cultures throughout history.
  • Identify the social, political, and economic factors that influenced religious expansion.
  • Evaluate the impact of religions on the shaping of civilizations and historical movements.
  • Foster a critical and contextualized perspective on the global religious phenomenon.
  • Apply historical analysis to the understanding of contemporary religious dynamics.

Main Topics

  • Introduction to the comparative history of religions.
  • Origins and development of Hinduism, Buddhism, and East Asian religions.
  • Formation and expansion of Judaism, Christianity, and Islam.
  • Indigenous religions and traditional spiritualities.
  • Interactions among religions and the emergence of new religious movements.
  • Religion and power: influence on empires, states, and social change.
  • Religious conflicts, dialogues, and syncretisms throughout history.
  • The role of religions in addressing contemporary global challenges.

Methodology

The course will combine virtual lectures, analysis of primary and secondary historical sources, case studies, critical debates, and thematic research projects. Autonomous learning, critical reflection, and comparison among different religious traditions will be encouraged through a rigorous and contextualized approach.

Assessment

  • Active participation in historical-religious analysis forums (20%).
  • Critical essays on religious historical processes (25%).
  • Analysis of historical cases of religious interaction (30%).
  • Final research project on the historical impact of a religious tradition (15%).
  • Integrative exam on the history of world religions (10%).

Course Significance

This course is essential for understanding the decisive role of religions in human history. It enables students to develop a critical, informed, and respectful perspective on religious diversity and its influence on the cultural, social, and political shaping of the world, preparing them for analysis and engagement in contemporary global contexts.
