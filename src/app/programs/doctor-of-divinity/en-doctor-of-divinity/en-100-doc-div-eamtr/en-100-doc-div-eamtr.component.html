DOC-DIV-EAMTR-100 – Epistemology and Advanced Methodology in Theological Research

This course offers a rigorous deepening in the epistemological foundations and advanced methodologies applied to contemporary theological research. Students will explore the philosophical bases of theological knowledge, the various methodological currents, and their practical application in the design and execution of original research projects. The course promotes a critical and contextualized understanding of the processes of theological knowledge construction in dialogue with the current challenges of a global and diverse society.

Course Objectives

  • Understand the epistemological foundations underlying theological research.
  • Identify and analyze the main methodological approaches used in advanced theological research.
  • Develop skills to formulate pertinent and relevant research problems in the field of theology.
  • Apply qualitative and quantitative research methodologies to rigorous theological projects.
  • Critically evaluate existing theological research, identifying strengths, limitations, and contributions to knowledge.
  • Design and present an original theological research project with academic rigor and contextual relevance.

Main Topics

  • Epistemology of theological research: fundamental concepts and contemporary debates.
  • Methodological currents in theological research: historical and current perspectives.
  • Designing theological research: problem formulation, research questions, and hypotheses.
  • Qualitative methodologies: interviews, focus groups, and content analysis in theology.
  • Quantitative methodologies: surveys, statistical analysis, and their application in theological studies.
  • Ethics in theological research: principles, challenges, and best practices.
  • Academic writing and publication of theological research findings.
  • Presentation and defense of research projects in international academic contexts.

Methodology

The course will be delivered through synchronous virtual lectures, online discussion forums, critical analysis of case studies, reflective essay writing, and the progressive development of an individual research project. Autonomous and collaborative learning will be fostered through specialized readings, methodological workshops, and personalized academic mentoring.

Assessment

  • Active participation in discussion forums (20%).
  • Critical analysis of selected theological research (20%).
  • Development of essays on epistemological foundations (20%).
  • Presentation of a theological research project proposal (20%).
  • Final research project with virtual defense (20%).

Course Significance

This course is essential within the Doctorate in Divinities, as it provides the conceptual and methodological foundations necessary for the production of original and relevant theological knowledge. Training in epistemology and advanced methodology not only strengthens the student’s academic rigor but also prepares them to contribute critically, ethically, and transformatively to the global theological discourse, responding to the needs of diverse and dynamic cultural contexts.
