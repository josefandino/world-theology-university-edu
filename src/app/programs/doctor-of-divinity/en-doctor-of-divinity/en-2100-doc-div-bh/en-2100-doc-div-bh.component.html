This course provides advanced training in Biblical Hebrew, aimed at achieving mastery of the original language of the Old Testament texts. Students will develop competencies in reading, translation, grammatical and exegetical analysis of Biblical Hebrew texts. The course emphasizes a deep understanding of linguistic structures, semantic nuances, and the literary richness of Biblical Hebrew, preparing students for rigorous academic work and more precise theological interpretation.

Course Objectives

  • Master the fundamental grammatical structures of Biblical Hebrew.
  • Read and accurately translate selected Old Testament texts in their original language.
  • Critically analyze the linguistic and literary nuances present in Hebrew texts.
  • Apply philological tools to the exegetical and theological study of Scripture.
  • Develop skills for direct interpretation without reliance on modern translations.
  • Foster a critical and profound appreciation of the Hebrew textual tradition.

Main Topics

  • The Hebrew alphabet and Masoretic vocalization.
  • Verbal morphology: roots, conjugations, and basic patterns.
  • Nominal grammar: nouns, adjectives, prepositions, and pronouns.
  • Syntactic constructions and sentence structures in Biblical Hebrew.
  • Reading, analyzing, and translating narrative, poetic, and prophetic texts.
  • Introduction to textual criticism and Hebrew biblical manuscripts.
  • Basic exegesis of significant Old Testament passages.
  • Digital tools and contemporary resources for the study of Biblical Hebrew.

Methodology

The course will combine virtual lectures, reading and translation exercises, practical workshops in grammatical analysis, guided exegetical studies, and the use of specialized digital tools. Progressive learning, active participation, and constant practice will be promoted to achieve an advanced command of the language.

Assessment

  • Participation in practical reading and translation classes (20%).
  • Regular exercises in grammatical and syntactic analysis (25%).
  • Written tests in translation and linguistic comprehension (30%).
  • Final project: exegesis of a Biblical text in Hebrew (15%).
  • Mastery exam on linguistic structures and advanced translation (10%).

Course Significance

Mastery of Biblical Hebrew is essential for any scholar or spiritual leader aspiring to interpret the Old Testament with rigor and depth. This course equips students to access the texts directly in their original language, strengthening their capacity for theological, exegetical, and pastoral analysis, and contributing to a more authentic and enriched understanding of biblical revelation.
