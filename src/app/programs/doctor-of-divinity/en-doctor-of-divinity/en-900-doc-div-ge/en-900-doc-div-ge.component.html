This course explores the critical application of theological ethical principles to contemporary moral dilemmas in a globalized world. Students will analyze the foundations of Christian ethics, evaluate various ethical approaches from a theological perspective, and develop thoughtful responses to challenges such as social justice, peace, human rights, environmental stewardship, and globalization. The course promotes informed and responsible ethical engagement within multicultural and pluralistic settings.

Course Objectives

  • Understand the fundamental principles of Christian ethics in dialogue with other ethical traditions.
  • Critically analyze contemporary moral dilemmas from a theological perspective.
  • Apply theological ethical principles to complex situations in global and local contexts.
  • Develop critical, contextualized, and socially engaged ethical thinking.
  • Foster the ability to formulate relevant ethical proposals for social transformation.
  • Integrate theological reflection with practical commitment to justice, peace, and human dignity.

Main Topics

  • Biblical and theological foundations of Christian ethics.
  • Ethical models in the Christian tradition and their historical development.
  • Ethics and human rights: challenges and theological perspectives.
  • Social justice, poverty, and global responsibility.
  • Ethics of peace and conflict resolution in violent contexts.
  • Environmental ethics and care for creation.
  • Bioethics and contemporary dilemmas: life, health, and technology.
  • Constructing a global ethic: interreligious dialogue and international cooperation.

Methodology

The course will be conducted through synchronous virtual classes, contemporary ethical case studies, discussion forums, critical analysis workshops, and practical application projects. Active participation, interdisciplinary dialogue, and the development of contextualized ethical proposals addressing current issues will be strongly encouraged.

Assessment

  • Participation in discussion forums and ethical analysis (20%).
  • Case studies: analysis and resolution from a theological perspective (25%).
  • Critical essays on global ethical dilemmas (30%).
  • Final project proposing an ethical application to a real-world context (15%).
  • Integrative exam on ethical and theological knowledge (10%).

Course Significance

This course is essential for the formation of leaders capable of integrating theological ethical principles into contemporary social reflection and action. It provides conceptual and practical tools to face the moral challenges of a globalized world, promoting an ethics committed to justice, human dignity, sustainability, and the building of more just and compassionate societies from a theological perspective.
