DOC-DIV-IE-1100 – Interreligious Ethics: Dialogue and Cooperation

This course promotes the study and practice of interreligious ethical dialogue as a fundamental tool for addressing contemporary global challenges. Through an academic and practical approach, students will analyze shared ethical principles among diverse religious traditions and explore ways of active cooperation on issues such as peace, human rights, the environment, and social justice. The course emphasizes mutual respect, intercultural understanding, and global ethical engagement.

Course Objectives

  • Understand the foundations of ethics within the world’s major religious traditions.
  • Analyze common ethical principles that facilitate interreligious dialogue and cooperation.
  • Develop skills for respectful and constructive dialogue among different faith traditions.
  • Evaluate models of interreligious cooperation in response to global challenges such as peace, justice, and sustainability.
  • Apply concepts of interreligious ethics to contexts of dialogue, mediation, and practical collaboration.
  • Foster critical awareness and ethical commitment in multicultural and multireligious settings.

Main Topics

  • Ethical foundations in Christianity, Judaism, Islam, Hinduism, and Buddhism.
  • Universal ethical principles and their identification across various religions.
  • Theory and practice of interreligious dialogue: models and strategies.
  • Ethics of respect, hospitality, and cooperation in pluralistic contexts.
  • Case studies of interreligious cooperation in social justice, peace, and sustainability.
  • Challenges and tensions in contemporary interreligious dialogue.
  • Theological and ethical perspectives on religious pluralism.
  • Interreligious action projects in local and global contexts.

Methodology

The course will be conducted through interactive virtual classes, interreligious dialogue forums, analysis of practical cases, ethical mediation workshops, and collaborative project development. Experiential learning, critical reflection, and active participation in interreligious cooperation simulations will be strongly encouraged.

Assessment

  • Active participation in interreligious discussion forums (20%).
  • Critical analysis of dialogue and cooperation models (25%).
  • Thematic essays on common ethical principles among religions (30%).
  • Final project proposing an interreligious cooperation initiative (15%).
  • Integrative exam on ethics and interreligious dialogue knowledge (10%).

Course Significance

This course is fundamental for preparing leaders capable of building bridges of dialogue and cooperation in a world characterized by religious and cultural diversity. By integrating ethical reflection with interreligious action, students will be equipped to promote peace, justice, and mutual respect at both local and international levels, contributing to the construction of more inclusive, compassionate, and sustainable societies.
