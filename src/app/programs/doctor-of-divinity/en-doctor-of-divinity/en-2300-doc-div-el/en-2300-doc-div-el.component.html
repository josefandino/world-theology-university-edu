DOC-DIV-EL-2300 – Ecclesiastical Latin

This course provides solid training in Ecclesiastical Latin, focusing on the comprehension, critical reading, and analysis of historical, theological, and doctrinal texts from the Christian tradition. Students will develop linguistic competencies to access primary sources in theology, liturgy, and Christian doctrine directly, strengthening their academic interpretation skills and deepening their connection to the Church’s intellectual heritage.

Course Objectives

  • Master the grammatical and syntactic structures of Ecclesiastical Latin.
  • Read, translate, and analyze liturgical, patristic, and doctrinal texts in Latin.
  • Understand the technical and theological vocabulary specific to Christian Latin.
  • Apply critical translation techniques and philological analysis to ecclesiastical documents.
  • Recognize the influence of Latin on theology, liturgy, and canon law.
  • Foster an appreciation of Latin as a means of transmitting Christian faith and culture.

Main Topics

  • Alphabet, pronunciation, and particularities of Ecclesiastical Latin.
  • Basic grammar: morphology of nouns, verbs, adjectives, and pronouns.
  • Reading and analysis of simple and complex sentences.
  • Theological, liturgical, and legal vocabulary in Christian Latin.
  • Critical reading of classical texts: the Vulgate, Church Fathers, conciliar documents.
  • Principles of textual criticism applied to ecclesiastical writings.
  • Translation and analysis of hymns, prayers, and liturgical formulas.
  • Digital tools for advanced study of Ecclesiastical Latin.

Methodology

The course will combine virtual lectures, critical reading and translation exercises, linguistic analysis workshops, discussion forums on patristic and doctrinal texts, and philological research projects. Progressive learning and practical application of acquired skills will be strongly emphasized.

Assessment

  • Participation in reading, translation, and textual analysis activities (20%).
  • Regular exercises in grammar and syntax (25%).
  • Critical essays on liturgical or doctrinal texts in Latin (30%).
  • Final project: translation and theological commentary on a classical text (15%).
  • Final exam assessing linguistic competencies in Ecclesiastical Latin (10%).

Course Significance

Mastery of Ecclesiastical Latin allows students to access foundational sources of Christian thought, spirituality, and theology directly. This course strengthens academic and pastoral formation, equipping students for rigorous study of patristic, liturgical, and doctrinal texts, which are essential for advanced theological research and for preserving the living tradition of the Church.
