DOC-DIV-CTIA-1700 – Comparative Theology: An Interreligious Approach

This course offers a comparative study of the world's major religious traditions, focusing on essential theological topics such as the conception of God, salvation, creation, and the afterlife. Through an interreligious approach, students will explore similarities, differences, and points of dialogue among diverse theologies, developing a critical and respectful understanding of global religious diversity. The course promotes comparative analysis as a tool to enrich theological reflection and foster interreligious cooperation.

Course Objectives

  • Understand the fundamental theological concepts across various religious traditions.
  • Analyze theological approaches to essential topics from a comparative perspective.
  • Develop a critical, respectful, and contextualized perspective on global theological diversity.
  • Identify opportunities for dialogue and mutual enrichment among different faith traditions.
  • Foster analytical skills for the comparative study of religious and theological systems.
  • Apply comparative analysis to strengthen contemporary interreligious dialogue.

Main Topics

  • Introduction to comparative theology: foundations and methodologies.
  • Conceptions of the divine: God, gods, and the Absolute across various religions.
  • Theological anthropology: understandings of human nature in different traditions.
  • Notions of salvation, liberation, and spiritual fulfillment.
  • Theologies of creation and the human-nature relationship.
  • Views of the afterlife: heaven, nirvana, resurrection, reincarnation.
  • Comparative study of sacred texts and doctrinal sources.
  • Contemporary implications of interreligious theological dialogue.

Methodology

The course will combine virtual lectures, comparative analysis of sacred texts and doctrines, interreligious debates in academic forums, case studies, and thematic research projects. Critical thinking, respect for diversity, and practical application of acquired knowledge in intercultural dialogue contexts will be strongly encouraged.

Assessment

  • Participation in debates and forums on comparative theological analysis (20%).
  • Critical analysis of theological texts and concepts from different religions (25%).
  • Comparative essays on essential theological topics (30%).
  • Final research project in comparative theology (15%).
  • Integrative exam on interreligious theological knowledge (10%).

Course Significance

This course is fundamental for understanding the richness and complexity of theological expressions in today’s world. Through comparative study, students will strengthen their capacity for dialogue, cooperation, and critical analysis, contributing to the building of deeper, more respectful interreligious relationships committed to the values of peace, justice, and mutual understanding at a global level.
