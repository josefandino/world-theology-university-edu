The Doctorate in Divinity consists of advanced courses, specialized seminars, and practical opportunities designed to strengthen high-level research competencies, rigorous theological analysis, and ministerial skills within globalized and multicultural contexts. The program includes core areas enabling specialization in specific disciplines, addressing both theoretical knowledge and its practical application:

DOC-DIV-EAMTR-100 - Epistemology and Advanced Methodology in Theological Research - In-depth exploration of epistemological frameworks and methodological approaches applied to the development of original theological research.

DOC-DIV-DRL-200 - Doctoral Research Seminar: Thesis Proposal Development - Guidance and preparation for effectively formulating doctoral proposals and rigorous methodological designs.

DOC-DIV-DSOTD-300 - Doctoral Supervision and Original Thesis Development - Specialized mentorship throughout the doctoral research process up to the successful completion and presentation of the doctoral thesis.

DOC-DIV-HCAST-400 - Critical Hermeneutics and Analysis of Sacred Texts - Advanced application of critical methods for the deep and contextualized interpretation of biblical texts.

DOC-DIV-IHBS-500 - Introduction to Hebrew Bible Studies - Detailed analysis of the historical, literary, and theological context of Old Testament texts.

DOC-DIV-NTCM-600 - New Testament: Contexts and Meaning - Advanced interpretation of the New Testament within its historical-cultural and theological context.

DOC-DIV-ASSCT-700 - Advanced Seminar in Systematic and Contemporary Theology - In-depth study of key contemporary theological themes and their implications for ministerial practice.

DOC-DIV-ST-800 - Systematic Theology - Examination of major theological topics such as God, salvation, and Christian life from contemporary perspectives and various religious traditions.

DOC-DIV-GE-900 - Global Ethics and Social Commitment from a Theological Perspective - Critical application of theological ethical principles to contemporary global moral dilemmas.

DOC-DIV-CESA-1000 - Christian Ethics and Social Application - Exploration of Christian ethical principles and their applications in areas such as social justice, bioethics, and human rights.

DOC-DIV-IE-1100 - Interreligious Ethics: Dialogue and Cooperation - Promoting ethical dialogue among religions to collaboratively address common global challenges.

DOC-DIV-TLSMM-1200 - Transformational Leadership and Strategic Ministerial Management - Advanced training in ethical, strategic, and transformational leadership for ministries and religious organizations.

DOC-DIV-PLTP-1300 - Pastoral Leadership: Theory and Practice - Development of skills required for effective leadership in diverse religious communities.

DOC-DIV-SCFTTA-1400 - Spiritual Counseling: Foundations and Therapeutic Approaches - Specialized training for providing emotional and spiritual support in various pastoral contexts.

DOC-DIV-LICSR-1500 - Leadership in Crisis Contexts: Spiritual and Community Response - Practical training in spiritual leadership during social or humanitarian crisis situations.

DOC-DIV-IID-1600 - Interreligious and Intercultural Dialogue: Global Challenges and Opportunities - Examination of interreligious dialogue as a means for mutual understanding and international cooperation.

DOC-DIV-CTIA-1700 - Comparative Theology: An Interreligious Approach - Comparative study of major world religions on essential theological themes.

DOC-DIV-RRP-1800 - Religious Rituals and Practices: A Comparative Approach - Analysis of religious practices across various traditions, and their cultural and social implications.

DOC-DIV-HWR-1900 - History of World Religions - Comprehensive historical and social analysis of the world’s most influential religions.

DOC-DIV-MMRR-2000 - Modern Religious Movements and Reforms - Critical examination of reform movements within contemporary religious traditions.

DOC-DIV-BH-2100 - Biblical Hebrew - Advanced mastery of the original language of Old Testament texts.

DOC-DIV-BG-2200 - Biblical Greek - In-depth training in critical and exegetical reading of the New Testament in its original language.

DOC-DIV-EL-2300 - Ecclesiastical Latin - Linguistic proficiency in Latin, focusing on historical and doctrinal texts within the Christian tradition.

DOC-DIV-PPRC-2400 - Practicum in Religious Communities - Direct application of academic knowledge in real ministerial contexts under specialized academic supervision.

DOC-DIV-TRS-2500 - Theological Research Seminar - Development and presentation of original academic research in specific areas of theological interest.

DOC-DIV-SOID-2600 - Seminar on Interreligious Dialogue - Academic forum for deep analysis of contemporary issues in interreligious dialogue.

DOC-DIV-TOL-2700 - Theology of Liberation - Critical analysis of theology’s role in addressing contemporary challenges of social justice and equity.

DOC-DIV-SAC-2800 - Spirituality and Climate Change - Study of spiritual and religious responsibilities regarding global environmental challenges.

DOC-DIV-EC-2900 - Ecumenism: Collaboration among Churches - Theoretical and practical exploration of cooperation among diverse Christian denominations.

DOC-DIV-GMC-3000 - Global Mission and Culture - Advanced training for effective Christian mission within multicultural contexts.

This comprehensive suite of courses allows students to fully develop their academic and research potential, strengthening advanced competencies to exercise ethical and transformational leadership in diverse international and multicultural settings.
