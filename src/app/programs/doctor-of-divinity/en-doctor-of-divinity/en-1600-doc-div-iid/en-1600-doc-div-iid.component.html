This course provides an in-depth analysis of interreligious and intercultural dialogue as a fundamental tool for mutual understanding, global peace, and international cooperation. Through an academic and practical approach, students will explore the principles, challenges, and opportunities arising from the encounter between diverse religious traditions and cultures in a globalized world. The course emphasizes the development of intercultural communication skills, mediation, and ethical collaboration in multicultural contexts.

Course Objectives

  • Understand the theoretical and theological foundations of interreligious and intercultural dialogue.
  • Analyze major historical and contemporary challenges in interreligious relations.
  • Develop skills for respectful communication and mediation in contexts of religious and cultural diversity.
  • Evaluate successful initiatives of interreligious cooperation at local and international levels.
  • Foster attitudes of respect, openness, and ethical commitment in response to religious and cultural pluralism.
  • Design dialogue and cooperation projects that address current global challenges.

Main Topics

  • Theories and models of interreligious and intercultural dialogue.
  • History of interreligious encounters and conflicts.
  • Ethical foundations of dialogue: respect, reciprocity, and cooperation.
  • Intercultural communication and resolution of religious conflicts.
  • Contemporary initiatives in interreligious dialogue: case studies.
  • The role of religions in building peace and social justice.
  • Challenges of pluralism, tolerance, and coexistence in multicultural societies.
  • Design of interreligious and intercultural cooperation projects.

Methodology

The course will combine virtual lectures, case study analyses, intercultural mediation skill workshops, critical discussion forums, and the development of interreligious initiative projects. Active learning, ethical reflection, and practical engagement in real and simulated scenarios will be promoted.

Assessment

  • Participation in interreligious dialogue workshops and forums (20%).
  • Critical analysis of interreligious cooperation initiatives (25%).
  • Thematic essays on global challenges and opportunities for dialogue (30%).
  • Final project: design of an international dialogue and cooperation proposal (15%).
  • Integrative exam on interreligious and intercultural dialogue knowledge (10%).

Course Significance

This course is essential for preparing leaders capable of promoting peace, cooperation, and understanding in global contexts characterized by religious and cultural diversity. It develops critical and practical competencies that enable students to build bridges between traditions and cultures, actively contributing to the construction of a more just, inclusive, and compassionate world.
