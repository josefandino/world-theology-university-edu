This seminar offers an advanced study of the central themes of systematic theology in dialogue with contemporary trends. Students will analyze foundational doctrines from both classical and current perspectives, evaluating their theological, ecclesial, and social relevance in a globalized world. The course fosters the development of a critical, contextualized theology oriented toward ministerial practice, with an emphasis on rigorous analysis and ethical commitment.

Course Objectives

  • Analyze in depth the principal themes of classical and contemporary systematic theology.
  • Critically evaluate emerging theological currents and their implications for Christian faith and praxis.
  • Develop skills to articulate pertinent theological responses to current challenges.
  • Integrate diverse theological perspectives within a framework of systematic and contextualized reflection.
  • Apply theological principles to ministerial practice in pluralistic and dynamic contexts.
  • Foster creative, rigorous, and ethically committed theological thinking.

Main Topics

  • Introduction to systematic theology: history, method, and function.
  • Fundamental doctrines: God, Christ, the Holy Spirit, salvation, and eschatology.
  • Contemporary trends: contextual, liberationist, feminist, and intercultural theologies.
  • Theology and ethics in a globalized world: justice, peace, and reconciliation.
  • Contemporary ecclesiology: the identity, mission, and relevance of the Church today.
  • Theological challenges in light of science, technology, and emerging spiritualities.
  • Ministerial practices grounded in critical theological reflection.
  • The future of systematic theology in a pluralistic and postmodern context.

Methodology

The course will be delivered through interactive virtual seminars, academic debates, critical analysis of theological texts, the development of reflective essays, and practical application projects. Interdisciplinary dialogue and contextualized theological reflection will be encouraged, combining autonomous learning with active participation in specialized forums.

Assessment

  • Active participation in seminars and theological debates (20%).
  • Critical analysis of contemporary theological works (25%).
  • Thematic essays on fundamental doctrines (30%).
  • Final project applying theology to a specific ministerial context (15%).
  • Theological integration exam (10%).

Course Significance

This seminar is essential for the formation of theological leaders capable of articulating a relevant and transformative faith amid contemporary challenges. By integrating the systematic study of doctrines with present-day concerns, the course equips students to exercise critical, creative, and ethically committed ministerial leadership, contributing to the transformation of the Church and society in an ever-changing world.
