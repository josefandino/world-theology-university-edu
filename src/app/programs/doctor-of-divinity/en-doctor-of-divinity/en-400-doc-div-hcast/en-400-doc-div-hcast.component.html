This course offers advanced training in critical hermeneutics, focusing on the in-depth analysis and interpretation of biblical and other sacred texts. Through an interdisciplinary approach, students will apply contemporary exegetical methods to understand both the original meaning and the current contextual relevance of sacred writings. The course promotes critical reading, intercultural dialogue, and the integration of historical, literary, and theological perspectives, preparing students for rigorous and contextualized interpretation in global settings.

Course Objectives

  • Understand the main approaches and methods of critical hermeneutics applied to sacred texts.
  • Apply advanced techniques of historical, literary, and theological exegesis in the analysis of biblical texts.
  • Develop skills to interpret sacred texts in dialogue with diverse cultural and contemporary contexts.
  • Critically analyze various interpretative traditions and their impact on contemporary theological understanding.
  • Foster the ability to produce rigorous and relevant academic interpretations.
  • Integrate an ethical and contextualized perspective in the interpretation of religious texts.

Main Topics

  • Foundations of critical hermeneutics and its historical development.
  • Contemporary models of biblical interpretation: literary, historical, and sociocultural approaches.
  • Exegesis of key texts from the Old and New Testaments.
  • The influence of cultural context on the interpretation of sacred texts.
  • Intercultural and interreligious hermeneutics: challenges and opportunities.
  • Analysis of the reception of biblical texts across different theological traditions.
  • Ethical and hermeneutical issues in the interpretation of controversial texts.
  • Development of hermeneutical studies applied to contemporary contexts.

Methodology

The course will combine virtual lectures, critical text analysis, case studies, academic forum debates, and advanced interpretation workshops. Students will work on the development of interpretative essays and research projects that integrate hermeneutical theory and practice, supported by personalized mentoring and continuous assessment.

Assessment

  • Active participation in discussion forums and hermeneutical workshops (20%).
  • Critical analysis of selected sacred texts (20%).
  • Interpretative essays on specific topics (30%).
  • Final project on applied hermeneutical analysis (20%).
  • Final exam on methodological understanding and application (10%).

Course Significance

This course is fundamental for the formation of theologians capable of interpreting sacred texts with critical depth, contextual sensitivity, and ethical commitment. Advanced hermeneutical competence enables students to offer relevant interpretations to contemporary challenges, enriching interreligious dialogue and contributing to global theological reflection from an academic perspective of excellence.
