This seminar offers an advanced academic space for the critical analysis of contemporary issues related to interreligious dialogue. Students will explore theoretical and practical challenges in religious encounters within a globalized world, developing skills in analysis, mediation, and the formulation of proposals that foster mutual understanding, cooperation, and peacebuilding in diverse contexts.

Course Objectives

  • Critically analyze the main challenges and tensions in contemporary interreligious dialogue.
  • Evaluate theological, philosophical, and practical approaches to interfaith dialogue.
  • Develop skills in mediation, active listening, and intercultural communication.
  • Propose innovative strategies for interreligious cooperation at local and global levels.
  • Theologically reflect on religious pluralism and its impact on Christian mission and witness.
  • Foster an ethical and practical commitment to the promotion of peace and interreligious justice.

Main Topics

  • Theological and philosophical foundations of interreligious dialogue.
  • Pluralism, exclusivism, and inclusivism: contemporary theological perspectives.
  • The role of religions in conflicts and peace processes.
  • Successes and failures in interreligious dialogue initiatives.
  • Models of intercultural mediation and resolution of religious tensions.
  • Ethical and political challenges of interreligious dialogue in the twenty-first century.
  • Interreligious dialogue in contexts of migration and globalization.
  • Designing interreligious cooperation projects in contemporary settings.

Methodology

The seminar will combine virtual lectures, case study analysis, critical discussion forums, workshops for designing interreligious initiatives, mediation simulations, and collaborative project development. Active participation, critical thinking, and reflective practice will be strongly encouraged.

Assessment

  • Active participation in seminars and dialogue workshops (20%).
  • Critical analysis of interreligious dialogue case studies (25%).
  • Essays on contemporary issues in interreligious dialogue (30%).
  • Final project: design of an interreligious cooperation strategy (15%).
  • Evaluation of mediation and intercultural communication skills (10%).

Course Significance

This seminar is essential for preparing leaders, theologians, and agents of change capable of building bridges of understanding and collaboration in a world characterized by religious and cultural diversity. It strengthens both academic and practical competencies for interreligious dialogue committed to peace, justice, and mutual respect in local and international contexts.
