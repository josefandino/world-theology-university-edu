This course offers a comparative analysis of rituals and religious practices across various world traditions, exploring their theological, cultural, and social significance. Students will examine rites of initiation, worship, purification, celebration, and transition, gaining an understanding of their role in shaping religious and communal identities. The course fosters a critical and respectful view of ritual diversity and its impact on contemporary social and cultural contexts.

Course Objectives

  • Understand the theological and sociocultural meaning of religious rituals.
  • Analyze religious practices comparatively across different world traditions.
  • Evaluate the role of rituals in the formation of individual and collective identities.
  • Interpret the symbolic, spiritual, and social dimensions of religious practices.
  • Foster critical respect for the diversity of global ritual expressions.
  • Apply acquired knowledge to the analysis of contemporary intercultural dynamics.

Main Topics

  • Theories on the origin, function, and meaning of religious rituals.
  • Rites of passage: birth, coming of age, marriage, and death in various religions.
  • Comparative practices of worship, prayer, and meditation.
  • Religious festivals: significance, practices, and cultural adaptation.
  • Rites of purification, penance, and spiritual renewal.
  • Social and political dimensions of rituals: cohesion, resistance, and transformation.
  • Contemporary rituals: transformations and re-significations in the modern world.
  • Case studies: analysis of rituals in intercultural contexts.

Methodology

The course will combine virtual lectures, case studies, comparative analysis of ritual practices, research projects, and critical discussion forums. Reflective learning, interdisciplinary analysis, and the practical application of knowledge in multicultural settings will be encouraged.

Assessment

  • Active participation in forums and ritual case analysis (20%).
  • Critical essays on comparative religious practices (25%).
  • Analysis of case studies on specific rituals (30%).
  • Final research project on a ritual practice and its sociocultural context (15%).
  • Integrative exam on rituals and religious practices (10%).

Course Significance

This course is essential for understanding the profound significance of religious practices in the spiritual, social, and cultural life of human communities. Through comparative study, students will develop analytical skills and a critical sensitivity to global ritual diversity, contributing to a deeper understanding of the religious phenomenon and its impact on contemporary contexts.
