This course addresses the connection between spirituality, religious traditions, and engagement with global environmental challenges, particularly climate change. Students will analyze theological, ethical, and spiritual perspectives on the care of creation, developing a critical and proactive awareness of ecological responsibility from a faith-based standpoint. The course seeks to inspire a transformative spirituality that promotes sustainability, environmental justice, and respect for life.

Course Objectives

  • Understand the theological and spiritual foundations for the care of creation across various religious traditions.
  • Critically analyze the role of religion in the global response to climate change.
  • Develop an ecological spirituality committed to sustainability and environmental justice.
  • Identify interreligious initiatives and actions addressing contemporary environmental challenges.
  • Formulate awareness-raising and ecological action projects from a spiritual perspective.
  • Foster ethical and spiritual leadership in environmental protection.

Main Topics

  • Biblical and theological foundations for the care of creation.
  • Ecotheology and contemporary ecological spirituality.
  • Impact of climate change: ethical and spiritual challenges.
  • Religious and interreligious responses to climate change.
  • Environmental justice, ecospirituality, and the rights of nature.
  • Religious movements for sustainability and social change.
  • Designing spiritual and ecological action projects within faith communities.
  • Spirituality, responsible consumption, and sustainable lifestyles.

Methodology

The course will combine virtual lectures, critical analysis of religious and ecological documents, case studies, discussion forums, ecological project design workshops, and practical community awareness projects. Reflective learning, practical commitment, and transformative action will be strongly encouraged.

Assessment

  • Active participation in ecological reflection forums and workshops (20%).
  • Critical essays on spirituality and climate change (25%).
  • Case study analysis of religious responses to climate change (30%).
  • Final project: design of an ecological awareness campaign (15%).
  • Integrative exam on ecological spirituality and environmental commitment (10%).

Course Significance

This course is fundamental for forming spiritual and theological leaders who integrate the care of creation into their reflection and practice. It promotes an active, ethical, and committed spirituality in the face of contemporary environmental challenges, contributing to the building of more conscious, responsible, and solidarity-driven communities dedicated to the protection of the planet.
