This course explores the foundations of Christian ethics and its practical application in key areas such as social justice, bioethics, and human rights. Through the analysis of biblical and theological principles, students will develop a critical and contextualized understanding of contemporary ethical challenges, learning to formulate relevant ethical responses that promote human dignity, equity, and solidarity in a diverse and globalized world.

Course Objectives

  • Understand the fundamental ethical principles of the Christian tradition.
  • Critically analyze the relevance of Christian ethics to current social challenges.
  • Apply Christian ethical criteria to issues of social justice, bioethics, and human rights.
  • Develop skills for ethical reflection and well-founded decision-making.
  • Foster active ethical engagement in favor of justice, peace, and human dignity.
  • Integrate Christian faith with transformative ethical action in local and global contexts.

Main Topics

  • Biblical and theological foundations of Christian ethics.
  • Human dignity and the image of God in social ethics.
  • Christian ethics and social justice: poverty, exclusion, and solidarity.
  • Christian bioethics: life, health, technology, and responsibility.
  • Ethics of human rights from a Christian perspective.
  • Contemporary challenges: migration, environment, and cultural diversity.
  • Christian responsibility in building just societies.
  • Practical application cases of Christian ethics in contemporary scenarios.

Methodology

The course will be delivered through interactive virtual classes, analysis of real ethical cases, academic forum debates, critical readings, and the development of practical application projects. Autonomous learning, deep ethical reflection, and the articulation of creative and informed responses to contemporary dilemmas will be strongly encouraged.

Assessment

  • Active participation in discussion forums and ethical workshops (20%).
  • Critical analysis of applied ethical cases (25%).
  • Essays on ethical principles and their current relevance (30%).
  • Final project proposing an ethical response to a specific social challenge (15%).
  • Written exam integrating ethical and theological knowledge (10%).

Course Significance

This course is crucial for the formation of theological leaders committed to social transformation based on Christian principles. It provides conceptual and practical tools to address contemporary ethical dilemmas, promoting responsible, compassionate, and transformative action in favor of human dignity, social justice, and respect for life in all its dimensions.
