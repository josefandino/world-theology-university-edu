DOC-DIV-BG-2200 – Biblical Greek

This course offers advanced study in Biblical Greek, focusing on critical reading, precise translation, and exegetical analysis of New Testament texts in their original language. Students will deepen their understanding of grammatical structures, semantic nuances, and literary forms of Koine Greek, enabling a more rigorous and faithful theological interpretation of Christianity's foundational texts.

Course Objectives

  • Master the grammatical and syntactic structures of Biblical Greek.
  • Read, translate, and critically analyze selected New Testament texts in Greek.
  • Understand the linguistic and stylistic nuances present in Koine Greek.
  • Apply exegetical techniques based on direct analysis of the original text.
  • Utilize specialized philological and lexical tools in the study of the New Testament.
  • Foster theologically responsible and contextualized reading of the biblical text.

Main Topics

  • Greek alphabet, pronunciation, and accentuation rules.
  • Verbal morphology: tenses, moods, voices, and aspects.
  • Nominal morphology: declensions of nouns, adjectives, and pronouns.
  • Complex syntactic constructions in New Testament Greek.
  • Critical reading and translation of the Gospels, Pauline Letters, and Revelation.
  • Principles of textual criticism and philological analysis of the New Testament.
  • Introduction to specialized digital and lexical resources for Biblical Greek.
  • Applied exegesis of significant passages within their linguistic and theological context.

Methodology

The course will combine intensive virtual classes, practical translation exercises, grammatical and syntactic analysis workshops, exegetical studies of key passages, and the use of digital tools. Progressive learning, autonomous work, and active participation in critical translation activities will be strongly emphasized.

Assessment

  • Participation in reading and textual analysis exercises (20%).
  • Tests on grammatical mastery and text translation (25%).
  • Exegetical essays applying original text analysis (30%).
  • Final project: critical exegesis of a New Testament passage (15%).
  • Final integrative exam on reading and analysis in Biblical Greek (10%).

Course Significance

Knowledge of Biblical Greek is indispensable for serious and profound interpretation of the New Testament. This course equips students to read and analyze texts in their original language, promoting a more precise, well-founded, and enriching theological approach—essential for academic, ministerial, and pastoral leadership in a global context.
