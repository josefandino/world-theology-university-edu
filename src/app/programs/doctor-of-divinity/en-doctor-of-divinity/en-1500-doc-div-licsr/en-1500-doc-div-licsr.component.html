This course provides practical and theological training in spiritual leadership to address social, humanitarian, and pastoral crises. Students will acquire strategic competencies to offer spiritual support, strengthen community resilience, and lead effective and ethical responses to emergencies, disasters, or social conflicts. The course emphasizes the integration of theological principles, crisis management skills, and a commitment to solidarity in challenging contexts.

Course Objectives

  • Understand the theological foundations of leadership in times of crisis.
  • Develop practical skills for providing spiritual accompaniment during emergencies.
  • Apply crisis management principles within religious and social communities.
  • Promote strategies for spiritual and community resilience.
  • Identify key ethical and cultural aspects in crisis interventions.
  • Design spiritual response plans adapted to various types of crises.

Main Topics

  • Theology of suffering, hope, and resilience in times of crisis.
  • Principles of pastoral and community leadership during emergencies.
  • Trauma psychology and spiritual accompaniment.
  • Crisis management: assessment, planning, and rapid response.
  • Effective communication and information management in crisis contexts.
  • Pastoral ethics and social responsibility in emergency settings.
  • Interinstitutional and interreligious collaboration in community responses.
  • Designing community preparedness and post-crisis recovery plans.

Methodology

The course will combine virtual classes, crisis response simulations, real case studies, action plan design workshops, ethical debates, and practical application projects. Teamwork, critical analysis, and experiential learning will be encouraged to ensure comprehensive training.

Assessment

  • Active participation in simulations and critical analysis forums (20%).
  • Case analysis of leadership in social and humanitarian crises (25%).
  • Theological and practical reflection essays on leadership in crisis contexts (30%).
  • Final project: design of a spiritual and community response plan (15%).
  • Integrative exam on theoretical and practical knowledge (10%).

Course Significance

This course is essential for preparing leaders capable of providing guidance, comfort, and hope in crisis and emergency contexts. It forms professionals who integrate solid theological training with practical leadership, crisis management skills, and ethical commitment, contributing to the resilience and reconstruction of communities affected by social and humanitarian adversities.
