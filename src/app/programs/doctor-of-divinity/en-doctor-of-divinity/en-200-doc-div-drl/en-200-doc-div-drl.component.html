DOC-DIV-DRL-200 – Doctoral Research Seminar: Thesis Proposal Development

This specialized seminar provides an intensive academic environment for the preparation, formulation, and design of doctoral thesis proposals in the field of theology. Through rigorous methodological and conceptual guidance, students will develop skills to define relevant research topics, structure coherent projects, and establish a solid methodological framework. The course promotes the development of original, feasible, and impactful doctoral proposals, aligned with international standards of academic research.

Course Objectives

  • Understand the essential components of a doctoral thesis proposal in theology.
  • Critically analyze relevant topics, problems, and research questions in the theological field.
  • Formulate clear, coherent, and achievable research objectives.
  • Develop skills to design pertinent and rigorous theoretical and methodological frameworks.
  • Apply ethical criteria in the formulation of research proposals.
  • Prepare and present a solid, well-argued, and viable doctoral proposal for academic approval.

Main Topics

  • Fundamental elements of a doctoral thesis proposal.
  • Selection and delimitation of the theological research topic.
  • Formulation of research problems, questions, and hypotheses.
  • Critical review of theological literature and construction of the theoretical framework.
  • Methodological design: qualitative, quantitative, and mixed approaches in theology.
  • Ethical aspects in doctoral research.
  • Development of a research work plan and timeline.
  • Presentation and defense of the doctoral proposal.

Methodology

The course will be conducted through interactive virtual sessions, proposal development workshops, critical analysis of research projects, individual advising, and collaborative work in small groups. Active participation will be encouraged through constructive feedback, proposal defense simulations, and practical activities aimed at strengthening research autonomy.

Assessment

  • Active participation in workshops and academic forums (20%).
  • Critical analysis of existing research proposals (15%).
  • Progressive development of the proposal components (30%).
  • Submission of the complete doctoral proposal (25%).
  • Oral presentation and defense of the proposal before the academic committee (10%).

Course Significance

This seminar constitutes a fundamental pillar in the doctoral journey, as it equips students to formulate relevant, rigorous, and feasible theological research projects. Through the development of a solid proposal, students demonstrate their critical capacity, methodological proficiency, and ethical commitment—essential skills for making significant contributions to theological knowledge in global, diverse, and dynamic contexts.
