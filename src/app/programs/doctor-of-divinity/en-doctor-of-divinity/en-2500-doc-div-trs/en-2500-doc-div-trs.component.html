This course provides a specialized academic setting for the development and presentation of original theological research in specific areas of each student's interest. Through advanced methodological guidance and personalized academic mentoring, students will consolidate skills in critical research, theological argumentation, and academic communication, preparing them to contribute meaningfully to the global theological field through the production of rigorous and relevant scholarship.

Course Objectives

  • Develop original theological research with methodological rigor and contextual relevance.
  • Apply epistemological, methodological, and ethical principles to academic production.
  • Strengthen competencies in critical analysis, argumentation, and academic communication in theology.
  • Present and defend theological research before specialized academic audiences.
  • Integrate theological reflection with contemporary challenges across various areas of specialization.
  • Foster the capacity for publication and dissemination of high-quality theological research.

Main Topics

  • Advanced methodology in theological research: approaches and strategies.
  • Formulation of research problems and design of theological projects.
  • Critical review of specialized literature and construction of the state of the art.
  • Research ethics and principles of academic integrity.
  • Strategies for argumentation and academic writing in theology.
  • Oral presentation of projects and effective communication of results.
  • Evaluation and critical feedback of research projects in progress.
  • Preparation of academic articles for publication in specialized journals.

Methodology

The course will be conducted through intensive virtual seminars, research workshops, personalized mentoring, oral presentations of research progress, academic discussion forums, and project defense simulations. Autonomous learning, applied research, and active participation in theological knowledge production will be promoted.

Assessment

  • Active participation in seminars and research workshops (20%).
  • Oral presentations of research progress (20%).
  • Submission of written research progress reports (30%).
  • Final project: original theological research (20%).
  • Assessment of academic communication skills and oral defense (10%).

Course Significance

This seminar is crucial for the formation of theological researchers capable of generating relevant, rigorous, and contextualized scholarship. It provides essential tools for original academic contribution in the theological field, preparing students for leadership in research, teaching, and critical reflection in both local and international contexts.
