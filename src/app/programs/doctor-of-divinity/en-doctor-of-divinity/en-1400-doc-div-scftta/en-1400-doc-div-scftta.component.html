This course provides specialized training in spiritual counseling, combining theological foundations with contemporary therapeutic approaches. Students will acquire the knowledge and skills needed to offer emotional and spiritual support in various pastoral contexts, responding ethically and sensitively to human needs. The course emphasizes the integration of theological reflection, basic psychological understanding, and practical competencies necessary to accompany processes of healing and personal growth within faith communities.

Course Objectives

  • Understand the theological and psychological foundations of spiritual counseling.
  • Develop active listening, empathy, and accompaniment skills for pastoral contexts.
  • Apply basic therapeutic approaches adapted to situations of emotional and spiritual crisis.
  • Identify ethical boundaries and responsible practices in spiritual counseling.
  • Design spiritual support interventions for situations involving grief, conflict, trauma, and personal growth.
  • Foster an intercultural and contextualized perspective in pastoral accompaniment.

Main Topics

  • Biblical and theological foundations of spiritual counseling.
  • Basic psychological principles applied to pastoral accompaniment.
  • Contemporary models and approaches in spiritual counseling.
  • Therapeutic communication skills and crisis management.
  • Ethics and professional boundaries in spiritual accompaniment.
  • Pastoral intervention in grief, trauma, family conflicts, and existential issues.
  • Spiritual counseling in multicultural and multireligious contexts.
  • Self-care for the spiritual counselor: preventing emotional burnout.

Methodology

The course will combine interactive virtual classes, case studies, counseling simulations, practical skills workshops, ethical reflection forums, and contextualized intervention projects. Experiential learning, supervised practice, and the critical integration of theory and practice will be strongly encouraged.

Assessment

  • Active participation in practical workshops and discussion forums (20%).
  • Critical analysis of spiritual counseling cases (25%).
  • Essays on theoretical and ethical foundations of spiritual counseling (30%).
  • Final project: design of a spiritual support intervention (15%).
  • Integrative exam on theoretical and practical content (10%).

Course Significance

This course is fundamental for the formation of leaders and pastors equipped to offer solid, ethical, and sensitive spiritual accompaniment in situations of human vulnerability. It contributes to the strengthening of holistic pastoral care, promoting healthier, more resilient communities committed to the dignity and well-being of all individuals.
