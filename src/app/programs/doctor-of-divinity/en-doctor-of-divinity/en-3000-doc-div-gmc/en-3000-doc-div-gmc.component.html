This course offers advanced training in intercultural Christian mission, preparing students to develop effective ministry in diverse and globalized cultural contexts. Through a theological, sociological, and strategic approach, students will analyze the principles of Christian mission, the dynamics of intercultural encounter, and the importance of cultural sensitivity for evangelization and service in the contemporary world.

Course Objectives

  • Understand the biblical and theological foundations of Christian mission.
  • Analyze the impact of culture on the communication of the Gospel and missionary praxis.
  • Develop skills in cultural sensitivity, adaptation, and ministerial contextualization.
  • Evaluate historical and contemporary missionary strategies in different cultural contexts.
  • Design ethical, sustainable, and culturally relevant missionary projects.
  • Foster missionary leadership committed to social transformation and intercultural respect.

Main Topics

  • Biblical and theological foundations of global mission.
  • Cultural anthropology applied to Christian mission.
  • Contextualization of the Christian message in diverse cultures.
  • Ethical and theological challenges in contemporary intercultural mission.
  • Historical and current models of cross-cultural mission.
  • Intercultural communication and mediation in missionary contexts.
  • Designing global missionary projects with culturally sensitive approaches.
  • Integral mission: evangelization, social justice, and cultural transformation.

Methodology

The course will combine interactive virtual lectures, analysis of missionary case studies, project design workshops, intercultural simulations, critical discussion forums, and the development of contextualized missionary strategies. Theological reflection, practical engagement, and the development of advanced intercultural competencies will be strongly encouraged.

Assessment

  • Active participation in workshops, simulations, and analysis forums (20%).
  • Critical essays on mission and culture (25%).
  • Analysis of cross-cultural mission case studies (30%).
  • Final project: design of an intercultural missionary plan (15%).
  • Integrative exam on foundations of global mission and culture (10%).

Course Significance

This course is essential for training missionary leaders equipped to serve with sensitivity, ethics, and effectiveness in globalized and multicultural contexts. It prepares students to develop intercultural ministry that combines the faithful proclamation of the Gospel with deep respect for local cultures, promoting justice, reconciliation, and holistic transformation in the contemporary world.
