This course provides a supervised practical experience in religious communities, enabling students to directly apply the theological, pastoral, and ethical knowledge acquired throughout the doctoral program. Through immersion in real ministerial contexts under specialized academic supervision, students will develop essential practical competencies for pastoral leadership, spiritual accompaniment, community management, and the promotion of social transformation grounded in theological principles.

Course Objectives

  • Apply theological knowledge and pastoral skills integratively in real-world settings.
  • Develop competencies in leadership, communication, and spiritual accompaniment within religious communities.
  • Reflect critically on ministerial praxis in light of ethical and theological principles.
  • Strengthen skills in community assessment, planning, and pastoral action.
  • Foster adaptability, intercultural sensitivity, and conflict resolution in diverse contexts.
  • Produce critical practice reports that integrate theological reflection with lived experience.

Main Topics

  • Introduction to supervised practices: objectives, methodology, and ethical criteria.
  • Assessment of community needs and dynamics.
  • Planning and implementation of pastoral and educational activities.
  • Individual and communal spiritual accompaniment.
  • Management of social projects and ministerial activities.
  • Conflict resolution and strengthening of community cohesion.
  • Theological reflection on ministerial and communal praxis.
  • Critical evaluation of pastoral experience: learning and challenges.

Methodology

The course will be conducted through the assignment of supervised internships in religious communities, personalized academic tutoring sessions, critical reflection seminars, the preparation of practicum reports, and the presentation of final pastoral intervention projects. The experience combines direct action with systematic academic reflection.

Assessment

  • Active participation in assigned practical activities (30%).
  • Academic supervision sessions and critical reflection (20%).
  • Practicum report based on lived pastoral experience (30%).
  • Final project for pastoral or community intervention (15%).
  • Assessment of attitudinal and ethical competencies (5%).

Course Significance

This course constitutes a fundamental stage of integration between theory and practice, preparing students to lead religious communities ethically, contextually, and transformatively. Direct experience in real ministerial settings strengthens the professional, pastoral, and theological competencies necessary for effective leadership committed to addressing the spiritual and social needs of the contemporary world.
