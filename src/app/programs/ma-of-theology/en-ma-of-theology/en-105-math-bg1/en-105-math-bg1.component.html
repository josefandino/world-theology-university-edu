MATH-BG1-105 - Koine Greek II

Koine Greek II continues the study of the New Testament language by exploring more complex grammatical structures, expanding vocabulary, and developing advanced skills in reading and translating biblical texts.

Course Objectives:

  • Strengthen the Koine Greek knowledge gained in the previous level.
  • Understand additional verb tenses and more complex syntactical structures.
  • Expand the vocabulary found in New Testament texts.
  • Translate longer and more complex passages with greater accuracy.
  • Analyze the linguistic and theological context of biblical texts.

Main Topics:

  • Advanced verb tenses (future, perfect, pluperfect)
  • Participles and subordinate clauses
  • Relative pronouns and complex constructions
  • Translation and analysis of extended New Testament passages

Methodology:

The course employs intensive biblical readings, translation exercises, discussions on advanced grammar structures, and theological analysis of translated texts.

Assessment:

  • Advanced grammar and vocabulary exams
  • Translation and textual analysis quizzes
  • Biblical passage interpretation projects
  • Comprehensive final reading and translation exam

Importance of the Course:

Koine Greek II equips students for more fluent and in-depth reading of the New Testament in its original language, essential for serious theological studies and scholarly biblical interpretation.
