MATH-ALBP-110 - Apocalyptic Literature and Biblical Prophecy

Apocalyptic Literature and Biblical Prophecy explores the apocalyptic literary genres in the Bible, with a focus on the prophetic texts of the Old and New Testaments, and their theological interpretation. The course analyzes how these texts predict future events and offer a message of hope, judgment, and redemption for the people of God.

Course Objectives:

  • Study the key apocalyptic and prophetic texts in the Bible.
  • Analyze the symbolism and imagery used in apocalyptic literature.
  • Explore the relevance of biblical prophecy for contemporary Christian life.
  • Develop skills to interpret apocalyptic texts contextually and theologically.
  • Reflect on the role of prophecy in forming hope and resistance within faith communities.

Main Topics:

  • Introduction to biblical apocalyptic literature
  • The Book of Revelation: structure and message
  • Prophecy in the Old Testament: Isaiah, Ezekiel, and Daniel
  • The minor prophets and their apocalyptic message
  • Interpreting apocalyptic imagery and symbols
  • The fulfillment of biblical prophecies in history and future Christian hope

Methodology:

This course combines lectures, studies of key biblical texts, and discussions on the theological implications of apocalyptic texts. An interdisciplinary approach will be encouraged, integrating history, literature, and theology.

Assessment:

  • Essays on the interpretation of apocalyptic texts.
  • Exams on major prophetic and apocalyptic passages.
  • Presentations on the relevance of biblical prophecies in today's context.
  • Group research on the symbols and messages of apocalyptic literature in the Bible.

Importance of the Course:

Apocalyptic literature and biblical prophecy are fundamental to understanding the complete message of the Bible, especially in relation to eschatological hope and the interpretation of the end times. This course provides the necessary tools to understand and apply these texts in Christian life and ministry.
