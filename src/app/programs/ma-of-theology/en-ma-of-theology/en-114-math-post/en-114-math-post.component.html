MATH-POST-114 - The Pentateuch: Origins, Structure, and Theology

The Pentateuch: Origins, Structure, and Theology offers a critical exploration of the composition, literary structure, and theological foundations of the Pentateuch. Students engage with contemporary scholarship to uncover its formative role in biblical theology and faith traditions around the world.

Course Objectives:

  • Analyze the historical and literary development of the Pentateuch.
  • Identify and interpret key theological themes within the five books of Moses.
  • Evaluate the main theories of composition and authorship, including source criticism.
  • Explore the Pentateuch's impact on Jewish, Christian, and interreligious traditions.
  • Develop skills in exegesis and theological reflection grounded in textual analysis.
  • Contextualize the Pentateuch within broader cultural, historical, and theological frameworks.

Main Topics:

  • Introduction to the Pentateuch: Genesis to Deuteronomy
  • Documentary Hypothesis and alternative models of composition
  • Creation, covenant, law, and promise as central theological motifs
  • The role of Moses and narrative development
  • Historical and literary contexts of ancient Israel
  • The Pentateuch in Jewish and Christian interpretation
  • Contemporary theological readings of the Pentateuch
  • Global perspectives and interfaith relevance

Methodology:

This course combines virtual lectures, critical reading assignments, exegetical exercises, discussion forums, and theological reflections. Emphasis is placed on scholarly analysis, interpretive engagement, and the integration of diverse global perspectives in biblical studies.

Assessment:

  • Exegetical papers on selected Pentateuchal texts
  • Theological essays addressing major themes
  • Participation in critical discussions and online forums
  • Oral or written presentation on a composition theory or theological issue
  • Final examination or integrative project demonstrating comprehensive understanding

Importance of the Course:

This course is foundational for biblical and theological studies, offering essential tools for understanding the narrative and theological core of the Old Testament. It enables students to engage critically with sacred texts and equips them to interpret the Pentateuch within their ministerial, academic, or intercultural contexts, fostering an informed and responsible faith practice.
