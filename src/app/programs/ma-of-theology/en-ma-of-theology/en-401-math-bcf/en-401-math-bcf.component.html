MATH-BCF-401 - Bioethics and Christian Faith

Bioethics and Christian Faith is a course that explores how Christian ethical principles are applied to contemporary bioethical issues such as medicine, biotechnology, abortion, euthanasia, stem cell research, and ethical dilemmas arising from scientific and technological advances. The course aims to help students integrate their Christian faith with current bioethical debates, promoting a Christian approach that values human dignity, life, and well-being. Through the study of the Bible, Christian tradition, and ethical principles, students will learn to address health and biotechnology ethical issues from a Christian perspective.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the principles of Christian bioethics and their application to contemporary dilemmas.
  • Develop skills to integrate Christian faith with ethical issues in biomedicine and biotechnology.
  • Analyze ethical issues regarding human life, healthcare, and human dignity from a Christian perspective.
  • Reflect on the ethical responsibility of healthcare professionals and technological advances in medicine.
  • Promote a Christian approach to ethical decision-making in medical practice and scientific research.

Main Topics:

  • Fundamental principles of Christian bioethics: Human dignity and the value of life.
  • Abortion and reproductive rights: Ethical and Christian considerations.
  • Euthanasia and dignified death: A Christian approach to suffering and death.
  • Stem cell research and biotechnology: Ethical challenges and scientific applications.
  • The role of Christians in healthcare and justice in medicine.

Methodology:

The course will be conducted through readings of biblical texts, academic articles on Christian bioethics, in-class debates on contemporary bioethical dilemmas, and case study analysis related to medical and biotechnological advances. Students will also engage in discussions and reflections on how Christian principles should influence ethical decisions in healthcare and research.

Assessment:

  • Essays and research papers on bioethical topics from a Christian perspective.
  • Case studies on ethical dilemmas in medicine and biotechnology.
  • Debates and class presentations on contemporary bioethical issues.
  • Exams on the principles of Christian bioethics and their practical applications.

Importance of the Course:

This course is essential for students who wish to address ethical dilemmas arising in medicine and biotechnology from a Christian perspective. By understanding how to integrate Christian ethical principles with modern bioethical concerns, students will be better equipped to make informed, faith-based decisions in key areas such as abortion, euthanasia, and healthcare.
