MATH-FT-801 - Feminist Theology

Feminist Theology explores how feminist perspectives challenge traditional theology by centering women’s experiences, offering new interpretations of Scripture and contributing to gender justice and equity within the church and broader society.

Course Objectives:

  • Study the origins and evolution of feminist theology.
  • Analyze the contributions of key feminist theologians.
  • Critique patriarchal religious structures.
  • Reflect on the impact of feminist theology today.

Main Topics:

  • Development of feminist theological thought.
  • Key figures: Elizabeth Johnson, Rosemary Radford Ruether, Ada María Isasi-Díaz.
  • Feminist interpretations of Scripture and tradition.
  • Gender justice and theological renewal.

Methodology:

The course uses critical readings, lectures, group debates, and reflection exercises to examine feminist theology’s transformative role in Christian thought and practice.

Assessment:

  • Essay on feminist theology’s contribution to Christian thought.
  • Presentation on a leading feminist theologian’s impact.
  • Participation in debates on gender, justice, and theology.

Importance of the Course:

Feminist theology invites a reimagining of Christian tradition through a liberating, inclusive lens, promoting gender equity and empowering the voices historically marginalized within theology.
