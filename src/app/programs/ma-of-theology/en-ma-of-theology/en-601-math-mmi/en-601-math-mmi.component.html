MATH-MMI-601 - Medieval and Modern Islam

Medieval and Modern Islam is a course that explores the evolution of Islam from its origins in the 7th century to the modern transformations. It examines the expansion of Islam during the medieval period, its influences on politics, culture, and science, and interactions with other civilizations. Additionally, it covers the contemporary developments of Islam, focusing on the impact of colonialism, globalization, and Islamic movements in the modern world. The course provides a deep understanding of Islam, its history, its main branches, and its relevance today.

Course Objectives:

  • Explore the history of Islam from its origins to the modern period.
  • Analyze the role of Islam in politics, culture, and science during the medieval period.
  • Examine the effects of colonialism and globalization on contemporary Islam.
  • Develop a critical understanding of modern Islamic movements and their impact in the globalized world.

Main Topics:

  • The rise of Islam and the expansion of the Islamic caliphate.
  • The Golden Age of Islam: contributions to science, philosophy, and the arts.
  • Islam in the medieval period: interactions with Christian and Byzantine civilizations.
  • Modern Islam: the impact of colonialism and decolonization.
  • Contemporary Islamic movements: political Islam and the challenges of the 21st century.

Methodology:

This course focuses on the historical and contemporary analysis of Islam, using academic readings, case studies, and debates. Students will have the opportunity to examine historical sources, key Islamic texts, and analyses of events and movements that have shaped modern Islamic practice.

Assessment:

  • Essays on historical and contemporary topics of Islam.
  • Written exams on major events and developments in the history of Islam.
  • Group presentations on topics related to medieval and modern Islam.
  • Research on the impact of Islam in contemporary societies.

Importance of the Course:

This course is essential for those who wish to understand Islam in both its historical and modern contexts, as well as its role in global politics and culture. It helps students develop an informed and critical perspective on one of the world's most influential religions.
