MATH-SFE-405 - Sexual and Family Ethics

Sexual and Family Ethics is a course that addresses the ethical principles related to human sexuality, family relationships, and the contemporary challenges that families face in modern society. The course explores fundamental topics such as sexual morality, marriage, parenthood, the family as a social institution, and the ethical implications of various practices and decisions within the family domain. A particular emphasis will be placed on how Christian principles influence the ethics of sexuality and family, and how these can be applied to current situations that families face.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the foundational ethical principles related to sexuality and family relationships from a Christian perspective.
  • Analyze the ethical implications of sexual and family practices in the contemporary context.
  • Study the impact of Christian values on the ethics of marriage and parenthood.
  • Explore the ethical challenges faced by modern families and possible solutions within a Christian framework.
  • Develop skills to apply Christian ethical principles in family counseling and intervention.

Main Topics:

  • Sexual morality: Christian principles on sexuality and their application in daily life.
  • Marriage: Ethics of marriage and the challenges of married life.
  • Parenthood: Ethical responsibilities of parents and Christian upbringing of children.
  • The family as an institution: The role of the family in society and the ethics of family relationships.
  • Ethics and family diversity: Ethical reflection on the various forms of family in modern society.

Methodology:

The course will be based on discussions of biblical and theological texts, case studies, and the analysis of sexual and family situations from a Christian ethical perspective. Students will participate in debates, research on sexual and family ethics, and reflections on how Christian principles can be applied to address current ethical issues within the family.

Assessment:

  • Essays on ethical issues related to sexuality and family.
  • Case studies on the impact of ethical decisions on family relationships.
  • Practical projects focused on Christian-based family counseling and intervention.

Importance of the Course:

This course is essential for those seeking to understand how Christian ethics influences family relationships and human sexuality. Students will be better prepared to address ethical dilemmas that arise in the family context and to apply Christian principles in their professional practice.
