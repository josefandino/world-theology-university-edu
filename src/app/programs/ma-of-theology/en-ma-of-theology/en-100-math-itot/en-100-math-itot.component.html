MATH-ITOT-100 - Introduction to the Old Testament

Introduction to the Old Testament provides a survey of the historical, literary, and theological aspects of the Old Testament. Students will explore the major themes, key figures, and historical context of the biblical texts, gaining a foundational understanding of their significance and message.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the historical and cultural background of the Old Testament.
  • Identify and analyze the major themes and messages of the Old Testament books.
  • Develop skills for interpreting Old Testament texts within their historical and literary contexts.
  • Explore the theological contributions of the Old Testament to Christian faith and practice.
  • Foster an appreciation for the Old Testamentâ€™s relevance in contemporary life.

Main Topics:

  • Overview of the Pentateuch, Historical Books, Wisdom Literature, and Prophets
  • Key theological themes: covenant, law, prophecy, kingship, and wisdom
  • Historical context of Israel and its neighbors
  • Methods of Old Testament interpretation
  • Application of Old Testament teachings in modern contexts

Methodology:

The course includes lectures, assigned readings, group discussions, thematic studies, and reflective essays to enhance comprehension and critical thinking about the Old Testament scriptures.

Assessment:

  • Reading quizzes and assignments
  • Written essays analyzing key themes or books
  • Group presentations on historical and theological topics
  • Final examination covering major concepts

Importance of the Course:

This course provides essential biblical literacy and critical tools for anyone seeking to understand the foundations of the Judeo-Christian tradition, equipping students for further theological study and personal spiritual growth.
