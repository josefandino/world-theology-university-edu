MATH-MTT-900 - Master’s Thesis in Theology

Master’s Thesis in Theology is the final project of the degree program, where students conduct original theological research, demonstrating mastery of theological disciplines, research methods, and academic writing through a significant contribution to theological scholarship.

Course Objectives:

  • Demonstrate advanced theological research competencies.
  • Produce an original theological thesis project.
  • Apply critical and analytical skills in theological inquiry.
  • Present and defend a formal academic thesis.

Main Topics:

  • Formulating research questions and objectives.
  • Theological research methodology and literature review.
  • Data analysis and discussion of findings.
  • Academic writing and formal defense of thesis.

Methodology:

The thesis involves independent research under faculty supervision, periodic evaluations, peer reviews, and participation in research seminars leading to a final defense before an academic panel.

Assessment:

  • Research proposal approval.
  • Chapter submissions and revisions.
  • Final thesis submission.
  • Oral defense before an academic committee.

Importance of the Course:

The Master’s Thesis solidifies students' theological education and research capabilities, enabling them to make a meaningful contribution to contemporary theological dialogue and practice.
