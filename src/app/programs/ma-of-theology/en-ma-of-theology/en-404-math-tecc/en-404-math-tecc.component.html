MATH-TECC-404 - Theology and Ecology: Care for Creation

Theology and Ecology: Care for Creation is a course that explores the relationship between Christian theology and ecology, emphasizing the care for creation as a central tenet of the Christian faith. The course examines how biblical principles regarding creation and human responsibility toward the natural world have been interpreted and applied within various Christian traditions. Students will investigate topics such as environmental ethics, sustainability, ecological justice, and the theology of caring for the Earth, and how these principles can be integrated into contemporary environmental protection practices and policies.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the theological foundations of care for creation from a Christian perspective.
  • Analyze the relationship between the Bible and contemporary ecological issues.
  • Study the importance of sustainability and ecological justice from a Christian viewpoint.
  • Develop skills to apply theological principles in promoting ecological policies and practices.
  • Reflect on the ethical responsibility of Christians in caring for the environment and creation.

Main Topics:

  • Creation and human responsibility: The biblical mandate to care for the Earth.
  • Ecological theology: The relationship between God, humanity, and the environment.
  • Christian environmental ethics: The role of the church in promoting sustainability.
  • Ecological justice: Addressing environmental inequalities from a Christian perspective.
  • The future of the Earth: Christian hope and the restoration of creation.

Methodology:

The course will be conducted through studies of biblical and theological texts, analysis of contemporary cases of ecological crises, and reflection on the ethical implications of care for creation. Students will participate in debates, practical projects focused on sustainability, and the integration of Christian principles into ecological practices.

Assessment:

  • Essays on the relationship between Christian theology and contemporary ecological issues.
  • Case studies on the application of Christian principles in ecological justice.
  • Presentations on the role of the church in environmental protection.
  • Practical projects focused on caring for creation in local communities.

Importance of the Course:

This course is crucial for students interested in the intersection of Christian faith and ecology. By understanding the theological responsibility of caring for creation, students will be better equipped to advocate for ecological justice and sustainability in their communities and the world.
