MATH-CTAR-303 - Covenant Theology and Redemption

Covenant Theology and Redemption is a course that explores the key concepts of covenant in the Bible and how this theological principle is related to the redemption of humanity through Jesus Christ. Through a detailed examination of the Scriptures, students will investigate how covenants, both in the Old and New Testaments, are fundamental to understanding God's redemptive plan. The course also delves into the relationship between covenant, divine grace, and salvation.

Course Objectives:

  • Study the theology of the covenant in Scripture and its development throughout biblical history.
  • Examine how God's covenant with His people is reflected in both the Old and New Testaments.
  • Analyze how redemption is accomplished through the fulfillment of divine covenants, particularly in the person of Jesus Christ.
  • Explore the relationship between covenant and salvation in the history of humanity.
  • Reflect on the practical implications of covenant theology for Christian life and the community of believers.

Main Topics:

  • The concept of covenant in the Bible and its different forms.
  • The covenant with Noah, Abraham, Moses, and David in the Old Testament.
  • The new covenant in Christ and its fulfillment.
  • The relationship between covenant and divine grace in salvation.
  • The theology of redemption and its connection to Christ's redemptive work.
  • Practical implications of covenant theology for Christian life.

Methodology:

The course will be conducted through lectures, Bible studies, theological readings, and debates on the key topics of covenant theology. Students will also engage in research and presentations to deepen their understanding of covenant and redemption.

Assessment:

  • Essays on the different covenants in the Bible and their relationship to redemption.
  • Written exams on covenant theology and redemption.
  • Research projects on the practical implications of covenant theology.
  • Presentations on the relationship between biblical covenants and salvation in Christ.

Importance of the Course:

This course is essential for understanding the theological principle of covenant, which is key to interpreting Scripture and comprehending God's redemptive plan in history. Covenant theology and redemption are not only foundational to systematic theology but also have profound implications for Christian life, the church community, and pastoral practice.
