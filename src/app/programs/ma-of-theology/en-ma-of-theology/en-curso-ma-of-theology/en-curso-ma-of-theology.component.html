These are the courses that make up the Master’s Degree in Theology at World University, an institution renowned for its academic excellence and its commitment to the holistic formation of spiritual and academic leaders. The program offers a solid theological, historical, and pastoral foundation, with a contemporary and global perspective, enabling students to engage in critical study of the Christian faith and to prepare for transformative leadership in diverse ecclesial and social contexts.

MATH-ITOT-100 - Introduction to the Old Testament - This course offers a comprehensive exploration of the Old Testament, examining its historical context, literary structure, and theological significance. Through critical analysis and scholarly engagement, students will develop a profound understanding of its enduring impact on faith, culture, and global theological thought. Essential for cultivating spiritual depth and academic excellence, it lays a vital foundation for professional ministry and research.

MATH-ITNT-101 - Introduction to the New Testament - This course presents a critical overview of the New Testament, exploring its historical backdrop, literary genres, and theological messages. Students engage with contemporary scholarship to enhance their understanding of its profound influence on global Christianity and modern thought, fostering spiritual maturity and academic excellence.

MATH-BH1-102 - Biblical Hebrew I - Designed for beginners, this course introduces the fundamentals of Biblical Hebrew, equipping students with essential skills for reading and interpreting Old Testament texts in their original language. Emphasis is placed on grammar, vocabulary, and linguistic analysis, promoting rigorous academic and theological formation.

MATH-BH1-103 - Biblical Hebrew II - Building on Biblical Hebrew I, this advanced course deepens students’ proficiency in reading, translating, and exegeting Hebrew texts. Through intensive practice and critical study, participants strengthen their linguistic capabilities, essential for scholarly research and professional theological engagement.

MATH-BG1-104 - Koine Greek I - This foundational course offers an introduction to Koine Greek, the original language of the New Testament. Students will master basic grammar and vocabulary, laying the groundwork for direct engagement with scriptural texts and fostering academic rigor and spiritual insight.

MATH-BG1-105 - Koine Greek II - Expanding on Koine Greek I, this course advances students' ability to read and interpret New Testament passages with precision. Emphasizing syntactical analysis and exegetical skills, it prepares future scholars and leaders for profound theological reflection and global ministry.

MATH-BT1-106 - Old Testament Exegesis - This course trains students in critical exegetical methods for interpreting the Old Testament. Through advanced hermeneutical techniques and theological analysis, participants develop the skills necessary for scholarly research, informed preaching, and thoughtful engagement with ancient texts in contemporary contexts.

MATH-NTE-107 - New Testament Exegesis - This course equips students with advanced exegetical tools to critically interpret New Testament texts. Emphasizing historical, literary, and theological dimensions, it fosters scholarly rigor, spiritual depth, and global relevance for academic and ministerial excellence.

MATH-BTOT-108 - Biblical Theology of the Old Testament - Students explore the central theological themes of the Old Testament, analyzing their development within historical and literary contexts. The course cultivates a critical and faith-informed understanding essential for profound academic and ministerial impact.

MATH-BTNT-109 - Biblical Theology of the New Testament - This course examines the theological framework of the New Testament, tracing key motifs across its diverse writings. Students develop a nuanced understanding of its enduring significance for Christian theology, leadership, and global scholarship.

MATH-ALBP-110 - Apocalyptic Literature and Biblical Prophecy - Offering a critical study of apocalyptic texts and prophetic traditions, this course highlights their literary features, theological insights, and eschatological visions. It equips students for informed interpretation and meaningful application in contemporary settings.

MATH-WPL-111 - Wisdom and Poetic Literature - Focusing on the Old Testament’s wisdom and poetic books, this course analyzes their literary artistry, theological depth, and ethical teachings. Students gain critical skills for engaging with timeless insights into human experience and divine revelation.

MATH-GJLJ-112 - The Gospels and the Life of Jesus - This course investigates the theological and historical portrayal of Jesus in the Gospels. Through rigorous analysis, students deepen their understanding of Christ’s life, teachings, and enduring influence on global Christianity and theological reflection.

MATH-PGE-113 - Pauline and General Epistles - An in-depth study of Pauline and General Epistles, this course explores their theological contributions, pastoral concerns, and historical contexts. Students cultivate expertise in interpreting apostolic writings vital for scholarly research and ecclesial leadership.

MATH-POST-114 - The Pentateuch: Origins, Structure, and Theology - This course offers a critical exploration of the Pentateuch’s composition, literary structure, and theological foundations. Students engage with contemporary scholarship to uncover its formative role in biblical theology and faith traditions worldwide.

Church History and Christian Thought

MATH-HC1ECAP-200 - History of Christianity I: Early Church and Apostolic Fathers - This course examines the origins and early development of Christianity, focusing on the Apostolic Fathers and the foundational structures of the Church. Students engage critically with historical texts to understand the enduring legacy of the early Christian witness.

MATH-HC2MAR-201 - History of Christianity II: Middle Ages and the Reformation - Through critical analysis, this course explores the complexities of Christianity during the Middle Ages and the transformative impact of the Reformation. Students investigate theological, social, and political shifts that continue to shape global Christianity.

MATH-HC3MCE-202 - History of Christianity III: Modern and Contemporary Eras - Focusing on Christianity from the Enlightenment to the present, this course analyzes theological developments, cultural encounters, and global movements. Students gain insight into the Church’s evolving role in a rapidly changing world.

MATH-ECDD-203 - Ecumenical Councils and Dogmatic Development - This course offers a detailed study of major Ecumenical Councils and the progression of Christian doctrine. Students critically assess historical debates and theological definitions that have shaped the unity and diversity of Christian thought.

MATH-PRCC-204 - The Protestant Reformation and the Counter-Reformation - Students critically examine the causes, key figures, and theological innovations of the Protestant Reformation and the Catholic Counter-Reformation. The course emphasizes their enduring influence on ecclesial structures, theology, and global Christianity.

MATH-LATG-205 - Latin American and Global South Theology - This course explores the theological voices emerging from Latin America and the Global South, highlighting their contributions to liberation theology, contextual hermeneutics, and global Christian discourse.

MATH-WTHC-206 - Women in the History of Christianity - This course investigates the often-overlooked contributions of women to the development of Christianity. Students critically engage with historical and theological narratives to appreciate the transformative roles of women across eras and traditions.

MATH-GC21C-207 - Global Christianity in the 21st Century - Analyzing the dynamic expansion and diversification of Christianity today, this course examines theological, cultural, and missional trends shaping the faith in a globalized world, preparing students for visionary leadership and scholarship.

Systematic and Philosophical Theology

MATH-STGC-300 - Systematic Theology I: God, Trinity, and Creation - This course explores the foundational doctrines of God, the Trinity, and Creation, emphasizing theological coherence, historical development, and global relevance. Students cultivate critical skills essential for advanced theological reflection and ministry.

MATH-STCP-301 - Systematic Theology II: Christology and Pneumatology - Focusing on the person and work of Christ and the Holy Spirit, this course deepens students’ understanding of central Christian doctrines, fostering a robust theological framework for scholarly inquiry and pastoral engagement.

MATH-STEE-302 - Systematic Theology III: Ecclesiology and Eschatology - Students examine the doctrine of the Church and the theology of last things, analyzing their biblical foundations and historical interpretations. The course prepares students for critical leadership in ecclesial and academic contexts.

MATH-CTAR-303 - Covenant Theology and Redemption - This course studies the biblical covenants and the unfolding narrative of redemption, highlighting their theological significance for understanding salvation history and their impact on contemporary Christian thought.

MATH-TKOG-304 - Theology of the Kingdom of God - Offering a comprehensive analysis of the Kingdom of God across Scripture and theology, this course equips students to interpret its eschatological and missional dimensions within global Christian contexts.

MATH-CT-305 - Contemporary Theology - Engaging with major movements and figures of modern theology, this course challenges students to critically evaluate contemporary responses to traditional doctrines in light of global cultural shifts and emerging challenges.

MATH-POR-306 - Philosophy of Religion - This course introduces critical philosophical issues related to religion, including arguments for the existence of God, the problem of evil, and religious epistemology, fostering rigorous intellectual engagement and theological sophistication.

MATH-IOCP-307 - Introduction to Christian Philosophy - Students explore key philosophical ideas shaping Christian theology, from classical theism to modern debates. The course emphasizes critical thinking and the integration of faith and reason in a global context.

MATH-NTGR-308 - Natural Theology and General Revelation - This course examines how knowledge of God can be discerned through nature, reason, and conscience. Students engage classical and contemporary perspectives to critically assess the role of general revelation in theology.

MATH-TCS-309 - Theology of the Cross and Suffering - Centering on the theological meaning of the cross and human suffering, this course offers profound insights into redemption, hope, and divine compassion, preparing students for pastoral ministry and scholarly contribution.

MATH-ACE-400 - Applied Christian Ethics - This course examines the application of Christian ethical principles to contemporary issues, fostering critical reflection and practical engagement. Students develop ethical frameworks rooted in faith, reason, and global responsibility.

MATH-BCF-401 - Bioethics and Christian Faith - Analyzing ethical dilemmas in medicine, technology, and human life, this course integrates Christian theological insights with bioethical reasoning, preparing students for informed leadership in academic, ecclesial, and healthcare contexts.

MATH-SJCD-402 - Social Justice and Christian Doctrine - This course explores the theological foundations of social justice within Christian doctrine, equipping students to address systemic injustice with a transformative, faith-driven vision for global impact.

MATH-PTHR-403 - Political Theology and Human Rights - Focusing on the intersection of theology, politics, and human rights, this course challenges students to critically assess the role of Christian thought in shaping just societies and promoting human dignity worldwide.

MATH-TECC-404 - Theology and Ecology: Care for Creation - Students explore theological responses to environmental challenges, grounding ecological stewardship in Scripture and Christian tradition. The course prepares leaders to advocate for sustainable and theologically informed care for creation.

MATH-SFE-405 - Sexual and Family Ethics - This course addresses complex ethical questions surrounding sexuality, marriage, and family life, offering a robust Christian framework that integrates biblical teaching, theological reflection, and pastoral sensitivity.

MATH-TPR-406 - Theology of Peace and Reconciliation - Examining the theological roots of peacebuilding and reconciliation, this course equips students to engage conflict transformation processes with theological depth, cultural awareness, and pastoral wisdom.

MATH-MPCE-407 - Migration, Poverty, and Christian Engagement - This course analyzes the theological, ethical, and pastoral challenges posed by migration and poverty. Students are prepared to respond with informed compassion, advocacy, and transformational Christian leadership.

MATH-PLDF-500 - Pastoral Leadership and Discipleship Formation - This course equips students with strategic leadership skills for pastoral ministry, emphasizing discipleship models rooted in biblical and theological foundations. It prepares leaders to guide spiritual growth with vision and integrity.

MATH-EBP-501 - Expository Biblical Preaching - Focusing on the art and theology of expository preaching, this course trains students to interpret, structure, and deliver sermons that faithfully communicate Scripture with clarity, depth, and transformative power.

MATH-PCSC-502 - Pastoral Care and Spiritual Counseling - Students develop critical skills in pastoral care, spiritual counseling, and crisis intervention, integrating theological reflection with compassionate practice to support holistic healing and spiritual formation.

MATH-CPR-503 - Church Planting and Renewal - This course explores theological, strategic, and practical frameworks for planting new churches and revitalizing existing congregations, preparing students for dynamic ministry in diverse cultural and global contexts.

MATH-TWL-504 - Theology of Worship and Liturgy - Engaging the theology and history of Christian worship, this course examines liturgical practices across traditions, equipping students to design and lead worship that is biblically grounded, theologically rich, and contextually relevant.

MATH-UCM-505 - Urban and Contextual Ministry - Focusing on ministry in urban and multicultural settings, this course prepares students to engage diverse communities with theological depth, cultural intelligence, and missional creativity.

MATH-TSA-506 - Theology of Spiritual Accompaniment - This course explores the theological and pastoral dimensions of spiritual accompaniment, forming students to walk alongside others in discernment, growth, and transformation within Christian community.

MATH-CSPF-507 - Christian Spirituality and Personal Formation - Students explore classical and contemporary approaches to Christian spirituality, developing practices and theological frameworks for personal formation and sustainable, lifelong ministry.

MATH-CMSC-508 - Chaplaincy and Ministry in Specialized Contexts - This course equips students for chaplaincy and ministry within healthcare, military, educational, and institutional settings, integrating theological depth, pastoral sensitivity, and contextual adaptability.

MATH-TSPH-509 - Theology of Suffering and Pastoral Hope - Centering on theological and pastoral responses to suffering, this course prepares students to minister with compassion, resilience, and hope, grounding their approach in the cross and the promise of redemption.

MATH-WRCI-600 - World Religions: A Comparative Introduction - This course offers a critical introduction to the world’s major religions, fostering comparative analysis and theological reflection. Students develop global awareness and interreligious competence essential for contemporary Christian engagement.

MATH-MMI-601 - Medieval and Modern Islam - Exploring the historical development and theological dimensions of Islam from its medieval roots to the present, this course equips students to engage thoughtfully with Islamic traditions in scholarly and pastoral contexts.

MATH-JAM-602 - Judaism in Antiquity and Modernity - This course examines the evolution of Judaism from its biblical foundations to contemporary expressions, providing students with critical insights into its theological, cultural, and historical significance.

MATH-CH-603 - Christianity and Hinduism - Analyzing the theological intersections and divergences between Christianity and Hinduism, this course cultivates deep understanding and prepares students for meaningful interfaith dialogue and engagement.

MATH-BCT-604 - Buddhism and Christian Theology - This course explores the philosophical and spiritual dialogue between Buddhism and Christian theology, encouraging critical reflection on points of convergence, divergence, and mutual enrichment.

MATH-IDCW-605 - Interfaith Dialogue and Christian Witness - Students engage theological and practical frameworks for interfaith dialogue, learning to bear faithful Christian witness while fostering respectful, transformative encounters across religious traditions.

MATH-RG-606 - Religion and Globalization - This course analyzes the dynamic interaction between religion and globalization, preparing students to address global challenges with theological depth, cultural intelligence, and strategic Christian engagement.

MATH-RPCE-607 - Religious Pluralism and Christian Exclusivity - Addressing one of the central theological challenges of our time, this course critically examines the tension between religious pluralism and Christian claims of exclusivity, equipping students for faithful, thoughtful, and globally relevant ministry.

MATH-TRM-700 - Theological Research Methodology - This course equips students with advanced research methodologies in theology, emphasizing critical inquiry, methodological rigor, and scholarly excellence for impactful academic contribution.

MATH-CRAW-701 - Critical Reading and Academic Writing - Students develop essential skills in critical reading, analytical thinking, and scholarly writing, laying a strong foundation for theological research and professional academic communication.

MATH-RSPD-702 - Research Seminar I: Project Design - This seminar guides students in the conception and design of their research projects, emphasizing clarity of purpose, methodological soundness, and scholarly originality in theological inquiry.

MATH-RSTP-703 - Research Seminar II: Thesis and Presentation - Building upon project design, this course leads students through thesis development, academic writing, and effective presentation, preparing them for successful defense and publication.

MATH-ETR-704 - Ethics in Theological Research - Students critically engage with ethical principles governing theological research, fostering integrity, responsibility, and respect for diverse perspectives within academic and ecclesial communities.

MATH-PTAP-705 - Public Theology and Academic Publishing - This course explores the intersection of theology, public discourse, and academic publishing, equipping students to contribute meaningfully to global theological conversations and scholarly forums.

MATH-LT-800 - Liberation Theology - This course examines the origins, principles, and global impact of Liberation Theology, equipping students to engage critically with theological responses to oppression, poverty, and social injustice.

MATH-FT-801 - Feminist Theology - Students explore the development and contributions of Feminist Theology, analyzing its challenge to traditional paradigms and its role in shaping inclusive theological discourse and praxis.

MATH-ICT-802 - Indigenous and Contextual Theology - This course engages indigenous and contextual theologies, highlighting their transformative insights and contributions to global Christian thought, cultural identity, and theological innovation.

MATH-AAAT-803 - African and African American Theology - Focusing on African and African American theological traditions, this course explores their historical development, spiritual depth, and contemporary relevance in shaping global Christianity.

MATH-GPCM-804 - Global Pentecostalism and Charismatic Movements - This course analyzes the theological, historical, and sociocultural dimensions of Pentecostal and Charismatic movements worldwide, offering critical perspectives on their dynamic growth and influence.

MATH-SAE-805 - Studies in Apocalyptic Eschatology - Students explore apocalyptic literature and eschatological thought, engaging critically with theological interpretations of hope, judgment, and the consummation of history across diverse traditions.

MATH-TDM-806 - Theology and Digital Media - This course investigates the interaction between theology and digital culture, equipping students to reflect critically on the opportunities and challenges of faith expression in the digital age.

MATH-CLEM-807 - Creative Liturgy and Emerging Ministries - Examining innovations in worship and ministry, this course prepares students to design creative liturgical expressions and lead emerging forms of Christian community with theological integrity.

MATH-TTCPW-808 - Theology in Times of Crisis: Pandemic and War - This course addresses theological responses to global crises, equipping students to offer resilient, compassionate, and prophetic leadership amid pandemics, conflicts, and social upheavals.

MATH-MTT-900 - Master’s Thesis in Theology - The capstone project of the program, this course guides students through the research, writing, and defense of a master's thesis, demonstrating scholarly excellence, theological depth, and original contribution to the field.
