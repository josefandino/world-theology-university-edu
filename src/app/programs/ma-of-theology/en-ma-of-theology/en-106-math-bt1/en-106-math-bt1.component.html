MATH-BT1-106 - Old Testament Exegesis

Old Testament Exegesis introduces students to the critical analysis and deep interpretation of Old Testament texts, using hermeneutical and contextual tools to understand their original meaning and contemporary relevance.

Course Objectives:

  • Master the basic principles of biblical exegesis applied to the Old Testament.
  • Analyze texts considering their historical, cultural, and literary contexts.
  • Apply hermeneutical methods for responsible interpretation.
  • Relate the messages of the Old Testament to contemporary life and ministry.
  • Develop research skills and present biblical studies effectively.

Main Topics:

  • Introduction to biblical exegesis and hermeneutics
  • Historical and cultural contextualization of texts
  • Literary analysis: genres and structures in the Old Testament
  • Key exegetical research tools
  • Practical application of exegetical findings

Methodology:

The course combines theoretical classes, case studies, practical exegesis exercises, and written and oral presentations of biblical interpretation projects.

Assessment:

  • Exegetical essays on selected passages
  • Biblical analysis presentations
  • Participation in interpretation debates and workshops
  • Final exam based on the application of exegetical methods

Importance of the Course:

Old Testament Exegesis equips students with a solid foundation for interpreting Scripture accurately and responsibly, strengthening both academic preparation and ministry effectiveness.
