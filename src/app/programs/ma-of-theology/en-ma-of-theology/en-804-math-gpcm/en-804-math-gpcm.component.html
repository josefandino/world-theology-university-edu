MATH-GPCM-804 - Global Pentecostalism and Charismatic Movements

This course analyzes the theological, historical, and sociocultural dimensions of Pentecostal and Charismatic movements worldwide, offering critical perspectives on their dynamic growth and global influence. It examines their origins, doctrinal development, worldwide impact, and diverse expressions in contemporary contexts.

Course Objectives

  • Explore the historical roots of Pentecostalism and its global expansion.
  • Analyze the core doctrines and distinctive charismatic expressions of the movement.
  • Critically examine the role of Pentecostalism in intercultural, political, and social contexts.
  • Reflect on Pentecostalismâ€™s impact on theology, liturgy, and Christian mission.
  • Evaluate the challenges and opportunities posed by the Charismatic movement for the global church.

Main Topics

  • Historical roots of Pentecostalism: revivals and social contexts.
  • Key doctrines: Spirit baptism, charismatic gifts, healing, eschatology.
  • The Charismatic movement within historic churches.
  • Pentecostalism and globalization: missions, migration, digital networks.
  • Critical theological perspectives: pneumatology, ecclesiology, ethics.
  • Case studies: Pentecostal expressions in Africa, Latin America, Asia, and the West.
  • Controversies, syncretism, and spiritual renewal.

Methodology

The course combines scholarly readings, case analysis, interactive lectures, theological debates, and critical reflection on global Pentecostal experiences. Active participation and interdisciplinary dialogue are expected.

Evaluation

  • Participation in discussions and critical analysis of readings (20%).
  • Theological essays on selected course topics (25%).
  • Comparative case study on Pentecostal expressions (20%).
  • Final research project on contemporary impact (25%).
  • Oral presentation of the final project (10%).

Course Significance

This course provides a deep understanding of one of the most influential and transformative movements in contemporary Christianity. It offers theological and sociological tools to analyze Pentecostal and Charismatic phenomena critically, fostering a global, contextual, and discerning perspective on the Christian faith today.
