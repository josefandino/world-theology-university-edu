MATH-RG-606 - Religion and Globalization

Religion and Globalization is a course that explores the interaction between religion and the processes of globalization in the contemporary world. Throughout the course, students will examine the ways in which globalization affects religious beliefs, practices, institutions, and movements. Additionally, the course will analyze how religions impact global processes and how cultural, economic, and technological changes influence the way religions are experienced and practiced globally.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the processes of globalization and their effects on religions.
  • Analyze how religions adapt and respond to the challenges of globalization.
  • Examine the impact of religion on global political, social, and cultural processes.
  • Explore how religion contributes to the construction of transnational identities and communities.
  • Study the role of religion in global conflict and cooperation.

Main Topics:

  • Definition and characteristics of globalization.
  • Religion and the construction of transnational identities.
  • Religion and the global economy: the impact of religions on globalized economies.
  • Religion, technology, and media: the role of technology in religious globalization.
  • Religion and global politics: religious movements in international politics.
  • Religion, conflict, and peace in a globalized world.
  • The globalization of religions: the spread of religions across national borders.
  • Religion and human rights in the global context.

Methodology:

The course employs an interdisciplinary approach that combines case studies, academic readings, debates, and research projects. Students will examine practical examples of the interaction between religion and globalization in various parts of the world, allowing them to understand the local and global impacts of religious phenomena.

Assessment:

  • Essays on the effects of globalization on various religious traditions.
  • Written exams on the theory and practice of religious globalization.
  • Presentations on the impact of religion on global political and social processes.
  • Research on the influence of religions on global movements and international relations.

Importance of the Course:

This course is essential for understanding how globalization is changing the global religious landscape. Students will gain a critical and in-depth perspective on how religions are adapting and responding to the challenges of a globalized world.
