MATH-GC21C-207 - Global Christianity in the 21st Century

Global Christianity in the 21st Century is a course that examines the development and transformation of Christianity in the contemporary global context. Throughout the course, students will explore how Christianity is adapting to social, cultural, and political changes, and how it is expanding into new regions of the world, especially in Africa, Asia, and Latin America. The course also addresses contemporary issues facing global Christian communities, including religious pluralism, globalization, and current ethical and theological challenges.

Course Objectives:

  • Explore the global expansion of Christianity and its presence in various regions of the world.
  • Analyze contemporary trends in global Christianity, including growth in the Global South and decline in the West.
  • Study the ethical, social, and theological challenges facing Christianity in the 21st century.
  • Examine the interactions between Christianity and other religions, as well as the role of the church in contexts of religious pluralism.
  • Investigate the impact of globalization on Christian practices and the relationships between churches in different regions.

Main Topics:

  • The expansion of Christianity in Africa, Asia, and Latin America.
  • Christianity in the West: challenges and decline in secularized societies.
  • Christianity and globalization: mutual influence.
  • Christianity and religious pluralism: relationships with other religious traditions.
  • Contemporary ethical and theological challenges: human rights, social justice, and climate change ethics.
  • Christianity in the context of global migration and religious diasporas.
  • New forms of church and mission in the 21st century: digital churches and grassroots movements.

Methodology:

The course will be delivered through lectures, case studies, academic readings, and group discussions on global Christianity in the 21st century. Students will also research the influence of Christianity in specific regions and how it adapts to contemporary challenges.

Assessment:

  • Essays on the impact of globalization on Christianity.
  • Exams on major trends and challenges facing Christianity in the 21st century.
  • Research projects on Christianity in specific regions of the world.
  • Presentations on how Christianity interacts with other religions and cultures in global contexts.

Importance of the Course:

This course is essential for understanding how Christianity is changing and adapting in the 21st century, especially in the context of globalization and religious diversity. Students will gain critical insight into how Christian communities worldwide are addressing contemporary challenges and what it means to be Christian in a globalized world.
