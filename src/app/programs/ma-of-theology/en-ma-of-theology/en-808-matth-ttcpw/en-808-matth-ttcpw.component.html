MATH-TTCPW-808 - Theology in Times of Crisis: Pandemic and War

Theology in Times of Crisis: Pandemic and War is a course designed to explore theological responses to global crises such as pandemics, wars, and social catastrophes. Through biblical, historical, and contemporary analysis, the course equips students to offer a theological perspective that is resilient, compassionate, and prophetic. This approach provides comprehensive preparation for leading faith communities during times of uncertainty, collective suffering, and social reconstruction.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the biblical and theological foundations of suffering, hope, and justice in crisis contexts.
  • Analyze the historical responses of the Church to pandemics, wars, and disasters.
  • Develop a contextual theology that offers comfort, ethical guidance, and a transformative vision during periods of crisis.
  • Critically evaluate the role of pastoral and community leadership in emergency and conflict scenarios.
  • Apply theological principles to contemporary cases through practical exercises and case studies.
  • Foster a global theological ethic that is sensitive to cultural diversity and the challenges of the 21st century.

Main Topics:

  • Theology of suffering, evil, and hope
  • Pandemics in the Bible and in Church history
  • War from a biblical and ethical perspective
  • Pastoral and spiritual leadership in times of crisis
  • Resilience, trauma, and community healing
  • Contemporary case studies: COVID-19, modern wars, and forced displacement
  • Public theology and the prophetic role of the Church in society
  • Toward a global, compassionate, and transformative theology

Methodology:

The course will be delivered through virtual lectures, analysis of historical and contemporary cases, online discussion forums, practical theological reflection exercises, and collaborative projects. Emphasis is placed on autonomous learning, critical engagement, and intercultural dialogue to foster deep and applicable understanding of the content.

Assessment:

  • Critical theological essays on specific topics
  • Analysis of real cases and proposed theological responses
  • Active participation in thematic discussion forums
  • Collaborative virtual presentations
  • Final exam or integrative project articulating a contextual theological proposal

Importance of the Course:

This course is essential to contemporary theological formation, as it prepares students to face the ethical and pastoral challenges of the modern world with wisdom and compassion. It promotes a global and practical approach to theology, equipping leaders capable of addressing the spiritual, emotional, and social needs of communities affected by crises from an ethical, inclusive, and transformative standpoint.
