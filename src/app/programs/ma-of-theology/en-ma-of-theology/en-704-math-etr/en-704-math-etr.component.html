MATH-ETR-704 - Ethics in Theological Research

Ethics in Theological Research addresses the essential ethical principles guiding research in theology. Students learn about academic integrity, the responsible handling of sacred texts, confidentiality, avoiding bias, and respecting religious traditions during research processes.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the ethical principles of theological research.
  • Maintain academic integrity in theological inquiry.
  • Apply ethical standards to data collection, analysis, and interpretation.
  • Reflect critically on ethical challenges in theological research.

Main Topics:

  • Ethical principles in theology and research responsibility.
  • Confidentiality and respect for religious traditions.
  • Bias, objectivity, and interpretation of sacred texts.
  • Real-world ethical cases in theological investigation.

Methodology:

The course uses case studies, critical readings, and discussions on ethical scenarios encountered during theological research, encouraging practical application of ethical decision-making.

Assessment:

  • Essay on an ethical case study.
  • Presentation of an analyzed ethical dilemma.
  • Final written exam covering course content.

Importance of the Course:

Ethical rigor is crucial in theological research to respect both religious traditions and academic standards, ensuring credible and responsible scholarship in the theological community.
