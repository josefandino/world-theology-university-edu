MATH-CRAW-701 - Critical Reading and Academic Writing

Critical Reading and Academic Writing is a course designed to develop students' critical reading and academic writing skills. Students will learn to read academic texts analytically and write clearly, coherently, and logically using academic conventions for essays, articles, and other works.

Course Objectives:

  • Develop critical reading skills to analyze academic texts.
  • Improve academic writing focusing on clarity, coherence, and logic.
  • Structure and organize academic essays and papers.
  • Practice proper citation and referencing of sources.
  • Enhance writing through revision and editing.

Main Topics:

  • Critical reading and evaluation of academic texts.
  • Principles of academic writing composition.
  • Essay structure: introduction, body, conclusion.
  • Citation and referencing styles (APA, MLA, etc.).
  • Revision and editing of academic works.
  • Writing summaries and critical analyses.

Methodology:

The course involves assigned readings, writing exercises, text discussions, peer reviews, and revision workshops to sharpen academic reading and writing abilities.

Assessment:

  • Essays and academic papers focusing on structure and citation.
  • Participation in critical reading discussions.
  • Review and revision exercises.
  • Final exam on critical reading and academic writing principles.

Importance of the Course:

This course equips students with essential skills for success in academic and professional environments, preparing them to read critically, write persuasively, and communicate ideas effectively.
