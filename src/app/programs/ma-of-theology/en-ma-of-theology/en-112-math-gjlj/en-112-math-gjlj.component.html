MATH-GJLJ-112 - The Gospels and the Life of Jesus

The Gospels and the Life of Jesus focuses on the study of the four Gospels (Matthew, Mark, Luke, and John) to understand the life, teachings, death, and resurrection of Jesus Christ. This course explores the Gospel narratives, the historical and cultural context in which they were written, and how the accounts of Jesus shape Christian theology and life today.

Course Objectives:

  • Study the accounts of the life of Jesus according to the four Gospels.
  • Analyze the key teachings of Jesus and their relevance for Christians.
  • Explore the historical and cultural context of the 1st century in which Jesus lived.
  • Reflect on the importance of the death and resurrection of Jesus in Christian salvation.
  • Develop a deep understanding of the influence of the Gospels on contemporary Christian faith.

Main Topics:

  • Introduction to the Gospels and their sources
  • The genealogy and birth of Jesus
  • The teachings of Jesus: the Kingdom of God, love, and forgiveness
  • Miracles, parables, and ethical teachings
  • The Passion: the crucifixion, death, and resurrection of Jesus
  • The relationship between the Gospels and Christian life today

Methodology:

This course is based on the reading, discussion, and analysis of Gospel texts, with an emphasis on their theological content and historical context. Students will engage in debates and activities that allow them to better understand the life of Jesus and how to apply his teachings in daily life.

Assessment:

  • Essays on key passages from the Gospels.
  • Exams on the historical and theological content of Jesus' life.
  • Presentations on the influence of Jesus' teachings in Christian practice.
  • Research projects on specific aspects of Jesus' life and ministry.

Importance of the Course:

This course is essential for anyone seeking a deeper understanding of the figure of Jesus, the interpretation of the Gospels, and the foundation of the Christian faith. Through this study, students will develop a richer and more meaningful understanding of the principles of Christianity.
