MATH-BG1-104 - Koine Greek I

Koine Greek I introduces students to the original language of the New Testament. The course covers the basics of the Greek alphabet, pronunciation, fundamental grammatical structures, and essential vocabulary needed to begin reading simple biblical texts.

Course Objectives:

  • Learn the Koine Greek alphabet and pronunciation.
  • Understand the basic concepts of Greek grammar.
  • Memorize essential New Testament vocabulary.
  • Start translating simple phrases and passages from Biblical Greek.
  • Develop basic skills in reading and textual analysis in Greek.

Main Topics:

  • Greek alphabet and phonetics
  • Definite articles and nouns
  • Basic verb conjugations
  • Adjectives and pronouns
  • Translation of short passages from the New Testament

Methodology:

The course focuses on active learning through readings, written exercises, pronunciation practices, and guided translations of biblical texts.

Assessment:

  • Short vocabulary and grammar quizzes
  • Reading and translation tests
  • Simple phrase analysis projects
  • Final exam on Koine Greek fundamentals

Importance of the Course:

Koine Greek I provides a solid foundation for understanding and directly studying the New Testament in its original language, enriching biblical interpretation.
