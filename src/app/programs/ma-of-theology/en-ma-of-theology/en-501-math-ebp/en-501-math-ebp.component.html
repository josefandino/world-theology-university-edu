MATH-EBP-501 - Expository Biblical Preaching

Expository Biblical Preaching is a course that teaches students how to preach sermons based on a detailed and faithful analysis of Scripture. Through this course, students will learn how to correctly interpret biblical texts, understand their original context, and apply their message to contemporary congregations. The expository approach allows preachers to present the message of the Bible clearly, accurately, and relevantly, ensuring that the preaching is faithful to the text and gospel-centered.

Course Objectives:

  • Develop skills to interpret and apply Scripture in an expository manner.
  • Learn how to construct sermons that follow the flow of the biblical text and highlight its central message.
  • Equip students to communicate the biblical message effectively in different cultural and congregational contexts.
  • Establish a solid biblical foundation for preaching that encourages teaching and spiritual transformation.
  • Develop the ability to preach with clarity, coherence, and practical application.

Main Topics:

  • The process of biblical interpretation: principles and methods of exegesis.
  • How to structure an expository sermon: introduction, development, and conclusion.
  • The importance of contextualization in preaching.
  • Christ-centered preaching: how to preach Christ from all of Scripture.
  • The practical application of biblical principles to contemporary Christian life.

Methodology:

This course will use a combination of biblical studies, sermon analysis, and preaching practice in a controlled environment. Students will be guided through the process of exegesis and sermon construction, with opportunities to preach in class and receive constructive feedback. Practical activities will be included to enhance communication and delivery skills, as well as discussions on contemporary challenges in preaching.

Assessment:

  • Exams on biblical interpretation methods and sermon construction.
  • Preaching expository sermons with feedback from the instructor and peers.
  • Essays on the application of exegesis in effective preaching.

Importance of the Course:

This course is essential for those who wish to preach the Word of God faithfully and effectively, developing an expository approach that leads the congregation to a deeper understanding of Scripture and fosters a transformed Christian life. Students will learn how to communicate the gospel message with accuracy and relevance, ensuring that their preaching becomes a powerful tool for the edification of the church.
