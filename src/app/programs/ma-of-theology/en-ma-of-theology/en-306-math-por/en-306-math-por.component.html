MATH-POR-306 - Philosophy of Religion

Philosophy of Religion is a course that explores the fundamental philosophical aspects of religious beliefs, religious language, and the metaphysical, ethical, and epistemological problems that arise in the religious context. The course addresses issues such as the existence of God, the nature of faith and reason, the problem of evil and suffering, and the issue of religious plurality. Through critical reflection on these topics, students will learn to analyze and evaluate the various philosophical positions on religion and its role in human life.

Course Objectives:

  • Develop a critical understanding of the main philosophical problems related to religion.
  • Analyze the various philosophical positions on the existence of God, the nature of faith, and the relationship between faith and reason.
  • Study the philosophical implications of evil and suffering in religious traditions.
  • Examine theories of religious plurality and the approach to religion in a pluralistic society.
  • Reflect on the role of religion in human morality and ethics.

Main Topics:

  • The existence of God: philosophical arguments for and against.
  • The problem of evil and suffering in religions.
  • Faith and reason: theories about the nature of religious belief.
  • Religious plurality: theologies and philosophical approaches to world religions.
  • Philosophy of religion and ethics: the role of religion in human morality.

Methodology:

The course will be conducted through essential philosophical readings, in-class debates, and analysis of key texts from philosophers of religion. Students will be encouraged to critically reflect on the philosophical problems presented and participate in discussions about the responses that philosophy offers to religious and spiritual challenges.

Assessment:

  • Essays on key philosophical problems in religion.
  • Presentations on philosophical theories about God and faith.
  • Exams on the relationship between religion and ethics.
  • Critical discussion of classic and contemporary philosophical texts.

Importance of the Course:

This course is essential for those interested in understanding the great philosophical questions about religion, faith, and morality. It helps students develop critical skills to address the most fundamental issues that arise in the interaction between philosophy and religious beliefs, and provides a framework for reflecting on the role of religion in the contemporary world.
