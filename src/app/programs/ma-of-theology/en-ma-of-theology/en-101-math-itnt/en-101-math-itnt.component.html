MATH-ITNT-101 - Introduction to the New Testament

Introduction to the New Testament offers an overview of the historical, literary, and theological aspects of the New Testament. Students will explore the Gospels, Epistles, and other writings, analyzing their major themes, historical background, and relevance for contemporary Christian life.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the historical and cultural background of the New Testament.
  • Identify key theological and literary themes across New Testament books.
  • Develop skills in critical and contextual interpretation of New Testament texts.
  • Explore the life and teachings of Jesus and the development of the early church.
  • Apply New Testament principles to personal and community life today.

Main Topics:

  • Overview of the Gospels, Acts, Epistles, and Revelation
  • Historical context: Roman Empire, Second Temple Judaism, and early Christianity
  • Theology of the Kingdom of God, grace, faith, and salvation
  • Authors, audiences, and purposes of New Testament books
  • Methods of interpreting New Testament texts

Methodology:

The course includes lectures, assigned readings, group discussions, thematic analyses, and reflective papers to foster a deep and practical understanding of New Testament writings.

Assessment:

  • Reading quizzes and assignments
  • Essays on specific topics or books
  • Group presentations on key figures and themes
  • Final exam covering overall course content

Importance of the Course:

This course provides a strong foundation for understanding Christian faith, Scriptures, and early church history, equipping students for advanced theological studies and for applying biblical teachings to daily life.
