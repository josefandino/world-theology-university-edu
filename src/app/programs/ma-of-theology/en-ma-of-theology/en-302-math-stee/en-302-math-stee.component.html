MATH-STEE-302 - Systematic Theology III: Ecclesiology and Eschatology

Students examine the doctrine of the Church and the theology of the last things, analyzing their biblical foundations and historical interpretations. The course equips students for critical leadership in both ecclesial and academic contexts.

Course Objectives

  • Understand the nature, mission, and structures of the Church according to Scripture and Christian tradition.
  • Analyze historical and contemporary models of Ecclesiology and their practical implications.
  • Study the main eschatological perspectives and their development throughout the history of theology.
  • Evaluate the impact of eschatology on Christian ethics, spirituality, and hope.
  • Develop an integrated ecclesiological and eschatological vision applicable to contemporary ministry.

Main Topics

  • Biblical foundations of the Church: people of God, body of Christ, temple of the Spirit.
  • Ministries, sacraments, and church governance.
  • The Church in history: models, reforms, and challenges.
  • Personal and cosmic eschatology: death, judgment, heaven, and new creation.
  • Premillennialism, amillennialism, and postmillennialism: theological approaches.
  • The Kingdom of God: already but not yet.
  • Eschatological hope and the mission of the Church in today's world.

Methodology

The course integrates lectures, analysis of biblical and patristic texts, theological debates, and practical application to church life. It promotes critical dialogue and the development of a contextualized theology.

Evaluation

  • Active participation in theological debates and analysis (20%).
  • Theological essays on ecclesiological or eschatological topics (30%).
  • Written exam on doctrinal and historical content (25%).
  • Final project: contextualized ecclesiological proposal (15%).
  • Oral presentation on an eschatological viewpoint (10%).

Course Significance

This course allows students to deepen their theological understanding of the Church and God's promised future. It provides tools for informed pastoral and academic leadership, capable of articulating hope and theological vision with biblical faithfulness and contemporary relevance.
