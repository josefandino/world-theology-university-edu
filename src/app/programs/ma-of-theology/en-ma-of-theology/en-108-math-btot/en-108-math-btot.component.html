MATH-BTOT-108 - Biblical Theology of the Old Testament

Biblical Theology of the Old Testament explores the major theological themes of the Old Testament, their development throughout biblical history, and their significance for a comprehensive understanding of divine revelation.

Course Objectives:

  • Identify and analyze the main theological themes of the Old Testament.
  • Understand the relationship between different books and their theological contributions.
  • Explore the progressive development of revelation within the Old Testament context.
  • Connect Old Testament theological concepts to contemporary Christian faith.
  • Develop critical skills for interpreting biblical texts within their theological framework.

Main Topics:

  • Introduction to Biblical Theology
  • The covenant as a central theological structure
  • God’s sovereignty and holiness in the Old Testament
  • The role of Israel in God’s redemptive plan
  • Messianic hope and prophetic writings

Methodology:

The course includes lectures, biblical case studies, theological debates, and analysis of key themes based on selected texts.

Assessment:

  • Theological essays
  • Midterm and final exams
  • Research projects on specific topics
  • Oral presentations on biblical-theological analysis

Importance of the Course:

Biblical Theology of the Old Testament provides a solid foundation for interpreting Scripture, understanding the continuity of divine revelation, and strengthening theological and ministerial life.
