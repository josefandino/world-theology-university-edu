MATH-TWL-504 - Theology of Worship and Liturgy

Theology of Worship and Liturgy is a course that explores the theological and biblical foundations of Christian worship and liturgy in the church. Students will learn about the importance of worship in the Christian life, the biblical principles behind liturgy, and how different Christian traditions have developed their worship practices over time. Additionally, the course will explore how liturgy reflects and shapes the believer's relationship with God and the faith community.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the theological basis of worship and liturgy in the Christian tradition.
  • Explore the role of worship in the life of the church and its connection to the believer's spiritual life.
  • Study the key elements of liturgy and its development throughout church history.
  • Examine liturgical practices in different Christian traditions and their impact on the faith community.
  • Develop a critical understanding of the theology of worship and its relationship to the everyday life of the Christian.

Main Topics:

  • The theological foundations of Christian worship.
  • The importance of liturgy in the formation of the faith community.
  • Study of different types of liturgy: sacraments, prayer, song, and other elements.
  • The relationship between public worship and personal worship.
  • The various liturgical traditions throughout church history: Catholic, Protestant, Orthodox, and others.
  • The influence of culture on worship and liturgy practices.

Methodology:

The course will combine theoretical classes with case study analysis, studies of liturgical elements, and observation of worship services in different Christian traditions. Students will also participate in planning worship services, considering their theology and liturgical structure, as well as their impact on the community.

Assessment:

  • Essays on the theology of worship and liturgy.
  • Participation in planning liturgical services.
  • Presentation of research papers on the role of liturgy in contemporary church life.

Importance of the Course:

This course is essential for those serving in worship ministry and church leadership. By studying the theology of worship and liturgy, students will be equipped to design and lead meaningful worship services that strengthen the spiritual life of the Christian community, connecting tradition with the everyday experience of believers.
