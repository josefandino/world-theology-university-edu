MATH-PLDF-500 - Pastoral Leadership and Discipleship Formation

This course equips students with strategic leadership skills for pastoral ministry, emphasizing discipleship models rooted in biblical and theological foundations. It prepares leaders to guide spiritual growth with vision and integrity, strengthening healthy and mission-oriented church communities.

Course Objectives

  • Develop competencies in pastoral leadership based on biblical, ethical, and strategic principles.
  • Analyze historical and contemporary models of discipleship and their ongoing relevance.
  • Design spiritual formation processes that foster Christian maturity and disciple multiplication.
  • Integrate theological principles into ministry decision-making and church leadership.
  • Promote visionary, integral, and context-aware pastoral leadership for the 21st century.

Main Topics

  • Biblical and theological foundations of Christian leadership.
  • Historical and contemporary discipleship models.
  • Vision, mission, and strategic planning in pastoral leadership.
  • Personal and corporate spiritual formation.
  • Mentoring, pastoral care, and emerging leadership development.
  • Conflict resolution, pastoral ethics, and leader well-being.
  • Assessment and sustainability of discipleship processes in the local church.

Methodology

The course includes theoretical lectures, case studies, practical leadership exercises, discussion forums, and the development of ministry projects. Collaborative, reflective, and contextual learning will be encouraged throughout.

Evaluation

  • Active participation in activities and discussion forums (20%).
  • Written reflections and analysis of discipleship models (25%).
  • Pastoral leadership practical exercises (20%).
  • Final project: design of a comprehensive discipleship formation plan (25%).
  • Oral presentation of the final project (10%).
