MATH-IDCW-605 - Interfaith Dialogue and Christian Witness

Interfaith Dialogue and Christian Witness is a course that explores the importance of dialogue between different religious traditions and the Christian witness in interreligious contexts. Students will study theological and philosophical principles that guide interfaith dialogue, the history of relations between Christianity and other religions, and Christian witnessing practices in a pluralistic world. Additionally, the challenges and opportunities that arise when Christians engage in dialogue with people from other religious traditions will be explored.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the theological foundations of interfaith dialogue.
  • Explore the history of Christian interactions with Judaism, Islam, Hinduism, and Buddhism.
  • Analyze the principles of Christian witness in interreligious contexts.
  • Identify the opportunities and challenges that arise from interreligious dialogue.
  • Develop skills for respectful and effective participation in interfaith dialogue.

Main Topics:

  • Theological foundations of interfaith dialogue: love, respect, and the search for truth.
  • History of Christian interactions with Judaism, Islam, Hinduism, and Buddhism.
  • Christian witness in a pluralistic context: evangelism and respect for other beliefs.
  • The concept of "mercy" and "compassion" in interfaith dialogue.
  • The role of the Church in the modern world and interreligious cooperation in building peace.
  • Theological and ethical challenges in interfaith dialogue.
  • Case studies of interfaith dialogue and its application in Christian mission.

Methodology:

The course combines academic readings, debates, case studies, and group work to develop a deep understanding of interfaith dialogue and Christian witness in the modern context. Students will engage in debates on theological, ethical, and philosophical issues related to interfaith dialogue, and will be challenged to reflect on how to live out their Christian faith in a pluralistic and diverse world.

Assessment:

  • Essays on key topics in interfaith dialogue and Christian witness.
  • Written exams on the history and principles of interfaith dialogue.
  • Presentations on the challenges and opportunities in Christian-Islamic, Christian-Jewish, and other relationships.
  • Research on how different religious traditions contribute to interfaith dialogue.

Importance of the Course:

This course is essential for understanding how Christians can witness their faith in a diverse and pluralistic world. By learning to engage respectfully and effectively in interfaith dialogue, students will be better prepared to interact with people from different religious traditions, promote peace, and share the gospel in a relevant and contextualized way.
