MATH-ICT-802 - Indigenous and Contextual Theology

Indigenous and Contextual Theology explores how indigenous communities and worldviews contribute to Christian theology, offering alternative and contextualized perspectives. The course focuses on integrating indigenous traditions respectfully into theological discourse and examines the challenges of decolonization and contextual evangelization.

Course Objectives:

  • Explore how indigenous worldviews enrich Christian theology.
  • Analyze tensions and opportunities between Christianity and indigenous traditions.
  • Study contextual theology as a response to contemporary indigenous challenges.
  • Reflect on colonization's impact on indigenous communities and respectful theological practices.

Main Topics:

  • Indigenous worldviews and Christian theology.
  • Principles and practices of contextual theology.
  • Decolonization of theology.
  • Evangelization in indigenous contexts.
  • Contemporary indigenous theological contributions.

Methodology:

Through academic readings, field research, interviews, and case studies, students reflect on indigenous theologies that emerge authentically from cultural and spiritual experiences.

Assessment:

  • Essay on indigenous and Christian theology integration.
  • Case study presentation on indigenous-Christian contexts.
  • Research project on evangelization practices among indigenous communities.

Importance of the Course:

Vital for contextualizing Christian theology in diverse cultures, this course fosters respect for indigenous traditions and enriches the global Christian theological landscape.
