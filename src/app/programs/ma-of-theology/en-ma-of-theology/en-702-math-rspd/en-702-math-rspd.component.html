MATH-RSPD-702 - Research Seminar I: Project Design

Research Seminar I: Project Design is an introductory course designed to guide students through the process of designing a research project. Students will learn to formulate research questions, select appropriate methodologies, and plan feasible, ethical, and impactful research projects.

Course Objectives:

  • Formulate clear and well-defined research questions.
  • Select appropriate methodologies and justify them.
  • Conduct literature reviews and identify knowledge gaps.
  • Develop detailed and achievable research plans.
  • Present and defend research project designs effectively.

Main Topics:

  • Formulating research questions.
  • Selecting and justifying research methodologies.
  • Literature review and gap analysis.
  • Designing a research plan: objectives and hypotheses.
  • Presenting and defending a research project.

Methodology:

The course includes theoretical presentations, group discussions, and practical activities where students develop and present their own research projects, receiving feedback and refining their plans.

Assessment:

  • Research proposal presentation with methodology justification.
  • Critical review of existing literature in the study area.
  • Development of a complete research plan.
  • Final presentation and defense of the research project.

Importance of the Course:

This course is fundamental for students who wish to design solid, rigorous research projects, laying the foundation for successful research and contributions to their academic or professional fields.
