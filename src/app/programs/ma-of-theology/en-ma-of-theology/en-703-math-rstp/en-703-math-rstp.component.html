MATH-RSTP-703 - Research Seminar II: Thesis and Presentation

Research Seminar II: Thesis and Presentation is an advanced course where students develop and present their research thesis. Students will work under academic supervision to structure, write, and defend their work, applying methodological principles learned previously, focusing on clarity, coherence, and logical argumentation.

Course Objectives:

  • Develop and write a research thesis based on the previous project.
  • Analyze and interpret research findings applying appropriate methodologies.
  • Prepare a coherent and effective thesis presentation.
  • Defend the thesis before an academic committee.

Main Topics:

  • Thesis structure: Introduction, objectives, framework, methodology, results, and conclusions.
  • Data analysis and discussion of results.
  • Scientific writing and argumentation clarity.
  • Thesis defense preparation: presenting and responding to questions.

Methodology:

Students will attend regular advisory sessions, group reviews for feedback, and practice presentations to prepare for the formal defense of their thesis work.

Assessment:

  • Chapter submissions and reviews throughout the course.
  • Mid-term progress presentation.
  • Final thesis defense before an academic committee.

Importance of the Course:

This course is essential to demonstrate students' research capabilities, academic rigor, and ability to present theological findings, contributing meaningfully to their field of study.
