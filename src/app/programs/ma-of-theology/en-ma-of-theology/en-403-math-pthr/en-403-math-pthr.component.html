MATH-PTHR-403 - Political Theology and Human Rights

Political Theology and Human Rights is a course that examines the relationship between Christian doctrines and politics, with a particular focus on human rights. The course explores how Christian teachings on justice, human dignity, and divine authority intersect with universal human rights, addressing topics such as freedom, equality, social justice, and peace. Students will investigate the historical influence of political theology on the formulation of human rights and how these principles apply in the contemporary context, including the role of the church in defending human rights.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the theological foundations of politics and its relationship to human rights.
  • Analyze Christian doctrines that influence the development and defense of human rights.
  • Study the role of the church in promoting and protecting human rights throughout history and today.
  • Develop skills to apply theological principles in analyzing contemporary political and social issues related to human rights.
  • Reflect on the interaction between political theology and human rights in various cultural and political contexts.

Main Topics:

  • Foundations of political theology: Theology, power, and authority in the Christian tradition.
  • Human rights in the Scriptures: Human dignity and justice in the Bible.
  • The church and human rights: The history and function of the church in defending human rights.
  • Social justice and politics: Christian theology and the promotion of social justice.
  • Contemporary political theology: The influence of theology on modern human rights policies.

Methodology:

The course will be conducted through studies of theological texts, analysis of historical documents, and discussion of contemporary human rights-related cases. Students will also engage in debates, theological reflections, and practical projects focused on analyzing political and human rights issues from a Christian perspective.
