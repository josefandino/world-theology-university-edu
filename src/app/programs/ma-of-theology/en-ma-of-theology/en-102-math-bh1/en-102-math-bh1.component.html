MATH-BH1-102 - Biblical Hebrew I

Biblical Hebrew I introduces students to the classical Hebrew language of the Old Testament. Focus is placed on learning the Hebrew alphabet, pronunciation, basic grammatical structures, and essential vocabulary for reading and interpreting biblical texts.

Course Objectives:

  • Recognize and correctly pronounce the Hebrew alphabet.
  • Understand basic grammatical and syntactical structures of Biblical Hebrew.
  • Acquire fundamental vocabulary from the Old Testament.
  • Read and translate simple phrases directly from the biblical text.
  • Develop basic skills for exegesis and biblical study in Hebrew.

Main Topics:

  • Hebrew alphabet and vocalization systems (niqqud)
  • Pronunciation and writing rules
  • Formation of nouns and adjectives
  • Basic verb conjugation (Qal imperfect and perfect)
  • Introduction to Hebrew syntax

Methodology:

The course includes practical lessons, reading and writing exercises, vocabulary memorization activities, and translation of simple passages from the Hebrew Bible.

Assessment:

  • Alphabet and vocabulary recognition quizzes
  • Grammar and translation exercises
  • Oral reading of selected texts
  • Final written exam

Importance of the Course:

Biblical Hebrew I provides essential tools for the serious study of the Scriptures in their original language, promoting a deeper and more accurate understanding of biblical texts.
