MATH-TDM-806 - Theology and Digital Media

Theology and Digital Media explores how digital technologies and social media influence Christian communication, ministry, evangelism, and theological reflection in the contemporary world.

Course Objectives:

  • Analyze the impact of digital media on Christian theology and practice.
  • Evaluate ethical challenges of digital engagement in religious contexts.
  • Explore strategies for digital evangelism and pastoral ministry.
  • Develop critical perspectives on theological use of digital technology.

Main Topics:

  • Foundations of theology and digital communication.
  • Impact of social media on worship and community life.
  • Ethical issues in technology and faith.
  • Opportunities and tensions in digital ministry.

Methodology:

Students engage in lectures, case studies, practical projects, and group discussions analyzing both the positive and negative impacts of digital technology on the church.

Assessment:

  • Essay on social media and Christian practice.
  • Practical digital ministry project.
  • Presentation on ethical challenges of digital media in church contexts.

Importance of the Course:

This course equips future Christian leaders to thoughtfully navigate digital realities, using technology as a tool for faithful and effective ministry in the 21st century.
