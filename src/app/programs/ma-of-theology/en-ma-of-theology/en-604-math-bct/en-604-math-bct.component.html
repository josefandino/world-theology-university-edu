MATH-BCT-604 - Buddhism and Christian Theology

Buddhism and Christian Theology is a comparative course that explores the similarities and differences between Buddhism and Christian theology. Throughout the course, students will examine the foundational principles of both religious traditions, their teachings on suffering, salvation, ethics, and the nature of reality. Special attention will be given to areas of convergence and the challenges of interfaith dialogue between Buddhism and Christianity, focusing on how both traditions address the issue of human suffering, the purpose of life, and hope for the future.

Course Objectives:

  • Examine the foundational principles of Buddhism and Christian theology.
  • Analyze teachings on suffering and salvation in both traditions.
  • Explore religious practices in Buddhism and Christianity.
  • Investigate areas of convergence and challenges in interfaith dialogue between Buddhism and Christianity.
  • Develop a critical understanding of the theological teachings of both traditions in relation to the nature of reality and the purpose of life.

Main Topics:

  • The Four Noble Truths in Buddhism and their relationship to the Christian concept of suffering.
  • The Eightfold Path and Christian teachings on morality and living according to the example of Christ.
  • Salvation in Buddhism: attaining Nirvana versus salvation in Christ.
  • Religious practices in Buddhism: meditation, monasticism, and rituals.
  • The role of compassion and love in Buddhism and Christian theology.
  • The afterlife: rebirth and the final judgment.
  • Interfaith dialogue: points of connection and differences between Buddhism and Christianity.

Methodology:

This course uses a comparative approach to study the teachings of Buddhism and Christian theology. Students will analyze foundational texts from both traditions, including Buddhist sutras and Christian scriptures, and participate in debates and discussions to explore their teachings in depth. The course will also encourage critical thinking about how these two religions can influence and challenge contemporary societies.

Assessment:

  • Essays on key teachings of Buddhism and Christian theology.
  • Written exams on the areas of convergence and differences between the two traditions.
