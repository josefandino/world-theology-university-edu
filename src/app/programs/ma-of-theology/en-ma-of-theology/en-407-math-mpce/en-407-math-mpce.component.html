MATH-MPCE-407 - Migration, Poverty, and Christian Engagement

Migration, Poverty, and Christian Engagement is a course that examines the intersection of migration and poverty in the global context, and how Christian principles can guide an ethical and compassionate response to these challenges. The course explores the underlying causes of migration, such as poverty, violence, and lack of opportunities, and how the church and Christians can engage in promoting justice, human dignity, and solidarity. Both theological aspects and practical actions to support migrants and the impoverished in an increasingly interconnected world will be explored.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the causes and effects of migration and poverty from a Christian perspective.
  • Explore the Christian engagement with migrants and people in poverty.
  • Analyze the role of the church in promoting social justice and human dignity.
  • Develop an ethical and practical approach to addressing the challenges of migration and poverty in local and global communities.
  • Encourage Christian commitment to the struggle for human rights, social inclusion, and the restoration of justice.

Main Topics:

  • Theology of migration: Biblical perspectives on human mobility.
  • Poverty in the biblical context: Understanding poverty and the call to justice.
  • The role of the church in helping migrants and impoverished people.
  • Solidarity and compassion: Christian responses to global migration and poverty challenges.
  • Social justice and human rights: Christian commitment to human dignity and equity.

Methodology:

The course will be based on the study of biblical texts, church teachings, and case studies on migration and poverty. The analysis of public policies and programs addressing these issues will be promoted, and various forms of Christian engagement in social action will be explored. Students will participate in debates, research, and practical projects that allow them to develop concrete responses to these challenges both locally and globally.

Assessment:

  • Essays on the theology of migration and poverty from a Christian perspective.
  • Analysis of policies and programs aimed at the social inclusion of migrants and the impoverished.
  • Christian engagement projects and social action in local and global contexts.

Importance of the Course:

This course is essential for those who wish to understand how Christian principles can be applied to address global issues of migration and poverty. Students will develop the necessary tools to work in social justice initiatives and promote human dignity, both within the church and in society at large. Through this course, the aim is to inspire active commitment to the most vulnerable in the contemporary global context.
