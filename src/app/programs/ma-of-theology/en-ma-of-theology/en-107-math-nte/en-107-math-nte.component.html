MATH-NTE-107 - New Testament Exegesis

New Testament Exegesis guides students through critical analysis, contextual interpretation, and practical application of New Testament texts, using exegetical and hermeneutical research tools.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand and apply the basic principles of biblical exegesis to the New Testament.
  • Analyze passages considering their historical, cultural, and linguistic context.
  • Use modern hermeneutical methods in biblical interpretation.
  • Interpret the theological message of the New Testament in relation to contemporary life and ministry.
  • Develop research projects and present biblical studies effectively.

Main Topics:

  • Introduction to New Testament exegesis and hermeneutics
  • Historical contextualization: the New Testament world
  • Study of literary genres (Gospels, Epistles, Apocalyptic)
  • Use of linguistic tools (Koine Greek)
  • Contemporary applications of New Testament texts

Methodology:

The course combines theoretical lectures, practical exegesis exercises, selected passage analysis, hermeneutics workshops, and written and oral presentations.

Assessment:

  • Exegetical essays on key passages
  • Biblical analysis presentations
  • Participation in debates and workshops
  • Final exam on critical interpretation

Importance of the Course:

New Testament Exegesis provides essential skills for faithfully interpreting and applying New Testament texts, strengthening academic, ministerial, and personal endeavors.
