MATH-JAM-602 - Judaism in Antiquity and Modernity

Judaism in Antiquity and Modernity is a course that explores the history of Judaism from its origins in antiquity to its evolution in the modern era. It addresses the formation of Jewish tradition in ancient Israel, its development during the diaspora, and its influence on the cultures of antiquity. Additionally, the course examines the impact of modern events, such as the rise of Zionism, the creation of the State of Israel, and the challenges faced by Judaism in the contemporary world. Students will analyze the transformations of Judaism, its religious practices, and its role in today's society.

Course Objectives:

  • Explore the origins and evolution of Judaism from antiquity to modernity.
  • Analyze the influences of the Jewish diaspora and its interactions with other ancient cultures.
  • Examine modern events that have transformed Judaism, such as Zionism and the creation of the State of Israel.
  • Develop a critical understanding of modern Judaism and its contemporary challenges.

Main Topics:

  • The origins of Judaism in ancient Israel: patriarchs, prophets, and laws.
  • The Jewish diaspora and its impact on culture and religion.
  • Judaism in the Middle Ages: interactions with other religions and cultures.
  • The rise of Zionism and the creation of the State of Israel.
  • Challenges of modern Judaism: identity, pluralism, and relations with other religions.

Methodology:

This course combines a historical and theological approach to study Judaism. Students will analyze ancient texts, historical documents, and contemporary studies. Academic resources, debates, and case studies will be used to deepen the understanding of the most relevant aspects of Judaism, from its foundation to its impact today.

Assessment:

  • Essays on historical and contemporary topics in Judaism.
  • Written exams on the development of Judaism in antiquity and modernity.
  • Group presentations on key events in Jewish history.
  • Research on the influence of Judaism in modern society.

Importance of the Course:

This course provides an in-depth understanding of one of the most influential religions in the world. By studying its history, modern development, and current challenges, students will be better equipped to understand the social, political, and religious dynamics that have shaped and continue to shape the contemporary world.
