MATH-IOCP-307 - Introduction to Christian Philosophy

Introduction to Christian Philosophy is a course that explores the major philosophical currents within Christian thought, addressing topics such as the nature of God, creation, morality, human freedom, and the relationship between faith and reason. Students will study how Christian philosophy has influenced the development of Western thought and how it addresses fundamental questions of human life. The course also examines the intersections between philosophy and theology, providing a deep understanding of how philosophical principles apply to Christian beliefs.

Course Objectives:

  • Develop an understanding of the major philosophical currents within Christianity.
  • Explore the relationship between philosophy and theology in the Christian tradition.
  • Analyze philosophical problems about the existence of God, good and evil, and human freedom.
  • Reflect on the impact of Christian philosophy on contemporary thought.
  • Examine the contributions of key Christian philosophers, from the Church Fathers to modern philosophers.

Main Topics:

  • The existence of God: philosophical arguments for and against.
  • The relationship between faith and reason in Christian thought.
  • Christian morality: the good, evil, and Christian ethics.
  • Human freedom and free will from a Christian perspective.
  • The influence of Christian philosophy on Western philosophy.

Methodology:

The course will be conducted through the study of key philosophical texts by Christian thinkers, in-class debates, and group discussions about the fundamental philosophical problems addressed by Christian beliefs. Students will be encouraged to critically reflect on the ideas presented and actively engage in discussing how philosophy can influence the understanding of the Christian faith.

Assessment:

  • Essays on key philosophical topics in the Christian context.
  • Presentations on the contributions of Christian philosophers to philosophy.
  • Exams on the relationship between philosophy and theology.
  • Critical discussion of Christian and contemporary philosophical texts.

Importance of the Course:

This course is fundamental for those interested in understanding how Christian philosophy has shaped Western thought and how it addresses fundamental philosophical issues. It provides a solid foundation for exploring the philosophical themes arising from religious beliefs, and offers a framework for integrating faith and reason into daily life.
