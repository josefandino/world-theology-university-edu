MATH-BTNT-109 - Biblical Theology of the New Testament

Biblical Theology of the New Testament examines the major theological themes that emerge in the New Testament, their development, and their significance for a comprehensive understanding of divine revelation through the life, teachings, and work of Jesus Christ.

Course Objectives:

  • Explore the key theological themes of the New Testament.
  • Understand the central role of Jesus Christ in divine revelation.
  • Analyze the relationship between the Gospels and apostolic letters in their theological context.
  • Study the development of early Christian thought through the New Testament texts.
  • Develop skills to interpret and apply New Testament theological themes in the contemporary context.

  • The centrality of Jesus Christ and His redemptive work

  • Christian eschatology and hope

Methodology:

This course is based on lectures, key passage studies, New Testament text analysis, and group discussions on the main theological themes.

Assessment:

  • Theological essays
  • Midterm and final exams
  • Oral presentations on specific topics
  • Research on the relationship between the Gospels and apostolic letters

Importance of the Course:

Biblical Theology of the New Testament provides a deep understanding of key Christian themes, from the life and work of Jesus Christ to the development of the early church, offering a solid foundation for theological and ministerial practice.
