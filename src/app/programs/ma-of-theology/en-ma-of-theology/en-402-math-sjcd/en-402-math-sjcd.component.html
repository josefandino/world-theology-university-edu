MATH-SJCD-402 - Social Justice and Christian Doctrine

Social Justice and Christian Doctrine is a course that explores how Christian principles of justice, love for neighbor, and solidarity are applied to contemporary social issues. The course addresses topics such as poverty, inequality, discrimination, human rights, and economic justice from a Christian perspective. Students will examine biblical teachings, church history, and Christian doctrine to understand how the Christian faith can be a transformative force in promoting social justice in the modern world.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the fundamental principles of social justice within the Christian context.
  • Analyze contemporary social issues from a biblical and theological perspective.
  • Develop skills to apply Christian teachings in promoting social justice in today’s society.
  • Reflect on the role of the church and Christians in the struggle for social justice.
  • Examine the implications of social justice in Christian doctrine, including issues of poverty, human rights, and economic ethics.

Main Topics:

  • Social justice in the Scriptures: A biblical approach to justice and love for neighbor.
  • Theology of justice: Theological foundations of social justice in the Christian tradition.
  • Poverty and inequality: Social challenges and Christian responses to poverty.
  • Human rights and human dignity: Christian teaching on human rights.
  • Economic justice: Christian ethics in economics and the distribution of wealth.

Methodology:

The course will be conducted through biblical readings, case studies on current social injustices, class debates on the relationship between faith and social justice, and theological reflections on how the church can respond to social challenges. Students will also engage in practical activities and projects to promote social justice in their local communities.

Assessment:

  • Essays and research papers on social justice issues from a Christian perspective.
  • Case studies on situations of poverty, inequality, and discrimination.
  • Presentations and debates on applying Christian principles to contemporary social problems.
  • Community service projects focused on promoting social justice.

Importance of the Course:

This course is crucial for students interested in understanding how the Christian faith can be a powerful response to today’s social problems. By integrating Christian doctrine with the struggles for social justice, students will be better equipped to be agents of change, working towards a more just and equitable society.
