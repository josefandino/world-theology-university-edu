MATH-BH1-103 - Biblical Hebrew II

Biblical Hebrew II continues the study of classical Hebrew, focusing on more complex grammatical structures, expanding vocabulary, and strengthening skills in translating and analyzing longer and more challenging biblical texts.

Course Objectives:

  • Reinforce knowledge of the Hebrew alphabet and pronunciation.
  • Master advanced grammatical structures and verb conjugations.
  • Increase biblical vocabulary for deeper comprehension.
  • Translate and analyze more complex Old Testament passages.
  • Apply basic principles of exegesis in the original language.

Main Topics:

  • Advanced verb conjugations (piel, pual, hiphil, hophal, hitpael)
  • Complex syntactic constructions
  • Subordinate clauses and connectors
  • Advanced use of prepositions and particles
  • Reading and analysis of narrative and poetic texts

Methodology:

The course combines theoretical instruction with reading practices, translation exercises, grammatical analysis, and discussions of selected biblical passages.

Assessment:

  • Translation and grammatical analysis exercises
  • Oral reading and comprehension of extended texts
  • Midterm exams on grammar and vocabulary
  • Final exam on translation and textual analysis

Importance of the Course:

Biblical Hebrew II enables students to engage more deeply with sacred texts, strengthening their ability to conduct rigorous and accurate biblical studies.
