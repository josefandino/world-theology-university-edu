MATH-CLEM-807 - Creative Liturgy and Emerging Ministries

Creative Liturgy and Emerging Ministries explores innovative approaches to Christian liturgy and the development of ministries that address the needs of contemporary and emerging communities, emphasizing creativity, cultural relevance, and faithful expression.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand Christian liturgical principles and their creative adaptation.
  • Develop inclusive, relevant liturgies for contemporary ministries.
  • Investigate current trends in emerging ministries and their challenges.
  • Propose innovative applications of liturgy in urban, youth, and diverse ministry contexts.

Main Topics:

  • Foundations of traditional and creative liturgy.
  • The role of music, storytelling, and technology in worship.
  • Emerging ministries for urban and youth contexts.
  • Contextualized liturgical practices.

Methodology:

The course combines theoretical instruction, creative workshops, and practical projects, with students designing contextual liturgies and studying real-world emerging ministry examples.

Assessment:

  • Creative liturgy design for a specific context.
  • Research report on trends in emerging ministries.
  • Presentation of an innovative ministry project.

Importance of the Course:

This course prepares students to lead ministries that creatively engage contemporary culture, helping them combine faithfulness to tradition with innovative, relevant expressions of Christian worship and ministry.
