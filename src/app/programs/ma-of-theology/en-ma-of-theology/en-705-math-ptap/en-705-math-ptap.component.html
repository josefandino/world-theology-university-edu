MATH-PTAP-705 - Public Theology and Academic Publishing

Public Theology and Academic Publishing explores how theology engages with the public sphere and how to communicate theological ideas through academic and non-academic formats, including journals, books, blogs, and public media, promoting theology's impact on society.

Course Objectives:

  • Analyze the role of theology in public discourse.
  • Develop skills for writing academic theological articles.
  • Understand the publication process in academic contexts.
  • Communicate theology effectively to broader audiences.

Main Topics:

  • Theology in the public sphere.
  • Writing theological topics for academic and general audiences.
  • Academic publishing: writing, peer review, and ethics.
  • Theology, social change, and justice promotion.

Methodology:

Students participate in writing workshops, analyze examples of public theology, and develop academic articles, while discussing the societal relevance of theology today.

Assessment:

  • Essay on theology's public impact.
  • Academic article writing project.
  • Participation in discussions on public theology and publishing ethics.

Importance of the Course:

This course is essential for theologians aiming to influence both academic debate and public thought, using theology as a tool for cultural, social, and spiritual engagement.
