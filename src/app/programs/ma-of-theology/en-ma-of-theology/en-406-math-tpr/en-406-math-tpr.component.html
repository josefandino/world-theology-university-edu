MATH-TPR-406 - Theology of Peace and Reconciliation

Theology of Peace and Reconciliation is a course that explores the foundational theological principles related to peace and reconciliation from a Christian perspective. The course focuses on how peace and reconciliation are central to the Christian message and how these concepts can be applied both personally and socially. Students will study biblical peace, forgiveness, the restoration of broken relationships, and the promotion of justice in conflict situations. Contemporary challenges to achieving peace in a world marked by violence, injustice, and division will also be examined.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the concept of peace from a Christian theological perspective.
  • Explore the role of forgiveness and reconciliation in biblical and Christian teachings.
  • Study the ethical and social challenges in promoting peace in conflict contexts.
  • Apply the principles of the theology of peace to contemporary situations of violence, injustice, and division.
  • Develop a comprehensive approach to conflict resolution and the restoration of relationships in society.

Main Topics:

  • Biblical peace: Definition and meaning of peace in the scriptures.
  • Forgiveness and reconciliation: The role of forgiveness in restoring human relationships.
  • Justice and peace: The relationship between social justice and lasting peace.
  • Theology and conflict: Addressing the challenges of violence and injustice in the modern world.
  • Restoration in Christ: How reconciliation with God leads to reconciliation with others.

Methodology:

The course will be based on the study of biblical scriptures, systematic theology, and reflection on contemporary cases of conflict and reconciliation. Discussion and analysis of how Christian principles can contribute to peace and restoration both personally and socially will be encouraged. Students will participate in debates, research projects, and case studies focused on the practice of peace and reconciliation in real-world contexts.

Assessment:

  • Essays on the theology of peace and reconciliation.
  • Analysis of contemporary cases of conflict and potential solutions based on Christian principles.
  • Practical projects focused on promoting peace and reconciliation in communities and societies.

Importance of the Course:

This course is essential for those interested in developing a deep theological understanding of peace and reconciliation and applying these principles in pastoral work, social ministry, and conflict resolution. Students will be better equipped to address issues of violence and division in today's world, promoting a faith-based approach to restoring peace.
