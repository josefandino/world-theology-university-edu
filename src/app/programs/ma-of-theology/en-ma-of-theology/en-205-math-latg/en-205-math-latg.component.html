MATH-LATG-205 - Latin American and Global South Theology

Latin American and Global South Theology is a course that examines the key theological and social currents that have emerged in Latin America and other regions of the Global South. The course analyzes how theology in these contexts has engaged with the social, political, and economic realities of these regions, especially concerning poverty, social justice, human rights, and liberation. Through a critical approach, students will explore how Latin American thinkers and movements have challenged dominant theological traditions and offered responses to inequalities and social struggles.

Course Objectives:

  • Explore the historical development and key currents of Latin American and Global South theology.
  • Analyze liberation theology and its impact on the region and global thought.

  • Investigate the role of the church and Christian communities in the social and political struggles of Latin America.
  • Examine the connections between Latin American theology and other Global South theological traditions.

Main Topics:

  • History of Latin American theology: from colonization to the present.
  • Liberation theology: principles and challenges.
  • Theology, politics, and social justice in the Latin American context.
  • The role of the church in promoting human rights in Latin America.
  • Global South theology: synergies with struggles in Africa, Asia, and other marginalized regions.
  • Feminist theology and its relationship to Latin American theology.
  • Theology and economics: the impact of globalization and neoliberal policies on the Christian faith.

Methodology:

The course will be delivered through lectures, readings of key texts by Latin American theologians, and analysis of contemporary debates on social justice and human rights. Students will participate in critical discussions about the texts and themes presented, reflecting on how theology has been a tool for change in contexts of poverty and exclusion.

Assessment:

  • Essays on the main topics and figures in Latin American theology.
  • Exams on the principles and impact of liberation theology.
  • Research projects on the intersections between theology and politics in the Global South.
  • Presentations on the role of the church in social struggles in Latin America.

Importance of the Course:

This course is essential for understanding how Latin American and Global South theology has responded to the social and political needs of its specific contexts. Through the study of this theological current, students will develop a critical understanding of how faith can be a transformative force in the struggle for justice and liberation.
