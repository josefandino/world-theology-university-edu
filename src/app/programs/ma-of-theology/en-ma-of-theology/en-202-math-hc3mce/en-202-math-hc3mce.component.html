MATH-HC3MCE-202 - History of Christianity III: Modern and Contemporary Eras

History of Christianity III: Modern and Contemporary Eras focuses on Christianity from the Enlightenment to the present day, analyzing theological developments, cultural encounters, and global movements. Students gain critical insight into the Church’s evolving role in a world shaped by profound social, political, and technological change.

Course Objectives:

  • Examine key theological developments from the 18th century to the present.
  • Analyze the impact of the Enlightenment, secularization, and modernity on the Church.
  • Study Christian responses to contemporary social, scientific, and cultural shifts.
  • Explore the global expansion of Christianity and the rise of World Christianity.
  • Reflect on Christianity’s role in contexts of religious pluralism and social justice.
  • Evaluate the challenges and opportunities for Christian faith in the digital and postmodern age.

Main Topics:

  • Christianity and the Enlightenment: reason, freedom, and religion
  • Revolutions, human rights, and new ethical frameworks
  • Revival movements, missions, and modern denominations
  • Liberal, neo-orthodox, and contextual theologies
  • Vatican II and contemporary Catholic renewal
  • Liberation theology, feminist, Black, and postcolonial theologies
  • Christianity in Africa, Asia, and Latin America
  • The Church and current global challenges: secularism, ecology, and technology

Methodology:

The course combines virtual lectures, analytical readings, online discussions, case studies, and research projects focused on contemporary contexts. Critical, interdisciplinary, and global reflection will be encouraged, integrating relevant theological and historical sources.

Assessment:

  • Critical essays on modern theological movements
  • Presentations or debates on contemporary issues
  • Active participation in academic discussion forums
  • Analysis of historical and current case studies
  • Final integrative project with a historical-theological focus

Importance of the Course:

This course provides essential tools for understanding Christianity’s transformation over the past centuries. It enables students to interpret Christian witness in relation to the challenges of the contemporary world and discern their vocation within diverse, dynamic, and technologically advanced societies from an ethical and contextualized perspective.
