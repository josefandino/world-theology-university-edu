MATH-WTHC-206 - Women in the History of Christianity

Women in the History of Christianity is a course that examines the crucial role women have played in the development of Christianity from its origins to the present day. Throughout history, women have held essential roles in religious life and ecclesiastical structures, even though they have often been marginalized in traditional narratives. This course explores how women have influenced theology, liturgy, ecclesiastical organization, and social activism within the Christian context.

Course Objectives:

  • Explore the contributions of women to Christianity from its early days to the present.
  • Analyze the impact of women in major Christian traditions, including Catholicism, Protestantism, and Eastern Orthodoxy.
  • Investigate the presence of women in early Christianity, including apostles, deaconesses, and leaders of early Christian communities.
  • Examine how women have been marginalized and their struggle for inclusion in religious spheres.
  • Study the role of women in social and reform movements within Christian history.

Main Topics:

  • The early women in Christianity: apostles, deaconesses, and leaders in the early church.
  • Women in the Middle Ages: mystics, theologians, and influential nuns.
  • The Protestant Reformation and the role of women in religious and social change.
  • Women in modern Christian history: social activism and the fight for gender equality.
  • Christian feminist theology and its challenges to religious patriarchy.
  • Women in ecclesiastical leadership: challenges and opportunities throughout the centuries.
  • Women and global Christianity: the impact of women in Christianity outside of Europe and North America.

Methodology:

The course will be delivered through readings, lectures, and critical discussions of key historical and theological texts related to women in Christianity. Students will have the opportunity to research and reflect on historical Christian women figures and their impact throughout church history.

Assessment:

  • Essays on the contributions of key women in Christian history.
  • Exams on major figures and Christian feminist movements.
  • Research projects on the influence of women in Christian traditions.
  • Presentations on the role of women in contemporary Christianity and their fight for leadership and inclusion.

Importance of the Course:

This course is essential for providing a comprehensive view of how women have influenced and continue to influence Christianity, both theologically and socially. Through a historical and contemporary approach, students will gain an understanding of the importance of women in shaping Christian beliefs and practices, as well as the ongoing struggle for gender justice within religious institutions.
