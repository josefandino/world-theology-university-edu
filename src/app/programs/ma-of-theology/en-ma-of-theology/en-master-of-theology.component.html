The Master’s Degree in Theology is designed for individuals seeking to engage in advanced theological studies, with an emphasis on critical reflection and ministerial practice. This program combines a solid academic foundation with practical training, preparing students for spiritual leadership and teaching roles in both Christian and academic contexts. Prior theological studies are not required, though a background in fields such as philosophy, social sciences, or basic theology is recommended.

Admission Requirements

To apply for the Master’s in Theology, applicants must meet the following requirements:

  • Academic Transcripts

    • Submit official transcripts from undergraduate or equivalent studies.

  • Curriculum Vitae

    • Include a detailed CV highlighting academic, ministerial, and community service experience.

  • Personal Statement

    • Provide a personal statement explaining your motivation for pursuing the Master’s in Theology and your professional goals in the ministerial or academic field.

  • Letters of Recommendation

    • Submit two letters of recommendation from Christian leaders or faculty members who can attest to the applicant’s spiritual and academic commitment.

  • Application Fee

    • The admission process includes a non-refundable application fee.

Selection Criteria

Applicants must have completed an undergraduate degree with a strong academic record. Experience in ministry or church-related activities is highly valued. The applicant’s potential to contribute to theological thought and Christian leadership will also be considered.

Application Process

The application process is fully online, allowing applicants to submit all required documents from anywhere in the world.

Requirements for the Master’s in Theology

  • University Degree: Applicants must have completed an undergraduate degree in any discipline. Academic background in areas such as religious studies, philosophy, or social sciences will be especially valued.
  • Experience in Ministry or Ecclesial Activities: While not mandatory, applicants are encouraged to have experience in ministerial roles or Christian service that reflects a commitment to theological and spiritual leadership.
  • Language Proficiency: The program is delivered in Spanish; therefore, an adequate level of written and oral proficiency in the language is required.
  • Required Documentation: Applicants must submit the following documents:
    • Official university transcripts.
    • Updated Curriculum Vitae.
    • A motivation letter explaining their interest in the Master’s in Theology and outlining their professional goals.
    • Letters of recommendation from spiritual leaders or faculty members attesting to their academic and ministerial aptitude.
    • Payment of the application fee.
  • Admission Interview: Applicants may be invited to attend a personal or virtual interview to assess their vocation, commitment, and suitability for the program.
