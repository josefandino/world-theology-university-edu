MATH-ACE-400 - Applied Christian Ethics

Applied Christian Ethics is a course that explores how Christian ethical principles are applied in real-world and everyday contexts. The course focuses on taking the foundational ethical teachings of the Christian faith and examining their relevance and application in practical decision-making in personal, professional, and social life. Students will learn to approach complex ethical dilemmas using a Christian perspective, considering biblical teachings and church traditions in the decision-making process. The course covers topics such as ethics in business, bioethics, social justice, and ethical issues in interpersonal relationships.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the basic principles of Christian ethics.
  • Apply Christian ethical principles to real-world dilemmas and situations.
  • Develop skills for making informed ethical decisions in personal, professional, and social contexts.
  • Analyze and reflect on contemporary ethical challenges from a Christian perspective.
  • Promote a commitment to justice, peace, and the well-being of others in daily life.

Main Topics:

  • Foundations of Christian ethics: Biblical morality and Christian life.
  • Ethics in business: Integrity, justice, and responsibility.
  • Bioethics: Ethical considerations in medicine and biotechnology.
  • Social ethics: Justice, poverty, and human rights from a Christian perspective.
  • Ethics in personal relationships: Marriage, family, and community.

Methodology:

The course will be taught through readings of Christian ethical texts, case studies on ethical dilemmas in various contexts, in-class debates on contemporary issues, and practical analysis of ethical decision-making based on Christian principles. Students will also engage in personal reflection exercises on how Christian ethical principles impact their daily lives.

Assessment:

  • Essays on the application of ethical principles to contemporary situations.
  • Debates and discussions on ethical dilemmas in class.
  • Case studies on ethical issues in different areas of life.
  • Exams on Christian ethical principles and theories.

Importance of the Course:

This course is essential for those who wish to integrate their Christian faith into their daily and professional lives, addressing ethical dilemmas with integrity in the modern context. Students will develop a Christian ethical vision that will enable them to make just, responsible, and compassionate decisions in their daily lives.
