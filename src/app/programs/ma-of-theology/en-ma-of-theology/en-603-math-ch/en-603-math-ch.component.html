MATH-CH-603 - Christianity and Hinduism

Christianity and Hinduism is a comparative course that explores two of the worldâ€™s most influential religions: Christianity and Hinduism. Throughout the course, students will examine the theological, historical, and cultural foundations of both traditions, their key beliefs, religious practices, and their evolution over time. The course will also address the interactions between Christianity and Hinduism in both historical and contemporary contexts, as well as the challenges and opportunities for interfaith dialogue between these two religions.

Course Objectives:

  • Examine the theological foundations and central beliefs of both Christianity and Hinduism.
  • Explore the main religious practices and rituals in both traditions.
  • Analyze the history and evolution of both religions over the centuries.
  • Investigate the historical and contemporary interactions between Christianity and Hinduism.
  • Develop a critical understanding of interfaith dialogue between Christianity and Hinduism.

Main Topics:

  • Foundations of Christianity: Jesus Christ, the Trinity, and salvation.
  • Foundations of Hinduism: Brahman, the cycle of reincarnation, and karma.
  • Religious practices in Christianity and Hinduism: prayer, rituals, and festivals.
  • The history and expansion of Christianity in the Western world.
  • Hinduism and its influence in South Asia and beyond.
  • Interfaith dialogue: challenges and opportunities between Christianity and Hinduism.

Methodology:

This course employs a comparative approach, using sacred texts, theological writings, and historical case studies to analyze the beliefs, practices, and evolution of both Christianity and Hinduism. Students will engage in debates, presentations, and group discussions to explore the similarities and differences between these two religions.

Assessment:

  • Essays on the theological principles and religious practices of Christianity and Hinduism.
  • Written exams on the history and interactions between the two religions.
  • Presentations on specific topics, such as the impact of each religion on contemporary societies.
  • Research on interfaith dialogue and cooperation between Christians and Hindus.

Importance of the Course:

This course provides an in-depth understanding of two global religions with rich histories and traditions that continue to influence the contemporary world. The study of these religions and their interaction helps foster greater understanding and respect between different religious communities.
