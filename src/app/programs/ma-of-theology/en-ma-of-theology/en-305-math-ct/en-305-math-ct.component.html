MATH-CT-305 - Contemporary Theology

Contemporary Theology is a course that examines the latest developments and major trends in theology in the modern world. The course covers current topics in Christian theology, including the analysis of contemporary theological movements, new streams of theological thought, and debates on the relationship between faith and culture in the contemporary context. Students will explore how contemporary theologians address issues of social relevance, ethics, and doctrine in an increasingly pluralistic and secularized world.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the major trends in contemporary theology.
  • Study current debates on the relationship between Christian faith and modern culture.
  • Analyze how contemporary theologians address issues such as ethics, politics, social justice, feminist theology, liberation theology, and religious pluralism.
  • Evaluate the impact of scientific and philosophical advancements on Christian theology.
  • Reflect on how contemporary theologians respond to the challenges of secularism and religious pluralism.

Main Topics:

  • Liberal theology and postliberal theology.
  • Liberation theology and its influence on Christian thought.
  • Feminist theology and advancements in gender inclusion and equity in the church.
  • The impact of religious pluralism on Christian theology.
  • Secularism and theology in the modern age.
  • Theology and science: the relationship between religion and scientific advancements.

Methodology:

The course will be conducted through lectures, readings of fundamental texts by contemporary theologians, critical analysis of current theological writings, and debates on issues relevant to the contemporary Christian context. Personal reflection and group discussions will be encouraged on the challenges of living the Christian faith in a modern and pluralistic world.

Assessment:

  • Essays on the main trends in contemporary theology.
  • Exams on the relationship between Christian faith and modern culture.
  • Presentations on the impact of new theological movements.
  • Research on the ethical and doctrinal challenges in contemporary theology.

Importance of the Course:

This course is essential for understanding how Christian theology adapts and responds to the social, political, and cultural changes of the contemporary world. It provides students with the necessary tools to engage in current debates on the relevance of Christian faith in modern society and to formulate relevant and contextualized theological responses.
