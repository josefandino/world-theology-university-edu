MATH-HC1ECAP-200 - History of Christianity I: Early Church and Apostolic Fathers

History of Christianity I: Early Church and Apostolic Fathers is a course that examines the origins and early development of Christianity, with special emphasis on the Apostolic Fathers and the foundational structures of the Church. Students critically engage with historical texts to understand the enduring legacy of early Christian witness during the first centuries.

Course Objectives:

  • Explore the historical origins of Christianity within its Jewish and Greco-Roman contexts.
  • Analyze the teachings and writings of the Apostolic Fathers.
  • Understand the formation and consolidation of the early Christian communities.
  • Evaluate the impact of persecution, martyrdom, and apologetics on early Christian identity.
  • Study the early development of ecclesiastical and liturgical structures.
  • Interpret key historical sources using academic tools and critical methodology.

Main Topics:

  • Christianity within the Roman Empire
  • The transition from Judaism to Christianity
  • The Apostolic Fathers: Clement, Ignatius, Polycarp, and others
  • Persecutions and martyrdom in the 1st and 2nd centuries
  • Early heresies and doctrinal responses
  • Formation of the biblical canon and apostolic authority
  • Church structures and community leadership
  • Spiritual and theological legacy of the early Church

Methodology:

The course will be delivered through synchronous virtual classes, analysis of primary sources, critical readings, academic forum discussions, and research-based activities. Critical thinking, active participation, and intercultural dialogue will be emphasized to foster a comprehensive understanding of the historical development of early Christianity.
