MATH-SAE-805 - Studies in Apocalyptic Eschatology

Studies in Apocalyptic Eschatology examines apocalyptic visions and eschatological themes in Scripture, focusing on interpretations of the end times, the second coming, final judgment, and their theological relevance today.

Course Objectives:

  • Analyze interpretive traditions of apocalyptic literature.
  • Study key texts: Daniel and Revelation.
  • Reflect on contemporary relevance of eschatological teachings.
  • Evaluate apocalyptic influence on historical and modern Christianity.

Main Topics:

  • Introduction to apocalyptic literature and theology.
  • Analysis of Daniel and Revelation.
  • Eschatological themes: judgment, Kingdom of God.
  • Historical impact and modern interpretations.

Methodology:

Students engage in critical readings, case studies, theological discussions, and reflections on apocalyptic themes in Christian life and practice.

Assessment:

  • Essay on an apocalyptic biblical passage.
  • Case study presentation on apocalyptic influence in Christianity.
  • Research project on eschatology's relevance today.

Importance of the Course:

This course provides a deep understanding of how apocalyptic thought has shaped Christian theology, offering critical tools for engaging these powerful biblical themes today.
