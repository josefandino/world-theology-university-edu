MATH-AAAT-803 - African and African American Theology

African and African American Theology examines theological expressions rooted in African and African American experiences, highlighting how faith, culture, and resistance shaped theology addressing issues of oppression, justice, and liberation.

Course Objectives:

  • Study historical and cultural roots of African and African American theology.
  • Analyze contributions of key theological figures.
  • Explore the relationship between Christianity and African traditions.
  • Reflect on the impact of racism and oppression on theological development.

Main Topics:

  • Historical and cultural foundations of African theological thought.
  • Theology of resistance and liberation.
  • Christian faith and African traditional beliefs.
  • African and African American liberation theology.

Methodology:

The course involves readings, case studies, text analysis, and discussions on key thinkers and historical experiences of African and African American communities.

Assessment:

  • Essay on African traditions in Christian theology.
  • Presentation on an influential African or African American theologian.
  • Research project on slavery's impact on Christian thought.

Importance of the Course:

Essential for understanding how faith intertwines with social justice and cultural identity, offering a theological response to historical and contemporary oppression.
