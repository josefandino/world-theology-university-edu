The Master's Degree in Clinical Pastoral Counseling is designed for pastors and Christian leaders who seek to acquire advanced knowledge in spiritual and emotional care, utilizing psychological principles applied to the pastoral context. This program offers a comprehensive approach, combining biblical wisdom with clinical counseling techniques to equip leaders to effectively address the emotional and spiritual needs of their congregations.

100% Online: Study from Anywhere

This program is entirely online, allowing you to study from the comfort of your home, regardless of your location. You can tailor your study schedule to fit your ministerial and family responsibilities while connecting with a global community of pastors and leaders who share your values.

Professional Opportunities

The master's program offers various professional opportunities in ecclesiastical and community settings. Graduates will be prepared to serve as clinical pastoral counselors in churches, Christian organizations, ministries, and spiritual support centers, providing emotional and spiritual assistance to individuals in crisis or with specific needs.

Key Skills for Pastoral Counseling

Throughout the program, you will develop critical skills that will enable you to make a difference in your ministry and the lives of those you serve. The most notable skills include:

  • Problem-solving and critical judgment: You will apply biblical and psychological principles to address complex situations.
  • Effective communication: You will learn to communicate clearly and compassionately, both verbally and in writing.
  • Project management: You will be able to develop and execute pastoral support and counseling programs within your church or ministry.

World-Class Training

Our faculty includes experts in clinical psychology and pastoral counseling with international experience. You will benefit from a program that integrates more than 50 years of leading global research in psychology and applied theology.

Global Support Network

You will study alongside Christian leaders from around the world, creating connections that will help you grow personally and professionally. Through our online learning platform, you will participate in real-time discussions and collaborate on projects with colleagues from diverse cultures and ministerial contexts.

A Transformative Approach

Previous studies in counseling or psychology are not required for admission, but a solid foundation in theological or ministerial studies is recommended.

Admission Requirements

To apply for the Master’s in Clinical Pastoral Counseling, applicants must meet the following requirements:

  • Academic Transcripts: Applicants must submit official transcripts from their previous university studies.
  • Curriculum Vitae or Resume: An updated CV reflecting ministerial or professional experience is required.
  • Statement of Purpose: Applicants must write a statement explaining their reasons for pursuing this master’s degree, how they plan to apply their learning in their ministry, and their motivation for seeking training in clinical pastoral counseling.
  • Letters of Recommendation: Two letters of recommendation are required from mentors, pastors, or leaders who can attest to the applicant's ministerial capacity and suitability for this program.
  • Application Fee: The application process includes a non-refundable fee.

Selection Criteria

Applicants must have completed a bachelor’s degree with a minimum academic GPA of B or higher. While prior studies in counseling or psychology are not required, experience in Christian leadership or pastoral work is recommended. Applicants with backgrounds in theological or ministerial studies will be given preferential consideration.

Application Process

The application process is entirely online and can be completed from anywhere in the world. For more information on application deadlines and specific admission requirements, applicants are encouraged to review the instructions in the program’s admissions section.

Applicants for the Master’s in Clinical Pastoral Counseling must meet a series of requirements to ensure their preparedness and ability to successfully complete the program. Below are the necessary requirements:

Official Academic Transcripts

Applicants must submit official transcripts from all undergraduate or higher education programs previously completed. Applicants must have completed a bachelor’s degree or equivalent in any discipline with a minimum cumulative GPA of B or higher. While a degree in counseling or psychology is not required, prior studies in fields related to ministry, theology, or the humanities will be positively considered.

Curriculum Vitae or Resume

Applicants must provide an updated CV that includes their relevant professional and ministerial experience, as well as any training or education in pastoral counseling, psychology, or leadership within the church or their community. This document should reflect the applicant’s professional background and highlight the competencies acquired in the ministerial field.

Statement of Purpose

This statement is a motivation letter where the applicant must explain their reasons for applying to the Master’s in Clinical Pastoral Counseling. It should include how the program will contribute to their ministerial and professional goals, and how they plan to apply the knowledge gained within their community or congregation. The statement must also demonstrate a clear understanding of the importance of integrating pastoral counseling with the principles of clinical psychology.

Letters of Recommendation

At least two letters of recommendation are required from ministerial leaders, pastors, or mentors who can attest to the applicant's leadership, pastoral service, and counseling capabilities. The letters should address the applicant’s suitability for the program and their potential to apply what they learn in a practical context.

Ministerial or Leadership Experience

While not a formal requirement, it is recommended that applicants have at least two years of experience in a leadership role within a church or Christian community. This experience may include informal pastoral counseling, teaching, preaching, or working in spiritual support programs.

Personal Interview

In some cases, the admissions committee may request a personal or online interview with the applicant. The purpose of this interview is to evaluate the applicant’s commitment to the program, academic and personal preparedness, and ministerial vision.

Application Fee

All applicants must pay a non-refundable application fee when submitting their application. This fee covers the administrative costs associated with the application review process.

Recommended Prior Knowledge

Although not mandatory, it is recommended that applicants have completed prior courses in topics related to pastoral counseling, psychology, or biblical studies. This foundational knowledge will facilitate understanding of the advanced topics addressed in the program.

Academic Requirements During the Program

Once admitted to the program, students must meet the following academic requirements to complete the Master’s in Clinical Pastoral Counseling:

  • Total Credits: Students must complete a total of 62 credits, distributed among core, elective, and optional courses.
  • Minimum Cumulative GPA: A minimum cumulative GPA of B or higher must be maintained in all courses to progress in the program and complete the final thesis or comprehensive exam.
  • Master’s Thesis or Comprehensive Exam: At the end of the program, students must choose to develop a research thesis (6 credits) focused on pastoral counseling or complete a comprehensive exam that evaluates their mastery of the program’s key areas.
  • Study Modality: The program is 100% online and can be completed on a part-time or full-time basis, depending on the student’s needs. However, all academic work and the final project (thesis or exam) must be completed within a maximum of five years.

These requirements ensure that students are adequately prepared both academically and spiritually to carry out the work of clinical pastoral counseling in their communities, combining a biblical approach with the principles of clinical psychology.
