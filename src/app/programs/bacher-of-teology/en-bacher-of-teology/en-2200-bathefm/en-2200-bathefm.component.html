BATHE-FM-2200 - Family Ministry

This course provides theological, pastoral, and practical tools for supporting, strengthening, and fostering the holistic development of families. It examines the role of the family in God's plan, as well as the contemporary challenges families face, equipping students to lead family ministry programs that promote spiritual, emotional, and relational growth in diverse contexts.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the biblical and theological foundation of the family and its role within the Christian community.
  • Analyze contemporary challenges impacting families from a Christian perspective.
  • Develop skills for the pastoral care of families across various life stages.
  • Apply principles of counseling, education, and spiritual support in family settings.
  • Design ministerial strategies that promote the spiritual, emotional, and relational health of families.
  • Promote an inclusive, sensitive, and relevant family ministry in diverse cultural contexts.

Main Topics:

  • Biblical and theological foundations of the family.
  • Family dynamics and spiritual development across the stages of life.
  • Principles of pastoral care and family counseling.
  • Contemporary challenges: marital crises, domestic violence, cultural diversity, and new family configurations.
  • Family ministry programs and strategies in the local church.
  • Christian education in the home: value formation and family discipleship.
  • Integrating the family into Christian mission and service.
  • The role of the church in strengthening and supporting families.

Methodology:

The course will be conducted through virtual lectures, pastoral skills workshops, analysis of real family case studies, ministry project design, and participation in discussion forums. Practical learning, pastoral discernment, and collaborative work will be emphasized, all oriented toward the holistic strengthening of families.

Assessment:

  • Biblical-theological analysis essays on the family.
  • Case studies on family dynamics and pastoral care strategies.
  • Active participation in pastoral skills workshops for family ministry.
  • Theological and pastoral comprehension written exams.
  • Final project: design of a contextualized family ministry program.

Course Significance:

Family Ministry is essential for equipping Christian leaders to accompany, strengthen, and guide families in their spiritual, emotional, and relational growth. This course prepares students to carry out sensitive, strategic, and transformative pastoral ministry on behalf of families, thereby contributing to the strengthening of both the Church and contemporary society.
