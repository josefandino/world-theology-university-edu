BATHE-ESC-1100 - Eschatology

This course offers an in-depth analysis of biblical teachings on the end times, the return of Christ, the final judgment, and eternal hope. It examines eschatological perspectives from biblical, theological, and pastoral approaches, considering their relevance for Christian life and their impact on the Church’s mission in the contemporary world.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the biblical and theological foundations of Christian eschatology.
  • Analyze the principal doctrines related to future events: the Second Coming, the Final Judgment, and eternal life.
  • Explore various historical and contemporary interpretations of eschatological themes.
  • Evaluate the relevance of eschatological hope for Christian life, ethics, and ministry.
  • Develop a balanced and pastoral perspective on the end times.
  • Apply eschatological teachings to the Church’s mission, preaching, and communal life.

Main Topics:

  • Biblical foundations of eschatology: Old and New Testaments.
  • The Second Coming of Christ: promises, signs, and expectations.
  • The Final Judgment and divine justice.
  • The resurrection of the dead and eternal life.
  • The Kingdom of God and the eschatological consummation.
  • Historical eschatological perspectives: premillennialism, amillennialism, and postmillennialism.
  • The impact of eschatology on Christian ethics and mission.
  • Christian hope in contexts of suffering and social transformation.

Methodology:

The course will be delivered through virtual lectures, exegetical analysis of key eschatological passages, discussion of historical and contemporary theological texts, reflection forums, and practical pastoral application projects. Autonomous learning, critical thinking, and respectful dialogue regarding different eschatological perspectives will be encouraged.

Assessment:

  • Theological and pastoral analysis essays on eschatological topics.
  • Case studies on the application of eschatology within ecclesial life.
  • Written examinations on doctrinal and biblical comprehension.
  • Participation in academic discussion forums.
  • Final project: eschatological reflection applied to a contemporary ministerial context.

Course Significance:

Eschatology is essential for a comprehensive understanding of the Christian faith, offering a vision of hope, justice, and renewal. This course equips students to interpret and proclaim the teachings on the end times in a theologically sound, pastorally sensitive, and ethically transformative manner amid the challenges of the contemporary world.
