BATHE-WM-2400 - Worship Ministries

This course examines the theology and practice of Christian worship, addressing its biblical foundations and its expression through music, liturgy, and other forms of spiritual devotion. It explores the personal, communal, and missional dimensions of worship, equipping students to lead worship ministries that are biblically sound, culturally relevant, and spiritually edifying.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the biblical and theological foundations of Christian worship.
  • Analyze the history, forms, and expressions of worship across various Christian traditions.
  • Develop theological and pastoral criteria for leading worship ministries.
  • Explore the role of music, liturgy, and the arts in the worship life of the church.
  • Design worship experiences that integrate biblical fidelity and cultural relevance.
  • Foster a spirituality of authentic, transformative, and missional worship.

Main Topics:

  • Biblical foundations of worship: Old and New Testament perspectives.
  • Theology of worship: God as the center of life and praise.
  • History and development of Christian worship practices.
  • Music and worship: principles, practice, and leadership.
  • Christian liturgy: structure, symbolism, and spirituality.
  • Contemporary expressions of worship and their theological evaluation.
  • Leadership in worship ministries: formation, ethics, and pastoral vision.
  • Worship and mission: worship as testimony and cultural transformation.

Methodology:

The course will be conducted through virtual lectures, workshops on liturgical and musical design, analysis of real worship ministry case studies, theological discussion forums, and practical projects for planning and evaluating worship services. Creativity, pastoral sensitivity, and theological discernment in worship leadership will be emphasized.

Assessment:

  • Theological essays on the foundations and practices of worship.
  • Design of worship services integrating biblical, musical, and liturgical elements.
  • Written examinations on theological and practical concepts.
  • Participation in creative workshops and discussion forums.
  • Final project: design and evaluation of a contextualized worship ministry.

Course Significance:

Worship Ministries is fundamental for forming leaders who understand and practice worship that honors God and edifies the church. This course equips students to integrate theology, music, and spiritual expression into vibrant worship ministries that are faithful to Scripture and relevant to the contemporary world, thus strengthening the spiritual life and mission of the Church.
