BATHE-HRSJ-2600 - Human Rights and Social Justice

This course examines the Christian commitment to human dignity, fundamental rights, and the pursuit of social justice from a biblical and theological perspective. It explores the biblical foundations for the respect of life and equity, as well as the prophetic role of the Church in defending human rights and promoting a more just and compassionate society.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the biblical and theological foundations of human dignity and social justice.
  • Analyze the historical development of human rights in dialogue with the Christian faith.
  • Reflect on the role of the Church in defending human rights and social justice.
  • Evaluate the ethical and theological challenges involved in pursuing justice in contemporary contexts.
  • Develop critical skills to promote the respect of fundamental rights based on Christian ethics.
  • Apply biblical principles of justice and mercy in personal, communal, and social engagement.

Main Topics:

  • Biblical foundations of human dignity and justice.
  • History and development of human rights in relation to the Christian tradition.
  • Social justice in the teachings of Jesus and the prophets.
  • The Church as an agent of social transformation and defender of the marginalized.
  • Major contemporary challenges: poverty, discrimination, migration, violence, and environmental issues.
  • Christian ethics and practical commitment to justice and human rights.
  • The role of public theology in the global debate on rights and justice.
  • Designing community projects that promote human rights and social justice.

Methodology:

The course will be conducted through expository virtual lectures, analysis of historical and contemporary documents on human rights, case studies, critical debate forums, and practical application projects. Reflective thinking, ethical commitment, and transformative action within the current social context will be encouraged.

Assessment:

  • Critical essays on human rights and justice from a biblical perspective.
  • Case study analyses of Christian social action initiatives.
  • Written exams assessing theological and ethical understanding of human rights.
  • Active participation in discussion forums and thematic debates.
  • Final project: design of a justice promotion initiative based on Christian principles.

Course Significance:

Human Rights and Social Justice equips students to integrate their Christian faith with an active commitment to human dignity and social transformation. This course forms leaders who are ethically aware and capable of defending and promoting fundamental rights in faithful adherence to gospel values in both local and global contexts.
