BATHE-TGM-3000 - Theology of Global Mission

This course explores the theology and practice of Christian mission on a global scale. It examines the biblical and theological foundation of mission, the sending of the church, intercultural evangelization, and community development as an expression of holistic mission. The course equips students to understand and actively participate in missionary work across diverse and changing cultural contexts.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the biblical and theological foundation of global Christian mission.
  • Analyze the principles and practices of intercultural evangelization.
  • Reflect on the role of the church in contemporary global mission.
  • Explore holistic missionary strategies that integrate evangelization and community development.
  • Evaluate ethical, cultural, and social challenges in the context of global mission.
  • Apply global mission principles to concrete ministerial projects in diverse cultures.

Main Topics:

  • Biblical foundations of mission: Old and New Testaments.
  • The missio Dei: God as the primary agent of mission.
  • History of Christian mission and global missionary movements.
  • Intercultural evangelization: principles, challenges, and effective practices.
  • Community development as an integral part of mission.
  • Missionary ethics: cultural respect, social justice, and holistic transformation.
  • The local church as the base for global mission.
  • Designing contextualized and sustainable missionary projects.

Methodology:

The course will be conducted through expository virtual lectures, case studies of global missionary projects, workshops for missionary strategy design, critical discussion forums, and practical contextual application projects. The course will promote theological reflection, intercultural discernment, and practical engagement in global mission.

Assessment:

  • Theological essays on global mission and its biblical foundation.
  • Case study analysis of intercultural missionary experiences.
  • Written exams on theological and strategic understanding of mission.
  • Active participation in discussion forums and missionary strategy workshops.
  • Final project: design of a global missionary plan adapted to a specific cultural context.

Course Significance:

Theology of Global Mission is essential for preparing Christian leaders who understand the global dimension of faith and the responsibility to actively participate in God's mission. This course equips students to integrate evangelization, social transformation, and community development into relevant, sustainable, and culturally sensitive missionary projects.
