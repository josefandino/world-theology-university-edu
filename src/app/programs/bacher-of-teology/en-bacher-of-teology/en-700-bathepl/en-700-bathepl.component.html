BATHE-PL-700 - Pastoral Leadership

This course explores the fundamental principles of pastoral leadership from a biblical and practical perspective. It focuses on the formation of leaders of integrity, the development of a clear ministerial vision, service as the foundation of leadership, and the effective management of ecclesial communities. The course provides comprehensive training to equip students to lead with responsibility, cultural sensitivity, and ethical commitment in diverse contexts.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the biblical and theological foundations of pastoral leadership.
  • Develop a leadership vision centered on service and discipleship formation.
  • Analyze different models of ecclesial leadership throughout history and in the contemporary world.
  • Identify principles of effective pastoral management and administration for Christian communities.
  • Apply communication, conflict resolution, and teamwork skills in ministerial contexts.
  • Foster pastoral leadership that is culturally sensitive and committed to social transformation.

Main Topics:

  • Biblical and theological foundations of Christian leadership.
  • Leadership as service: principles and practices.
  • Formation and implementation of a pastoral vision.
  • Management and administration of ecclesial communities.
  • Pastoral skills: communication, mentoring, and conflict resolution.
  • Historical and contemporary models of pastoral leadership.
  • Pastoral ethics and leader care.
  • Pastoral leadership in multicultural and globalized contexts.

Methodology:

The course will be delivered through virtual lectures, analysis of ministerial case studies, practical leadership workshops, online discussion forums, and the development of pastoral leadership plan projects. Critical reflection, collaborative learning, and the development of practical skills applicable to real-life ministry will be emphasized.

Assessment:

  • Essays on the biblical and theological foundations of leadership.
  • Case studies and analysis of pastoral leadership models.
  • Written examinations assessing understanding of key concepts.
  • Active participation in ministerial discussion forums.
  • Final project: development of a strategic pastoral leadership plan.

Course Significance:

Pastoral Leadership is crucial for forming leaders capable of guiding Christian communities with integrity, vision, and cultural sensitivity. This course equips students to exercise transformative and ethical leadership, promoting healthy, inclusive faith communities committed to God's mission in the contemporary world.
