BATHE-ITHE-100 - Introduction to Theology: - This course provides an overview of theological study, including its main disciplines, methods, and objectives. It explores the nature of revelation, faith, reason, and the role of theology in Christian life and society.

BATHE-ONT-200 - Old and New Testament: - This course offers a general overview of both Testaments of the Bible. It examines the books, historical contexts, authors, literary genres, and main messages, as well as their theological relevance and application to Christian life.

BATHE-HC-300 - History of Christianity: - Explores the development of Christianity from its origins in the first century to the present. It analyzes the major movements, councils, key figures, divisions (such as the Reformation), and their impact on society and the contemporary church.

BATHE-ST-400 - Systematic Theology: - Studies in an organized manner the main doctrines of the Christian faith, such as God, Jesus Christ, the Holy Spirit, salvation, the church, and the end times. It seeks to understand how these doctrines relate to each other and form a coherent system.

BATHE-BH-500 - Biblical Hermeneutics: - Explores the principles and methods of interpreting the Scriptures. It teaches how to understand the historical, literary, and cultural context of biblical texts in order to interpret them correctly and apply them to contemporary life.

BATHE-CE-600 - Christian Ethics: - Studies moral principles from a biblical perspective, addressing topics such as good and evil, justice, a dignified life, and ethical decisions in Christian living.

BATHE-PL-700 - Pastoral Leadership: - Explores biblical and practical leadership principles, focusing on the training, vision, service, and management of church communities.

BATHE-MM-800 - Ministry and Mission: - Examines the calling and practice of Christian ministry, with an emphasis on the mission of the Church at both the local and global levels.

BATHE-PC-900 - Pastoral Counseling: - Introduces methods of spiritual and emotional accompaniment, offering tools to help individuals in difficult situations from a pastoral perspective.

BATHE-CT-1000 - Contextual Theology: - Studies how theology is applied in specific cultural, social, and political contexts, integrating faith with the reality of the surrounding environment.

BATHE-ESC-1100 - Escatology: - Analyzes the biblical teachings on the end times, the return of Christ, final judgment, and eternal hope.

BATHE-APO-1200 - Apologetics: - Provides the foundations to defend the Christian faith against philosophical, scientific, and cultural objections.

BATHE-CE-1300 - Christian Education: - Explores methods and pedagogical principles for biblical teaching in churches, schools, and ministries.

BATHE-PS-1400 - Practical Spirituality: - Encourages the development of spiritual disciplines (prayer, fasting, Bible reading) and their integration into daily life.

BATHE-THER-1500 - Theological Research: - Introduces methodologies for researching theological topics with academic rigor and biblical fidelity.

BATHE-CE-1600 - Comprehensive Exam: - An integrative evaluation of the theological knowledge acquired during the degree.

BATHE-THEA-2000 - Theological Anthropology: - Explores human nature from the Christian perspective, including creation, sin, redemption, and the image of God in humanity.

BATHE-THEOM-2500 - Theology of Ministry: - Reflects on the nature, purpose, and functions of Christian ministry according to the Scriptures.
