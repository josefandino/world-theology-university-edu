BATHE-CE-1300 - Christian Education

This course explores the fundamental pedagogical principles and methods for biblical teaching within churches, schools, and ministries. It analyzes the educational process from a Christian perspective, emphasizing the holistic formation of students and the effective transmission of biblical values and content in various contemporary educational contexts.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the biblical and theological foundations of Christian education.
  • Analyze pedagogical principles applicable to biblical teaching in different settings.
  • Evaluate teaching methods and educational strategies from a Christian perspective.
  • Develop skills for planning, implementing, and evaluating Christian education programs.
  • Foster the holistic formation of students in knowledge, character, and Christian commitment.
  • Apply innovative educational strategies adapted to different ages and cultural contexts.

Main Topics:

  • Biblical and theological foundations of Christian education.
  • Pedagogical principles applied to Christian formation.
  • Curriculum design and educational planning in ecclesial and school contexts.
  • Methods and techniques for effective biblical teaching.
  • Christian education for children, adolescents, youth, and adults.
  • Assessment of learning and spiritual growth.
  • Educational innovation in Christian contexts: technology, media, and contemporary strategies.
  • The formation of trainers: leadership and mentoring in Christian education.

Methodology:

The course will be delivered through expository virtual classes, practical curriculum design workshops, analysis of educational program case studies, discussion forums, and educational planning projects. Active participation, collaborative work, and creativity will be encouraged in the development of didactic strategies applied to real contexts.

Assessment:

  • Critical essays on principles and practices of Christian education.
  • Design of lesson plans and Christian educational programs.
  • Written examinations assessing theoretical and pedagogical comprehension.
  • Active participation in educational discussion forums.
  • Final project: design of a comprehensive Christian education program.

Course Significance:

Christian Education is essential for the formation of leaders, teachers, and ministers capable of effectively transmitting the biblical message and forming committed disciples. This course equips students to design, implement, and evaluate educational processes that contribute to spiritual growth and the strengthening of Christian communities in a constantly changing world.
