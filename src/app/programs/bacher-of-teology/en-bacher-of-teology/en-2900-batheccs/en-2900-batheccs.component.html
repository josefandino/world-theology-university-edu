BATHE-CCS-2900 - Contemporary Christian Studies

This course addresses the main trends, challenges, and current movements within Christianity in the modern world. It analyzes the impact of social, cultural, technological, and political phenomena on the Christian faith, as well as the responses of various Christian traditions to global changes. The course aims to foster a critical, informed, and committed understanding of Christian witness in the twenty-first century.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the major trends and challenges facing contemporary Christianity.
  • Analyze current theological, ecclesial, and social movements within global Christianity.
  • Evaluate the impact of globalization, technology, pluralism, and secularization on Christian life.
  • Critically reflect on Christian responses to contemporary ethical and social dilemmas.
  • Develop skills for discerning and acting with faithfulness and creativity in changing contexts.
  • Promote an informed, relevant, and transformative Christian commitment in the modern world.

Main Topics:

  • Overview of global Christianity in the twenty-first century.
  • Contemporary ecclesial movements: Pentecostal growth, emerging churches, and global networks.
  • Challenges of secularization, pluralism, and cultural relativism.
  • Technology, media, and their impact on Christian faith.
  • Christian ethics facing contemporary dilemmas: bioethics, human rights, environment, and social justice.
  • The Christian mission in the context of globalization.
  • Contemporary spiritualities and the challenge of authentic Christian witness.
  • Theological and pastoral responses to cultural and social changes.

Methodology:

The course will be conducted through expository virtual lectures, analysis of documents and contemporary case studies, critical discussion forums, reflection workshops, and practical application projects. Interdisciplinary dialogue, critical analysis, and contextualized engagement with Christian witness in today's world will be emphasized.

Assessment:

  • Analytical essays on trends and challenges in contemporary Christianity.
  • Case studies on current Christian movements and responses.
  • Written exams on theological and sociocultural understanding.
  • Active participation in critical discussion forums and contextualized reflection.
  • Final project proposing Christian action in response to a specific contemporary challenge.

Course Significance:

Contemporary Christian Studies is essential for preparing Christian leaders capable of discerning, interpreting, and responding to the challenges of the modern world from a biblical, theological, and contextualized perspective. This course strengthens the relevance, commitment, and influence of Christian witness in a globalized and ever-changing society.
