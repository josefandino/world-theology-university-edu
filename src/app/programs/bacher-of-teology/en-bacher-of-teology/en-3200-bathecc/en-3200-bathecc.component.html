BATHE-CC-3200 - Christian Counseling in Crisis

This course offers theoretical and practical tools for accompanying individuals going through emotional, spiritual, or family crises from a Christian perspective. Students are trained to provide effective support during moments of trauma, grief, conflict, or transition, integrating biblical, psychological, and pastoral principles into the ministry of Christian counseling.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the biblical and theological foundations of crisis accompaniment.
  • Analyze the most common types of emotional, spiritual, and family crises.
  • Develop basic intervention and support skills for critical situations.
  • Apply Christian counseling principles to processes of grief, loss, trauma, and restoration.
  • Evaluate ethical challenges and boundaries in crisis counseling.
  • Design pastoral strategies for prevention, intervention, and follow-up in crisis contexts.

Main Topics:

  • Biblical and theological foundations for crisis accompaniment.
  • The nature and dynamics of human crises: types, phases, and responses.
  • Counseling in situations of grief, trauma, family conflicts, and loss.
  • Skills for active listening, empathy, and communication during crises.
  • Ethics and boundaries in Christian crisis counseling.
  • Interdisciplinary collaboration and referral to specialized resources when necessary.
  • Crisis prevention in Christian community life.
  • Designing support and pastoral accompaniment programs in crisis contexts.

Methodology:

The course will be delivered through expository virtual classes, analysis of real-life cases, counseling simulations, practical workshops on intervention skills, and projects for designing pastoral accompaniment strategies. Ethical reflection, pastoral sensitivity, and the integration of faith with crisis support practices will be emphasized.

Assessment:

  • Reflective essays on Christian accompaniment during crises.
  • Case studies on crisis situations and pastoral responses.
  • Written exams on theological, psychological, and ethical foundations.
  • Active participation in simulations and practical workshops.
  • Final project: design of a crisis intervention and accompaniment program for a Christian community.

Course Significance:

Christian Counseling in Crisis is fundamental for training sensitive, prepared, and ethically responsible leaders and ministers to accompany individuals during moments of vulnerability. This course equips students to exercise a ministry of healing, hope, and restoration, integrating Christian faith with emotional and spiritual support practices in crisis contexts.
