BATHE-PTHE-2300 - Pastoral Theology

This course studies the application of theology in the practical exercise of care, guidance, and accompaniment of the Christian community. It explores the biblical and theological foundations of pastoral ministry, as well as the principles and skills necessary for sensitive, ethical, and transformative leadership in the life of the Church, integrating theological reflection with the practical realities of Christian ministry.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the biblical and theological foundations of the pastoral vocation.
  • Analyze the essential functions of pastoral ministry: care, guidance, teaching, and accompaniment.
  • Develop pastoral skills for accompanying individuals, families, and communities.
  • Evaluate the ethical and practical challenges facing pastoral leadership in contemporary contexts.
  • Integrate theological reflection into the daily practice of pastoral ministry in a contextualized and relevant manner.
  • Promote pastoral leadership centered on service, empathy, and spiritual formation.

Main Topics:

  • Biblical and theological foundations of pastoral theology.
  • The vocation and role of the pastor in the Christian community.
  • Principles of pastoral care: crisis accompaniment, discipleship, and spiritual formation.
  • Pastoral ethics: integrity, boundaries, and ministerial responsibility.
  • Preaching, teaching, and sacramental administration in pastoral ministry.
  • Pastoral leadership in contexts of social and cultural change.
  • The communal and missional dimension of pastoral ministry.
  • The care of the pastor: maintaining healthy spiritual, emotional, and relational life.

Methodology:

The course will be conducted through expository virtual lectures, analysis of real ministerial case studies, simulations of pastoral interventions, practical skills workshops, discussion forums, and projects designing pastoral strategies. Collaborative learning, the development of pastoral empathy, and contextualized critical thinking will be promoted.

Assessment:

  • Critical essays on the foundations of pastoral theology.
  • Analysis of case studies on pastoral practices.
  • Active participation in discussion forums and practical workshops.
  • Theological and ministerial comprehension written exams.
  • Final project: design of a pastoral accompaniment plan for a local community.

Course Significance:

Pastoral Theology is key to forming Christian leaders capable of integrating theological thinking with pastoral action. This course equips students to exercise ethical, sensitive, and transformative leadership, strengthening the spiritual and communal life of the Church, and responding effectively to pastoral needs in a changing and challenging world.
