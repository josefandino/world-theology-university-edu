BATHE-SPW-2700 - Social and Pastoral Work

This course integrates the fundamental principles of social work with the practice of pastoral care, equipping students to serve ethically, compassionately, and strategically individuals and vulnerable communities. It explores interdisciplinary approaches to spiritual accompaniment, social intervention, and the promotion of human dignity and well-being from a holistic Christian perspective.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the biblical, theological, and ethical foundations of social and pastoral service.
  • Analyze the convergence of social work and pastoral care in contexts of human need.
  • Develop practical skills for pastoral intervention and social action based on Christian principles.
  • Evaluate ethical challenges in accompanying vulnerable populations.
  • Design integral service strategies for strengthening at-risk communities.
  • Promote a pastoral praxis that contributes to social justice and community well-being.

Main Topics:

  • Biblical and theological foundations of social and pastoral work.
  • Principles and values of social work applied to Christian ministry.
  • Models of pastoral intervention in crisis and vulnerability situations.
  • Care for populations facing poverty, exclusion, violence, and migration.
  • Professional ethics in pastoral and social accompaniment.
  • Design and management of social and pastoral community action projects.
  • Interdisciplinary work: collaboration among churches, social organizations, and communities.
  • Evaluation of social and pastoral intervention programs.

Methodology:

The course will be conducted through expository virtual lectures, analysis of real case studies, simulations of pastoral and social interventions, practical workshops, and social action program design projects. Collaborative learning, ethical reflection, and practical engagement in Christian service will be emphasized.

Assessment:

  • Reflective essays on the integration of social work and pastoral care.
  • Analysis of social and pastoral intervention case studies.
  • Active participation in workshops and critical discussion forums.
  • Written exams on biblical, theological, and ethical foundations.
  • Final project: design of a comprehensive social and pastoral intervention program.

Course Significance:

Social and Pastoral Work is crucial for training Christian leaders who respond holistically to the spiritual, social, and emotional needs of individuals and communities. This course equips students to act with compassion, justice, and effectiveness on behalf of the most vulnerable, integrating faith, service, and social transformation into their ministry.
