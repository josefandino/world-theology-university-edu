BATHE-HC-300 - History of Christianity

This course explores the historical development of Christianity from its origins in the first century to the present day. It examines major movements, ecumenical councils, influential figures, and ecclesiastical divisions such as the Protestant Reformation, as well as Christianityâ€™s transformative impact on society and the shaping of the contemporary church. Emphasis is placed on fostering a critical understanding of the historical and theological contexts that have shaped the Christian faith across the centuries.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the key events in the history of Christianity from its inception to the present.
  • Identify the major movements, figures, and councils that have defined the development of the Christian faith.
  • Analyze the causes, processes, and consequences of major Christian divisions, such as the Reformation and the Great Schism.
  • Evaluate the social, cultural, and political influence of Christianity across different eras and historical contexts.
  • Reflect on the relationship between historical changes and transformations in Christian doctrine and practice.
  • Apply historical knowledge to understand and respond to contemporary challenges facing the global church.

Main Topics:

  • Origins of Christianity: the Jewish and Greco-Roman contexts.
  • The expansion of the early church and the persecutions.
  • Ecumenical councils and the formation of fundamental doctrinal definitions.
  • Medieval Christianity: monasticism, the Crusades, and Scholasticism.
  • The Protestant Reformation and the Catholic Counter-Reformation.
  • Christianity in the context of modernity and the Enlightenment.
  • Movements of renewal, global missions, and contemporary Christianity.
  • Christianity in the twenty-first century: challenges and global perspectives.

Methodology:

The course will be conducted through virtual lectures, critical analysis of historical documents, online debates, case studies, and research projects. Analytical and reflective thinking will be encouraged, promoting comparisons between historical contexts and contemporary realities, with an emphasis on autonomous learning and active participation in collaborative environments.

Assessment:

  • Analytical essays on key historical events and figures.
  • Case studies on critical moments in Christian history.
  • Written examinations assessing comprehension and critical analysis.
  • Participation in academic discussion forums and debates.
  • Final research project applying historical analysis to contemporary realities.

Course Significance:

History of Christianity is fundamental for understanding the development of Christian identity over time. It enables students to theologically situate their faith within historical processes, recognizing the challenges, achievements, and shortcomings of the Christian tradition. This knowledge is crucial for informed, ethical, and relevant leadership in the context of a globalized church and a rapidly changing society.
