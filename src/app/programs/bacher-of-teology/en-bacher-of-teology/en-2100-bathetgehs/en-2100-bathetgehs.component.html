BATHE-THEHS-2100 - Theology of the Holy Spirit

This course offers an in-depth analysis of the person and work of the Holy Spirit as revealed in Scripture, manifested in the history of the Church, and active in the life of the believer. It explores the role of the Spirit in creation, redemption, sanctification, and Christian mission, integrating biblical, theological, and historical perspectives for a comprehensive and practical understanding of the ministry of the Holy Spirit.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the biblical foundations regarding the identity and action of the Holy Spirit.
  • Analyze the historical development of the doctrine of the Holy Spirit in Christian theology.
  • Explore the role of the Holy Spirit in the life of the believer, the Church, and Christian mission.
  • Evaluate different theological perspectives on spiritual gifts and their contemporary application.
  • Foster a personal and communal spirituality guided by the work of the Holy Spirit.
  • Apply principles from a theology of the Holy Spirit to current ministerial and social challenges.

Main Topics:

  • Biblical foundations of the person and work of the Holy Spirit.
  • The Holy Spirit in the Old and New Testaments.
  • Historical development of the doctrine of the Holy Spirit: patristic era, Reformation, and contemporary movements.
  • The Holy Spirit in regeneration, sanctification, and Christian life.
  • The gifts of the Holy Spirit: nature, purpose, and responsible use.
  • The Holy Spirit and the Church’s mission in the world.
  • Spiritual discernment and leadership under the guidance of the Spirit.
  • The role of the Holy Spirit in revival and social transformation.

Methodology:

The course will be conducted through expository virtual lectures, analysis of biblical and theological texts, case studies, thematic debates, practical workshops on spiritual reflection, and ministerial application projects. Experiential learning, critical thinking, and the integration of theology with spiritual and communal life will be encouraged.

Assessment:

  • Theological essays on the person and work of the Holy Spirit.
  • Written exams assessing doctrinal and biblical understanding.
  • Active participation in discussion forums and spiritual discernment workshops.
  • Case study analysis on the ministry of the Holy Spirit in history and today.
  • Final project: theological-ministerial application focused on the work of the Holy Spirit.

Course Significance:

Theology of the Holy Spirit is essential for understanding the dynamic nature of Christian life and the Church’s mission. This course equips students to live a vibrant faith, discern the work of the Spirit in personal and communal contexts, and lead with spiritual sensitivity and theological depth in a world in need of renewal and hope.
