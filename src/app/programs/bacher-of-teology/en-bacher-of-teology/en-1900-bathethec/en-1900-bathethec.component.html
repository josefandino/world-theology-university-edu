BATHE-THEC-1900 - Theology of Culture

This course reflects on the relationship between the Christian faith and contemporary cultural expressions. It analyzes the theological foundations for understanding culture, as well as the dynamics of interaction between the gospel and various cultural manifestations. The course aims to equip students to critically discern and creatively engage with culture, promoting a contextualized, relevant, and transformative faith.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the biblical and theological foundations of the relationship between faith and culture.
  • Analyze various conceptions of culture from a critical theological perspective.
  • Evaluate how the gospel interacts with, challenges, and transforms contemporary cultural expressions.
  • Develop skills to critically discern cultural values in light of the Christian faith.
  • Apply theological principles for creative and ethical engagement with culture.
  • Foster theological reflection that promotes a relevant faith in pluralistic and dynamic contexts.

Main Topics:

  • Biblical foundations for understanding culture and human creation.
  • Theological perspectives on culture: contextualization, transformation, and mission.
  • Contemporary culture: challenges and opportunities for the Christian faith.
  • Cultural discernment: media, art, technology, and globalization.
  • The gospel and cultural expressions: inculturation and counterculture.
  • Models of interaction between the church and culture throughout history.
  • The mission of the church in diverse and changing cultural contexts.
  • Theology of culture and contemporary social engagement.

Methodology:

The course will be conducted through expository virtual lectures, critical analysis of cultural phenomena, case studies, discussion forums, and contextual application projects. Interdisciplinary dialogue, critical thinking, and theological creativity will be encouraged to address the challenges and opportunities presented by contemporary culture.

Assessment:

  • Theological reflection essays on current cultural phenomena.
  • Critical analysis of cultural expressions in light of the Christian faith.
  • Written examinations on theological foundations and cultural concepts.
  • Active participation in critical and creative discussion forums.
  • Final project: proposal for cultural engagement from a theological perspective.

Course Significance:

Theology of Culture is fundamental for forming Christian leaders capable of living, communicating, and embodying their faith in a culturally diverse and dynamic world. This course equips students to discern the signs of the times, engage in dialogue with the cultural environment, and contribute to its transformation from a solid, ethical, and hope-filled Christian perspective.
