BATHE-APO-1200 - Apologetics

This course provides biblical, philosophical, and theological foundations for the rational defense of the Christian faith against objections arising from philosophical, scientific, and cultural fields. Students are equipped to present an intelligible, reasoned, and relevant faith within contemporary contexts of dialogue and confrontation, fostering a respectful and confident attitude in the proclamation of the gospel.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the biblical and theological foundations of Christian apologetics.
  • Identify the main objections to the Christian faith from philosophical, scientific, and cultural contexts.
  • Develop solid, coherent, and contextualized apologetic responses.
  • Evaluate different historical and contemporary schools and methods of apologetics.
  • Apply apologetic principles in conversations, debates, and spaces of interreligious or secular dialogue.
  • Foster a spirit of respect, humility, and clarity in defending the Christian faith.

Main Topics:

  • Introduction to apologetics: definition, history, and purpose.
  • Biblical foundations for the defense of the faith.
  • Philosophical objections to Christianity: existence of God, the problem of evil, moral relativism.
  • Scientific objections: faith and reason, creation and evolution, miracles and science.
  • Apologetic responses to contemporary cultural challenges.
  • Major apologetic approaches: classical, evidentialist, presuppositional, and existential.
  • Apologetics in contexts of religious pluralism and secularism.
  • Designing apologetic strategies for ministry and mission.

Methodology:

The course will be conducted through virtual lectures, critical analysis of apologetic texts, debate simulations, studies of contemporary cases, and argumentation projects. Autonomous learning, critical thinking, and respect in dialogue with diverse perspectives will be emphasized.

Assessment:

  • Apologetic argumentation essays addressing specific objections.
  • Simulated debates on relevant topics of faith and reason.
  • Written examinations on apologetic foundations and methods.
  • Active participation in critical discussion forums.
  • Final project: development of a contextualized apologetic strategy.

Course Significance:

Apologetics is fundamental for equipping students to defend the Christian faith intelligently, respectfully, and effectively in an increasingly pluralistic and skeptical world. This course strengthens the ability to reason about the faith, engage in respectful dialogue, and witness to the gospel with conviction and relevance across diverse contemporary cultural settings.
