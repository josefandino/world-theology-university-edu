BATHE-ITHE-100 - Introduction to Theology

This course provides an overview of theological study, addressing its main disciplines, methods, and objectives. It explores the nature of revelation, faith, reason, and the interaction between theology, Christian life, and contemporary society. The course aims to equip students with a critical and reflective understanding of theological work, preparing them for rigorous analysis and contextualized application in a pluralistic and diverse world.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the fundamental foundations of theological study and its historical development.
  • Identify the principal theological disciplines and their distinctive methodologies.
  • Analyze the role of revelation, faith, and reason in theological construction.
  • Develop critical skills for theological reflection in multicultural contexts.
  • Apply fundamental theological concepts to contemporary situations and ministerial practices.
  • Foster an ethical and responsible perspective in addressing theological issues in today's world.

Main Topics:

  • Introduction to the concept and purpose of theology.
  • Divine revelation: Scripture, tradition, and experience.
  • Relationship between faith and reason: historical and contemporary perspectives.
  • Major branches of theology: systematic, biblical, historical, and practical.
  • Theological methods: critical analysis, hermeneutics, and contextualization.
  • Theology in dialogue with science, philosophy, and contemporary culture.
  • Ethical and social responsibility in theological reflection.
  • The future of theology in a globalized and pluralistic world.

Methodology:

The course will be delivered through interactive virtual lectures, analysis of contemporary cases, guided online discussions, practical exercises, and the development of theological reflection projects. Active participation, peer collaboration, and autonomous learning will be encouraged, utilizing multimedia resources and selected readings from leading theological scholars.

Assessment:

  • Critical essays on fundamental theological topics.
  • Analysis of contemporary ethical cases and dilemmas.
  • Written examinations assessing theoretical and applied understanding.
  • Active participation in academic discussion forums.
  • Final project involving contextualized theological reflection.

Course Significance:

Introduction to Theology serves as an essential cornerstone in the education of professionals within the theological field, providing the conceptual and methodological foundations necessary for critical, ethical, and contextual theological practice. This course fosters analytical and dialogical skills in diverse cultural settings, promoting a conscious theological practice that addresses global challenges and is committed to the positive transformation of the communities and societies where students will exercise their vocation.
