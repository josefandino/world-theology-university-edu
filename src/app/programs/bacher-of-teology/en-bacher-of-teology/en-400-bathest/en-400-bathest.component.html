BATHE-ST-400 - Systematic Theology

This course offers an organized and methodical study of the principal doctrines of the Christian faith, such as God, Jesus Christ, the Holy Spirit, salvation, the church, and eschatology. It seeks to understand how these doctrines interrelate and form a coherent theological system that upholds the Christian faith, promoting critical reflection and contextualized application in contemporary life and ministry.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the main doctrines of the Christian faith and their biblical foundations.
  • Analyze the interrelationships among the different areas of systematic theology.
  • Evaluate historical and contemporary theological perspectives on essential doctrines.
  • Develop critical skills to formulate a coherent, solid, and contextualized faith.
  • Apply doctrinal principles to personal life, community engagement, and ministerial practice.
  • Foster ethical and responsible theological reflection in response to contemporary global challenges.

Main Topics:

  • Introduction to Systematic Theology: concept, history, and methods.
  • The Doctrine of God: attributes, Trinity, and relationship with the world.
  • Christology: the person and work of Jesus Christ.
  • Pneumatology: the Holy Spirit and His work in the church and the believer.
  • Soteriology: the nature and process of salvation.
  • Ecclesiology: the nature, mission, and governance of the church.
  • Eschatology: the end times, Christian hope, and eschatological fulfillment.
  • Systematic integration of doctrines for a coherent Christian worldview.

Methodology:

The course will be delivered through expository virtual lectures, critical analysis of classical and contemporary theological texts, online discussions, doctrinal case studies, and the development of theological integration projects. Analytical thinking, respectful dialogue, and autonomous learning will be promoted within a collaborative and intercultural environment.

Assessment:

  • Theological essays on specific doctrines.
  • Critical analysis of historical and contemporary texts.
  • Written examinations assessing doctrinal understanding and synthesis.
  • Active participation in theological forums and academic debates.
  • Final project: development of a contextualized systematic theology framework.

Course Significance:

Systematic Theology is an essential course for the development of a structured, reflective, and coherent faith. It enables students to integrate the various aspects of Christian theology into a comprehensive and relevant framework for their personal life, ministerial service, and participation in academic and social dialogue. This course strengthens the ability to respond ethically and insightfully to contemporary challenges from a robust and global theological perspective.
