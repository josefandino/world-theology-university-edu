BATHE-CP-3100 - Christian Psychology

This course integrates the fundamental principles of psychology with the Christian faith to understand and support individuals in their emotional, mental, and spiritual dimensions. Relevant psychological theories are analyzed from a Christian perspective, exploring strategies for counseling, accompaniment, and the holistic development of individuals in ministerial and community contexts.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the foundations of psychology applied to Christian counseling and accompaniment.
  • Analyze the interactions between emotional, mental, and spiritual health.
  • Evaluate psychological theories and models in light of biblical and theological principles.
  • Develop basic skills for emotional support and counseling in ministerial contexts.
  • Reflect on the importance of holistic care in pastoral and community ministry.
  • Apply principles of Christian psychology to the design of accompaniment and support strategies.

Main Topics:

  • Biblical and theological foundations for understanding the human person.
  • Principles of developmental, personality, and behavioral psychology.
  • Relationship between spirituality, mental health, and emotional well-being.
  • Stress, anxiety, grief, and depression: Christian understanding and support.
  • Models of Christian counseling and integrative approaches.
  • Ethics in counseling practice and pastoral accompaniment.
  • The role of the church in promoting emotional and spiritual health.
  • Design of emotional and pastoral support programs for Christian communities.

Methodology:

The course will be delivered through expository virtual classes, case study analyses, counseling session simulations, ethical discussion forums, practical workshops, and projects for designing holistic accompaniment strategies. Critical reflection, pastoral sensitivity, and the integration of psychology and faith in Christian service will be encouraged.

Assessment:

  • Reflective essays on the integration of psychology and theology.
  • Case studies on pastoral support in specific emotional situations.
  • Written exams on the understanding of psychological and biblical principles.
  • Active participation in counseling skills workshops.
  • Final project: design of an emotional and spiritual support program for a Christian community.

Course Significance:

Christian Psychology is fundamental for equipping leaders and ministers in the holistic accompaniment of individuals, recognizing the interaction between their emotional, mental, and spiritual dimensions. This course prepares students to serve more deeply, ethically, and effectively in promoting healing and holistic growth within community and ministerial life.
