BATHE-ECS-1800 - Studies on Early Christianity

This course studies the origins of Christianity, the early Christian communities, and the development of the primitive Church within its historical, social, and cultural contexts. It analyzes the emergence of fundamental doctrines, communal practices, the expansion of Christianity, and the challenges faced by the Christian faith during the first centuries, providing a profound understanding of the roots of the Christian movement.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the historical, religious, and social context in which Christianity emerged.
  • Analyze the development and organization of the early Christian communities.
  • Explore the main challenges, persecutions, and theological debates of the early Church.
  • Identify key figures and foundational writings from the first centuries of Christianity.
  • Evaluate the impact of early Christianity on the shaping of Christian faith and identity.
  • Relate the legacy of the early Church to the contemporary challenges faced by the modern Church.

Main Topics:

  • Historical and religious context of the first century: Judaism, Hellenism, and Roman culture.
  • The life and ministry of Jesus: foundations of Christianity.
  • The expansion of Christianity and apostolic missions.
  • The organization and communal life of the early churches.
  • Persecutions, martyrs, and Christian resistance.
  • The Apostolic Fathers and early Christian writings.
  • Initial councils and early doctrinal development.
  • The influence of early Christianity on the later history of the Church.

Course Significance:

Studies on Early Christianity provides an essential foundation for understanding the emergence and consolidation of the Christian faith. This course enables students to recognize the historical and theological relevance of the first generations of Christians, strengthening their theological identity, critical historical perspective, and ability to address contemporary challenges rooted in a well-grounded faith.
