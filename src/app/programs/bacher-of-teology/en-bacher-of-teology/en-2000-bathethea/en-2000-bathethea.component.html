BATHE-THEA-2000 - Theological Anthropology

This course explores human nature from a Christian perspective, examining fundamental topics such as the creation of humanity, the image of God in human beings, the impact of sin, the need for redemption, and the ultimate destiny of humankind. It provides a comprehensive theological understanding of the human condition, integrating biblical, historical, and contemporary reflection.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the biblical and theological foundations of Christian anthropology.
  • Analyze the concept of the image of God and its implications for human dignity.
  • Explore the effect of sin on human nature and on relationships with God and others.
  • Reflect on redemption in Christ and its impact on the restoration of humanity.
  • Evaluate different theological perspectives on the condition and destiny of human beings.
  • Apply a Christian anthropological vision to contemporary ethical, social, and cultural challenges.

Main Topics:

  • The creation of humanity: biblical exegesis and theological significance.
  • The image of God (Imago Dei) in humankind.
  • The Fall and its consequences: original sin and human corruption.
  • The need for redemption and Christâ€™s work in human restoration.
  • Human beings in relationship: community, vocation, and purpose.
  • Contemporary perspectives on human nature and its challenges.
  • Eschatological hope: resurrection and glorification of humanity.
  • Applications of theological anthropology in bioethics, human rights, and social justice.

Methodology:

The course will be conducted through expository virtual classes, analysis of biblical and theological texts, studies of contemporary ethical cases, discussion forums, and applied reflection projects. Critical thinking, interdisciplinary dialogue, and the integration of theology with current human realities will be encouraged.

Assessment:

  • Theological essays on human nature and dignity.
  • Written examinations on fundamental concepts.
  • Participation in academic and contemporary ethical discussion forums.
  • Critical analysis of case studies related to theological anthropology.
  • Final project applying theological anthropology to a current issue.

Course Significance:

Theological Anthropology is essential for understanding the identity, value, and purpose of human beings from a Christian perspective. This course equips students to reflect theologically on the human condition, respond to contemporary challenges with a solid biblical foundation, and contribute ethically and transformatively to society.
