BATHE-SOR-2800 - Sociology of Religion

This course analyzes the religious phenomenon from a sociological perspective, exploring how religion influences and is influenced by society and culture. It studies the major sociological theories of religion, the social function of religious beliefs and practices, and the changes in the role of religion within contemporary globalized, plural, and dynamic contexts.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the main sociological theories on religion and their historical development.
  • Analyze the role of religion in social structure, collective identity, and social change.
  • Explore the relationship between religion, culture, politics, and economy in various societies.
  • Evaluate processes of secularization, desecularization, and alternative spiritualities in modernity.
  • Develop critical skills to interpret contemporary religious phenomena in their social dimensions.
  • Apply concepts from the sociology of religion to the analysis of local and global religious communities.

Main Topics:

  • Introduction to the sociology of religion: definitions and approaches.
  • Major theorists of religion: Durkheim, Weber, Marx, Berger, and others.
  • Religion as a social phenomenon: identity, cohesion, and conflict.
  • Secularization, desecularization, and alternative spiritualities.
  • Religion and social change: religious movements and cultural transformation.
  • Religion, politics, and power in contemporary contexts.
  • Religious pluralism in globalized societies.
  • Case studies: analysis of current religious communities.

Methodology:

The course will be conducted through expository virtual lectures, analysis of fundamental sociological texts, contemporary case studies, critical discussion forums, and applied research projects. Analytical thinking, interdisciplinary reflection, and the ability to interpret complex social phenomena will be encouraged.

Assessment:

  • Critical essays on contemporary religious theories and phenomena.
  • Case studies on religious communities or movements.
  • Written exams on theoretical and conceptual understanding.
  • Active participation in critical discussion forums and analyses.
  • Final project: applied sociological research on a religious phenomenon.

Course Significance:

Sociology of Religion is fundamental for understanding religion as a dynamic and complex social phenomenon. This course equips students to critically analyze the role of faith in contemporary social life, fostering an informed and respectful engagement with religious diversity in a pluralistic and globalized world.
