BATHE-PC-900 - Pastoral Counseling

This course introduces the foundations and methods of pastoral counseling, providing theoretical and practical tools for spiritual and emotional accompaniment. It focuses on the development of skills such as active listening, empathy, discernment, and support, equipping students to assist individuals in situations of crisis, grief, personal conflict, and spiritual growth from a pastoral perspective centered on compassion and hope.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the biblical, theological, and psychological foundations of pastoral counseling.
  • Develop skills for spiritual and emotional accompaniment in various contexts of need.
  • Identify and apply appropriate methods and approaches for pastoral support in critical situations.
  • Analyze the ethical boundaries and responsibilities in the practice of pastoral counseling.
  • Strengthen the capacity for active listening, empathy, and pastoral discernment.
  • Apply pastoral counseling principles in the design of spiritual care and accompaniment plans.

Main Topics:

  • Biblical and theological foundations of pastoral counseling.
  • Basic principles of emotional and spiritual accompaniment.
  • Active listening and pastoral dialogue.
  • Counseling in situations of crisis, grief, and conflict.
  • Ethics and boundaries in the practice of pastoral counseling.
  • The relationship between pastoral counseling and other helping disciplines.
  • Designing plans for spiritual accompaniment.
  • Pastoral counseling in multicultural and contemporary contexts.

Methodology:

The course will be delivered through virtual lectures, practical simulations of pastoral interviews, analysis of real case studies, reflective discussion forums, and the development of accompaniment plan projects. Experiential learning, collaborative work, and the development of essential practical competencies for the ethical and effective practice of pastoral counseling will be encouraged.

Assessment:

  • Critical essays on the foundations and practices of pastoral counseling.
  • Analysis of case studies and resolution of simulated situations.
  • Practical evaluations of counseling and accompaniment skills.
  • Participation in discussion forums and reflection workshops.
  • Final project: design of a comprehensive pastoral accompaniment plan.

Course Significance:

Pastoral Counseling is a fundamental component in the formation of leaders and ministers who seek to provide holistic accompaniment to individuals throughout their life journeys. This course equips students to exercise sensitive, ethical, and transformative pastoral counseling in a world marked by diversity, emotional complexity, and the need for genuine care and hope.
