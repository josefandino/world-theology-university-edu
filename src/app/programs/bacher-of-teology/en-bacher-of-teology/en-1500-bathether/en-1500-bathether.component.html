BATHE-THER-1500 - Theological Research

This course introduces the fundamental methodologies and principles for theological research with academic rigor and biblical fidelity. Students are trained to develop research projects that integrate critical analysis, deep theological reflection, and a structured methodology, promoting significant contributions to the understanding and advancement of theological knowledge in contemporary contexts.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the epistemological and methodological foundations of theological research.
  • Analyze various research methods applicable to theological studies.
  • Develop skills for formulating relevant and pertinent theological research problems.
  • Apply principles of critical analysis, logical argumentation, and biblical fidelity in research.
  • Master techniques for searching, organizing, and managing specialized academic sources.
  • Produce a theological research project adhering to international academic standards.

Main Topics:

  • Foundations of research in theology: nature, purpose, and ethics.
  • Methodological designs in theological research: qualitative, quantitative, and mixed approaches.
  • Formulation of theological research questions, hypotheses, and objectives.
  • Search and critical analysis of biblical, theological, and interdisciplinary sources.
  • Academic writing and citation standards in theology (Turabian, SBL, APA styles).
  • Construction of solid and well-founded theological arguments.
  • Presentation, defense, and evaluation of theological research projects.
  • Ethical responsibility in the production of theological knowledge.

Methodology:

The course will be conducted through theoretical-practical virtual lectures, analysis of examples of theological research, project formulation workshops, critical discussion forums, and practical writing and argumentation exercises. Autonomous learning, guided research, and commitment to the highest standards of academic excellence will be encouraged.

Assessment:

  • Critical essays on methodological approaches in theology.
  • Progressive development of a theological research project.
  • Review and critical analysis of relevant theological sources.
  • Active participation in academic writing workshops and discussion forums.
  • Final project: presentation of a complete theological research proposal.

Course Significance:

Theological Research is essential for forming professionals capable of contributing critically, rigorously, and faithfully to contemporary theological reflection. This course strengthens commitment to academic excellence, ethical responsibility, and respect for biblical integrity, preparing students to make significant contributions to academia, the church, and global society.
