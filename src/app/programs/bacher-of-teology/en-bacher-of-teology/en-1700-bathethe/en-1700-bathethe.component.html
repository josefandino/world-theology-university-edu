BATHE-THE-1700 - Bachelor's Thesis

This course represents the academic culmination of the Bachelor of Theology program through the development of a theological research thesis. Students will undertake a project demonstrating their ability to rigorously, thoughtfully, and critically investigate a specific topic, integrating the knowledge acquired throughout the program and making a significant contribution to theological reflection.

Course Objectives:

  • Formulate a relevant, feasible, and academically rigorous theological research project.
  • Develop advanced skills in research, critical analysis, and theological argumentation.
  • Integrate biblical, theological, historical, and ethical principles in addressing the chosen topic.
  • Apply academic standards of writing, citation, and presentation in research projects.
  • Contribute originally and meaningfully to contemporary theological dialogue.
  • Defend the thesis before an academic committee with clarity, rigor, and critical thought.

Main Topics:

  • Formulation of the research problem and specific objectives.
  • Critical review of relevant theological literature.
  • Research methodology applied to theology.
  • Structure and writing of the thesis: introduction, argumentative body, and conclusions.
  • Ethics in research and academic responsibility.
  • Academic citation standards (Turabian, SBL, APA).
  • Presentation, defense, and evaluation of the bachelor's thesis.

Methodology:

The course will be conducted through individual academic tutoring, research methodology workshops, thesis progress seminars, and critical feedback sessions. Investigative autonomy, ethical commitment, and the development of a theologically grounded voice based on rigorous analysis and biblical fidelity will be encouraged.

Assessment:

  • Presentation and approval of the thesis project.
  • Progressive submission of structured research advances.
  • Final writing of the thesis according to international academic standards.
  • Oral defense of the thesis before an academic evaluation committee.
