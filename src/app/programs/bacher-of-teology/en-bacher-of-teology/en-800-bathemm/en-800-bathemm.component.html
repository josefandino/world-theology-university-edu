BATHE-MM-800 - Ministry and Mission

This course examines the calling, nature, and practice of Christian ministry, with a special emphasis on the holistic mission of the Church at both local and global levels. It reflects on the identity and purpose of the Church in the contemporary world, exploring ministerial and missional strategies that respond with sensitivity and relevance to diverse cultural and social contexts.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the biblical and theological foundations of Christian ministry and the Churchâ€™s mission.
  • Analyze the relationship between ministry, mission, and cultural context.
  • Evaluate different approaches and strategies for mission in local and global contexts.
  • Develop practical skills for designing and implementing ministerial and missional projects.
  • Foster a vision of Christian ministry centered on service, justice, and social transformation.
  • Apply ministerial and missional principles ethically, responsibly, and with intercultural sensitivity in real-world contexts.

Main Topics:

  • Biblical and theological foundations of Christian ministry.
  • The Mission of God (Missio Dei) and the role of the Church.
  • Pastoral, prophetic, and diaconal ministry in the local church.
  • Strategies for urban, rural, and cross-cultural mission.
  • The Church in dialogue with culture, justice, and social transformation.
  • Development of sustainable ministerial and missional projects.
  • Contemporary challenges for ministry and global mission.
  • Ethics and responsibility in ministerial and missional practice.

Methodology:

The course will be conducted through virtual lectures, analysis of real ministerial case studies, the development of mission projects, online discussions, and practical workshops. Experiential learning, critical thinking, and collaborative work will be promoted, focusing on the contextual application of ministerial and missional principles.

Assessment:

  • Theological reflection essays on ministry and mission.
  • Analysis of missional and ministerial case studies.
  • Written examinations assessing conceptual understanding.
  • Active participation in discussion forums and thematic debates.
  • Final project: design of a contextualized ministry or mission strategy.

Course Significance:

Ministry and Mission is key to forming Christian leaders capable of integrating theological reflection with practical action in both local and global contexts. This course fosters a deep understanding of the ministerial and missional calling, preparing students to serve effectively, with cultural sensitivity and transformative commitment in a dynamic and diverse world.
