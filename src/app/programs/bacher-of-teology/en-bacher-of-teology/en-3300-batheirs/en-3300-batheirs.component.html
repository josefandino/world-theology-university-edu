BATHE-IRS-3300 - Interreligious Studies

This course studies the world's major religions, promoting dialogue, understanding, and interreligious respect from a Christian perspective. It analyzes the beliefs, practices, and values of various religious traditions, evaluating both their convergences and fundamental differences with Christianity, with the aim of fostering respectful coexistence and faithful witness in a religiously plural world.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the basic principles of the world's major religions.
  • Analyze fundamental similarities and differences between Christianity and other religious traditions.
  • Reflect on the challenges and opportunities of contemporary interreligious dialogue.
  • Develop an attitude of respect, openness, and discernment in diverse religious contexts.
  • Evaluate the Christian witness in religiously pluralistic environments.
  • Apply principles of dialogue and interreligious understanding in Christian ministry and public life.

Main Topics:

  • Theological and biblical foundations of interreligious dialogue.
  • Introduction to Judaism, Islam, Hinduism, Buddhism, and traditional religions.
  • Theological and anthropological comparisons between Christianity and other religions.
  • Ethical and theological challenges in interreligious dialogue.
  • The Christian witness in a religiously plural world.
  • Contemporary movements of interreligious dialogue and cooperation.
  • Case studies of successful dialogue and lessons for Christian mission.
  • Designing strategies for respectful engagement and sensitive evangelism in interreligious contexts.

Methodology:

The course will be delivered through expository virtual classes, analysis of sacred texts from various religions, case studies of interreligious dialogue, critical discussion forums, and practical application projects. Critical thinking, theological discernment, and ethical commitment in dialogue with people of different faiths will be encouraged.

Assessment:

  • Comparative essays on Christianity and other religions.
  • Case studies on experiences of interreligious dialogue.
  • Written exams on theological and comparative principles.
  • Active participation in critical discussion forums.
  • Final project: design of a Christian strategy for dialogue and witness in an interreligious context.

Course Significance:

Interreligious Studies is essential for preparing Christian leaders who can live and minister in religiously plural contexts with discernment, respect, and evangelical commitment. This course strengthens the ability to bear witness to the Christian faith with love, truth, and cultural sensitivity in an increasingly interconnected and diverse world.
