BATHE-THEOM-2500 - Theology of Ministry

This course offers a theological reflection on the nature, purpose, and functions of Christian ministry according to Scripture. It studies the biblical and theological foundations of ministry, its various expressions in the life of the Church, and its relevance to the contemporary Christian mission. The course equips students to exercise their ministerial calling with a biblical, strategic, and contextualized focus.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the biblical and theological foundations of Christian ministry.
  • Analyze the diversity of ministerial functions and roles within the life of the Church.
  • Reflect on the purpose and mission of ministry in the light of Scripture.
  • Develop theological criteria to evaluate and guide contemporary ministerial practices.
  • Apply principles of ministry theology to diverse ecclesial, social, and cultural contexts.
  • Foster an ethical, sensitive, transformative ministry centered on service to others.

Main Topics:

  • Definition and nature of Christian ministry according to Scripture.
  • Vocation and calling to ministry: biblical and theological foundations.
  • Functions of ministry: proclamation, teaching, pastoral care, and mission.
  • The ministry of all believers: universal priesthood and communal service.
  • Historical and contemporary models of Christian ministry.
  • Ministerial ethics: integrity, service, and spiritual leadership.
  • Ministry in dialogue with contemporary global challenges.
  • Designing ministerial projects based on solid and contextualized theology.

Methodology:

The course will be conducted through expository virtual lectures, analysis of biblical and theological texts, case studies on ministerial practices, critical discussion forums, and practical application projects. Theological thinking, ethical reflection, and commitment to contextualized Christian service will be emphasized.

Assessment:

  • Theological reflection essays on the nature and purpose of ministry.
  • Analysis of historical and contemporary models of ministry.
  • Active participation in academic discussion forums.
  • Written exams assessing biblical and theological understanding of ministry.
  • Final project: design of a ministerial plan grounded in theological principles.

Course Significance:

Theology of Ministry is crucial for forming Christian leaders who are deeply aware of the meaning and purpose of their vocation. This course equips students to exercise ministry with biblical faithfulness, cultural sensitivity, pastoral excellence, and a transformative commitment to God's mission in the world.
