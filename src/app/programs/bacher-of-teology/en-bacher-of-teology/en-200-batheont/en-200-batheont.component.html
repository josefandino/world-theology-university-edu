BATHE-ONT-200 - Old and New Testament

This course offers an overview of the books comprising the Old and New Testaments. It examines the historical contexts, authorship, literary genres, and principal messages of each biblical section. The theological significance of the Scriptures and their practical application in contemporary Christian life are also explored, providing students with tools for a comprehensive and contextualized understanding of the Bible.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the general structure and primary content of the books of the Old and New Testaments.
  • Identify the historical and cultural contexts in which the biblical texts were written.
  • Analyze the main literary genres present in the Bible and their impact on interpretation.
  • Recognize the fundamental theological themes and messages of each Testament.
  • Develop hermeneutical skills for the critical and responsible interpretation of biblical texts.
  • Apply biblical principles and teachings to the challenges of contemporary Christian life in diverse contexts.

Main Topics:

  • Introduction to the biblical canon: composition and transmission.
  • Historical and cultural overview of the Old Testament.
  • Historical and cultural overview of the New Testament.
  • Literary genres in the Bible: narrative, poetic, prophetic, and epistolary.
  • Central theological themes of the Old Testament: creation, covenant, law, prophecy.
  • Central theological themes of the New Testament: incarnation, redemption, church, eschatology.
  • Unity and diversity in the biblical message.
  • Principles for the contemporary application of biblical teachings.

Methodology:

The course will be conducted through virtual lectures, exegetical analysis of selected passages, online discussions, collaborative forums, and practical application exercises. Active learning will be promoted through research projects and critical participation in the study of biblical texts, respecting the diversity of cultural and confessional perspectives.

Assessment:

  • Analytical essays on specific biblical books or sections.
  • Written examinations assessing content comprehension and theological concepts.
  • Participation in debates and discussion forums.
  • Practical hermeneutical exercises on key texts.
  • Final project focused on contemporary biblical interpretation and application.

Course Significance:

Old and New Testament constitutes a central pillar in theological education, providing students with a solid foundation for the critical, responsible, and contextualized interpretation of the Scriptures. This course fosters a profound understanding of the biblical message, essential for theological reflection, Christian leadership, and mission in a globalized world, emphasizing ethical commitment and intercultural sensitivity.
