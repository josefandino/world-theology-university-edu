Bachelor of Theology

The Bachelor of Theology is designed to train leaders with profound biblical, doctrinal, and pastoral knowledge, committed to serving the church and society. This program offers a solid academic formation by integrating the study of the Scriptures, the history of Christianity, systematic theology, and ministerial skills, with a contemporary approach that responds to the current challenges of both the world and faith. The goal is to prepare theologians capable of critical thinking, compassionate service, and leading with integrity in ecclesial, educational, and social contexts.

100% Online: Flexibility to Study from Anywhere

This program is entirely online, allowing you to study from anywhere in the world without neglecting your personal or ministerial responsibilities. Its flexible structure enables you to progress at your own pace, facilitating self-directed learning and providing continuous access to a global academic community.

Professional Opportunities:

Upon completing the Bachelor of Theology, you will be prepared to work in a wide variety of ministerial and academic contexts. Graduates can serve as pastors, church leaders, chaplains, missionaries, educators in Christian institutions, ethical advisors, and theological writers. Additionally, this training provides a solid foundation for postgraduate studies in theology, philosophy, biblical studies, or pastoral ministry.

Key Theology Skills:

Throughout the program, you will develop essential competencies for relevant and transformative theological and spiritual leadership. Among the most notable skills are:
• Biblical Interpretation: You will learn to interpret the Scriptures in a contextual, historical, and exegetical manner, applying modern hermeneutical tools.
• Critical Theological Thinking: You will be capable of analyzing, formulating, and communicating theological ideas with depth, coherence, and respect for Christian diversity.
• Pastoral and Ministerial Ability: You will develop skills in counseling, preaching, church leadership, and spiritual accompaniment.
• Applied Christian Ethics: You will reflect on contemporary issues from a Christian ethical perspective, addressing social, cultural, and personal dilemmas with moral responsibility and spiritual sensitivity.

High-Level Academic Training:

Our faculty is composed of theologians, biblical scholars, and internationally recognized researchers with extensive experience in both academia and ministry. This program is built on a tradition of rigorous theological thought, spanning over 2,000 years of history, offering an education that honors the Christian heritage while engaging with the realities of the 21st century.

Global Support Network:

As a student at World Theology Academy, you will be part of an international community of believers, pastors, scholars, and students from various denominations and backgrounds. Through our virtual platform, you will participate in forums, collaborative projects, and theological discussions that will enrich your experience and broaden your global perspective of the Kingdom of God.

A Transformative Approach:

This program is designed to impact your personal life, spiritual understanding, and ministerial service. More than just acquiring knowledge, the Bachelor of Theology seeks to transform hearts and minds, training servants who live and teach with fidelity, passion, and wisdom. You will be equipped to face the moral, spiritual, and social challenges of our time with hope and truth, guiding others toward a life centered on God and His eternal purpose.

The Bachelor of Theology at World Theology Academy will provide you with the necessary tools to live your calling with theological depth, pastoral commitment, and a global vision of the Gospel.

Admission Requirements

To apply for the Bachelor of Theology, applicants must meet the following requirements:

  • Curriculum Vitae

  • Applicant’s Statement

    • Write a personal statement explaining the motivations for studying Theology, how the acquired knowledge is expected to be applied in one’s life and ministry, and personal interests in the theological field.

  • Letters of Recommendation

    • Provide two letters of recommendation from ministerial leaders, educators, or professionals who can attest to the applicant’s suitability for theological studies and their commitment to spiritual and academic development.

Selection Criteria

Applicants must have successfully completed secondary education, with an academic record that reflects commitment and the ability to study. Candidates with prior studies in areas such as humanities, philosophy, social sciences, education, or communication will also have an advantage.

Credits and Courses

    The program consists of a total of 120 credits, distributed among foundational theological courses, ministerial electives, and optional courses. This structure allows students to customize their educational experience according to their vocational interests and areas of specialization.

    The key courses include:

  • Introduction to Theology
  • Old and New Testament
  • History of Christianity
  • Systematic Theology
  • Biblical Hermeneutics
  • Christian Ethics
  • Pastoral Leadership
  • Ministry and Mission

    • Elective courses allow students to delve deeper into areas such as pastoral counseling, contextual theology, eschatology, apologetics, Christian education, and practical spirituality.

Thesis or Comprehensive Exam

Upon completion of the program, students must choose between completing a theological research thesis or a comprehensive exam, which will allow them to demonstrate their mastery of the acquired content, their critical ability, and their preparation for professional service in the theological field. The program can be completed flexibly, either part-time or full-time, with a maximum duration of five years.

Applicants for the Bachelor of Theology must meet a series of requirements that ensure their academic preparation, vocational commitment, and capacity to successfully complete this program of spiritual and academic formation. The admission criteria are detailed below:

Official Academic Transcripts

Applicants must submit the official academic transcripts for all previous studies, whether from secondary or higher education. Completion of secondary education is required, and having taken courses in humanities, philosophy, social sciences, or religious studies with outstanding academic performance will be viewed favorably.

Curriculum Vitae

An updated curriculum vitae must be provided, including any ministerial experience, educational background, volunteer work in churches, faith communities, or religious organizations. This document should reflect the applicant’s commitment to service, Christian leadership, or spiritual formation.

Applicant’s Statement

The applicant must write a personal statement of motivation, explaining their calling or interest in theological studies, how this program aligns with their personal and professional vocation, and how they expect to apply the knowledge acquired in ecclesial, academic, or social contexts.

Letters of Recommendation

At least two letters of recommendation are required from spiritual leaders, pastors, professors, or mentors who can attest to the applicant's character, spiritual commitment, and academic potential. These letters should support the applicant's suitability for studying theology and their ability to contribute to the advancement of God's Kingdom through biblical and theological knowledge.

Ministerial or Vocational Experience (Recommended)

Although not mandatory, previous experience in Christian ministries, biblical teaching, group leadership, or missions is recommended. This experience will strengthen the learning process and facilitate the practical application of theological content.

Personal Interview

In some cases, the admissions committee may request a personal or virtual interview with the applicant. This interview aims to assess the applicant’s level of spiritual maturity, vocational commitment, and motivation to undertake a rigorous and transformative theological education.

Application Fee

All applicants must pay a non-refundable application fee, which covers the administrative costs associated with the review and evaluation of the submitted documents.

Recommended Prior Knowledge

Although not an exclusionary requirement, it is recommended to have taken previous courses in areas such as Bible studies, church history, Christian ethics, or philosophical thought, which will facilitate the understanding of the theological topics covered in the program.

Academic Requirements During the Program

Once admitted, the student must meet the following requirements to complete the Bachelor of Theology:

  • TOTAL CREDITS: Complete a total of 120 credits distributed among core, elective, and optional courses.
  • MINIMUM OVERALL GPA: Maintain a minimum overall grade of a B or higher in all completed courses.
  • BACHELOR'S THESIS OR COMPREHENSIVE EXAM: Upon completion of the program, the student must choose either to develop a theological thesis (worth 6 credits) on a relevant research topic or to take a comprehensive exam that assesses their mastery of the key areas of theology.
  • STUDY MODE: The program is 100% online, with the option to study part-time or full-time. The maximum time allowed to complete the degree is five years.

    • These requirements ensure that students enter and complete the program with a deep, biblically solid, and spiritually committed theological formation, in line with the highest standards of excellence at World Theology Academy.

The Bachelor of Divinity program at the Faculty of Divinities of World Theology Academy offers a comprehensive and practical theological formation designed to prepare students not only in their theoretical knowledge but also in their ministerial and vocational application. As a completely online program, it provides flexibility, accessibility, and thorough preparation for those seeking a career in religious ministry, spiritual leadership, or community engagement. Below are the main benefits of the program:

1. Flexibility of Online Study

All courses are offered 100% online, allowing students to study from anywhere in the world. This is ideal for those with work, family, or ministerial commitments, as the program can be adapted to their schedules and needs. Students can study full-time or at their own pace, which allows them to better balance their responsibilities.

2. Personalized and Vocational Training

The program offers students the opportunity to customize their curriculum according to their particular vocation. After the first year, they can choose from seven specializations:

  • Pastoral Ministry
  • Global and Community Engagement
  • Religion and Academia
  • Organizational Management
  • Chaplaincy
  • Interreligious Leadership
  • Theology and the Arts

This flexibility ensures that students receive training aligned with their personal and professional goals.

3. Balance Between Theory and Practice

The program combines rigorous academic courses in fundamental areas of theology, biblical studies, church history, and ethics with practical experiences through Contextual Education internships. These internships provide students the opportunity to apply what they have learned in real ministry settings, such as congregations, non-profit organizations, and community environments. This balance between theory and practice is essential for forming leaders who not only think theologically but also act effectively within their communities.

4. Spiritual Development and Personal Guidance

In addition to academic and practical training, the program focuses on the spiritual and personal growth of its students. Through Spiritual Guidance programs and opportunities for the Practice of Faith, students receive support in their emotional, ethical, and spiritual development. This comprehensive approach ensures that graduates are not only well-trained leaders but also individuals with a deep awareness of their own faith and purpose.

5. Preparation for Leadership in Diverse Contexts

Graduates are prepared to take on leadership roles in a variety of settings, including pastoral ministries, non-profit organizations, community leadership, and more. The specializations allow students to focus on specific areas of interest, such as Chaplaincy, Interreligious Leadership, or Organizational Management, providing them with the necessary tools to lead effectively in both traditional and emerging contexts.

6. Training in a Multicultural and Pluralistic World

The program places a strong emphasis on preparing students to serve in an increasingly diverse religious and cultural environment. Students learn to engage in interreligious dialogues, respect and value cultural differences, and minister in pluralistic settings. This is especially important for those who seek to work in multicultural communities or environments where interreligious cooperation is key.

7. Fulfillment of Ordination Requirements

The program is designed to meet the ordination requirements of most faith traditions. Students can tailor their studies, with the help of academic advisors, to ensure that they align with the specific demands of their denomination or religious community. This means that upon completion of the program, graduates will be well-positioned to pursue ordination or certification within their faith tradition.

8. Academic Support and Personalized Mentorship

Students receive strong academic support throughout the program. Each student works closely with an academic advisor who guides them in selecting courses and internships, ensuring that their formation is aligned with their vocational goals. Additionally, students have access to a network of experienced professors and mentors from various disciplines and ministerial contexts.

9. Development of Interdisciplinary Skills

Thanks to the flexibility of the program, students have the opportunity to develop interdisciplinary skills that extend beyond traditional religious ministry. This includes skills in areas such as organizational leadership, social justice, ecumenism, and interreligious studies, which broadens their career prospects and enables them to be effective leaders in a variety of fields and organizations.

10. Continuing Education and Lifelong Learning

The program fosters a mindset of continuous learning, encouraging students to develop study habits, reflection, and personal growth that will accompany them throughout their ministerial careers. This commitment to ongoing education is essential for those seeking to address the dynamic and complex challenges of ministry in the 21st century.

The Bachelor of Divinity program at World Theology Academy offers a rich and multidimensional education that prepares students for leadership roles in a variety of religious and community settings. With its online flexibility, focus on personalized vocational training, and emphasis on personal and spiritual growth, it provides students with all the necessary tools to make a significant impact in the world.
