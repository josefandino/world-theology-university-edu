BATHE-CE-1600 - Comprehensive Examination

This course consists of an integrative evaluation of the theological knowledge acquired throughout the Bachelor of Theology program. Through a written examination and/or a final project, students will demonstrate their ability to critically analyze, synthesize, and apply fundamental theological content at an advanced academic level, reaffirming their preparation for professional, ministerial, or academic practice.

Course Objectives:

  • Integrate biblical, theological, historical, ethical, and ministerial knowledge acquired during the program.
  • Demonstrate critical analysis skills, theological argumentation, and conceptual synthesis.
  • Apply theological principles to the interpretation of contemporary realities.
  • Formulate theologically grounded responses to complex and current issues.
  • Evaluate oneâ€™s own academic and spiritual formation process in theology.
  • Consolidate preparation for graduate studies, specialized ministries, or advanced theological research.

Main Topics:

  • Biblical foundations: Old and New Testaments.
  • Systematic theology and fundamental doctrines.
  • History of Christianity and its global development.
  • Christian ethics and social responsibility.
  • Biblical hermeneutics and contextual theology.
  • Pastoral ministry, mission, and counseling.
  • Theological research and critical thinking.
  • Integration of faith, knowledge, and practice in contemporary contexts.

Methodology:

The course will be conducted through directed preparation for the comprehensive examination, thematic reviews, personalized academic tutoring, evaluation simulation sessions, and the development of an integrative project if required. Critical reflection, systematic content review, and self-assessment of theological competencies will be encouraged.

Assessment:

  • Comprehensive written examination covering the fundamental areas of theology.
  • Integrative project (if required) that articulates theological knowledge on a relevant topic.
  • Reflective self-assessment of the personal and academic formation process.
  • Participation in tutoring sessions and academic preparation activities.

Course Significance:

The Comprehensive Examination represents a key academic milestone certifying the student's theological maturity, critical thinking, and integration skills. This course ensures that graduates of the Bachelor of Theology program are well prepared to face, with excellence, the challenges of ministerial leadership, teaching, research, or advanced studies in a globalized and pluralistic world.
