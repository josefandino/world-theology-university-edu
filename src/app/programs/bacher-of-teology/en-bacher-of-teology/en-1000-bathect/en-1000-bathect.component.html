BATHE-CT-1000 - Contextual Theology

This course studies the development and application of theology within specific cultural, social, and political contexts, exploring how the Christian faith dynamically interacts with the particular realities of each community. Emphasis is placed on the need to integrate theological reflection with concrete life, promoting a living, relevant, and transformative theology in diverse and contemporary settings.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the theoretical and biblical foundations of contextual theology.
  • Analyze the cultural, social, and political implications in theological formulation.
  • Evaluate various models of contextual theology developed in different global settings.
  • Develop skills to produce theological reflections pertinent to specific contexts.
  • Apply principles of contextual theology in ministerial, social, or educational projects.
  • Foster an ethical and transformative commitment that integrates Christian faith with contemporary reality.

Main Topics:

  • Introduction to contextual theology: definition, history, and foundations.
  • The cultural, social, and political dimensions of theological reflection.
  • Models of contextual theology: Latin American, African, Asian, and minority theologies.
  • Feminist, liberationist, indigenous, and decolonial theologies.
  • Principles of biblical contextualization and situated hermeneutics.
  • Challenges and opportunities for contextual theology in a globalized world.
  • Integrating Christian faith with social justice, human rights, and environmental stewardship.
  • Designing contextualized theological projects.

Methodology:

The course will be delivered through interactive virtual classes, analysis of contextual theological documents, discussion forum debates, real case studies, and the development of applied reflection projects. Critical thinking, respect for cultural diversity, and the integration of faith with transformative action in various environments will be encouraged.

Assessment:

  • Critical analysis essays on models of contextual theology.
  • Case studies on experiences of theological contextualization.
  • Written examinations assessing theoretical and practical understanding.
  • Active participation in discussion forums and academic debates.
  • Final project: development of a contextual theological proposal for a specific community.

Course Significance:

Contextual Theology equips students to integrate their faith with the cultural, social, and political realities around them. This course fosters theological reflection committed to the transformation of society, developing leaders who interpret and live out their faith in a relevant, ethical, and compassionate manner in a diverse and ever-changing world.
