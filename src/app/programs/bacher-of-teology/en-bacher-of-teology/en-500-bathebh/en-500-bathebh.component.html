BATHE-BH-500 - Biblical Hermeneutics

This course explores the fundamental principles and methods of Scripture interpretation, providing critical tools for the proper reading and understanding of the biblical text. Students will learn to recognize the historical, literary, and cultural contexts in which biblical texts were written, fostering responsible interpretation and relevant application to contemporary life in diverse and global settings.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the basic principles of biblical hermeneutics.
  • Identify the various literary genres present in the Bible and their influence on interpretation.
  • Analyze the historical, cultural, and linguistic contexts of biblical texts.
  • Apply appropriate hermeneutical methods to interpret complex biblical passages.
  • Develop critical skills to evaluate different interpretations of the same text.
  • Apply biblical teachings pertinently and ethically in todayâ€™s world.

Main Topics:

  • Introduction to hermeneutics: concept, history, and significance.
  • Basic principles of biblical interpretation.
  • Literary genres in the Bible and their hermeneutical implications.
  • Historical and cultural contextualization of biblical texts.
  • Classical and contemporary hermeneutical methods.
  • Common interpretative errors and how to avoid them.
  • Application of biblical interpretation to personal, communal, and social life.
  • Hermeneutics in dialogue with contemporary challenges: multiculturalism, social justice, and global ethics.

Methodology:

The course will be conducted through virtual lectures, practical interpretation workshops, analysis of selected passages, online discussion forums, and hermeneutical contextualization exercises. Active participation, critical thinking, respect for interpretive diversity, and autonomous learning will be encouraged through practical cases and contemporary application projects.

Assessment:

  • Hermeneutical analysis essays on biblical texts.
  • Written examinations on principles and methods of interpretation.
  • Participation in academic debates and discussion forums.
  • Presentations of hermeneutical case studies.
  • Final project: applied interpretation addressing a contemporary issue.

Course Significance:

Biblical Hermeneutics is fundamental for every theology student, as it provides the essential tools for responsible and relevant interpretation of the Scriptures. This course strengthens critical capacity, fosters contextualized reading, and prepares students to apply the biblical message meaningfully in diverse cultural environments, promoting ethical, conscious, and globally committed ministerial practice.
