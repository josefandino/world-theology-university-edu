BATHE-CE-600 - Christian Ethics

This course studies moral principles from a biblical and theological perspective, providing a solid foundation for comprehensive ethical reflection. It addresses key concepts such as good and evil, justice, human dignity, and the process of ethical decision-making in Christian life, while considering contemporary challenges and promoting an ethical practice committed to human dignity and social transformation.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the biblical and theological foundations of Christian ethics.
  • Analyze essential concepts such as good, evil, justice, and human dignity.
  • Evaluate different ethical theories and their relationship to Christian ethics.
  • Develop criteria for ethical decision-making grounded in biblical principles.
  • Apply Christian ethical principles to contemporary situations in personal, social, and ministerial life.
  • Foster an ethical commitment that promotes justice, peace, and respect for life in all its dimensions.

Main Topics:

  • Introduction to ethics: definition, history, and major schools of thought.
  • Biblical and theological foundations of Christian ethics.
  • The nature of good and evil from a biblical perspective.
  • Justice as a central ethical principle in Scripture.
  • Human dignity and fundamental rights in Christian ethics.
  • Ethical discernment and decision-making in Christian life.
  • Christian ethics and contemporary challenges: bioethics, social justice, environmental stewardship, and human rights.
  • The ethics of the Kingdom of God: love, mercy, and social responsibility.

Methodology:

The course will be delivered through virtual lectures, the study of contemporary ethical cases, analysis of biblical and theological texts, discussion forums, and applied reflection projects. Collaborative learning, critical thinking, and ethical argumentation in multicultural and complex contexts will be encouraged.

Assessment:

  • Critical essays on ethical principles and their practical application.
  • Case studies on current ethical dilemmas.
  • Written examinations assessing understanding of fundamental concepts.
  • Participation in ethical discussion forums.
  • Final project: an ethical proposal applied to a contemporary social context.

Course Significance:

Christian Ethics is crucial in the formation of leaders committed to integrity, justice, and social responsibility. It enables students to develop a strong ethical framework based on biblical principles, equipping them for conscious and transformative Christian action in a pluralistic and challenging world. This course strengthens ethical discernment and action across personal, community, and global spheres.
