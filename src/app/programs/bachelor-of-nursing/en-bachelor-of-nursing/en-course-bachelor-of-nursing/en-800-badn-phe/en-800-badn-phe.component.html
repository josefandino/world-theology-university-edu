BADN-PHE-800 – Public Health and Epidemiology I

Intensive 105-hour course (30 synchronous, 45 asynchronous, 30 applied work) for the Bachelor of Science in Nursing. Introduces the foundations of public health and epidemiology, addressing the social determinants of health, levels of prevention (primary, secondary, tertiary), and the analysis of population health indicators. Explores the relationship between health policies, healthcare systems, and community practice, preparing students to design, implement, and evaluate interventions with an impact on both local and global health. The course emphasizes the integration of intercultural and evidence-based approaches, as well as understanding disease burden, epidemiological surveillance, and health promotion as core pillars of community-centered care.

  • Purpose and scope: Provide students with the knowledge and skills to analyze population health status, interpret epidemiological indicators, and apply preventive and health promotion strategies that improve health outcomes, considering social, cultural, environmental, and economic factors.
  • Workload and modality: 105 total hours (30h live classes, 45h self-directed study, 30h applied work and data analysis). Online modality with interactive lectures, practical exercises, community case studies, and personalized mentoring.
  • Learning outcomes (measurable):
    1) Upon completion of this course, the student will be able to describe the social determinants of health and their impact on population well-being with ≥90% accuracy .
    2) Apply the levels of prevention in community cases and programs with performance ≥4/5 .
    3) Interpret health indicators (mortality, morbidity, life expectancy) using official sources with ≥90% accuracy .
    4) Analyze health policies and plans to propose evidence-based community interventions .
    5) Design a community intervention proposal with a preventive focus aligned with local and global health goals .
    6) Evaluate the impact of an intervention using epidemiological indicators and sustainability criteria .
  • Main topics (module sequence): M1 Fundamentals of public health; M2 Social determinants of health; M3 Levels of prevention and health promotion strategies; M4 Health indicators and data sources; M5 Health systems and policies; M6 Basic epidemiology and surveillance methods; M7 Analysis of disease burden; M8 Design and evaluation of community interventions.
  • Educational methodology: Interactive lectures; analysis of epidemiological data; case studies of community interventions; surveillance simulations; collaborative program design; mentoring with personalized feedback.
  • Assessment system: Data analysis and indicator interpretation (25%); Critical essay on social determinants and health policies (20%); Community intervention project (30%); Participation in forums and practical activities (15%); Quizzes and follow-up exercises (10%).
  • Resources and tools: LMS with access to epidemiological databases (WHO, PAHO, Ministries of Health); national and international health policy and plan guides; intervention design templates; basic data analysis software.
  • Professional relevance and application: Essential for community nursing and public health practice, enabling students to understand and address health problems from a population perspective and contribute to the design of effective policies and programs. Strengthens leadership in the implementation of prevention and promotion strategies with impact at both local and global levels.
