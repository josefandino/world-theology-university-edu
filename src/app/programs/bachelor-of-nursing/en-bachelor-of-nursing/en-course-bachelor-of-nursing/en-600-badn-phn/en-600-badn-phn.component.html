BADN-PhN-600 – Pharmacology for Nursing I

Intensive 105-hour course (30 synchronous, 45 asynchronous, 30 laboratory/simulation) for the Bachelor of Science in Nursing. Introduces the principles of pharmacokinetics (absorption, distribution, metabolism, excretion) and pharmacodynamics (mechanisms of action, therapeutic and adverse effects) as applied to clinical practice. Emphasizes medication safety through mastery of dosage calculation, application of the “rights” of administration (patient, drug, dose, route, time, indication, documentation, response, and education), and medication reconciliation. Aligned with safe practice recommendations from the Institute for Healthcare Improvement (IHI) and hospital standards, it integrates simulation of administration, alert management, and error prevention, strengthening professional responsibility and the patient safety culture.

  • Purpose and scope: Provide a solid foundation in applied pharmacology for nursing, ensuring that professionals can administer medications safely, accurately, and knowledgeably, minimizing risks and improving therapeutic effectiveness.
  • Workload and modality: 105 total hours (30h live classes with experts, 45h self-directed study, 30h practice and simulation). Online modality with dosage calculation workshops, administration simulations, case analysis of medication errors, and medication reconciliation in simulated hospital environments.
  • Learning outcomes (measurable):
    1) Upon completion of this course, the student will be able to explain principles of pharmacokinetics and pharmacodynamics in relation to nursing practice with ≥90% accuracy .
    2) Upon completion of this course, the student will be able to perform dosage calculations for different presentations and routes achieving ≥95% accuracy .
    3) Upon completion of this course, the student will be able to apply the “rights” of medication administration in clinical simulations with checklist ≥90% .
    4) Upon completion of this course, the student will be able to carry out medication reconciliation processes in simulated cases following hospital protocols .
    5) Upon completion of this course, the student will be able to identify adverse reactions and side effects to act according to safety protocols .
    6) Upon completion of this course, the student will be able to document completely and accurately the administration and observed effects in simulated clinical records .
  • Main topics (module sequence): M1 Principles of pharmacology and terminology; M2 Pharmacokinetics: absorption, distribution, metabolism, excretion; M3 Pharmacodynamics: mechanisms of action and effects; M4 Dosage calculation and conversions; M5 Rights of administration and clinical application; M6 Medication reconciliation and error prevention; M7 Pharmacovigilance and adverse event reporting; M8 Administration simulation and case analysis.
  • Educational methodology: Interactive master lectures; practical dosage calculation workshops; clinical simulations of safe administration; role-play for medication reconciliation; incident and near-miss analysis; virtual lab with safety checklists; personalized mentoring with feedback.
  • Assessment system: Theoretical exam on pharmacological principles (25%); Practical test on dosage calculation and administration (30%); Medication reconciliation project in a clinical case (25%); Participation in simulations, forums, and case analyses (20%).
  • Resources and tools: LMS with pharmacology guides and manuals; dosage calculators and simulators; hospital safety protocols; templates for clinical record documentation; procedural videos; checklist of medication “rights.”
  • Professional relevance and application: Essential for all clinical areas of nursing, reduces medication errors, strengthens the safety culture, and improves quality of care. Prepares students to administer medications safely, knowledgeably, and ethically in hospitals, clinics, and community settings.
