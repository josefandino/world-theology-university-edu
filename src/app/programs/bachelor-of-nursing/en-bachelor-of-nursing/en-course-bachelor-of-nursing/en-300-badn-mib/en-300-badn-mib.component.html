BADN-MIB-300 – Microbiology, Immunology, and Biosafety

Intensive 105-hour course (30 synchronous, 45 asynchronous, 30 laboratory/simulation) for the Bachelor of Science in Nursing. Covers the main groups of pathogens (bacteria, viruses, fungi, parasites), transmission routes, and the foundations of innate and adaptive immune responses, with clinical correlation for decision-making in infection prevention and control (IPC). Integrates WHO/CDC guidelines and essential biosafety practices: hand hygiene (5 moments), correct use of PPE (donning and doffing), standard and transmission-based precautions (contact, droplet, airborne), cleaning–disinfection–sterilization (Spaulding classification), waste and sharps management, specimen handling, and healthcare-associated infection (HAI) surveillance. Through basic simulation, guided audits, and case studies, it strengthens critical competencies for patient safety, staff protection, and quality of care in intercultural and digital contexts (simulated EHR, checklists, and debriefing).

  • Purpose and scope: Establish a solid microbiological–immunological foundation to support safe decision-making in clinical environments, standardizing IPC practices aligned with WHO/CDC and a patient safety culture (QSEN) to reduce HAI and occupational transmission risks.
  • Workload and modality: 105 total hours (30h live sessions with demonstrations, 45h guided study and literature analysis, 30h laboratory/OSCE-lite simulation). Online modality with PPE donning/doffing role-play, hand hygiene audits, outbreak simulators, and structured debriefing.
  • Learning outcomes (measurable):
    1) Upon completion of this course, the student will be able to identify pathogens and transmission routes to explain chains of infection in clinical discussion with ≥90% accuracy .
    2) Upon completion of this course, the student will be able to explain innate/adaptive immune responses relating them to signs, vaccines, and tests meeting rubric ≥4/5 .
    3) Upon completion of this course, the student will be able to apply hand hygiene and PPE (donning/doffing) in simulation achieving WHO/CDC checklist ≥90% .
    4) Upon completion of this course, the student will be able to implement standard and transmission-based precautions in case scenarios with performance ≥4/5 .
    5) Upon completion of this course, the student will be able to execute cleaning, disinfection, and sterilization according to Spaulding in laboratory passing OSCE ≥80% .
    6) Upon completion of this course, the student will be able to manage specimen collection/transport, waste, and sharps exposure in compliance with regulations meeting rubric ≥4/5 .
    7) Upon completion of this course, the student will be able to analyze basic HAI and antimicrobial resistance indicators to propose improvement actions technical presentation ≥4/5 .
    8) Upon completion of this course, the student will be able to document incidents and IPC measures in simulated EHR with ≥90% accuracy .
  • Main topics (module sequence): M1 Principles of microbiology and chain of infection; M2 Pathogens and mechanisms of pathogenicity; M3 Fundamentals of clinical immunology (innate/adaptive, vaccines, serology); M4 Standard and transmission-based precautions; M5 Hand hygiene and PPE (practical workshop); M6 Cleaning, disinfection, and sterilization (Spaulding, environment, and equipment); M7 Waste, sharps, exposure management, and prophylaxis; M8 HAI surveillance, outbreaks, and antimicrobial resistance (AMR) with case analysis; M9 Biosafety and risk assessment in the clinical setting; M10 Integrated simulation and debriefing.
  • Educational methodology: Synchronous classes with IPC experts; case studies and mini-outbreak simulations; guided practice in hand hygiene and PPE with checklist evaluation; simulated audits in virtual clinical environments; EHR documentation lab; mentoring and continuous feedback.
  • Assessment system: Skills OSCE (hand hygiene, PPE, Spaulding) 30%; Local IPC/HAI surveillance plan project 30%; Critical essay (ethics, biosafety, and safety culture) 20%; Participation in forums, audits, and debriefings 20%. Formative assessments: quizzes, self-audits, and checklists (non-summative).
  • Resources and tools: LMS and digital library; WHO/CDC guidelines; posters and checklists (5 moments of hand hygiene, PPE); fluorescence kits for technique verification; incident report templates; outbreak simulators and HAI indicators.
  • Professional relevance and application: Core to patient safety and occupational protection competencies; foundation for Adult Nursing, Community Health, Epidemiology, and Quality Management. Improves clinical outcomes, reduces HAI, and strengthens IPC leadership in hospital and community settings with intercultural sensitivity.
