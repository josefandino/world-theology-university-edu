BADN-TCHR-400 – Therapeutic Communication and Helping Relationship

Intensive 105-hour course (30 synchronous, 45 asynchronous, 30 practice/simulation) for the Bachelor of Science in Nursing. Develops skills in active listening, clinical interviewing, and the effective use of the SBAR model for interprofessional communication in healthcare settings. Integrates cultural competence and communication sensitive to the patient’s spiritual context, strengthening empathy, the therapeutic bond, and the ability to foster trust. The course emphasizes adapting language and communication strategies to the cultural, emotional, and spiritual characteristics of each individual, improving treatment adherence, patient safety, and the overall care experience.

  • Purpose and scope: Train professionals capable of establishing strong and effective therapeutic relationships by applying clinical communication tools that foster interprofessional collaboration, conflict resolution, and shared decision-making, always grounded in respect, ethics, and empathy.
  • Workload and modality: 105 total hours (30h live sessions, 45h self-directed study, 30h case practice and simulation). Online modality with practical activities, role-play, interview recording and analysis, and personalized mentoring.
  • Learning outcomes (measurable):
    1) Upon completion of this course, the student will be able to apply active listening and clinical interviewing techniques in simulated contexts achieving ≥4/5 on performance rubric .
    2) Upon completion of this course, the student will be able to use the SBAR model to convey precise and structured clinical information in interprofessional communication achieving ≥90% on checklist .
    3) Upon completion of this course, the student will be able to integrate cultural competence strategies in interactions with patients and families demonstrating cultural sensitivity ≥4/5 .
    4) Upon completion of this course, the student will be able to adapt communication to the patient’s emotional and spiritual needs in clinical scenarios with score ≥4/5 .
    5) Upon completion of this course, the student will be able to evaluate communication barriers and propose solutions to optimize treatment adherence in case studies .
    6) Upon completion of this course, the student will be able to document relevant therapeutic interactions in the clinical record meeting legal and quality criteria ≥90% .
  • Main topics (module sequence): M1 Fundamentals of therapeutic communication and helping relationship; M2 Active listening and clinical empathy; M3 Interview techniques (structured, semi-structured, open); M4 Interprofessional communication and SBAR model; M5 Cultural competence and communication adaptation; M6 Communication in contexts of spirituality and holistic care; M7 Strategies to improve adherence and patient experience; M8 Case simulation and debriefing.
  • Educational methodology: Interactive lectures; supervised practice in interviewing and SBAR; role-play and clinical simulation with immediate feedback; recording analysis; discussion of real and intercultural cases; personalized mentoring and formative feedback.
  • Assessment system: Practical evaluation of clinical interviewing and active listening (30%); SBAR model demonstration in simulated scenario (25%); Critical essay on intercultural communication and spirituality (20%); Active participation in forums, role-play, and debriefing (15%); Follow-up quizzes and self-assessments (10%).
  • Resources and tools: LMS with audiovisual materials and guides; clinical interview and SBAR rubrics; case simulators; cultural competence guides; templates for clinical record documentation.
  • Professional relevance and application: Essential across all areas of nursing and healthcare, enhances patient safety, optimizes team coordination, and fosters a positive user experience. Strengthens leadership in clinical environments, collaborative decision-making, and comprehensive care that respects cultural and spiritual diversity.
