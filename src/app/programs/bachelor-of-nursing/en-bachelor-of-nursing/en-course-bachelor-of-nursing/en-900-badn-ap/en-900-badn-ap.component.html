BADN-AP-900 – Anatomy and Physiology II

Intensive 105-hour course (30 synchronous, 45 asynchronous, 30 laboratory/simulation) for the Bachelor of Science in Nursing. Provides an in-depth study of the endocrine, renal, digestive, and reproductive systems, with emphasis on pathophysiological correlation and the anatomical–functional integration required for clinical decision-making. Addresses the structure and function of organs and tissues, as well as regulatory mechanisms and responses to alterations, to support care prioritization and patient education. Combines theoretical foundations, clinical case analysis, and practical activities that promote the application of knowledge in hospital and community settings, with a preventive and health promotion approach.

  • Purpose and scope: Consolidate and expand the anatomical and physiological knowledge acquired in Anatomy and Physiology I, integrating common pathophysiological processes affecting the systems studied, to enhance assessment, prioritization, and nursing care planning skills.
  • Workload and modality: 105 total hours (30h live classes with experts, 45h self-directed study, 30h laboratory and simulation). Online modality with interactive resources, 3D atlases, imaging studies, and clinical simulations.
  • Learning outcomes (measurable):
    1) Upon completion of this course, the student will be able to describe the anatomy and physiology of the endocrine, renal, digestive, and reproductive systems with ≥90% accuracy .
    2) Analyze hormonal regulation, fluid–electrolyte balance, digestion, and reproduction in relation to clinical practice .
    3) Identify common pathophysiological alterations in the systems studied relating them to signs and symptoms .
    4) Apply anatomical–functional integration to prioritize care in simulated scenarios achieving performance ≥4/5 .
    5) Educate patients and families about functions, preventive care, and management of common conditions.
    6) Interpret basic related diagnostic tests (hormones, renal function, digestive and reproductive studies) to support decision-making .
  • Main topics (module sequence): M1 Endocrine system: glands, hormones, and regulation; M2 Common endocrine pathophysiology; M3 Renal system: anatomy, physiology, and fluid–electrolyte balance; M4 Common renal pathologies; M5 Digestive system: anatomy, physiology, and nutrient absorption; M6 Common digestive pathologies; M7 Male and female reproductive systems; M8 Reproductive disorders and sexual health; M9 Clinical integration and care prioritization; M10 Patient and family education.
  • Educational methodology: Interactive lectures; clinical case analysis; practice in virtual lab and simulation; use of 3D anatomical atlas; interpretation of diagnostic studies; collaborative work and personalized mentoring.
  • Assessment system: Theoretical–practical exam on anatomy and physiology (30%); Clinical case analysis with pathophysiological correlation (25%); Clinical simulation evaluation and care prioritization (25%); Participation in forums and practical activities (10%); Quizzes and follow-up exercises (10%).
  • Resources and tools: LMS with interactive anatomical atlas; study guides; clinical case bank; physiological simulators; imaging resources (ultrasound, CT, MRI); patient education templates.
  • Professional relevance and application: Essential for strengthening clinical reasoning, decision-making, and patient education skills in key areas of comprehensive health. Prepares students to competently address common health problems in the systems studied and integrate them into nursing care plans.
