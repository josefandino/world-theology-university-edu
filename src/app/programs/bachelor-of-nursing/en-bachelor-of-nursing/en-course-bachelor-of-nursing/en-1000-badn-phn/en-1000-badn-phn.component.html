BADN-PhN-1000 – Pharmacology for Nursing II

Intensive 105-hour course (30 synchronous, 45 asynchronous, 30 laboratory/simulation) for the Bachelor of Science in Nursing. Reviews the main high-prevalence therapeutic groups in clinical practice (analgesics, antimicrobials, antihypertensives, antidiabetics, anticoagulants, among others) and the management of high-alert medications according to international lists (e.g., ISMP). Develops competencies for active monitoring of adverse reactions, detection of drug interactions, and patient and family education in both chronic and acute care contexts. The course emphasizes the application of pharmacovigilance protocols, accurate clinical documentation, and interprofessional communication to ensure patient safety.

  • Purpose and scope: Strengthen the student's pharmacological knowledge of the most frequently used therapeutic groups and the safe handling of high-alert medications, promoting evidence-based clinical practice and the prevention of medication errors.
  • Workload and modality: 105 total hours (30h live master classes, 45h self-directed study and guideline analysis, 30h clinical laboratory/simulation). Online modality with practical workshops, safe administration simulations, and patient education activities.
  • Learning outcomes (measurable):
    1) Upon completion of this course, the student will be able to describe the pharmacology of the main high-prevalence therapeutic groups with ≥90% accuracy .
    2) Identify high-alert medications and apply specific safety measures in clinical simulations with checklist ≥90% .
    3) Detect and document adverse reactions and drug interactions following pharmacovigilance protocols .
    4) Educate patients and their families on the safe use of medications in acute and chronic contexts .
    5) Evaluate the effectiveness of pharmacological treatment using clinical and laboratory criteria .
    6) Collaborate with the interdisciplinary team to optimize pharmacological therapy .
  • Main topics (module sequence): M1 Principles of pharmacotherapy and pharmacovigilance; M2 Analgesics and anti-inflammatories; M3 Antimicrobials and bacterial resistance; M4 Antihypertensives and cardiovascular disease management; M5 Oral antidiabetics and insulin; M6 Anticoagulants and antiplatelet agents; M7 Management of high-alert medications (ISMP); M8 Patient education and therapeutic adherence; M9 Documentation and reporting of adverse reactions; M10 Integrated clinical simulation.
  • Educational methodology: Interactive master lectures; dosage calculation and adjustment workshops; safe administration simulations; real case analysis of adverse reactions; patient education role-play; use of updated clinical guidelines and hospital protocols.
  • Assessment system: Theoretical exam on applied pharmacology (25%); Practical assessment of administration and medication safety (30%); Pharmacovigilance project and case report (25%); Participation in forums, simulations, and case analyses (20%).
  • Resources and tools: LMS with clinical guidelines and protocols; high-alert medication lists (ISMP, WHO); dosage calculators; patient education templates; software for recording and reporting adverse events.
  • Professional relevance and application: Essential for ensuring the safe and effective management of the most commonly used medications in nursing practice, reducing medication errors, improving therapeutic adherence, and optimizing clinical outcomes. Prepares students to lead patient safety processes in both hospital and community settings.
