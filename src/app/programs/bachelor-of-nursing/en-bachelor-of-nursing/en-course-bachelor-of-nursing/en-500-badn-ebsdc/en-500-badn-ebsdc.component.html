BADN-EBSDC-500 – Ethics, Bioethics, and the Spiritual Dimension of Care

Intensive 105-hour course (30 synchronous, 45 asynchronous, 30 analysis and simulation) for the Bachelor of Science in Nursing. Examines ethical and bioethical principles applied to clinical practice, patient rights, informed consent, confidentiality, and professional duty. Analyzes real dilemmas related to healthcare, exploring the role of ethics committees and their influence on decision-making. Integrates the spiritual dimension as an essential component of person-centered care, combining clinical excellence with respect for human dignity and cultural sensitivity. The approach blends theory, critical analysis, and practical application, preparing students to address complex ethical challenges in diverse healthcare settings.

  • Purpose and scope: Develop competencies for identifying, analyzing, and resolving ethical and bioethical dilemmas in nursing practice, applying universal principles and national and international regulatory frameworks, incorporating respect for the cultural and spiritual diversity of patients.
  • Workload and modality: 105 total hours (30h live classes with clinical ethics experts, 45h self-directed study, 30h case analysis and simulations). Online modality with lectures, moderated debates, review of real cases, simulation of ethics committee meetings, and guided reflection on the spiritual dimension of care.
  • Learning outcomes (measurable):
    1) Upon completion of this course, the student will be able to identify bioethical principles (autonomy, beneficence, nonmaleficence, justice) and apply them to clinical cases with ≥90% accuracy .
    2) Upon completion of this course, the student will be able to analyze legislation and regulations related to patient rights, informed consent, and confidentiality in national and international contexts achieving rubric ≥4/5 .
    3) Upon completion of this course, the student will be able to evaluate complex ethical dilemmas through structured discussion demonstrating ethical reasoning ≥4/5 .
    4) Upon completion of this course, the student will be able to participate actively in ethics committee simulations applying formal procedures and evidence-based argumentation.
    5) Upon completion of this course, the student will be able to integrate the spiritual dimension of care in patient assessment and support respecting the patient’s beliefs and values .
    6) Upon completion of this course, the student will be able to design ethical and bioethical strategies for problem-solving in clinical scenarios ensuring respect for human dignity .
  • Main topics (module sequence): M1 Principles of ethics and bioethics applied to health; M2 Patient rights and legal framework; M3 Informed consent and confidentiality; M4 Common ethical dilemmas in nursing; M5 Role and functioning of ethics committees; M6 Spiritual dimension in care and its ethical integration; M7 Tools for ethical decision-making; M8 Simulation of ethical dilemma analysis and resolution.
  • Educational methodology: Interactive master lectures; analysis of real and simulated cases; structured debates; ethics committee simulations; guided reflection on spirituality and care; review of regulatory documents; personalized mentoring with individual feedback.
  • Assessment system: Critical essay on a real ethical dilemma (25%); Participation in ethics committee simulation (25%); Theoretical exam on bioethical principles and regulations (20%); Integrative case analysis with spiritual dimension (20%); Participation in forums and debates (10%).
  • Resources and tools: LMS with access to codes of ethics and bioethics guidelines; ethical analysis rubrics; videos and interactive simulations; assessment guides for the spiritual dimension; documentation templates for ethics committees.
  • Professional relevance and application: Essential for the responsible practice of nursing in any setting, ensuring ethical and safe decision-making, promoting the defense of patient rights, and strengthening the quality and humanity of care. Prepares students to lead clinical ethics processes and contribute to a culture of respect and dignity in healthcare services.
