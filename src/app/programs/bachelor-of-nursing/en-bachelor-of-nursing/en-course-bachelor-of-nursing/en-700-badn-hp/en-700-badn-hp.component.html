BADN-HP-700 – Health Psychology and the Life Cycle

Intensive 105-hour course (30 synchronous, 45 asynchronous, 30 practice/application) for the Bachelor of Science in Nursing. Explores the fundamentals of health psychology and its application in supporting individuals throughout the life cycle, from childhood to old age. Addresses coping with illness, adherence to treatment, and health education as key strategies to improve clinical outcomes and promote overall well-being. Emphasizes the design and implementation of evidence-based psychoeducational interventions, integrating the emotional, social, cultural, and spiritual dimensions of care, with a person- and family-centered perspective.

  • Purpose and scope: Train students to understand the psychological factors influencing health and illness, identify barriers to therapeutic adherence, and apply communication and patient education strategies adapted to each stage of the life cycle.
  • Workload and modality: 105 total hours (30h live classes, 45h study and case analysis, 30h practice and intervention design). Online modality with interactive classes, simulations, debates, study reviews, and personalized mentoring.
  • Learning outcomes (measurable):
    1) Upon completion of this course, the student will be able to explain principles of health psychology and their relation to nursing practice with ≥90% accuracy .
    2) Upon completion of this course, the student will be able to identify psychological and social factors affecting health at each stage of the life cycle achieving rubric ≥4/5 .
    3) Upon completion of this course, the student will be able to apply coping and motivational strategies in clinical and community contexts with performance ≥4/5 .
    4) Upon completion of this course, the student will be able to design evidence-based psychoeducational interventions to improve adherence and self-care .
    5) Upon completion of this course, the student will be able to evaluate the effectiveness of educational interventions using clinical and patient satisfaction indicators .
    6) Upon completion of this course, the student will be able to integrate the cultural and spiritual dimensions into interventions for inclusive, person-centered care .
  • Main topics (module sequence): M1 Fundamentals of health psychology; M2 Human development and the life cycle; M3 Coping and resilience strategies; M4 Treatment adherence: factors and improvement techniques; M5 Health education and behavior change; M6 Design and application of psychoeducational interventions; M7 Evaluation of clinical and psychosocial outcomes; M8 Cultural and spiritual integration in care.
  • Educational methodology: Interactive lectures; real and simulated case studies; role-play of motivational interviews; educational program design; collaborative group work; personalized mentoring with ongoing feedback.
  • Assessment system: Critical essay on health psychology and the life cycle (25%); Psychoeducational intervention project (30%); Practical evaluation of motivational interviewing (20%); Participation in forums and debates (15%); Quizzes and follow-up activities (10%).
  • Resources and tools: LMS with evidence-based articles and guides; psychological assessment scales and questionnaires; educational program design templates; best-practice videos; evaluation rubrics.
  • Professional relevance and application: Essential for delivering comprehensive care that considers the psychological dimension of health, improving therapeutic adherence, coping with illness, and enhancing patient quality of life at all stages of the life cycle. Strengthens the nursing role as an educator and health promoter.
