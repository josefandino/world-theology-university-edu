BADN-CSHA-11000 – Semiology and Clinical Assessment

Intensive 105-hour course (30 synchronous, 45 asynchronous, 30 practice/simulation) for the Bachelor of Science in Nursing. Provides systematic training in the competencies required to conduct an effective clinical interview, perform a system-based physical examination, and record findings in standardized clinical formats, integrating legal and quality criteria into documentation. Consolidates the ability to collect and organize relevant data to formulate accurate nursing diagnoses (NANDA-I), plan care based on NIC interventions, and evaluate outcomes using NOC indicators, thereby strengthening clinical judgment and safe decision-making.

  • Purpose and scope: Prepare students to conduct comprehensive clinical assessments, establish evidence-based nursing diagnoses, and develop person-centered care plans that address physical, psychosocial, cultural, and spiritual aspects.
  • Workload and modality: 105 total hours (30h live classes, 45h self-directed study, 30h laboratory practice and clinical simulation). Online modality with interview workshops, physical examination labs, and practice in simulated electronic health records (EHR).
  • Learning outcomes (measurable):
    1) Upon completion of this course, the student will be able to conduct complete clinical interviews following structured guidelines with rubric ≥4/5 .
    2) Perform a system-based physical examination using inspection, palpation, percussion, and auscultation techniques with checklist ≥90% .
    3) Record clinical findings in standardized formats and EHR meeting legal and quality criteria .
    4) Integrate subjective and objective data to formulate NANDA-I nursing diagnoses .
    5) Develop care plans based on NIC interventions and NOC outcomes aligned with clinical priorities .
    6) Evaluate the effectiveness of care using outcome indicators and make adjustments according to patient progress .
  • Main topics (module sequence): M1 Principles of semiology and clinical communication; M2 Structure and techniques of the nursing interview; M3 General and system-based physical examination (cardiovascular, respiratory, neurological, musculoskeletal, gastrointestinal, genitourinary, skin and appendages); M4 Clinical documentation: SOAP and DAR formats, EHR, and legal criteria; M5 Interpretation and integration of findings; M6 Formulation of nursing diagnoses (NANDA-I); M7 Development of care plans (NIC/NOC); M8 Outcome indicators and evaluation of care effectiveness; M9 Integrated clinical simulation and debriefing.
  • Educational methodology: Interactive lectures; supervised interview and physical examination practice; clinical simulations with standardized patients; simulated EHR use for documentation; case analysis; individualized mentoring with formative feedback.
  • Assessment system: OSCE for clinical interview and physical examination (35%); Case documentation in EHR (20%); Care plan project with NANDA-I diagnoses and NOC outcomes (25%); Participation in forums, simulations, and debriefings (20%).
  • Resources and tools: LMS with interview and physical examination guides; SOAP and DAR templates; simulated EHR; performance checklists and rubrics; clinical case bank; updated bibliography on NANDA-I, NIC, and NOC.
  • Professional relevance and application: Core to the nursing process, enabling comprehensive and accurate assessment, improving care planning and effectiveness, optimizing communication within the healthcare team, and enhancing patient safety. Serves as a foundation for all clinical areas and nursing specialties.
