BADN-FNPCC-100 – Fundamentals of Nursing and Person-Centered Care

Intensive 105-hour course (30 synchronous, 45 asynchronous, 30 practice/simulation) for the Bachelor of Science in Nursing, introducing nursing models and theories, the professional role, and the principles of safety and quality of care. Aligned with the standards of the International Council of Nurses (ICN) and QSEN competencies, it develops person-centered care with a biopsychosocial and spiritual approach, effective therapeutic communication, and standardized clinical documentation (SOAP/DAR and simulated EHR use). Through case studies and basic simulation, students integrate humanistic values, cultural competence (attention to diversity and interculturality), and ethical discernment for real-world clinical practice in contemporary health care systems.

  • Purpose and scope: Establish the professional foundations of nursing around person-centered care, integrating nursing theories (Nightingale, Orem, Roy, Watson, Pender), ethics and the ICN code, patient safety and quality culture (QSEN), SBAR communication, clinical documentation, and initial use of the electronic health record (EHR) in simulated settings.
  • Workload and modality: 105 total hours (30h synchronous classes with experts, 45h guided/asynchronous study, 30h simulation/OSCE-lite and skills lab). 100% online modality with live sessions, guided practical activities, and one-on-one mentoring with formative feedback.
  • Learning outcomes (measurable format):
    1) Upon completion of this course, the student will be able to analyze nursing models and theories to support person-centered care in basic care units according to ICN/QSEN (rubric ≥4/5) .
    2) Upon completion of this course, the student will be able to apply patient safety measures (identification, hand hygiene, fall prevention) in simulated scenarios meeting QSEN checklist ≥90% .
    3) Upon completion of this course, the student will be able to document assessment and care plans in SOAP/DAR formats and simulated EHR with ≥90% accuracy according to rubric .
    4) Upon completion of this course, the student will be able to integrate therapeutic communication and SBAR in patient-team interactions maintaining performance ≥4/5 .
    5) Upon completion of this course, the student will be able to assess biopsychosocial and spiritual needs using FICA/HOPE during initial admission demonstrating cultural competence ≥4/5 .
    6) Upon completion of this course, the student will be able to design a care plan for prevalent conditions (e.g., acute pain) in primary care settings aligned with NANDA-I, NOC, and NIC .
    7) Upon completion of this course, the student will be able to demonstrate aseptic technique, infection control, and vital signs measurement in the skills lab passing OSCE ≥80% .
    8) Upon completion of this course, the student will be able to critically reflect on professional ethics, boundaries, and self-care in high-demand settings proposing evidence-based strategies .
  • Main topics (module sequence): M1 Professional Identity and Ethics (ICN code, dignity, confidentiality); M2 Nursing Theories and Models (Orem, Roy, Watson, Pender) and Person-Centered Care; M3 Patient Safety and QSEN Competencies (safety culture, event prevention); M4 Initial Comprehensive Assessment (vital signs, pain, risks, prioritization); M5 Therapeutic Communication and Teamwork (active listening, SBAR, effective charting); M6 Clinical Documentation and Data Quality (SOAP/DAR, legal aspects, simulated EHR); M7 Spiritual Dimension and Cultural Competence (FICA/HOPE, referrals, professional limits); M8 Basic Simulation and Integrative Cases with Debriefing.
  • Educational methodology: Synchronous lectures with experts; demonstrations and supervised practice in basic simulation; contextualized clinical case studies; collaborative forums and role-play for therapeutic communication/SBAR; EHR simulated documentation lab; personalized mentoring with transparent rubrics and structured debriefing after each simulation.
  • Assessment system (rigor and variety): Critical essay on person-centered care and professional ethics (27%); Simulation + OSCE-lite (skills and QSEN safety checklists) (23%); Final project: NANDA-I/NOC/NIC care plan with complete documentation in simulated EHR and patient education (30%); Active participation in seminars, forums, and debriefings (20%). Formative assessments: short quizzes, self-assessments, and ongoing feedback (non-summative).
  • Resources and tools: LMS platform with digital library; simulated EHR for documentation practice; patient safety guides, QSEN competencies, and ICN code; SOAP/DAR templates; checklists for hand hygiene, identification, and fall prevention; performance rubrics and intercultural case banks.
  • Professional relevance and application: Foundational course in the clinical and quality/safety track of the Bachelor of Science in Nursing; prepares for courses in adult care, community health, leadership, and quality management. Directly applicable to practice in hospitals, clinics, and community programs, strengthening servant leadership, interprofessional communication, and holistic care with cultural sensitivity and attention to the spiritual dimension of care.
