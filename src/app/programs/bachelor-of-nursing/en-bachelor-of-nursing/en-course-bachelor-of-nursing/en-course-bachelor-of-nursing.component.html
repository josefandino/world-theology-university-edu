BADN-FNPCC-100 - Foundations of Nursing and Person-Centred Care: Introduces nursing models and theories, the professional role, and patient safety, aligned with ICN and QSEN standards. Through case studies and basic simulation, students integrate humanistic values and the spiritual dimension of care with clinical documentation and quality principles.

BADN-ANP-200 - Anatomy and Physiology I: Examines body organization, tissues, and the neuromuscular, cardiovascular, and respiratory systems with clinical correlation. Develops biomedical terminology and initial clinical judgment for assessment, building the foundation for practice scenarios and OSCE-style examinations.

BADN-MIB-300 - Microbiology, Immunology, and Biosafety: Addresses prevalent pathogens, routes of transmission, and immune response with emphasis on hand hygiene, PPE, and isolation. Integrates WHO/CDC guidelines and HAI surveillance, strengthening critical competencies in infection prevention and control.

BADN-TCHR-400 - Therapeutic Communication and Helping Relationship: Develops active listening, clinical interviewing, and SBAR for interprofessional teams. Integrates cultural competence and communication sensitive to spiritual context, improving adherence and patient experience.

BADN-EBSDC-500 - Ethics, Bioethics, and the Spiritual Dimension of Care: Studies bioethical principles, patients’ rights, informed consent, and confidentiality. Analyses real clinical dilemmas and the role of ethics committees, aligning clinical excellence with respect for human dignity.

BADN-PhN-600 - Pharmacology for Nursing I: Introduces pharmacokinetics, pharmacodynamics, and medication safety. Emphasises dose calculation, the “rights” of administration, and medication reconciliation, aligned with IHI-recommended safe practices and hospital standards.

BADN-HP-700 - Health Psychology and the Lifespan: Explores coping, adherence, and patient education across the lifespan. Promotes evidence-based psychoeducational interventions aimed at clinical outcomes and holistic well-being.

BADN-PHE-800 - Public Health and Epidemiology I: Introduces social determinants, levels of prevention, and population indicators. Links health policy with community practice, preparing for interventions with local and global impact.

BADN-AP-900 - Anatomy and Physiology II: Explores endocrine, renal, digestive, and reproductive systems with pathophysiological correlation. Reinforces anatomo-functional integration for care prioritisation and patient education.

BADN-PhN-1000 - Pharmacology for Nursing II: Reviews high-prevalence therapeutic classes and high-risk medications. Develops monitoring of adverse reactions and patient education in chronic and acute contexts.

BADN-CSHA-11000 - Clinical Semiology and Health Assessment: Trains history-taking, systems-based physical examination, and standardised clinical records. Consolidates data for nursing diagnoses and care plans with outcome indicators.
