BADN-ANP-200 – Anatomy and Physiology I

Intensive 105-hour course (30 synchronous, 45 asynchronous, 30 laboratory/simulation) for the Bachelor of Science in Nursing. Examines body organization, levels of organization (cells, tissues, organs, and systems), and provides in-depth study of the neuromuscular, cardiovascular, and respiratory systems with clinical correlation. Develops biomedical terminology, anatomical–functional reasoning, and initial judgment for assessment, preparing students for practice scenarios and OSCE-type examinations. The curriculum integrates patient safety, hygiene, and ergonomics, as well as basic examination skills (inspection, palpation, percussion, and auscultation) using digital resources (3D atlas, virtual microscopy, ECG simulators, and cardiopulmonary sound simulators) and brief documentation of findings in standardized clinical formats.

  • Purpose and scope: Build structural and functional understanding of the human body to support assessment and care decisions in Nursing I; emphasis on anatomical terminology, planes and axes, basic histology, introductory neurophysiology, muscle physiology, hemodynamics, and ventilatory mechanics, with application to common cases (chest pain, dyspnea, muscle weakness, syncope).
  • Workload and modality: 105 total hours (30h live classes with demonstrations, 45h guided/asynchronous study, 30h laboratory and simulation/OSCE-lite). Online modality with interactive sessions, guided practice in the virtual lab, and mentoring with formative feedback.
  • Learning outcomes (measurable):
    1) Upon completion of this course, the student will be able to identify body organization and anatomical terminology to describe structures and relationships in virtual laboratories with ≥90% accuracy in rubric .
    2) Upon completion of this course, the student will be able to analyze characteristics of epithelial, connective, muscle, and nervous tissues to correlate structure–function in virtual microscopy achieving ≥4/5 .
    3) Upon completion of this course, the student will be able to explain basic neurophysiology (action potential, synapse, reflex arcs) in relation to neuromuscular assessment meeting criteria ≥4/5 .
    4) Upon completion of this course, the student will be able to apply skeletal muscle physiology (contraction, fatigue) to interpret strength and tone in guided practice with checklist ≥85% .
    5) Upon completion of this course, the student will be able to assess cardiovascular parameters (cardiac cycle, BP, HR) using basic measurement techniques in the laboratory with OSCE ≥80% .
    6) Upon completion of this course, the student will be able to integrate respiratory physiology (ventilation, diffusion, and gas exchange) to interpret respiratory patterns in simulated cases with performance ≥4/5 .
    7) Upon completion of this course, the student will be able to correlate anatomical–functional findings with signs and symptoms in basic clinical scenarios using precise biomedical language .
    8) Upon completion of this course, the student will be able to document initial examination findings in abbreviated formats ensuring clarity and patient safety according to rubric ≥90% .
  • Main topics (module sequence): M1 Body organization and terminology; M2 Essential histology (epithelial, connective, muscle, nervous); M3 Homeostasis, membranes, and transport; M4 Neuromuscular system (neurons, synapses, reflexes, skeletal muscle); M5 Cardiovascular system (cardiac anatomy, cycle, BP and hemodynamics, perfusion); M6 Respiratory system (anatomy, volumes and capacities, ventilation/perfusion, regulation); M7 Integrated clinical correlation (chest pain, dyspnea, syncope, weakness); M8 Skills lab and OSCE preparation (vitals, basic cardiopulmonary auscultation, technique, and safety).
  • Educational methodology: Synchronous classes with demonstrations; virtual laboratories (3D atlas, microscopy, and digital histology); case studies with guiding questions; guided practice in measuring vital signs, BP, and auscultation; ECG and cardiopulmonary sound simulators; structured debriefing and personalized mentoring.
  • Assessment system: Integrative theoretical exams (30%); laboratory and guided practice reports (20%); OSCE/basic clinical stations (30%); participation in forums and case analysis (10%); follow-up quizzes (10%). Formative assessments: checklists, self-assessments, and continuous feedback (non-summative).
  • Resources and tools: LMS with digital library; 3D anatomical atlas; virtual microscopy; cardiopulmonary sound bank; ECG simulators and virtual sphygmomanometer; technique, safety, and ergonomics guides; rubrics and checklists for lab and OSCE.
  • Relevance and application: Essential foundation for Physiology II, Nursing Assessment, Adult Nursing, and Pathophysiology. Strengthens initial clinical reasoning, communication with the healthcare team, and preparation for practice scenarios and OSCE-type evaluations in real contexts.
