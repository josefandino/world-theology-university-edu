Bachelor's Degree in Nursing

The Bachelor’s Degree in Nursing at World Theology University is designed to educate professionals with strong scientific, humanistic, and ethical competencies, capable of delivering comprehensive, person-centered care across clinical and community settings. The program integrates foundational health sciences, evidence-based practice, and leadership in care delivery, with an intercultural perspective that values diversity and advances health equity on a global scale.

100% Online: Flexibility to Study from Anywhere

The program is delivered fully online, enabling you to study from anywhere in the world with a flexible structure that adapts to your personal and professional responsibilities. Through our virtual platform, you will access cutting-edge resources, case studies, academic simulations, and interactive tools that strengthen clinical decision-making and therapeutic communication.

Professional Opportunities

Upon completion of the Bachelor’s Degree in Nursing, you will be prepared to work in hospital care, primary care and community health, public health programs, care management and quality, health education, international cooperation, and applied research. You will be able to join interprofessional teams to address contemporary challenges such as population aging, chronic diseases, mental health, and responses to public health emergencies. Note: requirements for professional practice and nursing licensure/registration vary by country and competent authorities; obtaining specific credentials depends on local regulations.

Key Nursing Competencies

Throughout the program, you will develop essential competencies for safe, ethical, and high-quality practice across diverse health settings:

  • Clinical assessment and nursing reasoning: Mastery of history-taking, physical examination, basic data interpretation, and care planning with a focus on patient outcomes.
  • Patient safety and quality of care: Application of guidelines and protocols, prevention of adverse events, risk management, professional ethics, and evidence-based practice.
  • Therapeutic communication and interprofessional collaboration: Cultural competence, patient and family education, use of health information technologies, and collaborative leadership.

High-Level Academic Training

The program is supported by a faculty with clinical and research expertise, committed to excellence and international relevance. The curriculum integrates theory and practice through case analyses, integrative projects, and competency-based assessment, ensuring rigorous, up-to-date preparation aligned with globally recognized quality standards.

Global Support Network

By studying the Bachelor’s Degree in Nursing, you will join an international community of students and health professionals. You will participate in forums, seminars, and collaborative projects that enrich your intercultural perspective and expand your professional network, fostering the exchange of best practices and lifelong learning.

A Transformative Approach

The program prepares you to respond effectively to the foremost challenges of contemporary health, incorporating social determinants of health, health promotion and disease prevention, as well as care management in complex and evolving environments. You will develop competencies to lead improvements, design person-centered interventions, and contribute sustainable, human-centered solutions.

Admission Requirements

To apply for the Bachelor’s Degree in Nursing, applicants must meet the following requirements:

  • Academic Transcripts: Submit official transcripts from secondary education or higher. If the applicant has prior higher education, the corresponding transcripts must be included.
  • Identification Document: Copy of a valid government-issued ID. International applicants must include, where applicable, certifications or recognitions required by their country of origin.
  • Curriculum Vitae (CV): An up-to-date CV reflecting academic, professional, or volunteer experience in health, community care, or caregiving roles.
  • Applicant Statement: An essay outlining motivations for studying Nursing, an ethical commitment to care, and how the applicant plans to apply their learning in their professional trajectory.
  • Letters of Recommendation: Two letters from faculty, employers, or health professionals attesting to the applicant’s academic potential and personal qualities.
  • Language Proficiency: Proof of proficiency in the language of instruction (e.g., Spanish or English), as applicable to the applicant’s profile.
  • Technical Requirements: Stable internet access, a computer, and a camera for synchronous activities and assessments.
  • Conditions for Practicums/Simulations: Depending on local regulations and partner institutions, proof of immunisations, health insurance, and/or background checks may be required prior to clinical or field activities.
  • Application Fee: A non-refundable fee is required to submit the application.

Selection Criteria

Completion of secondary education with strong academic performance—particularly in biology, chemistry, and mathematics—will be valued. Prior nursing training is not required; however, experience in community care, health services, volunteering, or caregiving roles will be positively considered. The admissions committee prioritises critical reasoning, effective communication, intercultural sensitivity, teamwork, and evidence of ethical commitment to public health and human well-being.

Application Process

The process is 100% online and can be completed from anywhere in the world. The general steps include:

  • Registration and Form: Create an account and complete the application form on the platform.
  • Document Upload: Submit transcripts, identification, CV, personal statement, and letters of recommendation.
  • Fee Payment: Pay the non-refundable application fee.
  • Additional Assessments: Interview or supplementary test, if required.
  • Review and Decision: The committee reviews the file and communicates its decision within the timelines indicated in the admissions section.

This process ensures that candidates possess the preparation and readiness to succeed in the Bachelor’s Degree in Nursing and to continue their professional development in clinical and community settings. Important: admission and graduation do not automatically confer licensure or professional registration; requirements for nursing practice are determined by the competent authorities of each country or jurisdiction.

Credits and Courses

The program comprises 120 credits distributed across core, optional, and elective courses, allowing students to tailor their academic pathway according to their interests and professional goals. Core courses include:

  • Foundations of Nursing and Clinical Assessment
  • Anatomy, Physiology, and Pharmacology for Nursing
  • Medical–Surgical Nursing
  • Maternal–Child and Pediatric Nursing
  • Public Health, Epidemiology, and Patient Safety

Electives enable in-depth study in areas such as critical care, geriatrics, mental health, nursing management and leadership, health information technologies, and international cooperation.

Thesis or Comprehensive Examination

Upon completion, students choose between a thesis or a comprehensive examination. The thesis may take the form of an evidence-based applied project, including outcomes analysis and a proposal for quality improvement in care. The program offers flexible pacing—full-time or part-time—with a maximum completion time of five years. Note: depending on local regulations and institutional agreements, advanced simulation and/or supervised practicums may be required.

Academic Requirements During the Program

Once admitted, the student must meet the following requirements to complete the Bachelor’s Degree in Nursing:

  • Total Credits: Completion of 120 credits distributed among core, elective, and optional courses, in accordance with the current curriculum.
  • Minimum Cumulative GPA: Maintain a cumulative GPA of B or higher (or equivalent) across all courses, including practical components and competency-based assessments.
  • Thesis or Comprehensive Examination: Upon completion, choose either a thesis (6 credits) or a comprehensive examination integrating the program’s core domains.
  • Supervised Simulations/Practicums: Depending on availability and local regulation, complete required hours of simulation and/or practicum in approved settings with satisfactory clinical evaluation.
  • Modality and Timeframe: A 100% online program that may be completed part-time or full-time, with a maximum of five years for degree completion.

These requirements ensure rigorous and ethical training aligned with international quality standards and oriented toward safe, inclusive, person-centred care. Important: admission and graduation do not automatically confer licensure or professional registration; the practice of nursing is subject to requirements established by the competent authorities of each country or jurisdiction.
