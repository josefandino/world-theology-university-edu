DOC-FB-RAM-400 - Religions of the Ancient Mediterranean

An advanced study of the religious traditions that emerged in the Mediterranean region, with an in-depth analysis of their historical, philosophical, and cultural interactions. The course explores the enduring legacy of post-exilic Judaism, early Christianity, and early Islam, examining their influence on worldviews, social structures, and theological currents that have shaped global thought to the present day.

Course Objectives

  • Understand the origins and evolution of the religious traditions of the ancient Mediterranean.
  • Analyze the historical, philosophical, and theological interactions among Judaism, Christianity, and Islam during their formative periods.
  • Examine the literary, cultural, and theological legacy of these traditions within global history.
  • Identify the convergences and tensions among Mediterranean religions in their historical contexts.
  • Critically evaluate the philosophical contributions of Mediterranean religions to ethics, politics, and global culture.
  • Develop skills in comparative, critical, and interdisciplinary research in religious and historical studies.

Main Topics

  • Historical and religious overview of the ancient Mediterranean world.
  • Post-exilic Judaism: theological and social transformations.
  • Early Christianity: expansion, consolidation, and theological challenges.
  • Early Islam: genesis, core beliefs, and initial expansion.
  • Religious interactions: dialogue, conflict, and syncretism.
  • Philosophy and religion: reciprocal influences in the ancient Mediterranean.
  • Sacred literature and textual traditions: Torah, New Testament, and Qur'an.
  • Cultural and theological legacy of Mediterranean religions in the contemporary world.

Methodology

The course will be delivered through virtual lectures, critical analysis of religious and philosophical texts, historical case studies, academic forum debates, and applied research projects. Interdisciplinary thinking, active participation, and the development of analytical skills in multicultural contexts will be strongly encouraged.

Assessment

  • Critical research essays on Mediterranean religious traditions (30%).
  • Participation in discussion forums and comparative analysis activities (20%).
  • Tests on comprehension of historical and religious texts (20%).
  • Final research project on a topic related to religious interaction in the Mediterranean (30%).

Course Relevance

This course is a key component of the Doctorate in Biblical Philosophy program, as it provides a profound understanding of the religious and philosophical roots that shaped Western civilization and much of the modern world. It equips students with the necessary tools to critically interpret religious and cultural phenomena, promoting an ethical, strategic, and inclusive approach to interreligious studies and contemporary global dialogue.
