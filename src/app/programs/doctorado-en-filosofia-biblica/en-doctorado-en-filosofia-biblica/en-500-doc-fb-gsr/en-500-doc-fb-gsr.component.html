DOC-FB-GSR-500 - Gender, Sexuality, and Religion

This course critically addresses the intersections between religion, gender, and sexuality, exploring how various religious traditions have interpreted, regulated, and shaped these dimensions throughout history. Through an interdisciplinary approach, students will engage with feminist, queer, and trans perspectives in dialogue with sacred texts and religious practices, fostering critical, inclusive, and ethical reflection on human diversity within religious and social contexts.

Course Objectives

  • Understand the historical and theological frameworks that have shaped conceptions of gender and sexuality within religious traditions.
  • Critically analyze the representation and regulation of gender and sexuality in sacred texts and religious doctrines.
  • Explore feminist, queer, and trans perspectives in the study of religion and biblical interpretation.
  • Assess the impact of religious constructions of gender and sexuality on contemporary social and cultural dynamics.
  • Develop skills in critical analysis, intercultural dialogue, and ethical reasoning regarding sexual and gender diversity in religious contexts.
  • Promote an inclusive and respectful perspective in the academic study and practical engagement with religious traditions.

Main Topics

  • Introduction to gender, sexuality, and religion studies.
  • Representations of gender and sexuality in biblical and other sacred texts.
  • Feminist perspectives in theological and biblical interpretation.
  • Queer theologies: foundations and contemporary debates.
  • Trans perspectives in religious and theological discourse.
  • The impact of religious norms on the social construction of gender and sexuality.
  • Religion, human rights, and sexual diversity: tensions and progress.
  • Case studies: experiences of inclusive religious communities.

Methodology

The course will be conducted through interactive virtual lectures, critical analysis of theological and scholarly texts, contemporary case studies, discussion forums, and applied research projects. Emphasis will be placed on active participation, critical thinking, respect for diversity, and collaborative learning within an inclusive academic environment.

Assessment

  • Critical essays on gender, sexuality, and religion (30%).
  • Participation in debates and discussion forums (20%).
  • Case analyses on experiences of inclusion and religious diversity (20%).
  • Final research project on a topic related to gender, sexuality, and religion (30%).

Course Relevance

This course is fundamental to the comprehensive formation of the Doctorate in Biblical Philosophy, as it equips students to critically, ethically, and inclusively engage with the complex intersections of religion, gender, and sexuality.
