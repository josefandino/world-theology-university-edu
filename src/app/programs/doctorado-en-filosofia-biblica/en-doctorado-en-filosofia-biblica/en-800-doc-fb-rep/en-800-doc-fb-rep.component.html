DOC-FB-REP-800 – Religion, Ethics, and Politics

This course explores the role of religions in shaping ethical principles and informing public policy. Through a critical and interdisciplinary approach, students will examine how religious beliefs have influenced—and continue to influence—contemporary debates on social justice, human rights, the environment, and global governance, fostering ethical and strategic reflection on current challenges.

Course Objectives

  • Understand the historical and contemporary influence of religions on the ethical and political formation of societies.
  • Analyze religious ethical foundations underlying current debates on human rights, social justice, and environmental sustainability.
  • Critically examine the relationships between religion, political power, and public policy formation.
  • Evaluate the tensions and collaborations between religious principles and secular political systems.
  • Develop critical and interdisciplinary analytical skills regarding global ethical and political issues.
  • Promote an ethical, inclusive, and global perspective in the analysis of religious participation in the public sphere.

Main Topics

  • Religion and ethics in the shaping of historical political models.
  • Religious foundations of social justice and human rights.
  • Religion and contemporary debates on equity, gender, and diversity.
  • Religious ethics and global environmental responsibility.
  • Global governance, multiculturalism, and interreligious dialogue.
  • Religion, conflict, and peacebuilding: case study analysis.
  • Religion in contemporary public policy: challenges and perspectives.
  • Case studies: religious participation in recent social and political movements.

Methodology

The course will be delivered through virtual lectures, critical analysis of texts and public policy documents, online debates, contemporary case studies, and applied research projects. Active participation, critical thinking, and interdisciplinary dialogue will be encouraged to support autonomous and collaborative learning.

Assessment

  • Critical essays on religion, ethics, and politics (30%).
  • Participation in discussion forums and analysis of contemporary debates (20%).
  • Case study analysis on religious participation in public policy (20%).
  • Final research project on an ethically and politically relevant religious topic (30%).

Course Relevance

This course is essential to the Doctorate in Biblical Philosophy, as it equips students to critically understand and analyze the influence of religious beliefs on global ethical and political processes. It provides tools for thoughtful and ethical engagement in contemporary public debates, promoting academic, social, and spiritual leadership committed to justice, human rights, and the construction of a more equitable and sustainable world.
