DOC-FB-IBPS-100 - Introduction to Biblical and Philosophical Studies

This course provides a solid foundation in the critical analysis of biblical texts and the main philosophical approaches applied to their interpretation. Through a rigorous exploration of historical and contemporary schools of thought, students will develop essential competencies to understand the interplay between philosophy, theology, and biblical studies, preparing their thinking to address ethical, social, and cultural challenges in a globalized and diverse world.

Course Objectives

  • Understand the fundamentals of biblical and philosophical studies from a critical and interdisciplinary perspective.
  • Identify the major philosophical traditions that have influenced biblical interpretation throughout history.
  • Analyze biblical texts using relevant philosophical conceptual frameworks.
  • Develop critical thinking skills to evaluate biblical interpretations in both historical and contemporary contexts.
  • Apply ethical and philosophical principles to the analysis of current biblical themes.
  • Promote an intercultural and inclusive understanding in the interpretation of sacred texts.

Main Topics

  • Foundations of biblical studies and their historical development.
  • Introduction to major schools of philosophical thought.
  • Relationship between philosophy, theology, and biblical hermeneutics.
  • Historical models of biblical interpretation: patristic, scholastic, modern, and contemporary.
  • The influence of critical and postmodern thought on biblical studies.
  • Philosophical ethics applied to biblical analysis.
  • Intercultural and global challenges in the interpretation of Scripture.
  • Case studies: application of philosophical theories to selected biblical texts.

Methodology

The course will be conducted through online lectures, real-world case analyses, discussion forum debates, applied research activities, and individual projects. Active learning, critical reflection, and independent work will be encouraged, with continuous support from faculty through personalized online tutoring.

Assessment

  • Critical essays on philosophical traditions and biblical interpretation (30%).
  • Practical case analyses and participation in discussion forums (20%).
  • Written tests on conceptual understanding (20%).
  • Final interdisciplinary application project (30%).

Course Relevance

This course is a core component of the Doctorate in Biblical Philosophy program, as it lays the conceptual, methodological, and critical foundations for further academic development. Its ethical, strategic, and global approach enables students to integrate philosophical and theological thought into the interpretation of sacred texts, equipping them to address contemporary cultural, social, and religious challenges from an inclusive, rigorous, and transformative perspective.
