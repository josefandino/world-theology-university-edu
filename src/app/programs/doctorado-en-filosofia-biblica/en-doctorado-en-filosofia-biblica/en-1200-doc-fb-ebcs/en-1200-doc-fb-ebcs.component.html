This course offers an interdisciplinary analysis of how the ethical principles found in biblical texts engage with the ethical challenges of contemporary society. Through a critical perspective, students will explore topics such as human rights, bioethics, social justice, and ecological responsibility, examining how the biblical tradition can inform ethical responses that are relevant, inclusive, and transformative in global contexts.

Course Objectives

  • Understand the fundamental ethical principles present in biblical texts.
  • Critically analyze the relationship between biblical ethics and contemporary ethical issues.
  • Explore the application of biblical values in current debates on human rights, bioethics, social justice, and ecology.
  • Evaluate the transformative potential of biblical ethics in diverse sociocultural settings.
  • Develop critical analysis skills and applied ethical interpretation in response to global challenges.
  • Foster an inclusive, critical, and contextualized ethical reflection grounded in the biblical tradition.

Main Topics

  • Introduction to biblical ethics: foundations and traditions.
  • Biblical ethics and human rights: convergences and challenges.
  • The Bible and bioethics: life, health, and human dignity.
  • Social justice and equity in the prophetic and wisdom traditions.
  • Ecological responsibility in the biblical vision of creation.
  • Contemporary applications of biblical ethics: practical cases.
  • Global ethical debates: pluralism, interreligious dialogue, and social engagement.
  • Case studies: biblical interpretation in response to current ethical dilemmas.

Methodology

The course will be conducted through virtual lectures, critical analysis of biblical and ethical texts, contemporary case studies, academic forum debates, and applied research projects. Active participation, critical ethical reasoning, and interdisciplinary reflection will be encouraged within an inclusive and collaborative learning environment.

Assessment

  • Ethical-biblical analysis essays addressing contemporary issues (30%).
  • Participation in ethical discussion forums and biblical interpretation sessions (20%).
  • Case study analysis of current ethical dilemmas (20%).
  • Final research project on biblical ethics and global challenges (30%).

Course Relevance

This course is essential to the Doctorate in Biblical Philosophy, as it integrates the ethical richness of biblical texts with the social, cultural, and environmental challenges of our time. It prepares students to offer ethical responses that are relevant, engaged, and transformative across professional, academic, and community contexts, promoting a critical, inclusive, and global vision of human responsibility in contemporary society.
