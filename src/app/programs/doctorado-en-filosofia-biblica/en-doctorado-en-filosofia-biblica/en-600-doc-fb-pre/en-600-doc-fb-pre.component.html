DOC-FB-PRE-600 - Philosophy and Religious Ethics

This course offers a rigorous examination of fundamental ethical issues within religious thought, addressing philosophical debates on justice, truth, morality, and responsibility. Through a critical and interdisciplinary perspective, students will reflect on the contemporary application of religious principles in resolving global ethical dilemmas, fostering a profound, ethical, and contextualized understanding of religion in today’s world.

Course Objectives

  • Understand major philosophical and ethical theories within the context of religion.
  • Analyze the concepts of justice, truth, morality, and responsibility across various religious traditions.
  • Critically evaluate the interaction between religious principles and contemporary ethical challenges.
  • Reflect on the application of religious values in addressing global ethical dilemmas.
  • Develop critical analysis and ethical argumentation skills in multicultural and interreligious contexts.
  • Promote an ethical, inclusive, and responsible perspective in contemporary religious interpretation and practice.

Main Topics

  • Introduction to moral philosophy and religious ethics.
  • Concepts of justice, truth, and morality in the world’s major religions.
  • Personal and social responsibility from a religious perspective.
  • Ethics of war, peace, and human rights in religious thought.
  • Religion and bioethics: contemporary debates.
  • Ethical challenges of globalization and religious pluralism.
  • Religion, the environment, and ecological responsibility.
  • Case studies: ethical analysis of current issues from religious perspectives.

Methodology

The course will be delivered through virtual lectures, critical readings of philosophical and theological texts, ethical case analyses, academic forum debates, and applied reflection projects. Emphasis will be placed on critical thinking, ethical reasoning, and active participation in a collaborative and respectful learning environment.

Assessment

  • Philosophical-ethical essays on contemporary religious issues (30%).
  • Participation in applied ethics discussion forums (20%).
  • Critical analysis of ethical case studies (20%).
  • Final research project or ethical resolution proposal based on religious principles (30%).

Course Relevance

This course is essential to the Doctorate in Biblical Philosophy program, as it integrates philosophical and ethical analysis with the practical dimension of religion in contemporary society. It equips students to apply religious principles to complex ethical issues, fostering academic, social, and spiritual leadership grounded in respect, justice, and global responsibility.
