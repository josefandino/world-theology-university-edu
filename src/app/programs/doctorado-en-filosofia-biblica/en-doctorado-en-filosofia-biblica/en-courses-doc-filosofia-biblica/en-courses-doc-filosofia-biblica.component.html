DOC-FB-IBPS-100 - Introduction to Biblical and Philosophical Studies - This course provides a solid foundation in the critical analysis of biblical texts and the main philosophical approaches applied to their interpretation. Students will develop essential competencies to understand the interrelation between philosophy, theology, and biblical studies in historical and contemporary contexts.

DOC-FB-CRS-200 - Comparative Religious Studies - Through a critical and interdisciplinary approach, this course examines the world's major religious traditions, highlighting their philosophical convergences and divergences. It analyzes the impact of religions on ethical, cultural, and spiritual construction at a global level.

DOC-FB-BTEC-300 - Biblical Theology and Early Christianity - This course delves into the theological thought of the early Christian communities, their historical development, and their philosophical influences. It critically analyzes the evolution of biblical theology from early Christianity to its projection in contemporary thought.

DOC-FB-RAM-400 - Religions of the Ancient Mediterranean - An advanced study of the religious traditions that emerged in the Mediterranean, analyzing their historical and philosophical interaction. The course explores the cultural, theological, and literary legacy of post-exilic Judaism, early Christianity, and early Islam.

DOC-FB-GSR-500 - Gender, Sexuality, and Religion - This course critically addresses the intersection of religion, gender, and sexuality, analyzing how diverse religious traditions have interpreted and shaped these dimensions over time. It explores feminist, queer, and trans perspectives in relation to sacred texts and religious practices.

DOC-FB-PRE-600 - Philosophy and Religious Ethics - A rigorous examination of fundamental ethical questions in religious thought, addressing debates on justice, truth, morality, and responsibility. The course fosters philosophical reflection on the contemporary application of religious principles to global ethical dilemmas.

DOC-FB-RE-700 - Religions in Europe - A critical analysis of the evolution and influence of religions in Europe from antiquity to modernity. The course studies the impact of religious traditions on European cultural identity, politics, and social transformation.

DOC-FB-REP-800 - Religion, Ethics, and Politics - This course explores the role of religions in shaping ethical principles and public policies. Students will examine how religious beliefs inform current debates on social justice, human rights, the environment, and global governance.

DOC-FB-IS-900 - Islamic Studies - An in-depth introduction to Islamic philosophy, theology, and practice, from foundational texts to contemporary expressions. The course analyzes reformist thought currents, ethical debates, and the impact of Islam on global social and cultural dynamics.

DOC-FB-PHST-1000 - Philosophical Hermeneutics of Sacred Texts - This course offers a critical deepening of the main hermeneutical approaches applied to Scripture, analyzing biblical interpretation from the perspectives of contemporary philosophy, phenomenology, and critical theory.

DOC-FB-PAR-1100 - Philosophical Anthropology and Religion - A rigorous study of philosophical conceptions of the human being in relation to religious experience, exploring topics such as the nature of the spirit, the sense of transcendence, and cultural manifestations of faith.

DOC-FB-EBCS-1200 - Ethics and the Bible in Contemporary Society - An interdisciplinary analysis of how ethical principles contained in biblical texts engage with current ethical challenges, including human rights, bioethics, social justice, and ecological responsibility.

DOC-FB-PHR-1300 - Phenomenology of Religion - This course examines religion as a human phenomenon from a philosophical perspective, addressing experiences of the sacred, faith, worship, and revelation through the thought of leading contemporary phenomenologists.

DOC-FB-PPBT-1400 - Political Philosophy and Biblical Theology - An exploration of the intersections between political thought and biblical theological ideas, analyzing themes such as power, law, justice, and community from classical and contemporary philosophical perspectives.

DOC-FB-CTR-1500 - Critical Theory and Religion - A course dedicated to the application of critical theory to the study of religion, addressing how structures of power, ideology, and culture influence the interpretation and practice of religion in the modern world.

DOC-FB-CDCPR-1600 - Contemporary Debates in Philosophy of Religion - An analysis of current philosophical discussions, addressing questions such as the existence of God, religious diversity, the rationality of faith, mystical experience, and the interactions between science and religion.

DOC-FB-PGS-1700 - Public Theology and Global Society - This course addresses the role of theology in the contemporary public sphere, exploring how religious voices contribute to ethical, political, and social debates in a pluralistic and globalized world.
