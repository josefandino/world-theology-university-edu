The Doctoral Program in Biblical Philosophy at the Divinity School of World Theology University (World Theology University) requires candidates to meet a series of prerequisites to ensure that students possess a solid foundation in religious studies or related fields. These requirements ensure that students are prepared to engage at the advanced academic level demanded by the program and can fully benefit from the research and academic development opportunities offered by the doctorate.

Previous Academic Background

To be considered for the Doctorate in Biblical Philosophy, candidates must hold a master's degree in religious studies, theology, philosophy, or a related discipline. This requirement is fundamental as it ensures that students have adequate knowledge of the key disciplines and research methodologies needed to conduct advanced studies in biblical philosophy. Students from other disciplines may be considered, provided they can demonstrate a strong foundation in subjects related to religious or philosophical studies.

Research Proposal

Applicants to the program must submit a detailed research proposal aligned with the objectives of the doctorate. This proposal should address a relevant topic within the field of biblical or philosophical studies, demonstrating the candidate's ability to develop original and meaningful research. The proposal must include a description of the research problem, specific objectives, the proposed methodology, and a preliminary literature review. This research proposal is a key part of the admission process as it evaluates the candidate's preparedness to undertake rigorous academic research.

Proficiency in Relevant Languages

Since many of the biblical and philosophical texts studied in the program are in ancient languages, students are required to have advanced proficiency in at least one language relevant to biblical or philosophical studies. The most common languages are Hebrew, Greek, and Latin, although other ancient or modern languages may be considered depending on the student's research focus. This requirement ensures that students can access and analyze primary sources in their original language, which is essential for conducting precise and rigorous research in biblical studies.

Research Experience

Candidates are expected to have prior experience in academic research, preferably in areas related to philosophy, theology, or biblical studies. This experience may have been gained through the completion of a master's thesis, the publication of academic articles, or participation in research projects. Previous research experience demonstrates the student's ability to conduct independent and rigorous studies, a critical skill for successfully completing a doctorate.

  • Hold a master’s degree in religious studies or a related discipline.
  • Submit a research proposal aligned with the program’s objectives.
  • Demonstrate proficiency in at least one language relevant to biblical studies (Hebrew, Greek, Latin, etc.).
  • Provide evidence of prior academic research experience.

In summary, the requirements for admission to the Doctorate in Biblical Philosophy are designed to ensure that students have the training, knowledge, and skills necessary to successfully meet the academic and research challenges of the program. These requirements guarantee that students are prepared to make significant contributions to the field of biblical and philosophical studies and to develop original research that enriches knowledge in this field.
