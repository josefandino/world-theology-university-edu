DOC-FB-RE-700 – Religions in Europe

This course offers a critical analysis of the evolution and influence of religions in Europe, from antiquity to modernity. It examines how religious traditions have shaped European cultural identity, political systems, and social dynamics—exploring both periods of integration and episodes of conflict. The course fosters a critical and interdisciplinary understanding of the profound religious imprint on Europe’s history and present reality.

Course Objectives

  • Understand the historical development of major religious traditions in Europe.
  • Analyze the impact of religions on the formation of European cultural and political identity.
  • Examine processes of religious interaction, conflict, and reform across European history.
  • Critically evaluate secularization and religious resurgence in contemporary contexts.
  • Develop analytical and interpretive skills regarding the role of religion in European social change.
  • Promote a critical and inclusive perspective on religious and cultural diversity in Europe.

Main Topics

  • Religions in Ancient Europe: from Paganism to Christianity.
  • Medieval Christianity and its sociopolitical influence.
  • The Protestant Reformation and the Catholic Counter-Reformation: causes, impacts, and consequences.
  • Religion, the Enlightenment, and secularization in modern Europe.
  • Jewish and Islamic presence in Europe: historical and cultural contributions.
  • Secularism, pluralism, and religious freedom in contemporary Europe.
  • Religious conflicts and peacebuilding: historical and current perspectives.
  • Case studies: religion and national identity in present-day Europe.

Methodology

The course will be conducted through virtual lectures, critical readings of primary and secondary sources, historical case analyses, online debates, and applied research projects. It will emphasize autonomous learning, interdisciplinary reflection, and active participation in collaborative settings.

Assessment

  • Analytical essays on the religious evolution of Europe (30%).
  • Participation in critical discussion forums (20%).
  • Analysis of historical and contemporary case studies (20%).
  • Final research project on a topic related to religion and European identity (30%).

Course Relevance

This course is a key component of the Doctorate in Biblical Philosophy, as it offers a deep understanding of how religions have shaped—and continue to influence—European history, culture, and politics. It prepares students to analyze religious and social change through an ethical, critical, and global lens, which is essential for academic research and professional engagement in international and multicultural contexts.
