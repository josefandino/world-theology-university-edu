BACH-ICH-900 - Introduction to Chaplaincy

This course provides a comprehensive overview of chaplaincy ministry, exploring its historical, theological, and practical foundations. Students will examine the evolution of chaplaincy from its origins to its development across diverse contexts, including hospitals, the armed forces, educational institutions, correctional facilities, and communities.

Course Objectives:

  • Historical and Theological Foundations of Chaplaincy: Students will learn about the origins and development of the chaplaincy ministry, gaining an understanding of its theological basis and its role in various ministerial contexts.
  • Essential Functions of the Chaplain: The course will explore the core responsibilities of chaplains, such as spiritual support, pastoral counseling, crisis mediation, and grief care, preparing students to address the emotional and spiritual needs of those they serve.
  • Ethical and Legal Principles: Students will study the ethical principles and legal regulations governing chaplaincy practice, ensuring a professional approach that respects the rights of individuals receiving care.
  • Effective Communication Strategies: The course will include training in effective communication within chaplaincy settings, focusing on how to engage with individuals from diverse cultural, social, and emotional backgrounds.
  • Interdisciplinary Collaboration: Students will examine collaborative work with other professionals within institutions and organizations where chaplains serve, enhancing their ability to contribute effectively to multidisciplinary teams.

Methodology:

The course will combine theoretical lectures, case studies, practical simulations, and online discussions. Students will be challenged to apply what they learn through activities that simulate real-life situations in which chaplains are expected to respond with sensitivity, ethics, and effectiveness. Course assessment will include active participation in debates and simulations, the development of a final project focused on chaplaincy practice in a specific context, and an exam evaluating the knowledge acquired on the ethical, historical, and practical principles of chaplaincy.
