Bachelor of Science in Chaplaincy

Program Overview

The Bachelor of Science in Chaplaincy is a program designed to train students in providing spiritual, emotional, and moral care in diverse contexts. Fully offered online, this program combines biblical foundations, practical leadership tools, and intercultural communication skills to prepare future chaplains for the unique challenges of their calling.

This program focuses on shaping spiritual leaders with a solid foundation in biblical theology and professional competencies that enable them to support individuals, communities, and organizations. The flexibility of online learning allows students to complete their studies from anywhere in the world, adapting the program pace to their personal and professional needs.

The curriculum includes specialized courses in biblical counseling, chaplaincy ethics, leadership, and biblical studies, along with a practical internship that ensures the direct application of acquired knowledge in real-world scenarios.

Program Details

The Bachelor of Science in Chaplaincy (Chaplaincy B.S.) is a comprehensive academic program that combines robust theological training with practical leadership and communication skills. This program is specifically designed for those seeking to serve as chaplains in various settings, such as hospitals, military institutions, prisons, religious communities, and corporations.

The program is offered entirely online, enabling students to access quality education from anywhere in the world. The courses are designed to provide a solid foundation in biblical theology, pastoral counseling, ethical leadership, and intercultural skills, all essential for chaplaincy work in a globalized world.

Key Program Features:

  • 100% Online: All courses are delivered online through a modern and accessible learning platform, providing flexibility for students with busy schedules or personal responsibilities.
  • Comprehensive Education: The curriculum includes courses in theology, ethics, counseling, intercultural communication, and pastoral leadership.
  • Practical Focus: A supervised Chaplain Internship ensures students gain hands-on experience in the chaplaincy field.
  • Flexibility: The program is designed to align with students’ professional and personal goals, allowing them to progress at their own pace.
  • Personalized Mentorship: Experienced chaplaincy professors and mentors guide students throughout the program, providing academic and professional support.

The program is structured for students starting their studies between Fall 2024 and Summer 2025 and will remain valid for those maintaining continuous enrollment without changing their program or concentration.

Core Study Areas:

  • Chaplaincy and Leadership: Courses provide a strong foundation for understanding the ethical and practical principles of chaplaincy leadership.
  • Biblical Studies: Includes advanced courses in the Old and New Testaments, biblical theology, and contextual studies of Scripture.
  • Communication Skills: Emphasis on effective and intercultural communication to serve in diverse environments.
  • Pastoral Counseling: Training to provide emotional and spiritual support based on biblical principles.

With a focus on service and leadership, this program prepares students for meaningful roles in chaplaincy ministry, combining academic knowledge and practical skills to make a difference in the lives of those they serve.

Program Objectives

The Bachelor of Science in Chaplaincy aims to equip students with the skills and knowledge needed to serve effectively as chaplains in a wide range of contexts. The specific objectives of the program are as follows:

  • Develop a strong theological foundation: Provide students with a deep understanding of Scripture and theological principles underlying chaplaincy work.
  • Strengthen leadership skills: Prepare students to lead with integrity and effectiveness in multicultural environments and challenging contexts.
  • Competence in pastoral counseling: Train students to offer spiritual, emotional, and moral support to individuals and communities in crisis or difficulty.
  • Ethics in chaplaincy: Teach fundamental ethical principles to help students make informed and responsible decisions in their professional practice.
  • Intercultural communication skills: Develop competencies in effective communication to interact with people from diverse cultures, religions, and backgrounds.
  • Practical preparation: Provide hands-on experience through a supervised internship, ensuring students apply their learning in real-world situations.
  • Contribute to community well-being: Equip students to be agents of positive change in the communities they serve, promoting values of compassion, respect, and hope.

These objectives ensure that graduates are academically and professionally prepared to address the spiritual and emotional needs of individuals across various settings, becoming trusted and compassionate leaders in their field.

Program Courses

The curriculum for the Bachelor of Science in Chaplaincy is designed to provide comprehensive training in biblical theology, leadership, counseling, and intercultural communication. Below is a detailed list of the core courses included in the program:

Core Foundational Courses:

  • BACH-CII-100 - Communication and Intercultural Interaction: This course explores communication strategies for effective interaction in multicultural environments, developing essential skills for chaplains working in global contexts.
  • BACH-PD-200 - Developmental Psychology: An introduction to human development throughout the lifespan, providing a solid foundation to understand the emotional and psychological needs of the individuals served.
  • BACH-IPT-300 - Introduction to Practical Theology: A course that provides the foundations for applying theology in practical contexts, preparing students for effective service as chaplains.
  • BACH-CPE-400 - Christian and Pastoral Ethics: Study of ethical principles in pastoral care, focusing on how chaplains should make moral decisions in complex situations.
  • BACH-HC-500 - History of the Church: An analysis of the history of Christianity, from its origins to the present day, with a focus on the development of the chaplaincy ministry.

Core Program Courses:

  • BACH-IAP-600 - Introduction to Apologetics: A study of the foundations of defending the Christian faith, preparing students to articulate their beliefs with clarity and confidence.
  • BACH-IBC-700 - Introduction to Biblical Counseling: Training in the principles and practices of counseling based on Scripture, with a focus on spiritual and emotional support.
  • BACH-PE-800 - Pentateuch: An in-depth analysis of the first five books of the Bible, highlighting their theological and practical relevance.
  • BACH-ICH-900 - Introduction to Chaplaincy: This course introduces the foundations of chaplaincy ministry, including its history, functions, and practical applications.
  • BACH-FLC-1000 - Fundamentals of Leadership in Chaplaincy: A focus on developing ethical and effective leadership skills in pastoral and spiritual contexts.
  • BACH-ETH-1100 - Ethics in Chaplaincy: A course that addresses the ethical principles necessary for chaplaincy practice, preparing students to face ethical dilemmas with integrity.
  • BACH-MSS-1200 - Methodical Study of the Scriptures: Advanced techniques for studying and interpreting the Bible, with an emphasis on practical applications.
  • BACH-CHI-1300 - Chaplaincy Internship: A supervised practical experience that allows students to apply their knowledge and skills in a real chaplaincy environment.
  • BACH-APC-1400 - Advanced Pastoral Counseling: An advanced course in spiritual and emotional counseling techniques, focusing on complex situations that chaplains must handle.
  • BACH-CCES-1500 - Crisis and Chaplaincy in Emergency Situations: Training to address emergencies and crises in religious and civil settings, developing pastoral intervention skills.
  • BACH-LHS-1600 - Leadership in the Hospital Setting: A course that addresses the role of the chaplain in the hospital environment, including spiritual support for patients, families, and medical staff.

Elective Courses in Chaplaincy:

  • BACH-CHPL-480, 481, 482 or 483: Specialized options in specific areas of chaplaincy, allowing students to personalize their training according to their interests and professional goals.

General Education and Free Electives:

  • Courses in communication, mathematics, social sciences, and natural sciences: Courses in communication, mathematics, social sciences, and natural sciences that complement the core training.
  • Free electives (9-18 credits): Free electives (9-18 credits) offering flexibility to explore additional interests or complementary areas.

In total, the program requires 120 credits, of which 63 correspond to core courses, ensuring a comprehensive academic and practical preparation for chaplaincy service.

Program Requirements

To be admitted into the Bachelor of Science in Chaplaincy program, students must meet specific academic and administrative requirements designed to ensure success in this rigorous online program. Below are the admission and graduation requirements:

Admission Requirements:

  • High school diploma or equivalent: Applicants must have completed secondary-level education or its recognized equivalent in their home country.
  • English proficiency: Since the program is offered in English, students must demonstrate proficiency through standardized tests (such as TOEFL or IELTS) or prior studies in English.
  • Completed application form: Interested candidates must complete the online admission form, providing the required personal and academic information.
  • Statement of purpose: A letter or essay explaining the student’s reasons for choosing the program and how they plan to apply their learning to their professional career.
  • Official transcripts: Submit certified copies of previous academic transcripts, including high school and any higher-level education achieved.

Graduation Requirements:

  • Total credits: Complete a total of 120 credits, distributed among core courses, free electives, and general education.
  • Minimum GPA: Maintain an overall GPA of at least 2.0 throughout the program.
  • Upper-level courses: Complete at least 30 credits in upper-level courses (300-400).
  • Institutional credits: At least 25% of the program’s credits must be completed through World Theology University.
  • Graduation application: Submit a formal graduation application in the final semester of the program.
  • Supervised internship: Successfully complete the mandatory chaplaincy internship to ensure practical application of acquired knowledge and skills.

These requirements ensure students are academically prepared and professionally equipped to serve as effective chaplains, ready to meet the unique challenges of their vocational calling.
