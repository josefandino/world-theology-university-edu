BACH-HC-500 - History of the church

The course BACH-HC-500 - Church History offers a detailed journey through the evolution of Christianity from its origins to the present day, with a special focus on the development of chaplaincy ministry. Students will explore key moments, influential figures, and doctrinal transformations that have shaped the Church over the centuries.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the origins of Christianity and its expansion throughout history.
  • Analyze the major councils, doctrines, and movements that have influenced the Church.
  • Explore the development of chaplaincy ministry in different historical contexts.
  • Reflect on the relevance of ecclesiastical history in contemporary pastoral ministry.
  • Examine the impact of the Church on society and its role in times of crisis and transformation.

Main Topics:

  • Early Christianity: the Apostolic Church and persecution.
  • Ecumenical councils and the development of Christian doctrine.
  • The rise of monasticism and its influence on chaplaincy.
  • The Protestant Reformation and its effects on the modern Church.
  • The role of the Church in conflicts and social change.
  • The evolution of chaplaincy ministry throughout history.

Methodology:

The course is conducted online with academic readings, analysis of historical documents, discussion forums, and critical essays that will allow students to delve deeply into ecclesiastical history.

Evaluation:

The evaluation will include participation in discussion forums, online exams, analysis of historical texts, and a final project on the evolution of chaplaincy ministry in a specific period of Church history.

Importance of the Course:

This course provides future chaplains with an essential understanding of Church history, allowing them to contextualize their ministry within a rich and dynamic tradition. It also helps them develop an informed perspective to address contemporary challenges in faith and spiritual leadership.
