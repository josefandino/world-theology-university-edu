BACH-IAP-600 - Introduction to apologetics

This course offers an in-depth study of the fundamental principles of Christian apologetics, providing students with the necessary tools to defend and explain their faith with clarity, logic, and confidence in various contexts.

Course Objectives:

  • Philosophical and theological foundations of the Christian faith: Students will explore the philosophical foundations that support the existence of God and how Christian theology interrelates with philosophy to defend the core beliefs of Christianity.
  • Confidence and clarity in defending the faith: Throughout the course, students will develop argumentative skills to defend their faith in a clear, logical, and respectful manner, addressing doubts and objections from a theological and philosophical perspective.
  • Contemporary challenges to the Christian worldview: The course will analyze the major contemporary challenges to the Christian worldview, such as secularism, religious pluralism, and scientific objections to theism, equipping students with tools to effectively respond to these challenges.
  • Development of dialogical and argumentative skills: Through debates and practical exercises, students will learn to present and defend their beliefs with respect, using dialogical strategies that facilitate interreligious and academic dialogue.

Methodology:

The course will be conducted through theoretical lectures combined with practical argumentation exercises. Students will participate in debates, online discussions, and case studies on key topics of Christian apologetics, with a practical focus on defending the faith in current contexts.

Evaluation:

The course evaluation will be based on active participation in debates, a research paper on a specific apologetic topic, and a final exam where students will demonstrate their ability to apply the apologetic principles learned throughout the course.
