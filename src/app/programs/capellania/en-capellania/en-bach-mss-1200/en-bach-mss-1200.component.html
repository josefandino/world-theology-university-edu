BACH-MSS-1200 - Methodical study of the scriptures

This course offers an in-depth study of advanced biblical interpretation techniques, providing students with the necessary tools to analyze, understand, and apply Scripture accurately and contextually.

Course Objectives:

  • Fundamental Principles of Hermeneutics: Students will learn the essential principles of hermeneutics, including exegetical analysis, grammatical and literary interpretation, and the study of the historical-cultural context of biblical texts.
  • Relationship between the Old and New Testament: The course will explore how the texts of the Old and New Testament relate to and complement each other, providing a comprehensive understanding of biblical revelation.
  • Contemporary Critical Approaches: Students will be introduced to contemporary critical approaches, such as theological, canonical, and narrative hermeneutics, and will learn how these approaches impact the teaching and preaching of Scripture.
  • Practical Application of Biblical Interpretation: The course emphasizes the application of interpretation techniques in ministerial life, preparing students to use Scripture effectively in counseling, preaching, and teaching.
  • Analysis of Key Texts: Through the study of key biblical passages, students will develop practical skills for interpreting Scripture with faithfulness and theological depth.

Methodology:

The course will combine theoretical classes with exegetical analysis and practical exercises. Students will study key biblical texts, participate in case studies, and work on interpretation exercises to apply what they have learned in real ministry situations.

Evaluation:

Evaluation will include participation in text discussions, the completion of an exegetical paper on a biblical passage, and a final exam that will assess the understanding of hermeneutical principles and the ability to apply them in the ministerial context.
