BACH-FLC-1000 - Fundamentals of leadership in chaplaincy

This course offers a comprehensive approach to developing leadership skills within pastoral and spiritual contexts, with an emphasis on ethical principles and effective ministry management. Throughout the program, students will explore biblical leadership models, principles of authority and service, and strategies for decision-making based on Christian values.

Course Objectives:

  • Biblical Leadership Models: Students will learn about the leadership models presented in Scripture, understanding how biblical principles guide authority and service within chaplaincy.
  • Integrity and Ethics in Leadership: Key topics such as integrity in leadership will be addressed, ensuring that students understand the importance of leading with authenticity and in alignment with Christian values.
  • Management of Ministerial Resources: Students will learn effective strategies for managing resources within ministry, including planning, budgeting, and the responsible distribution of those resources.
  • Conflict Resolution in Faith Communities: Tools will be provided for resolving conflicts in a healthy and constructive manner within faith communities, promoting unity and understanding.
  • Formation of Pastoral Teams: The course will delve into the importance of training and leading strong pastoral teams, creating a collaborative and effective work environment within ministry.
  • Impact of Leadership on Spiritual Life: Students will reflect on the impact of Christian leadership on the spiritual life of believers and how the personal testimony of the leader influences their community.

Methodology:

The course will combine theory and practice through case studies, group discussions, and practical exercises focused on ethical decision-making, conflict resolution, and teamwork. Students will develop key competencies to exercise transformative and responsible pastoral leadership, learning to apply biblical principles in real ministry contexts.

Evaluation:

Evaluation will include active participation in discussions and simulations, the development of a leadership plan for a pastoral context, and a final exam that will assess the understanding of ethical leadership principles and the ability to implement them in ministerial situations.
