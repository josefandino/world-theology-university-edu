BACH-ETH-1100 - Ethics in chaplaincy

This course provides an in-depth study of the fundamental ethical principles in chaplaincy practice, preparing students to face moral dilemmas with integrity, discernment, and responsibility.

Course Objectives:

  • Philosophical and Theological Principles of Christian Ethics: Students will explore the philosophical and theological foundations of Christian ethics, understanding its application in the practice of chaplaincy ministry and how these principles guide ethical decision-making.
  • Pastoral Confidentiality: The fundamental value of confidentiality in chaplaincy and its impact on the trust between the chaplain and the individuals they serve will be studied.
  • Resolution of Ethical Dilemmas: Students will learn to make informed ethical decisions in critical situations, balancing biblical principles with the demands of ministerial contexts.
  • Respect for Religious and Cultural Diversity: The course will address how to respectfully handle religious and cultural diversity, promoting inclusion and understanding within the ministry.
  • Personal Faith and Institutional Regulations: The balance between the chaplain's personal faith and institutional regulations and policies will be discussed, teaching how to navigate this balance with integrity and professionalism.

Methodology:

The course will be conducted through theoretical classes, case study analysis, and group discussions. Students will evaluate real ethical situations in various chaplaincy contexts, such as hospitals, prisons, and the military, using the principles learned to resolve conflicts and provide moral guidance.

Evaluation:

The course evaluation will include participation in debates and case studies, an essay demonstrating the practical application of ethical principles in chaplaincy, and a final exam that will assess students' ability to face ethical dilemmas with discernment and responsibility.
