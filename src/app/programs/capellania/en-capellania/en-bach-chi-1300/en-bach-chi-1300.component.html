BACH-CHI-1300 - Chaplaincy internship

This course offers a supervised practical experience that allows students to apply their theoretical knowledge and ministerial skills in a real chaplaincy environment. Under the guidance of experienced chaplains, participants will develop essential competencies for spiritual accompaniment, pastoral counseling, and assistance in critical situations within various institutions such as hospitals, prisons, military forces, educational centers, and communities.

Course Objectives:

  • Application of Theoretical Knowledge: Students will be able to apply the theoretical teachings acquired in previous courses to real-life situations, facing the challenges and responsibilities of chaplaincy ministry.
  • Development of Ministerial Competencies: Fundamental skills such as active listening, conflict mediation, spiritual accompaniment, and pastoral counseling will be developed, which are essential in the chaplaincy context.
  • Assistance in Critical Situations: Students will learn to intervene and provide support in emotional crisis situations such as grief, anxiety, or trauma, adapting to the needs of the people they serve.
  • Adaptation to Multicultural and Multi-Religious Contexts: The course emphasizes students' ability to work in diverse environments, learning to respect and adapt to different religious and cultural traditions.
  • Reflection and Supervision: Throughout the course, students will participate in supervision and reflection sessions, where they will receive constructive feedback on their performance, enhancing their ministerial practice and personal and vocational growth.

Methodology:

The course will be carried out through the direct participation of students in chaplaincy situations in various institutions, with constant supervision by experienced chaplains. It will be complemented by reflection sessions and case studies to promote personal and professional growth.

Evaluation:

The course evaluation will include observation of the students' practices, feedback from supervisors, participation in reflection sessions, and a final report in which students will reflect on their experience and learning during the internship.
