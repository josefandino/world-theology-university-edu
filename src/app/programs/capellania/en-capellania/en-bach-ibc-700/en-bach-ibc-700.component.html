BACH-IBC-700 - Introduction to biblical counseling

This course provides comprehensive training in the theological and methodological foundations of Christian counseling, equipping students with the necessary tools to offer spiritual and emotional support based on Scripture.

Course Objectives:

  • Foundations of Biblical Counseling: Students will explore the essential principles underlying Christian counseling, understanding the fundamental role of God's Word as a guide in pastoral counseling.
  • The Role of the Holy Spirit in Personal Transformation: The course will examine how the Holy Spirit plays a key role in the spiritual and emotional transformation of individuals, promoting growth in faith and overall well-being.
  • Practical Application of Scripture: Strategies for applying biblical teachings in situations of conflict, relationship restoration, and faith strengthening will be discussed, providing practical solutions for accompanying people in crisis.
  • Support in Critical Emotional Areas: Students will learn how to provide support in specific areas such as grief, anxiety, depression, and how to accompany those facing significant emotional and spiritual challenges.
  • Development of Counseling Skills: The course will provide tools to develop active listening, empathy, and spiritual discernment skills, necessary to guide others with love, wisdom, and biblical truth.

Methodology:

The course will combine theoretical teaching with practical exercises, using case studies, counseling simulations, and online discussions. Students will practice their listening and accompaniment skills through interactions in simulated situations, fostering experiential learning.

Evaluation:

The evaluation will be continuous and will include participation in discussions and counseling simulations, the development of a practical project where students demonstrate their skills in applying the principles learned, and a final exam that will assess both theoretical knowledge and practical counseling skills.
