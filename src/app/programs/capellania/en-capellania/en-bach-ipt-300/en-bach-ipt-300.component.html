BACH-IPT-300 - Introduction to practical theology

The course BACH-IPT-300 - Introduction to Practical Theology provides students with a deep understanding of how to apply theological principles in real-world contexts. Through a comprehensive approach, this course prepares future chaplains to address practical situations based on solid theological knowledge and effective ministerial tools.

Course Objectives:

  • Develop a clear understanding of practical theology and its application in ministry.
  • Explore the role of the chaplain in various settings and its impact on the community.
  • Encourage the ability to integrate theological principles into pastoral service.
  • Equip students with skills for resolving ethical and spiritual issues within their ministerial practice.
  • Promote the development of skills for counseling and spiritual guidance.

Main Topics:

  • Fundamentals of practical theology and its relevance in chaplaincy.
  • Models of pastoral ministry and spiritual leadership.
  • The role of the chaplain in hospitals, military institutions, prisons, and communities.
  • Ministerial ethics and informed decision-making.
  • Strategies for counseling and spiritual guidance.

Methodology:

The course is taught entirely online, through readings, case studies, discussion forums, and practical exercises that promote the application of theology in real-world situations.

Evaluation:

The evaluation includes participation in forums, analysis of ministerial cases, online exams, and a final project where students will demonstrate their ability to apply theological principles in practical contexts.

Importance of the Course:

This course is essential for students seeking an effective ministry, as it provides practical tools and foundational theological knowledge for their work as chaplains in various settings.
