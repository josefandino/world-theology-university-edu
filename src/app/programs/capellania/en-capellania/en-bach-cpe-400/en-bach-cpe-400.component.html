BACH-CPE-400 - Christian and pastoral ethics

The course BACH-CPE-400 - Christian Ethics and Pastoral Care provides students with a deep understanding of the fundamental ethical principles applied in the context of chaplaincy and pastoral ministry. Through the study of case studies and theological foundations, students will learn to make informed moral decisions in complex ministerial situations.

Course Objectives:

  • Explore the fundamental principles of Christian ethics and their application in pastoral ministry.
  • Develop skills to evaluate ethical dilemmas from a theological and moral perspective.
  • Equip students with the ability to make ethical decisions in challenging contexts such as hospitals, prisons, and vulnerable communities.
  • Understand the importance of character and integrity in pastoral leadership.
  • Promote respect and cultural sensitivity in ministerial practice.

Main Topics:

  • Biblical and theological foundations of Christian ethics.
  • Ethical principles in pastoral counseling and spiritual guidance.
  • Moral dilemmas in chaplaincy: decision-making in crisis situations.
  • Ethics and leadership: the role of the chaplain in the moral formation of the community.
  • Ethical challenges in ministry: confidentiality, justice, and pastoral responsibility.

Methodology:

The course is delivered online through readings, forum discussions, case studies, and ethical reflections that allow students to analyze real-life ministerial situations from a Christian perspective.

Evaluation:

Students will be evaluated through participation in discussions, analysis of ethical cases, online exams, and a final project in which they will present an ethical response to a complex pastoral dilemma.

Importance of the Course:

This course is crucial for future chaplains and spiritual leaders, as it provides them with a solid ethical foundation to face the moral challenges of their vocation with wisdom, integrity, and pastoral sensitivity.
