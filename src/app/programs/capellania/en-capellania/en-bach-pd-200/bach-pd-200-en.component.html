BACH-PD-200 - Developmental Psychology

The course BACH-PD-200 - Developmental Psychology offers a comprehensive introduction to human development across the life cycle. Through a theoretical and practical approach, students will explore the key stages of development, from childhood to old age, understanding the biological, emotional, cognitive, and social factors that influence the well-being of individuals served in the chaplaincy field. This course is essential for future chaplains as it will enable them to adapt their pastoral service to the various stages of human development.

Course Objectives:

  • Develop a comprehensive understanding of the life cycle: Explore the main stages of human development, from childhood to old age, with a focus on how these stages affect spiritual, emotional, and social life.
  • Study key theories of human development: Examine important theories such as those of Piaget, Erikson, and Vygotsky and their application in the context of chaplaincy.
  • Identify specific spiritual needs at each stage of the life cycle: Recognize the spiritual needs associated with different phases, such as identity in adolescence or acceptance of mortality in old age.
  • Recognize factors that influence human development: Analyze factors such as resilience, existential crises, traumatic experiences, and the formation of spiritual identity.
  • Apply knowledge in chaplaincy: Adapt teachings to pastoral and counseling work, addressing the emotional and spiritual needs of people at various stages of life.

Main Topics:

  • Introduction to developmental psychology.
  • Classic and contemporary theories of human development (Piaget, Erikson, Vygotsky).
  • Physical, cognitive, and socio-emotional development in childhood, adolescence, adulthood, and old age.
  • Environmental and cultural factors that impact human development.
  • Support strategies in chaplaincy for different age groups.

Methodology:

The course is taught entirely online, with an active methodology that includes:

  • Interactive materials.
  • Case studies.
  • Debates in forums.

Students will engage in the analysis of real-life situations, reflecting on how to apply psychological principles in the context of chaplaincy.

Evaluation:

The evaluation will be continuous and based on:

  • Active participation in discussions.
  • Practical application projects.
  • Online exams.
  • A final paper in which students will analyze a case of human development from the chaplaincy perspective.

Importance of the Course:

This course is fundamental to the training of future chaplains, as it provides them with the necessary tools to understand the different stages of human development. With this knowledge, they will be able to offer more effective and empathetic pastoral support, tailored to the spiritual and emotional needs of individuals at various stages of their lives.
