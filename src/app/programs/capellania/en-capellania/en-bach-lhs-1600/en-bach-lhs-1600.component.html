BACH-LHS-1600 - Leadership in the hospital setting

The course BACH-LHS-1600 - Leadership in the Hospital Setting is designed to train chaplains and spiritual leaders in the hospital context, providing them with the necessary tools to support patients, families, and medical staff during times of illness, crisis, and recovery. Through theological, psychological, and ethical approaches, participants will develop counseling and spiritual support skills within a healthcare environment.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the role of the chaplain in the hospital setting.
  • Develop spiritual and emotional support skills for patients and their families.
  • Strengthen communication with medical staff and other healthcare professionals.
  • Apply ethical principles and confidentiality in pastoral work within the hospital.
  • Identify strategies for supporting situations of grief, suffering, and hope.

Main Topics:

  • Foundations of pastoral leadership in hospitals.
  • Psychology of the patient: emotions, faith, and resilience.
  • Accompaniment in terminal illnesses and palliative care.
  • Crisis intervention in hospital settings and grief management.
  • Ethics and confidentiality in spiritual care.
  • Interdisciplinary work with doctors, nurses, and therapists.
  • Practical cases of chaplaincy care in hospitals.

Methodology:

The course will be delivered through online classes, case study analysis, forum debates, and practical counseling exercises. Collaborative learning and the development of action plans for hospital care will be promoted.

Evaluation:

The course evaluation will include:

  • Theoretical exams on leadership and hospital chaplaincy.
  • Case studies on intervention in critical medical situations.
  • Development of accompaniment strategies for patients and families.
  • Simulations of spiritual care in hospitals.
  • Written reflections on the impact of pastoral leadership in the hospital setting.

Importance of the Course:

Leadership in the hospital setting is crucial for providing comfort, strength, and hope to those facing illness and health crises. This course trains chaplains and spiritual leaders to play a key role in the emotional and spiritual well-being of patients, their families, and medical staff, contributing to more comprehensive care in healthcare centers.
