BACH-CII-100 - Communication and Intercultural Interaction

This course offers a comprehensive opportunity to acquire a deep understanding of communication strategies essential for effective interaction in multicultural and globalized environments—key elements in chaplaincy work. It is designed to equip students with both theoretical and practical tools to address the challenges of intercultural communication in diverse settings, such as hospitals, prisons, religious communities, and other social environments involving individuals from different cultural, linguistic, and religious backgrounds.

Course Objectives:

Upon completion of the course, students will be able to apply intercultural communication strategies that enable them to perform their pastoral duties in a variety of contexts. The course focuses on the following objectives:

  1. Understanding theories of intercultural communication: Students will explore the main theories underpinning communication in multicultural contexts, including high- and low-context communication models, and how these influence the interpretation and reception of spiritual messages in various environments.
  2. Development of cultural perception competencies: The course will examine how cultural perceptions affect human relationships and the way individuals interpret and respond to messages. Tools will be provided to manage these perceptions effectively within chaplaincy settings.
  3. Semiotics applied to religious communication: This component delves into the use of signs and symbols in religious communication. Students will learn how spiritual symbols may carry different meanings across cultures and how to adapt them to be more effective and respectful of the beliefs of those they serve.
  4. Managing intercultural conflict: The course will include strategies for addressing and resolving cultural and ethical conflicts that may arise in chaplaincy scenarios. Students will learn to identify sources of conflict and apply principles of mediation and peaceful resolution in diverse settings.
  5. Adapting spiritual messages to sociolinguistic contexts: Students will explore how language and social customs influence how spiritual messages are received and interpreted. They will learn to adapt sermons, messages, and guidance to ensure cultural relevance and clarity in communities with diverse languages and traditions.
  6. Models of interreligious dialogue: This module will analyze principles and models of interfaith dialogue that promote mutual respect, understanding, and collaboration among people of different religions. Students will learn how to facilitate such dialogues, which are essential in today’s diverse and globalized world.
  7. Impact of values, beliefs, and cultural norms on pastoral interaction: Students will learn how cultural values, beliefs, and norms influence religious practices and pastoral interactions. The importance of acknowledging and respecting these factors when offering spiritual care will be discussed.

Methodology:

The course will employ a variety of active methodologies, including case studies, intercultural communication simulations, online discussions, and real-life scenario analysis. Students will have the opportunity to reflect on their own cultural identity and how it influences their chaplaincy practice, promoting experiential learning that prepares them to face challenges in pastoral work.

Assessment:

Continuous assessment will be employed, based on active participation in discussions, completion of practical projects and case studies, and a final exam evaluating the understanding of concepts and the application of intercultural communication strategies in chaplaincy contexts. Students will also be required to demonstrate practical skills in adapting spiritual messages to diverse cultural and linguistic settings.

This course is an essential component of the Bachelor of Science in Chaplaincy, as it prepares future chaplains to serve diverse and globalized communities. Upon completion, students will be better equipped to interact effectively with individuals from various cultures, ethnicities, and religions, enabling them to fulfill their role with sensitivity, respect, and effectiveness in an interconnected world.
