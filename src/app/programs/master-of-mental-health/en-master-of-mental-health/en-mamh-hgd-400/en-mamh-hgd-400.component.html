MAMH-HGD-400 - Human Growth and Development

The course MAMH-HGD-400 - Human Growth and Development explores the main theories of human development throughout the life cycle, from birth to death. Biological, psychological, social, and cultural factors influencing development are analyzed, with a focus on their application in real contexts with children, adolescents, and adults.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the main theories of human growth and development.
  • Examine biological, psychological, and sociocultural factors influencing development.
  • Analyze the impact of culture and environment on human development.
  • Apply acquired knowledge in practical situations with different age groups.
  • Evaluate intervention strategies to promote healthy development.

Main Topics:

  • Introduction to the study of human growth and development.
  • Main development theories: Piaget, Erikson, Vygotsky, and others.
  • Biological and genetic factors in development.
  • Influence of the family and social environment.
  • Cognitive, emotional, and moral development at each stage of life.
  • Application of theories in professional practice.

Methodology:

The course is developed through analytical readings, case studies, forum discussions, and practical application exercises. Critical reflection and the evaluation of development models in various contexts are emphasized.

Assessment:

Course assessment will include:

  • Essays on theories of human growth and development.
  • Case studies on the impact of environmental and cultural factors.
  • Forum debates on development at different stages of life.
  • Written reflections on practical application experiences.
  • Theoretical assessments on the fundamentals of human development.

Importance of the Course:

The study of human growth and development is essential for understanding the evolution of individuals in different contexts. This course provides key tools to analyze development at different stages and apply intervention strategies that promote individual and social well-being.
