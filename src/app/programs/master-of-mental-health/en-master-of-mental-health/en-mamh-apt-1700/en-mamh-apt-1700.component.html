MAMH-APT-1700 - Advanced Techniques in Psychotherapy

The course MAMH-APT-1700 - Advanced Techniques in Psychotherapy covers advanced therapeutic strategies, including dialectical-behavioral therapy (DBT) and psychodynamic psychotherapy. Students will learn how to integrate these techniques in treating complex disorders, such as borderline personality disorder.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the fundamental principles of advanced psychotherapy techniques.
  • Develop skills to apply DBT and psychodynamic psychotherapy.
  • Evaluate the effectiveness of different therapeutic approaches in complex clinical cases.
  • Explore intervention strategies in the treatment of severe disorders.
  • Analyze ethical aspects in the application of advanced psychotherapeutic techniques.

Main Topics:

  • Fundamentals of dialectical-behavioral therapy (DBT).
  • Psychodynamic psychotherapy and its clinical application.
  • Advanced strategies for managing personality disorders.
  • Intervention in self-destructive behaviors and emotional dysregulation.
  • Use of evidence-based psychotherapy in various psychological disorders.
  • Ethics and legal considerations in advanced psychotherapy.

Methodology:

The course combines theoretical classes, case studies, clinical simulations, and professional supervision sessions. Practical skill development is emphasized through interactive exercises and teamwork.

Assessment:

Course assessment will include:

  • Exams on theories and advanced psychotherapy techniques.
  • Analysis of cases of personality disorders.
  • Essays on the application of therapeutic strategies.
  • Presentations on innovations in psychotherapy.
  • Debates on ethical dilemmas in psychotherapeutic treatment.

Importance of the Course:

The knowledge and mastery of advanced psychotherapy techniques are fundamental for the effective treatment of complex disorders. This course provides essential tools for clinical practice and professional development in the field of psychotherapy.
