The Master’s in Mental Health at World Theology University offers a wide range of courses that cover the fundamentals and key concepts of clinical psychology and mental health. Each course is designed to provide students with both theoretical knowledge and practical skills necessary to excel in the field. Below are the courses in the program, along with their respective code and description:

  • MAMH-FMHCP-100 - Fundamentals of Mental Health Counseling and Perspective: In this course, students examine interventions to improve mental illness and promote psychological well-being. Research and treatment models are integrated to consider mental well-being in terms of pathology and optimal psychological and physical development. The meanings of health and illness in different identities (such as ethnicity, culture, gender, sexuality, class, and age) are explored. Disorders such as depression, anxiety, psychosis, and constructs like resilience, hope, wisdom, and spirituality are also reviewed.
  • MAMH-ICSL-200 - Individual Counseling Skills Laboratory: Students participate in a practical laboratory in small groups, using simulations, modeling, and microcounseling. This course focuses on learning fundamental communication skills necessary for interviews and counseling, applicable to a wide range of professional situations such as counseling with children, adolescents, and adults.
  • MAMH-ICTP-300 - Individual Counseling: Theory and Process: This course reviews the major counseling theories and their application in the social and emotional development of individuals at various life stages. Classical and contemporary counseling theories are addressed, with emphasis on normal and abnormal personality development, as well as the evidence base for the various theories.
  • MAMH-HGD-400 - Human Growth and Development: This course explores the major theories of human growth and development, covering the life cycle from birth to death. Students will learn to assess the multiple factors influencing human development, such as biology and culture, and apply knowledge in real-life situations with children, adolescents, and adults.
  • MAMH-PCEI-500 - Professional Counseling and Ethical Issues in Mental Health: This course offers an in-depth view of the history and ethical issues in the mental health counseling profession. Topics such as professional roles, licenses, certifications, and ethics applied to decision-making in the mental health counseling context are discussed. The ethical aspects of intervention in crisis situations are also explored.
  • MAMH-PMHCP-600 - Practicum in Mental Health Counseling and Perspective: Students apply counseling skills learned in practical settings under professional supervision. This course requires students to complete at least 100 hours of practical experience, with at least 40 hours of direct contact with clients, and participate in weekly supervision sessions. It is a foundational course for developing competencies as professional counselors.
  • MAMH-AP-700 - Abnormal Psychology: This course examines the most common psychological disorders, such as anxiety disorders, mood disorders, eating disorders, substance-related disorders, and more. Students will learn about the classification, etiology, symptoms, and treatments associated with these disorders, as well as the impact they have on individuals throughout their lives.
  • MAMH-REBS-800 - Research and Evaluation in Behavioral Sciences Students will learn to evaluate research studies in the behavioral sciences, with a particular focus on research across the lifespan. Emphasis is placed on scientific methodology, hypothesis development, and ethical evaluation in applied research within the field of clinical psychology.
  • MAMH-GDTP-900 - Group Dynamics Theory and Practice This course offers an introduction to group process theory and the underlying dynamics of group counseling. Students will develop an understanding of group interactions and how to use groups in counseling interventions with children, adolescents, and adults. Emotional, behavioral, and interpersonal dynamics within the group context will be emphasized.
  • MAMH-VDD-1000 - Vocational Development Dynamics: This course explores the nature of work, career patterns, occupational choice, and job satisfaction. Students will analyze how factors such as work, leisure, and education influence vocational development, and how these aspects can be applied to the counseling context.
  • MAMH-IC-1100 - Intercultural Counseling: This course examines how the behavior and experience of individuals are influenced by culture and intersectionality. Students will increase their self-awareness and learn to apply counseling theories and techniques in diverse cultural contexts, improving their skills for working with populations from different backgrounds.
  • MAMH-POV-1200 - Psychology of Violence: This course provides an in-depth analysis of the psychological effects of violence and how these can be treated from a clinical psychology perspective. Students will study the different types of violence, their impact on victims, and the most effective intervention strategies for treatment.
  • MAMH-CBTT-1300 - Cognitive-Behavioral Therapy Theories This course provides an in-depth understanding of the theories and applications of cognitive-behavioral therapy (CBT). Students will learn how CBT can be applied to the treatment of disorders such as anxiety, depression, and behavioral disorders.
  • MAMH-PSYP-1400 - Psychopharmacology: In this course, students are introduced to the use of medications in the treatment of psychological disorders. Students will learn about the different types of drugs used in clinical psychology, how they work, and their effects on patients, as well as studying the interaction between medications and psychotherapy.
  • MAMH-NR-1500 - Neuropsychological Rehabilitation This course focuses on the study of neuropsychology and its application in the rehabilitation of patients with neurological disorders. Students will learn about the assessment and treatment of cognitive, emotional, and behavioral problems related to brain damage.
  • MAMH-IPC-1600 - Intervention in Psychological Crises This course trains students on how to intervene effectively in crisis situations, such as suicide, abuse, severe trauma, or disasters. Students will learn immediate intervention techniques and how to manage the emotional stability of patients in the short and long term.
  • MAMH-APT-1700 - Advanced Psychotherapy Techniques In this course, advanced therapeutic techniques such as dialectical-behavioral therapy (DBT) and psychodynamic psychotherapy are covered. Students will learn how to integrate these techniques into the treatment of complex disorders such as borderline personality disorder.
  • MAMH-CAP-1800 - Child and Adolescent Psychology: This course delves into common psychological disorders in children and adolescents, addressing their diagnosis, treatment, and emotional development in the young. Disorders such as ADHD, childhood depression, and anxiety in youth are covered.
  • MAMH-FCT-1900 - Family and Couples Therapy: This course teaches students to work with families and couples in crisis, using therapeutic approaches that improve family dynamics. Topics such as domestic violence, relational disorders, and conflict management are addressed.
  • MAMH-PAGD-2000 - Psychology of Aging and Geriatric Disorders This course explores aging from a psychological perspective, addressing disorders such as dementia and depression in older age. Students will learn strategies to support elderly patients and improve their quality of life.
  • MAMH-ASP-2100 - Applied Social Psychology: Students will examine how social factors influence behavior and individual decisions. This course addresses group behavior, prejudice and discrimination, and how these phenomena impact the mental health of individuals.
  • MAMH-ED-2200 - Eating Disorders: This course covers the most common eating disorders, such as anorexia and bulimia. Students will learn about the psychological and biological causes, as well as effective treatment strategies in this field.
  • MAMH-FP-2300 - Forensic Psychology: This course provides an understanding of how psychology is applied in the legal context, addressing topics such as competence evaluation and criminal violence. Students will learn to use psychological tools within the judicial system.
