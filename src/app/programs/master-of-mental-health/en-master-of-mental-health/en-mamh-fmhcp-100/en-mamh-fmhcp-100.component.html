MAMH-FMHCP-100 - Fundamentals of Mental Health Counseling and Perspective

The course MAMH-FMHCP-100 - Fundamentals of Mental Health Counseling and Perspective allows students to examine interventions aimed at improving mental illness and promoting psychological well-being. Research and treatment models are integrated to consider mental well-being in terms of pathology and optimal psychological and physical development. The meanings of health and illness across various identities (such as ethnicity, culture, gender, sexuality, class, and age) are explored, along with disorders such as depression, anxiety, and psychosis, alongside constructs such as resilience, hope, wisdom, and spirituality.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the foundations of mental health and its relationship with counseling.
  • Identify intervention models for treating mental illnesses.
  • Explore the meanings of health and illness in diverse identities.
  • Analyze common mental disorders and their treatment approaches.
  • Reflect on the importance of resilience, hope, and spirituality in psychological well-being.

Main Topics:

  • Introduction to mental health counseling.
  • Psychological intervention and treatment models.
  • Mental health and its relationship with ethnicity, culture, gender, and sexuality.
  • Mental disorders: depression, anxiety, and psychosis.
  • Key constructs in psychological well-being: resilience, hope, and wisdom.
  • Exploring spirituality as a supportive factor in mental health.

Methodology:

The course is conducted through online classes, clinical case analysis, discussion forums, and practical counseling exercises. Participatory learning and critical analysis of mental health models are encouraged.

Assessment:

Course assessment will include:

  • Theoretical exams on counseling and mental health models.
  • Case studies on psychological interventions.
  • Developing support strategies for different populations.
  • Simulations of counseling sessions.
  • Written reflections on the impact of mental health in everyday life.

Importance of the Course:

Mental health is an essential component of overall well-being. This course equips students to understand and intervene in psychological problems from an integrative perspective, helping them promote well-being across various populations and contexts.
