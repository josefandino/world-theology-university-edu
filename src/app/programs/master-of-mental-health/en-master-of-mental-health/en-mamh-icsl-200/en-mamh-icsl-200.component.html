MAMH-ICSL-200 - Individual Counseling Skills Laboratory

The course MAMH-ICSL-200 - Individual Counseling Skills Laboratory provides students with a practical space in small groups, where simulations, modeling, and microcounseling are used to strengthen their communication and interviewing skills. It focuses on learning fundamental counseling skills applicable in various professional settings, including work with children, adolescents, and adults.

Course Objectives:

  • Develop essential communication skills for counseling.
  • Practice interviewing strategies in professional contexts.
  • Apply microcounseling techniques in simulated scenarios.
  • Reflect on the importance of empathy and active listening.
  • Adapt counseling strategies to different ages and populations.

Main Topics:

  • Fundamentals of communication in individual counseling.
  • Interviewing techniques and active listening.
  • Microcounseling: application in real and simulated environments.
  • Intervention strategies with children, adolescents, and adults.
  • Modeling and supervised practice in counseling.
  • Assessment and feedback in counseling practice.

Methodology:

The course is conducted through practical workshops, interview simulations, role plays, and observation exercises. Active learning and gradual skill improvement through guided feedback are encouraged.

Assessment:

Course assessment will include:

  • Practical exercises in microcounseling and simulated interviews.
  • Self-assessment and performance analysis in counseling.
  • Feedback from instructors and peers.
  • Case studies on different counseling scenarios.
  • Written reflections on the application of skills in professional contexts.

Importance of the Course:

The development of individual counseling skills is essential for effective communication in professional environments. This course allows students to strengthen their listening, intervention, and support competencies, promoting better understanding and support for people in various life stages.
