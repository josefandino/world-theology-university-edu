MAMH-ICTP-300 - Individual Counseling: Theory and Process

The course MAMH-ICTP-300 - Individual Counseling: Theory and Process examines the main counseling theories and their application in the social and emotional development of individuals throughout their lives. Classic and contemporary counseling theories are addressed, highlighting the normal and abnormal development of personality and its foundation in scientific evidence.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the main theories of individual counseling.
  • Analyze social and emotional development at different stages of life.
  • Explore the application of classical and modern approaches in counseling.
  • Evaluate the scientific basis of personality theories.
  • Relate theories to the professional practice of counseling.

Main Topics:

  • History and fundamentals of individual counseling.
  • Classic counseling theories: psychoanalysis, humanism, and behaviorism.
  • Contemporary models in counseling.
  • Personality development: normality and pathology.
  • Scientific evidence in the application of theories.
  • Integration of theories into professional practice.

Methodology:

The course is developed through critical readings, case analysis, forum discussions, and practical application exercises. Reflective thinking and the evaluation of strategies in various counseling contexts are emphasized.

Assessment:

Course assessment will include:

  • Analytical essays on the main counseling theories.
  • Case studies on social and emotional development.
  • Forum debates on the application of counseling models.
  • Written reflections on the evolution of personality.
  • Theoretical assessments on the fundamentals of counseling.

Importance of the Course:

Understanding the theories of individual counseling is key to developing effective interventions in the professional field. This course provides students with tools to understand and apply evidence-based approaches, promoting a positive impact on people's lives.
