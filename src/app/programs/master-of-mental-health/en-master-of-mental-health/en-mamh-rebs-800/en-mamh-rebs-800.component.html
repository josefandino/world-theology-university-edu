MAMH-REBS-800 - Research and Evaluation in Behavioral Sciences

The course MAMH-REBS-800 - Research and Evaluation in Behavioral Sciences equips students with the tools necessary to analyze and evaluate research studies in the field of behavioral sciences. Particular emphasis is placed on scientific methodology, hypothesis development, and the ethical evaluation of research applied to clinical psychology and other related disciplines.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the fundamentals of research in behavioral sciences.
  • Develop skills to critically evaluate scientific studies in psychology.
  • Apply principles of scientific methodology in research design.
  • Explore ethical aspects in research and its application in professional practice.
  • Analyze research studies in the context of the human life cycle.

Main Topics:

  • Introduction to research in behavioral sciences.
  • Scientific methodology and research design.
  • Hypothesis development and data collection.
  • Ethical evaluation in applied research.
  • Statistics and data analysis in psychology.
  • Interpreting results and applying scientific findings.

Methodology:

The course is developed through theoretical classes, review of scientific articles, practical case analysis, and research project design. Active participation in debates on methodological and ethical issues is encouraged.

Assessment:

Course assessment will include:

  • Theoretical exams on research methodology and design.
  • Critical analysis of scientific studies.
  • Development of a research proposal in clinical psychology.
  • Presentations on ethics in applied research.
  • Final paper on the application of research in the context of the human life cycle.

Importance of the Course:

Research in behavioral sciences is fundamental for advancing knowledge in psychology and related fields. This course provides the necessary foundations for critical understanding and application of scientific studies in clinical and professional contexts, promoting informed and ethically responsible decision-making.
