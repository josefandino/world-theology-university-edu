MAMH-ED-2200 - Eating Disorders

The course MAMH-ED-2200 - Eating Disorders covers the most common eating disorders, such as anorexia and bulimia. Students will learn about the psychological and biological causes, as well as effective treatment strategies in this field.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the main eating disorders and their characteristics.
  • Analyze the psychological, biological, and social causes of these disorders.
  • Explore evidence-based treatments for anorexia, bulimia, and other disorders.
  • Evaluate the impact of eating disorders on physical and mental health.
  • Develop strategies for prevention and early detection of these disorders.

Main Topics:

  • Classification and diagnosis of eating disorders.
  • Psychological and biological factors in the development of these disorders.
  • Impact of media and culture on body image.
  • Therapeutic interventions: cognitive-behavioral therapy and alternative approaches.
  • Prevention strategies and health education on eating habits.

Methodology:

The course combines theoretical classes, case analysis, review of clinical studies, and psychological intervention practices. Discussion and research-based learning are promoted.

Assessment:

Course assessment will include:

  • Exams on theories and diagnosis of eating disorders.
  • Case analysis on anorexia and bulimia.
  • Essays on the social and cultural impact on body image.
  • Presentations on effective treatments and prevention strategies.
  • Debates on the role of society and media in body perception.

Importance of the Course:

Understanding eating disorders is essential for intervention and prevention in clinical and social settings. This course provides key tools for professional practice in psychology, health, and education.
