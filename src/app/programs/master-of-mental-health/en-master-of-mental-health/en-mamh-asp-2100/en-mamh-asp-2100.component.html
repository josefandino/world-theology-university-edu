MAMH-ASP-2100 - Applied Social Psychology

The course MAMH-ASP-2100 - Applied Social Psychology examines how social factors influence individual behavior and decisions. This course covers group behavior, prejudice, and discrimination, and how these phenomena impact individuals' mental health.

Course Objectives:

  • Analyze the impact of social factors on human behavior.
  • Understand the influence of prejudice and discrimination on mental health.
  • Explore group behavior and its effects on decision-making.
  • Apply social psychology theories in clinical and community settings.
  • Develop strategies to mitigate the negative effects of social pressure.

Main Topics:

  • Fundamental theories of social psychology.
  • Prejudice, discrimination, and their psychological effects.
  • The influence of culture and environment on individual behavior.
  • The role of conformity and obedience in society.
  • Strategies to improve social interaction and reduce marginalization.
  • Applications of social psychology in mental health and well-being.

Methodology:

The course combines theoretical classes, case studies, behavior experiments, and analysis of interventions in social settings. Discussion and experiential learning are encouraged.

Assessment:

Course assessment will include:

  • Exams on key theories and concepts in social psychology.
  • Case analysis on prejudice and discrimination in society.
  • Essays on social influence in decision-making.
  • Presentations on interventions to improve social interaction.
  • Debates on the impact of culture and media on behavior.

Importance of the Course:

Understanding applied social psychology is essential to address problems like discrimination, group behavior, and social influence. This course provides key tools for professional practice in various psychological and community contexts.
