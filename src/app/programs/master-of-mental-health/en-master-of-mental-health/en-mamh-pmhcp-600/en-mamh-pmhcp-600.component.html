MAMH-PMHCP-600 - Practicum in Mental Health Counseling and Perspective

The course MAMH-PMHCP-600 - Practicum in Mental Health Counseling and Perspective allows students to apply the skills they have acquired in practical environments under professional supervision. Participants must complete at least 100 hours of practical experience, with a minimum of 40 hours of direct client contact, in addition to attending weekly supervision sessions. This course is crucial for the development of competencies as professional counselors.

Course Objectives:

  • Apply counseling techniques in supervised environments.
  • Develop communication skills and therapeutic relationships with clients.
  • Reflect on practical experiences and improve professional intervention.
  • Participate in weekly supervision for feedback and skill improvement.
  • Encourage professional and ethical growth in counseling practice.

Main Topics:

  • Fundamentals of supervised practice in counseling.
  • Development of therapeutic skills in real-life contexts.
  • Ethics and confidentiality in professional practice.
  • Assessment and diagnosis in counseling.
  • Case management and clinical documentation.
  • Supervision and feedback in counseling.

Methodology:

The course is developed through supervised practice in clinical environments, case analysis, feedback sessions, and written reflections on professional experiences. Experience-based learning and the application of theoretical knowledge in practice are emphasized.

Assessment:

Course assessment will include:

  • Recording of supervised practice hours.
  • Performance evaluations by supervisors.
  • Case reports and analysis of interventions.
  • Written reflections on counseling experiences.
  • Participation in supervision sessions and professional discussions.

Importance of the Course:

This practicum is essential for consolidating the necessary competencies in professional counseling. It provides practical experience, specialized supervision, and professional development opportunities, ensuring the formation of ethical counselors prepared to intervene effectively in mental health.
