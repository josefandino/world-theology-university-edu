MAMH-AP-700 - Abnormal Psychology

The course MAMH-AP-700 - Abnormal Psychology examines the most common psychological disorders, including anxiety disorders, mood disorders, eating disorders, and substance-related disorders. Students will learn about the classification, etiology, symptoms, and treatments of these disorders, as well as their impact on individuals throughout their lives.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the classification and characteristics of psychological disorders.
  • Explore the causes and risk factors associated with mental disorders.
  • Analyze treatment approaches and intervention strategies.
  • Examine the impact of disorders on individuals' development and daily functioning.
  • Reflect on ethical and social aspects in the assessment and treatment of psychological disorders.

Main Topics:

  • Introduction to psychopathology and clinical diagnosis.
  • Anxiety disorders and mood disorders.
  • Eating disorders and substance-related disorders.
  • Psychotic disorders and personality disorders.
  • Therapeutic approaches in the treatment of psychological disorders.
  • Biological, social, and environmental factors in psychopathology.

Methodology:

The course is developed through theoretical classes, case studies, scientific research analysis, and group debates. Emphasis is placed on applying knowledge in the assessment and treatment of psychological disorders in various contexts.

Assessment:

Course assessment will include:

  • Theoretical exams on the classification and diagnosis of disorders.
  • Case study analysis of patients with different disorders.
  • Written reflections on the application of therapeutic approaches.
  • Participation in debates on ethical dilemmas in the treatment of mental disorders.
  • Final paper on a specific disorder and its clinical implications.

Importance of the Course:

Knowledge of abnormal psychology is essential for understanding and effectively addressing mental disorders. This course provides the theoretical and practical foundations necessary for identifying, assessing, and intervening in mental health problems, contributing to individuals' well-being and improving quality of life.
