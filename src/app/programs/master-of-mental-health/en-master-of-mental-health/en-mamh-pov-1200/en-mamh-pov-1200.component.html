MAMH-POV-1200 - Psychology of Violence

The course MAMH-POV-1200 - Psychology of Violence offers a deep analysis of the psychological effects of violence and how these can be treated from a clinical psychology perspective. Students will study different types of violence, their impact on victims, and the most effective intervention strategies for their treatment.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the psychological foundations of violence.
  • Identify different types of violence and their effects on victims.
  • Explore intervention strategies and treatments for addressing violence.
  • Analyze the impact of violence on psychological and social well-being.
  • Apply prevention and recovery models for victims of violence.

Main Topics:

  • Fundamental concepts of violence and its psychological impact.
  • Disorders associated with exposure to violence.
  • Intervention strategies in cases of violence.
  • Prevention and treatment of violence in different contexts.
  • Ethical considerations in addressing violence.
  • Case studies and experiences in clinical practice.

Methodology:

The course is developed through case analysis, academic research, forum debates, and intervention simulations. Experiential learning and the development of practical skills for the treatment of violence are promoted.

Assessment:

Course assessment will include:

  • Exams on theories and intervention models for violence.
  • Analysis of case studies on violence and its effects.
  • Development of intervention and prevention strategies.
  • Simulations of counseling in cases of violence.
  • Written reflections on the impact of violence on mental health.

Importance of the Course:

Violence is a global issue with profound psychological consequences. This course trains students to understand its effects, develop intervention strategies, and contribute to the recovery and well-being of affected individuals.
