MAMH-FCT-1900 - Family and Couples Therapy

The course MAMH-FCT-1900 - Family and Couples Therapy teaches students to work with families and couples in crisis, using therapeutic approaches to improve family dynamics. Topics such as domestic violence, relational disorders, and conflict management are addressed.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand family and couple dynamics in different contexts.
  • Identify and evaluate relational problems within families and couples.
  • Apply therapeutic strategies to improve communication and conflict resolution.
  • Explore the impact of domestic violence on mental health.
  • Analyze intervention techniques to strengthen family structure.

Main Topics:

  • Fundamentals of family and couples therapy.
  • Theories and models of intervention in family therapy.
  • Conflict management and problem-solving in couples.
  • Impact of domestic violence on mental health.
  • Communication strategies and family mediation.
  • Therapeutic interventions in family crises.

Methodology:

The course combines theoretical classes, case studies, supervised practices, and therapeutic simulations. The application of strategies in clinical settings and collaborative work is encouraged.

Assessment:

Course assessment will include:

  • Exams on theories of family and couples therapy.
  • Case analysis of family dynamics.
  • Essays on therapeutic intervention strategies.
  • Presentations on relational disorders and domestic violence.
  • Debates on the importance of therapy in family stability.

Importance of the Course:

Understanding family and couples therapy is essential to address relational crises and strengthen emotional bonds. This course provides key tools for clinical practice and professional development in family counseling.
