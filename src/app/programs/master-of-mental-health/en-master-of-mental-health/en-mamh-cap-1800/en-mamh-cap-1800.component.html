MAMH-CAP-1800 - Child and Adolescent Psychology

The course MAMH-CAP-1800 - Child and Adolescent Psychology explores the most common psychological disorders in children and adolescents, addressing their diagnosis, treatment, and emotional development. Disorders such as ADHD, childhood depression, and anxiety in young people are covered.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the emotional and psychological development in children and adolescents.
  • Identify and diagnose common psychological disorders in childhood and adolescence.
  • Explore effective intervention and treatment strategies.
  • Analyze the impact of environment and family on emotional development.
  • Evaluate the importance of prevention in child mental health.

Main Topics:

  • Fundamentals of child and adolescent psychology.
  • Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD).
  • Childhood and adolescent depression.
  • Anxiety disorders in young people.
  • Risk and protective factors in emotional development.
  • Psychotherapeutic interventions for children and adolescents.

Methodology:

The course combines theoretical classes, case studies, clinical simulations, and professional supervision sessions. Practical skill development is emphasized through interactive exercises and teamwork.

Assessment:

Course assessment will include:

  • Exams on theories of child and adolescent development.
  • Case analysis of clinical issues in children and adolescents.
  • Essays on mental health interventions for youth.
  • Presentations on psychological disorders in childhood.
  • Debates on prevention and treatment strategies.

Importance of the Course:

Knowledge of child and adolescent psychology is essential for early intervention and proper treatment of emotional disorders in young people. This course provides essential tools for clinical practice and professional development in the field of child mental health.
