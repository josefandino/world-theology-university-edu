MAMH-FP-2300 - Forensic Psychology

The course MAMH-FP-2300 - Forensic Psychology provides an understanding of how psychology is applied within the legal context, addressing topics such as competency evaluation and criminal violence. Students will learn to use psychological tools within the judicial system.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the fundamental principles of forensic psychology.
  • Explore psychological assessment processes in legal settings.
  • Analyze the relationship between psychological disorders and criminal behavior.
  • Evaluate the impact of psychology on judicial procedures.
  • Develop skills in drafting psychological reports for forensic cases.

Main Topics:

  • Competency evaluation and criminal responsibility.
  • Psychopathology and criminal behavior.
  • Criminal profiling and behavioral analysis.
  • Witness testimony and credibility in the judicial system.
  • Ethics and professional practices in forensic psychology.

Methodology:

The course combines theoretical lectures, analysis of court cases, review of empirical studies, and psychological assessment simulations. Emphasis is placed on critical thinking and the practical application of acquired knowledge.

Assessment:

Course evaluation will include:

  • Examinations on forensic psychology theory and methodology.
  • Analysis of real cases involving psychological assessments in legal proceedings.
  • Essays on the relationship between psychopathology and crime.
  • Presentations on criminal profiling techniques and expert testimony.
  • Debates on ethical dilemmas in forensic practice.

Course Relevance:

Forensic psychology is essential for understanding criminal behavior and improving the administration of justice. This course provides essential tools for professionals in psychology, criminology, and law.
