MAMH-CBTT-1300 - Cognitive Behavioral Therapy Theories

The course MAMH-CBTT-1300 - Cognitive Behavioral Therapy Theories provides an in-depth understanding of the theories and applications of Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT). Students will learn how CBT can be applied to the treatment of disorders such as anxiety, depression, and conduct disorders.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the theoretical foundations of Cognitive Behavioral Therapy.
  • Explore the application of CBT in various psychological disorders.
  • Analyze the processes of modifying thought and behavior.
  • Develop practical skills for applying cognitive-behavioral techniques.
  • Evaluate the effectiveness of CBT in various clinical contexts.

Main Topics:

  • Fundamentals and principles of Cognitive Behavioral Therapy.
  • Identification and modification of dysfunctional thoughts.
  • Behavioral techniques for changing habits and behavior patterns.
  • Application of CBT in anxiety and depression disorders.
  • Evaluating the effectiveness of CBT in clinical practice.
  • Case studies and practical examples of CBT application.

Methodology:

The course is conducted through case studies, practical exercises, group discussions, and academic research analysis. Active learning is emphasized through the application of CBT techniques in simulated contexts.

Assessment:

Course assessment will include:

  • Exams on CBT theories and models.
  • Practical application of cognitive-behavioral techniques.
  • Case study analysis on CBT treatment.
  • Designing intervention plans based on CBT.
  • Written reflections on the effectiveness of CBT in various disorders.

Importance of the Course:

Cognitive Behavioral Therapy is one of the most effective interventions in clinical psychology. This course equips students with practical knowledge to address psychological disorders in a structured, evidence-based manner.
