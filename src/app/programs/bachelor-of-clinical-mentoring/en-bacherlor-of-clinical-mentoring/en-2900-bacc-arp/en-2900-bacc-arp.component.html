BACC-ARP-2900 - Applied Research in Psychology

The course Applied Research in Psychology introduces students to research methods used in the fields of clinical and social psychology. Emphasis is placed on the design, execution, and analysis of scientific studies aimed at understanding and improving human behavior.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the fundamental principles of the scientific method in psychology.
  • Design relevant research in clinical and social contexts.
  • Apply quantitative and qualitative techniques for data collection and analysis.
  • Interpret results of psychological research with academic rigor.
  • Develop skills to write scientific research reports.

Main Topics:

  • Introduction to the scientific method and types of research.
  • Experimental and non-experimental designs.
  • Measurement instruments in clinical and social psychology.
  • Basic statistics for analyzing psychological data.
  • Writing reports and presenting results.

Methodology:

The course combines theoretical classes, practical workshops, analysis of scientific articles, development of research projects, and group discussions. Active participation and critical thinking are encouraged.

Assessment:

The course assessment will include:

  • Theoretical exams on research methods and designs.
  • Development of a research project, either individually or in teams.
  • Critical analysis of scientific articles.
  • Oral presentations of research progress and results.

Importance of the Course:

Applied research is fundamental to generating evidence-based knowledge and improving clinical and social practices. This course prepares students to contribute to the advancement of psychology with solid and relevant research.
