BACC-FCT-1600 - Family and Couples Therapy

The course Family and Couples Therapy covers clinical techniques and approaches for intervening in conflict dynamics within the family unit and romantic relationships. Students will learn therapeutic strategies to foster communication, resolve conflicts, and promote healthy relationships.

Course Objectives:

  • Study the theoretical foundations of family and couples therapy.
  • Explore different intervention models for family and marital relationships.
  • Develop skills to identify dysfunctional patterns in relational dynamics.
  • Apply therapeutic techniques to improve communication and conflict resolution.
  • Promote the strengthening of emotional bonds in family and couples contexts.

Main Topics:

  • Sociological and structural models in family therapy.
  • Power dynamics and roles in couples relationships.
  • Strategies for managing conflicts and relational crises.
  • Effective communication techniques and active listening.
  • Assessment and intervention in common family and couples issues.

Methodology:

The course includes theoretical classes, clinical case analysis, dramatizations, and therapeutic session simulations. Active student participation is encouraged to develop practical skills in family and couples counseling.

Assessment:

The course assessment will include:

  • Written tests on theories and intervention models.
