BACC-CAP-1800 - Child and Adolescent Psychology

The course Child and Adolescent Psychology focuses on the study of disorders, emotional development, and the psychological needs of children and adolescents. The factors influencing their behavior are explored, as well as intervention strategies adapted to these stages of development.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand psychological development in childhood and adolescence.
  • Identify common emotional and behavioral disorders at these stages.
  • Explore therapeutic approaches appropriate for children and adolescents.
  • Analyze the impact of the family and social environment on youth development.
  • Design effective interventions to promote emotional well-being.

Main Topics:

  • Stages of cognitive and emotional development.
  • Developmental, behavioral, and learning disorders.
  • Risk and protective factors in childhood and adolescence.
  • Early intervention and emotional support.
  • Family-school-therapist relationship in the intervention process.

Methodology:

The course combines lectures, case study analysis, group discussions, and observation practices. An integrative perspective is encouraged, based on respect for the stages of child and adolescent development.

Assessment:

The course assessment will include:

  • Theoretical tests on development and psychological disorders.
  • Case studies related to children and adolescents.
  • Design of intervention strategies.
  • Active participation in discussions and therapeutic simulations.

Importance of the Course:

This course provides a solid foundation for the psychological support of children and adolescents, preparing students to detect, understand, and address their emotional needs from a clinical and ethical perspective.
