BACC-PI-800 - Psychopathology I

The course Psychopathology I offers an introductory study of the most common mental disorders, their symptoms, diagnostic criteria, and treatment approaches. The clinical classification of psychological disorders is analyzed from an integrative perspective.

  • Learn about the main mental disorders according to current diagnostic classifications.
  • Understand the symptoms, causes, and manifestations of each disorder.
  • Explore the therapeutic approaches used in the treatment of mental illnesses.
  • Develop basic clinical observation and diagnostic skills.
  • Reflect on the impact of psychological suffering on the individualâ€™s life and their environment.

Main Topics:

  • Anxiety disorders and mood disorders.
  • Personality disorders.
  • Psychotic disorders and schizophrenia.
  • Diagnostic assessment: DSM and ICD criteria.
  • General principles of psychological intervention.

Methodology:

The course will be delivered through theoretical classes, case analysis, review of diagnostic manuals, and reflection activities. Critical thinking and a deep understanding of mental functioning will be encouraged.

Assessment:

The course assessment will include:

  • Written tests on classification and diagnosis of disorders.
  • Analysis of real or simulated clinical cases.
  • Essays on therapeutic approaches and the evolution of psychopathology.
  • Active participation in debates and presentations.

Course Significance:

Knowledge of psychopathology is essential for understanding human behavior in its various forms. This course lays the foundation for informed, empathetic, and professional clinical practice.
