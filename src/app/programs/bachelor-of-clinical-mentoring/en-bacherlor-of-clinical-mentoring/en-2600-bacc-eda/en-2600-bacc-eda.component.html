BACC-EDA-2600 - Eating Disorders and Addictions

The course Eating Disorders and Addictions addresses the main issues associated with addictions and eating disorders. It analyzes their causes, clinical characteristics, diagnosis, and therapeutic approaches from a comprehensive perspective.

Course Objectives:

  • Identify the types and characteristics of eating disorders and addictions.
  • Understand the psychological, biological, and social factors influencing these issues.
  • Explore clinical and community intervention strategies.
  • Develop skills for therapeutic support of individuals with these conditions.
  • Promote an empathetic and ethical attitude in professional practice.

Main Topics:

  • Classification and diagnosis of eating disorders.
  • Types of addictions: substance, behavioral, and technological.
  • Risk factors and explanatory models.
  • Multidisciplinary treatments and therapeutic approaches.
  • Prevention, education, and social reintegration.

Methodology:

The course is conducted through theoretical classes, clinical case analysis, group discussions, review of scientific literature, and simulations of therapeutic interventions.

Assessment:

The course assessment will include:

  • Theoretical exams on addictions and eating disorders.
  • Group presentations on real or hypothetical clinical cases.
  • Critical essays on intervention models.
  • Participation in discussion forums and practical activities.

Importance of the Course:

This course trains students to identify, understand, and effectively address two of the most common mental health issues in today's society. It is essential for the development of informed, sensitive, and effective clinical practice.
