BACH-SP-1200 - Social Psychology

The course Social Psychology examines how society, culture, and the environment influence human thoughts, emotions, and behaviors. Topics such as social identity, prejudice, conformity, persuasion, and interpersonal relationships are explored.

  • Analyze how the social environment affects individual and group behavior.
  • Understand the processes of social influence, leadership, and group dynamics.
  • Explore key concepts such as prejudice, discrimination, and conformity.
  • Apply principles of social psychology to real-life situations.
  • Foster critical awareness of the social factors that shape human behavior.

Main Topics:

  • Social perception and identity construction.
  • Attitudes, persuasion, and behavior change.
  • Social influence, obedience, and conformity.
  • Group dynamics and leadership.
  • Prejudice, stereotypes, and intergroup relations.

Methodology:

The course will be delivered through interactive classes, case analysis, debates, guided readings, and group exercises. Collaborative learning and reflective thinking are encouraged.

Assessment:

The course assessment will include:

  • Theoretical exams on key concepts of social psychology.
  • Analysis of social situations from a psychological perspective.
  • Written assignments on topics such as identity, prejudice, or leadership.
  • Participation in debates and group dynamics.

Course Significance:

Understanding social psychology is essential for those working in counseling, education, leadership, or any field involving human interaction. This course provides fundamental tools for analyzing behavior from a social and relational perspective.
