BACC-PAI-1900 - Psychological Assessment I

The course Psychological Assessment I introduces the fundamental methods and tools used in psychological assessment in various clinical, educational, and organizational contexts. Students will learn to apply basic assessment instruments to obtain relevant information about individuals' psychological functioning.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the theoretical foundations of psychological assessment.
  • Explore different methods of collecting psychological data.
  • Learn how to select and apply psychological tests according to the context.
  • Develop skills in interpreting results.
  • Begin the preparation of basic psychological reports.

Main Topics:

  • Introduction to psychological assessment.
  • Clinical interview and projective techniques.
  • Psychometric tests: reliability and validity.
  • Assessment in clinical, educational, and work settings.
  • Ethical principles in psychological assessment.

Methodology:

The course will be conducted through theoretical lectures, analysis of real instruments, supervised practices, case discussions, and group activities. Emphasis will be placed on the ethical and contextual application of assessment tools.

Assessment:

The course assessment will include:

  • Theoretical tests on key concepts and psychological tools.
  • Practical application of assessment instruments.
  • Analysis of case studies with result interpretation.
  • Preparation of basic psychological reports.
  • Active participation in practical activities and discussions.

Importance of the Course:

Psychological assessment is an essential tool for understanding human behavior and making informed decisions in clinical and educational settings. This course provides the basic knowledge to begin assessing in an ethical, accurate, and professional manner.
