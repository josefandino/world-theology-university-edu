BACC-IP-100 - Introduction to Psychology

The course Introduction to Psychology presents the basic foundations of psychology as a science, exploring its history, key principles, and the theoretical approaches that have influenced its development. It provides an essential foundation for understanding human behavior from a scientific perspective.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand psychology as a scientific discipline.
  • Familiarize with the main psychological approaches and theories.
  • Explore the historical milestones in the development of psychology.
  • Identify research methods used in psychology.
  • Relate psychological concepts to everyday situations and counseling contexts.

Main Topics:

  • Definition and objectives of psychology.
  • Psychological schools and theories (behaviorism, psychoanalysis, humanism, etc.).
  • History and evolution of psychology.
  • Research methods in psychology.
  • Basic applications of psychology in personal and community contexts.

Methodology:

The course will be developed through theoretical classes, guided readings, analysis of fundamental concepts, reflective debates, and practical exercises aimed at understanding human behavior.

Assessment:

The course assessment will include:

  • Written exams on key psychology concepts.
  • Essays on psychological theories and their practical application.
  • Participation in discussions on the impact of psychology on daily life.
  • Short projects analyzing historical and scientific aspects.

Course Significance:

This course provides the foundation for understanding how the mind and human behavior work. It is essential for students preparing for counseling roles, offering key tools to understand people from both a scientific and human perspective.
