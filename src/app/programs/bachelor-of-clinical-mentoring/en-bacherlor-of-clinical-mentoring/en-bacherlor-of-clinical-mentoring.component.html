Bachelor's Degree in Clinical Counseling

The Bachelor’s Degree in Clinical Counseling is designed for professionals committed to the emotional and psychological well-being of individuals and communities. This program offers comprehensive training, combining theoretical and practical foundations in the field of clinical psychology, adapted to address the diverse needs of individuals, regardless of their beliefs or cultural backgrounds. The goal is to train counselors who are equipped to provide effective care in crisis situations, emotional trauma, and other psychological challenges.

100% Online: Flexibility to study from anywhere

This program is fully online, offering you the opportunity to study from anywhere in the world. With a flexible structure, you can adapt your study schedule to your professional and personal responsibilities, allowing you to progress at your own pace while connecting with a global community of students and professionals.

Professional Opportunities

Upon completing the Bachelor’s Degree in Clinical Counseling, you will be prepared to work in a wide range of sectors. Graduates of this program will be able to work as clinical counselors in mental health centers, non-profit organizations, private clinics, educational institutions, and the corporate sector. Additionally, you will have the necessary training to support individuals with specific emotional and psychological needs, providing assistance through effective and personalized interventions.

Key Skills in Clinical Counseling

Throughout the program, you will acquire a series of essential skills that will enable you to make a significant difference in the lives of those you serve. Among the most prominent skills are:

  • Psychological assessment: You will develop the ability to identify and assess various psychological disorders using evidence-based diagnostic techniques.
  • Intervention and treatment: You will learn to apply therapeutic methods tailored to the needs of each individual, including cognitive-behavioral therapy, humanistic therapy, and integrative approaches.
  • Active listening and empathy: You will improve your communication skills, with a focus on active listening and empathy, essential for building an effective therapeutic bond.

High-Level Academic Training

Our academic staff is composed of experts in clinical psychology, with extensive experience in both research and professional practice. This program is supported by over 50 years of advancements in the field of psychology, providing you with world-class education, integrating the best of theory and clinical practice.

Global Support Network

By studying the Bachelor’s Degree in Clinical Counseling, you will be part of a global network of professionals committed to emotional and psychological well-being. The online learning platform will allow you to interact with colleagues from around the world, participating in discussions, collaborative projects, and intercultural learning experiences, enriching your perspective and professional development.

A Transformative Approach

This program prepares you to work effectively in the field of mental health, addressing a wide range of emotional challenges, from anxiety and depression to eating disorders and existential crises. Throughout your training, you will learn to offer support that promotes both emotional health and the overall well-being of individuals, with a focus on restoration and personal development.

The Bachelor’s Degree in Clinical Counseling is designed to provide you with the necessary tools to work competently, ethically, and effectively, both in clinical settings and other environments where emotional well-being is a priority.

Credits and Courses

The program consists of a total of 120 credits, distributed between core courses, electives, and optional courses, allowing students to personalize their learning based on their interests and specializations. The core courses include:

  • Clinical Psychology
  • Foundations of Counseling
  • Crisis Intervention
  • Cognitive and Behavioral Therapies
  • Psychopathology

The elective courses offer the opportunity to explore specialized areas of interest, such as family counseling, group therapy, or child mental health.

Thesis or Comprehensive Exam

Upon completing the program, students must choose to complete either a thesis or a comprehensive exam, which will allow them to demonstrate their mastery of the concepts learned. The program is designed to be completed within a flexible period, with the option of part-time or full-time study, and a maximum timeframe of five years for completion.

Applicants for the Bachelor’s Degree in Clinical Counseling must meet a series of requirements that ensure their preparation and ability to successfully complete the program. The following are the necessary requirements:

Official Academic Transcripts

Official academic transcripts from all previous studies, whether at the secondary or higher education level, must be submitted. The applicant must have completed secondary education and preferably a program in fields related to social sciences, health, or related areas, with outstanding academic performance.

Curriculum Vitae

The applicant must provide an up-to-date curriculum vitae that includes their professional experience, volunteering, or any relevant training in the fields of health, social well-being, or counseling. This document should reflect the competencies acquired through experiences related to psychology or related fields.

Statement of Purpose

The applicant must write a motivation letter explaining their reasons for applying to the Bachelor’s Degree in Clinical Counseling. They should detail how the program will contribute to their professional and personal goals, and how they plan to apply the knowledge gained in their work or community setting.

Letters of Recommendation

At least two letters of recommendation are required from professors, employers, or mentors who can attest to the applicant’s abilities in leadership, community work, or mental health. The letters should address the candidate's suitability for the program and their potential to apply what they learn in a practical context.

Relevant Experience

Although not a formal requirement, it is recommended that applicants have prior experience in support or care roles in areas related to health, psychology, or social well-being. This experience may include volunteer work in mental health organizations, support for vulnerable groups, or participation in community projects.

Personal Interview

In some cases, the admissions committee may request a personal or online interview with the applicant. This interview aims to assess the applicant’s commitment to the program, their academic and personal preparation, as well as their motivation to contribute to the field of clinical counseling.

Application Fee

All applicants must pay a non-refundable application fee when submitting their application. This fee covers administrative costs related to the review of the application.

Recommended Prior Knowledge

Although not mandatory, it is recommended that applicants have completed prior courses in topics related to psychology, mental health, or social studies. This prior knowledge will facilitate understanding of advanced topics addressed in the program.

Academic Requirements During the Program

Once admitted to the program, students must meet the following academic requirements to complete the Bachelor’s Degree in Clinical Counseling:

  • Total Credits: The student must complete a total of 120 credits, distributed among core courses, electives, and optional courses.
  • Minimum GPA: A minimum GPA of B or higher must be maintained in all courses in order to advance in the program and complete the final thesis or comprehensive exam.
  • Thesis or Comprehensive Exam: At the end of the program, students must choose between developing a research thesis (6 credits) focused on an area of clinical psychology or presenting a comprehensive exam that assesses their mastery of the key areas of the program.
  • Study Mode: The program is 100% online and can be completed on a part-time or full-time basis, depending on the student's needs. However, all academic work and the final project (thesis or exam) must be completed within a maximum period of five years.

These requirements ensure that students are adequately prepared both academically and professionally to perform clinical counseling work in various contexts, combining scientific approaches with solid ethical principles.
