BACC-BBB-2400 - Biological Bases of Behavior

The course Biological Bases of Behavior provides an understanding of the neurobiological foundations that influence human behavior. The nervous and endocrine systems are studied, as well as their relationship with emotions, perception, learning, and mental disorders.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the structure and function of the nervous system.
  • Explore how biological processes affect human behavior.
  • Analyze the influence of neurotransmitters on mental health.
  • Understand the relationship between biology, emotions, and behavior.
  • Relate neurobiological knowledge to clinical approaches.

Main Topics:

  • Basic neuroanatomy and brain functions.
  • Neurotransmitters and their impact on behavior.
  • Endocrine system and its relationship with emotions.
  • Relationship between biology and psychological disorders.
  • Brain plasticity and human development.

Methodology:

The course combines theoretical classes, review of scientific literature, clinical case analysis, and audiovisual resources. Active learning is promoted through discussion forums and practical observation activities.

Assessment:

The course assessment will include:

  • Written tests on neurobiology and behavior.
  • Essays on the link between biology and mental health.
  • Case studies on disorders with a neurobiological basis.
  • Participation in practical activities and discussions.
  • Final project integrating course content.

Importance of the Course:

Understanding the biological bases of behavior allows for a more comprehensive and accurate clinical approach. This knowledge is key to developing effective interventions in counseling and psychology, and for improving the treatment of mental disorders from a multidisciplinary perspective.
