BACC-PI-1700 - Psychosocial intervention

The course Psychosocial Intervention introduces students to the approaches and strategies used in community and group contexts. Intervention methods aimed at strengthening resilience, social cohesion, and psychological well-being in vulnerable or at-risk populations are explored.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the theoretical foundations of psychosocial intervention.
  • Analyze the role of the professional in group and community settings.
  • Design intervention strategies focused on community empowerment.
  • Identify risk and protective factors in diverse social contexts.
  • Develop intervention projects applicable to specific groups.

Main Topics:

  • Crisis and collective disaster intervention models.
  • Community psychology and networking.
  • Design and evaluation of psychosocial programs.
  • Group dynamics and leadership in change processes.
  • Ethics and the role of the professional in psychosocial intervention.

Methodology:

The course will be conducted through lectures, practical workshops, case studies, and the development of intervention projects in simulated communities. Collaborative work and critical reflection will be encouraged.

Assessment:

The course assessment will include:

  • Theoretical exams on key concepts.
  • Design of intervention proposals in specific contexts.
  • Analysis of real or simulated community experiences.
  • Active participation in group activities and discussions.

Importance of the Course:

Psychosocial intervention is key to addressing complex social issues from a preventive and restorative approach. This course prepares students to effectively support groups and communities toward overall well-being.
