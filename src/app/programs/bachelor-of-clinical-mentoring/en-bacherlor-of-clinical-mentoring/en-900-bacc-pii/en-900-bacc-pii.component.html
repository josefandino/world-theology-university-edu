BACC-PII-900 - Psychopathology II

The course Psychopathology II is a continuation of the study of mental disorders, focusing on more complex diagnoses and less common clinical conditions. It delves into understanding the symptoms, causes, and advanced treatments for more effective intervention.

Course Objectives:

  • Expand knowledge on more complex mental disorders.
  • Analyze differential diagnoses and common comorbidities.
  • Study clinical cases that require in-depth evaluations and specialized therapeutic approaches.
  • Strengthen clinical skills for observation, analysis, and intervention.
  • Integrate acquired knowledge with the ethical and professional practice of counseling.

Main Topics:

  • Dissociative and neurodevelopmental disorders.
  • Somatoform and psychosomatic disorders.
  • Disorders related to trauma and stress factors.
  • Differential diagnosis and clinical comorbidities.
  • Advanced clinical assessment and intervention strategies.

Methodology:

The course is based on lectures, the study of complex cases, guided discussions, reading of specialized articles, and practical diagnostic exercises. The integration of theory and practice is encouraged.

Assessment:

The course assessment will include:

  • Theoretical exams on complex diagnoses.
  • Clinical analysis of cases with multiple disorders.
  • Essays on specialized topics in psychopathology.
  • Presentations on advanced clinical interventions.

Course Significance:

Psychopathology II strengthens students' ability to tackle challenging clinical cases with an integrative and professional perspective. This knowledge is essential for providing effective and ethical therapeutic support to individuals facing severe mental conditions.
