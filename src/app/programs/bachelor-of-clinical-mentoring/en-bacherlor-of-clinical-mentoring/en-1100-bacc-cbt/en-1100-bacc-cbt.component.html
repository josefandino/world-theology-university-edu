BACH-CBT-1100 - Cognitive behavioral therapy

The course Cognitive Behavioral Therapy introduces the principles, techniques, and foundations of this therapeutic approach, widely used in the treatment of emotional and behavioral disorders. It focuses on identifying and modifying dysfunctional thoughts and behaviors to improve the individualâ€™s well-being.

  • Understand the theoretical foundations of Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT).
  • Identify the relationship between thoughts, emotions, and behaviors.
  • Apply basic cognitive restructuring techniques.
  • Design behavioral intervention plans for different types of cases.
  • Develop practical skills for therapeutic accompaniment based on CBT.

Main Topics:

  • Origins and foundations of CBT.
  • Cognitive distortions and their impact on behavior.
  • Cognitive restructuring techniques.
  • Basic behavioral interventions.
  • Applications of CBT in anxiety, depression, and other disorders.

Methodology:

The course combines theoretical classes with practical exercises, case analysis, role-playing, and supervised practices. Active learning is encouraged through training in therapeutic skills.

Assessment:

The course assessment will include:

  • Written exams on the principles and techniques of CBT.
  • Analysis of clinical cases with a cognitive-behavioral approach.
  • Development of therapeutic intervention plans.
  • Participation in simulations and practical activities.

Course Significance:

Cognitive Behavioral Therapy is one of the most effective and recognized methodologies in current clinical psychology. This course provides the necessary tools to understand and apply this approach in real counseling and emotional support contexts.
