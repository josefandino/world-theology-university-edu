BACC-AECP-3000 - Advanced Ethics in Clinical Psychology

The course Advanced Ethics in Clinical Psychology delves into the most complex ethical dilemmas faced by professionals in clinical contexts. Advanced cases, ethical principles, legal frameworks, and contemporary challenges in clinical decision-making are analyzed.

Course Objectives:

  • Analyze ethical principles applied to complex clinical situations.
  • Evaluate ethical conflicts in the therapist-patient relationship.
  • Understand the legal and professional impact of clinical decisions.
  • Apply professional ethical codes to real and simulated cases.
  • Develop sound criteria for making ethically informed decisions.

Main Topics:

  • Confidentiality and the limits of professional secrecy.
  • Informed consent in vulnerable populations.
  • Dual relationships and conflicts of interest.
  • Interventions in situations of risk or harm.
  • Legal regulations and professional ethical codes.

Methodology:

The course will be conducted through clinical case analysis, ethical debates, review of regulations, decision-making simulations, and directed readings. Critical reflection and ethical argumentation will be encouraged.

Assessment:

The course assessment will include:

  • Critical essays on clinical ethical dilemmas.
  • Participation in ethical debates and forums.
  • Case studies with ethical conflict resolution.
  • Written tests on regulations and professional codes.

Importance of the Course:

Understanding and applying advanced ethics is essential to ensure responsible and professional psychological practice. This course strengthens the student's ethical judgment in the face of the complex challenges of modern clinical practice.
