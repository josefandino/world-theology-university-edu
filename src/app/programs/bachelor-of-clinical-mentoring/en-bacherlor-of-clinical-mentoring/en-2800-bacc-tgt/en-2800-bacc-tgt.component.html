BACC-TGT-2800 - Third-Generation Therapies

The course Third-Generation Therapies introduces students to contemporary therapeutic approaches such as Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT) and Dialectical Behavior Therapy (DBT), which integrate mindfulness, acceptance, and behavioral change for the treatment of various psychological conditions.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the theoretical foundations of third-generation therapies.
  • Explore the core principles and techniques of ACT and DBT.
  • Analyze the effectiveness of these therapies in diverse clinical contexts.
  • Apply intervention strategies based on acceptance, commitment, and emotional regulation.
  • Encourage the use of practical skills for present-focused therapeutic work.

Main Topics:

  • Principles of third-generation therapies.
  • Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT): values, acceptance, mindfulness.
  • Dialectical Behavior Therapy (DBT): emotional regulation, distress tolerance, interpersonal skills.
  • Clinical applications of ACT and DBT in anxiety, depression, personality disorders, and more.
  • Integration of these therapies into individual and group clinical work.

Methodology:

The course includes theoretical presentations, guided mindfulness practices, clinical case analysis, therapeutic simulations, and group discussions on the application of ACT and DBT in contemporary clinical settings.

Assessment:

The course assessment will include:

  • Theoretical tests on the fundamentals of ACT and DBT.
  • Clinical case presentations applying the principles of the studied therapies.
  • Reflective essays on the implementation of acceptance and change strategies.
  • Practical exercises and participation in simulated sessions.

Importance of the Course:

Third-generation therapies have proven highly effective in the treatment of emotional and behavioral disorders. This course provides innovative, evidence-based tools that enrich clinical practice and improve therapeutic outcomes.
