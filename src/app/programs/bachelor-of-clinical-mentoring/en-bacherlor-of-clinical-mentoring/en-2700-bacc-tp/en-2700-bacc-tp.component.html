BACC-TP-2700- Trauma Psychology

The course Trauma Psychology focuses on the treatment and evaluation of psychological trauma, post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD), and its consequences on mental health. Clinical approaches are discussed to understand and support individuals affected by traumatic experiences.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the fundamentals of psychological trauma and its clinical manifestations.
  • Identify the symptoms and diagnostic criteria for post-traumatic stress disorder.
  • Explore the effects of trauma on emotional, cognitive, and behavioral functioning.
  • Apply appropriate evaluation and intervention strategies in trauma treatment.
  • Promote clinical sensitivity and empathy in therapeutic support.

Main Topics:

  • Definition and types of trauma.
  • Post-traumatic stress disorder: symptoms, diagnosis, and progression.
  • Neurobiology of trauma and stress response.
  • Clinical intervention techniques for trauma treatment.
  • Complex trauma, resilience, and emotional recovery.

Methodology:

The course combines theoretical classes, clinical case analysis, debates, review of specialized literature, and practical application exercises focused on therapeutic work with traumatized individuals.

Assessment:

The course assessment will include:

  • Written tests on the fundamentals of trauma and PTSD.
  • Analysis and presentation of real or simulated clinical cases.
  • Essays on the effects of trauma at different stages of development.
  • Active participation in discussions and practical workshops.

Importance of the Course:

Understanding trauma is essential for any mental health professional. This course provides key tools to effectively identify, assess, and treat trauma, promoting recovery and well-being for those who have experienced adverse events.
