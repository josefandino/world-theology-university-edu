BACC-GP-300 - General Psychology

The course General Psychology introduces students to the fundamental principles of psychology as a science, exploring key theories and their applications in everyday and professional life.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the basic concepts of psychology and its evolution as a scientific discipline.
  • Explore major psychological theories and their proponents.
  • Analyze mental and behavioral processes from various psychological approaches.
  • Apply psychological principles in personal, community, and professional contexts.
  • Develop critical thinking around contemporary psychological issues.

Main Topics:

  • History and methods of psychology.
  • Processes of perception, learning, and memory.
  • Motivation and emotions.
  • Personality and developmental theories.
  • Social psychology and group behavior.

Methodology:

The course is delivered through lectures, guided readings, case analysis, simple experiments, and debates on real-life situations related to human behavior.

Assessment:

The course assessment will include:

  • Exams on psychological fundamentals and theories.
  • Essays on the practical applications of psychology.
  • Case studies on human behavior.
  • Participation in discussion forums and group presentations.

Course Significance:

General psychology provides an essential foundation for understanding human behavior. This knowledge is key for those seeking to apply psychological principles in areas such as counseling, leadership, education, and emotional support.
