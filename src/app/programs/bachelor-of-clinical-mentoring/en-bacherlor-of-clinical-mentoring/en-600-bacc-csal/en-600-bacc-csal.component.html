BACC-CSAL-600 - Communication Skills and Active Listening

The course Communication Skills and Active Listening provides a solid foundation in effective communication techniques, with an emphasis on empathetic listening and the development of key interpersonal skills for emotional and spiritual support.

  • Develop verbal and non-verbal communication skills in counseling contexts.
  • Encourage the practice of active listening as an emotional support tool.
  • Identify common barriers in communication and strategies to overcome them.
  • Promote empathy and deep understanding in interpersonal relationships.
  • Apply principles of effective communication in crisis situations and pastoral care.

Main Topics:

  • Fundamental elements of interpersonal communication.
  • Active listening and empathy.
  • Body language and non-verbal cues.
  • Communication in conflict or crisis contexts.
  • Ethics in communication in counseling contexts.

Methodology:

The course includes practical exercises, counseling simulations, case analysis, group discussions, and workshops focused on improving expression and conscious listening.

Assessment:

The course assessment will include:

  • Recorded practices of active listening sessions.
  • Reflective reports on communicative experiences.
  • Presentations on effective communication techniques.
  • Active participation in workshops and debates.

Course Significance:

The development of communication and active listening skills is essential for establishing trust and support relationships. This course prepares students to interact with sensitivity, respect, and effectiveness in counseling contexts.
