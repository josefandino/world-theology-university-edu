BACC-ACI-3100 - Advanced crisis intervention

The course Advanced Crisis Intervention provides specialized training in techniques for addressing complex psychological and emotional crises. It is designed to prepare students to manage critical situations that require rapid, ethical, and effective clinical responses.

Course Objectives:

  • Identify different types of complex crises and their psychological implications.
  • Apply advanced intervention techniques in high emotional risk situations.
  • Develop skills for rapid and effective crisis assessment.
  • Implement clinical and ethical protocols in psychological emergency contexts.
  • Strengthen resilience and self-care for professionals during crisis intervention.

Main Topics:

  • Advanced models of crisis intervention.
  • Suicidal crises and management of self-harming behaviors.
  • Intervention in collective trauma and natural disasters.
  • Crisis in clinical and hospital settings.
  • Emotional containment strategies and acute stress management.

Methodology:

The course is delivered through theoretical classes, clinical simulations, real case analysis, and practical exercises in crisis scenarios. Critical discussion and collaborative learning are encouraged.

Importance of the Course:

Complex crises require rapid and specialized intervention. This course equips future professionals with essential tools to provide effective psychological support in high-impact emotional and psychological situations.
