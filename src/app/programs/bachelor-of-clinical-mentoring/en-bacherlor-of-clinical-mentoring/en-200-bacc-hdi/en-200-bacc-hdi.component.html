BACC-HDI-200 - Human Development I

The course Human Development I offers a comprehensive view of human development from childhood to adolescence, exploring the physical, emotional, cognitive, and social changes that occur during these crucial stages of growth.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the stages of human development from birth to adolescence.
  • Identify the factors that influence physical, emotional, and cognitive development.
  • Explore key theories of child and adolescent development.
  • Relate human development to family, social, and educational contexts.
  • Apply development principles in counseling and guidance scenarios.

Main Topics:

  • Theories of human development.
  • Development during prenatal stages and early childhood.
  • Development in middle and late childhood.
  • Physical and emotional changes during adolescence.
  • Family, cultural, and social influences on development.

Methodology:

This course is taught through theoretical lectures, case analysis, documentary research, practical activities, and discussions based on real-life experiences of child and adolescent development.

Assessment:

The course assessment will include:

  • Written tests on development theories and stages.
  • Reflective essays on growth and maturity experiences.
  • Case studies on development at different ages.
  • Group presentations on specific human development topics.

Course Significance:

Understanding human development from childhood to adolescence is essential for those working in counseling, education, and community leadership. This course helps identify key emotional and cognitive needs for appropriate guidance at each life stage.
