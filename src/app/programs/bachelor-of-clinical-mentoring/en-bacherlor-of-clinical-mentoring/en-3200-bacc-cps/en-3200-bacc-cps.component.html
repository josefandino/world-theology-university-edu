BACC-CPS-3200 - Consultation and Professional Supervision

The course Consultation and Professional Supervision provides tools and skills for supervising, advising, and guiding other professionals in the field of clinical counseling. It focuses on the development of leadership, evaluation, and professional mentoring competencies.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the principles and models of supervision in clinical counseling.
  • Develop skills for providing effective and constructive feedback.
  • Learn consultation techniques for ongoing professional development.
  • Strengthen professional ethics in supervision contexts.
  • Apply strategies to support the growth of other clinical counselors.

Main Topics:

  • Models of clinical supervision.
  • Supervisor-supervisee relationship.
  • Evaluation of professional performance.
  • Consultation in clinical and organizational contexts.
  • Ethics and boundaries in professional supervision.

Methodology:

The course combines lectures, case studies, simulated supervision exercises, analysis of recorded sessions, and ethical reflection workshops. Supervised practice and collaborative learning are encouraged.

Assessment:

The course assessment will include:

  • Case studies on supervision and consultation.
  • Simulated supervision sessions.
  • Essays on supervision models and professional ethics.
  • Individual presentations on consultation strategies.

Importance of the Course:

Supervision and consultation are essential to maintaining the quality of clinical work. This course prepares students to assume leadership and professional mentoring roles with responsibility, ethics, and effectiveness.
