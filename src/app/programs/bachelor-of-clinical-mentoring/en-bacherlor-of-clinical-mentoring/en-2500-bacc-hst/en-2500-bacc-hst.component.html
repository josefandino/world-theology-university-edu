BACC-HST-2500 - Humanistic and Systemic Therapy

The course Humanistic and Systemic Therapy addresses the main humanistic and systemic approaches applied in the treatment of individuals and families. The theoretical and practical foundations of these approaches are studied, focusing on understanding the human being within their relational and emotional context.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the fundamentals of humanistic and systemic therapy.
  • Explore therapeutic techniques focused on the individual and family.
  • Develop skills for working with complex family systems.
  • Encourage empathy, authenticity, and active listening in the therapeutic process.
  • Analyze the role of the therapist as a facilitator of personal and relational change.

Main Topics:

  • Principles of person-centered therapy.
  • Existential and Gestalt approaches in therapy.
  • Systemic models of family intervention.
  • Family dynamics and communication patterns.
  • Integration of humanistic and systemic perspectives.

Methodology:

The course is conducted through theoretical classes, case analysis, experiential exercises, simulated therapeutic sessions, and group discussions. Personal reflection is encouraged as a learning tool.

Assessment:

The course assessment will include:

  • Essays on humanistic and systemic theories.
  • Case studies focused on family intervention.
  • Supervised practices with feedback.
  • Integration projects of therapeutic approaches.
  • Active participation in class exercises.

Importance of the Course:

This course provides a comprehensive view of the human being and their environment, allowing counseling professionals to apply strategies focused on empathy, emotional connection, and relational change. It is essential for those working with individuals and families from a humanistic and systemic perspective.
