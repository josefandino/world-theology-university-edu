BACC-EPC-700 - Ethics in Psychology and Counseling

The course Ethics in Psychology and Counseling explores the fundamental ethical principles applied to clinical practice and counseling. It examines how to make responsible decisions in complex situations, respecting human values and professional guidelines.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the ethical frameworks that govern the practice of psychology and counseling.
  • Identify common ethical dilemmas in clinical and spiritual counseling.
  • Apply ethical principles in clinical decision-making.
  • Promote respect for the dignity, privacy, and autonomy of the patient.
  • Develop critical judgment in facing contemporary ethical challenges.

Main Topics:

  • Basic ethical principles: beneficence, non-maleficence, autonomy, and justice.
  • Confidentiality and handling sensitive information.
  • Professional boundaries and therapeutic relationships.
  • Informed consent and patient rights.
  • Christian ethics applied to counseling.

Methodology:

The course includes the analysis of ethical codes, real-life case studies, guided debates, and practical exercises for resolving ethical dilemmas in clinical and pastoral contexts.

Assessment:

The course assessment will include:

  • Essays on ethical dilemmas and their resolution.
  • Critical analysis of clinical cases with an ethical focus.
  • Written tests on ethical principles and guidelines.
  • Participation in ethical forums and simulations.

Course Significance:

Ethical knowledge is essential for practicing counseling with integrity. This course prepares students to act responsibly and sensitively in the face of ethical challenges in both psychological and pastoral practice.
