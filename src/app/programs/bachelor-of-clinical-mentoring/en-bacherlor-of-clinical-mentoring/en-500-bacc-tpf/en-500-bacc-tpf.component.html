BACC-TPF-500 - Theology and Psychology: Foundations

The course Theology and Psychology: Fundamentals offers an in-depth exploration of the relationship between psychology and theological principles, seeking points of convergence between the Christian faith and the understanding of human behavior.

  • Explore the foundations of psychology and theology as complementary disciplines.
  • Analyze how theological beliefs influence mental and emotional health.
  • Reflect on the role of spirituality in healing processes and personal growth.
  • Promote an integrative view between faith and psychological science.
  • Develop conceptual tools to apply psychological principles in faith contexts.

Main Topics:

  • Introduction to psychology and theology.
  • Historical perspectives on the relationship between faith and the mind.
  • The role of spirituality in mental health.
  • Christian ethics and psychological practice.
  • Integrative models between theology and psychology.

Methodology:

The course includes theoretical readings, debates, case studies, personal reflections, and analysis of biblical and psychological texts. Active participation and dialogue between perspectives are encouraged.

Assessment:

The course assessment will include:

  • Reflective essays on the integration of faith and psychology.
  • Case analysis involving theological and psychological principles.
  • Presentations on integrative models.
  • Participation in discussion forums and thematic debates.

Course Significance:

This course enables students to develop a comprehensive understanding of the human being, considering both its spiritual and psychological dimensions, essential for those wishing to provide informed and sensitive counseling from a Christian perspective.
