BACC-CCPIII-2300 - Clinical Counseling Practices III

The course Practical Training in Clinical Counseling III represents the final stage of practical training, where the student assumes a more autonomous role with greater responsibility within a real clinical environment. Under professional supervision, the student is expected to lead counseling processes with a high level of competence and ethical commitment.

Course Objectives:

  • Perform clinical counseling functions with increased autonomy and leadership.
  • Integrate evaluation, diagnosis, and intervention skills in real contexts.
  • Strengthen the ability to make well-founded clinical decisions.
  • Demonstrate professional maturity and ethical handling of complex situations.
  • Contribute actively to therapeutic processes within multidisciplinary teams.

Main Topics:

  • Autonomous management of clinical cases.
  • Development of long-term therapeutic strategies.
  • Advanced supervision and critical review of professional practice.
  • Ethical reflection and clinical decision-making.
  • Evaluation of therapeutic outcomes and process closure.

Methodology:

The course is based on direct practice in clinical settings, where the student handles their own cases under professional supervision. It includes feedback sessions, discussions of complex cases, and the preparation of highly rigorous clinical reports.

Assessment:

The course assessment will include:

  • Autonomous performance in managing clinical cases.
  • Supervision and evaluation of advanced therapeutic skills.
