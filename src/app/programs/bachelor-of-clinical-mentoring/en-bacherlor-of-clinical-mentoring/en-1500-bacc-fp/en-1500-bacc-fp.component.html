BACC-FP-1500 - Family Psychology

The course Family Psychology analyzes family dynamics and their impact on the psychological development of individuals. It studies roles, communication, patterns of interaction, and how these factors influence the mental and emotional health of family members.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the main theories about the structure and functioning of the family.
  • Analyze family influences on psychological development.
  • Explore different parenting styles and their emotional impact.
  • Identify dysfunctional patterns within the family system.
  • Develop intervention strategies to improve family dynamics.

Main Topics:

  • Sociological models of the family.
  • Family roles and interpersonal relationships.
  • Communication and conflict resolution in the family.
  • Parenting, discipline, and emotional development.
  • The impact of the family environment on mental health.

Methodology:

The course will be developed through theoretical classes, case analysis, group discussions, and family intervention simulations. Critical reflection on real family experiences and their relationship to clinical psychology will be encouraged.

Assessment:

The course assessment will include:

  • Exams on theories and key concepts of family psychology.
  • Case studies on family dynamics.
  • Analytical essays on parenting styles and emotional development.
  • Family intervention projects from a psychological approach.

Course Significance:

The family is the core of emotional and psychological development. This course is essential for understanding how family relationships influence mental health and for developing tools to improve well-being in the family context.
