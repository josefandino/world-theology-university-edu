BACH-CCPI-2100 - Clinical Counseling Practices I

The course Practical Training in Clinical Counseling I provides students with the opportunity to apply their theoretical knowledge in controlled and supervised counseling environments. It focuses on the development of essential practical skills for therapeutic interaction and emotional support.

Course Objectives:

  • Apply basic counseling techniques in simulated and real scenarios.
  • Develop interpersonal and active listening skills.
  • Strengthen the ability to establish an effective therapeutic relationship.
  • Receive feedback on counseling performance.
  • Encourage ethical and professional reflection during clinical practice.

Main Topics:

  • Simulation of counseling sessions with feedback.
  • Building rapport and managing the therapeutic framework.
  • Observation and analysis of clinical interventions.
  • Documentation and preparation of clinical notes.
  • Ethics in supervised counseling practice.

Methodology:

The course combines supervised practice, simulated sessions, case analysis, direct observation, and group discussions. Learning through experience and guided reflection by professionals in the field is promoted.

Assessment:

The course assessment will include:

  • Performance in supervised practical sessions.
  • Self-assessments and reflective journals.
  • Active participation in clinical case discussions.
  • Evaluations by clinical supervisors.
  • Presentations of practical experiences.

Importance of the Course:

This course is essential for the transition from theoretical learning to clinical practice. Through direct experience, students strengthen their confidence, communication skills, and ethical sensitivity in the practice of counseling.
