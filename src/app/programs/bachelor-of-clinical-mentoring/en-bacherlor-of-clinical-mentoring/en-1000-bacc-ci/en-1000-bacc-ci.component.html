BACC-CI-1000 - Crisis intervention

The course Crisis Intervention offers fundamental techniques and strategies for effectively intervening in critical situations. It focuses on providing immediate support to individuals going through traumatic or stressful events, promoting emotional stability and preventing long-term psychological consequences.

  • Understand the basic principles of crisis intervention.
  • Identify different types of crises and their psychological implications.
  • Learn techniques for rapid assessment and response in critical situations.
  • Apply strategies for emotional containment and support during the crisis.
  • Develop effective and empathetic communication skills in high-tension contexts.

Main Topics:

  • Definition and types of crises.
  • Crisis intervention model.
  • Immediate emotional containment strategies.
  • Crisis due to loss, trauma, or unexpected events.
  • Role of the counselor and professional boundaries during intervention.

Methodology:

The course combines theoretical classes, real case analysis, dramatizations, and practical exercises for crisis management. Experiential learning and personal reflection on intervention processes are encouraged.

Assessment:

The course assessment will include:

  • Written tests on crisis intervention models and phases.
  • Case studies and intervention simulations.
  • Essays on the psychological impact of crises.
  • Active participation in group dynamics and practical exercises.

Course Significance:

Knowledge of crisis intervention is essential for any professional working with individuals in vulnerable moments. This course equips students to act quickly, ethically, and effectively, promoting mental health and immediate well-being for the individual.
