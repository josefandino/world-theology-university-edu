BACC-PT-400 - Personality Theory

The Personality Theory course explores the principal models and theories that explain the development and structure of human personality, analyzing their foundations, applications, and contributions to understanding individual behavior.

  • Understand classical and contemporary theories of personality.
  • Analyze the biological, psychological, and social factors that influence personality.
  • Explore psychodynamic, behavioral, humanistic, cognitive, and trait-based models.
  • Apply theoretical concepts to the analysis of real-life cases and situations.
  • Develop a critical and comparative perspective on different personality theories.

Main Topics:

  • Definition and assessment of personality.
  • Freud's psychoanalytic theory and subsequent psychodynamic approaches.
  • Behavioral and social learning models.
  • Humanistic and existential approaches.
  • Trait theories and cognitive-behavioral theory.

Methodology:

The course includes theoretical readings, model analysis, case studies, group discussions, and reflections on the application of each approach to personal and professional contexts.

Assessment:

Course assessment will include:

  • Theoretical exams on personality models.
  • Comparative essays on various theories.
  • Case studies and the development of psychological profiles.
  • Oral presentations on prominent theorists.

Course Relevance:

Understanding personality theories enables a deeper comprehension of human behavior and individual patterns of thought and emotion. This knowledge is essential for those engaged in counseling, education, and leadership.
