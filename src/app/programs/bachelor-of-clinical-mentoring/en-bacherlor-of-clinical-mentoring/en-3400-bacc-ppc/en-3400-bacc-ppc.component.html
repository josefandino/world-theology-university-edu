BACC-PPC-3400 - Professional for professional certification

The course Professional Certification Preparation is designed to equip students with the knowledge, skills, and strategies necessary to successfully take professional certification exams in clinical psychology and counseling.

Course Objectives:

  • Review key content required for professional certification exams.
  • Familiarize students with the format and structure of standardized assessments.
  • Develop effective study and time management strategies.
  • Identify individual areas for improvement through mock exams and diagnostic assessments.
  • Strengthen emotional readiness and self-confidence to face certification exams.

Main Topics:

  • Review of theories and approaches in clinical psychology.
  • Professional ethics and legal regulations.
  • Counseling and intervention models.
  • Psychological assessment and psychopathology.
  • Professional practices and applied clinical case studies.

Methodology:

The course combines intensive theoretical classes, mock exams, practical exercises, group and individual tutoring, as well as feedback sessions aimed at improving studentsâ€™ academic and emotional performance.

Assessment:

The course assessment will include:

  • Mock exams with detailed feedback.
  • Short quizzes on specific exam topics.
  • Practical activities and clinical case resolution.
  • Ongoing evaluation of individual progress throughout the course.

Importance of the Course:

This course is essential to ensure that future professionals are adequately prepared to obtain certification in clinical psychology and counseling, which represents a crucial step toward practicing with credibility and professional recognition.
