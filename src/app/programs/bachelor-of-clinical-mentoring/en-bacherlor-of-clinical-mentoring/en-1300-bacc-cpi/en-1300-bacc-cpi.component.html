BACC-CPI-1300 - Counseling and Psychotherapy I

The course Counseling and Psychotherapy I offers an introduction to the fundamental principles of counseling and psychotherapy, focusing on their application in common life situations. It addresses theoretical models, basic skills, and practical approaches for emotional and psychological support.

  • Introduce key concepts of counseling and psychotherapy.
  • Develop listening, empathy, and therapeutic communication skills.
  • Explore approaches and models applicable to everyday contexts.
  • Identify common emotional needs and intervention strategies.
  • Foster the development of an ethical and sensitive attitude towards the therapeutic process.

Main Topics:

  • Principles and objectives of counseling and psychotherapy.
  • Therapeutic relationship and basic counselor skills.
  • Classical models of psychological intervention.
  • Emotional management in everyday situations.
  • Ethics in basic counseling practice.

Methodology:

The course is delivered through theoretical classes, practical case analysis, simulations, and group exercises. Personal reflection and active practice of basic counseling techniques are encouraged.

Assessment:

The course assessment will include:

  • Theoretical tests on the foundations of counseling.
  • Practical exercises in listening and accompaniment.
  • Analysis of common cases and proposed interventions.
  • Reflective essays on the counselor's practice.

Course Significance:

This course is key for those beginning their training in counseling, as it establishes the foundation for effective professional practice. It provides essential tools for addressing emotional needs and promoting well-being in various contexts.
