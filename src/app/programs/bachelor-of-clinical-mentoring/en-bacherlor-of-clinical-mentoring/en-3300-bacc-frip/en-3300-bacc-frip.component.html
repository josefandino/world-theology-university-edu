BACC-FRIP-3300 - Final Research or Integrative Project

The course Final Research or Integrative Project is designed for students to carry out in-depth research on a relevant topic in the field of clinical counseling, or to develop a practical project that integrates the knowledge acquired throughout the program.

Course Objectives:

  • Apply the theoretical and practical knowledge acquired during the program.
  • Develop scientific research skills in the area of clinical counseling.
  • Design a project that addresses a real-world issue in clinical settings.
  • Foster critical thinking and analytical skills.
  • Demonstrate professional competencies through an innovative or research-based proposal.

Main Topics:

  • Formulation of research or intervention problems.
  • Review of scientific literature and clinical practice.
  • Qualitative and quantitative research methodologies.
  • Design and implementation of clinical projects.
  • Presentation and defense of results.

Methodology:

The course is conducted through personalized tutoring, research seminars, and practical advising. Students will work individually or in small groups to formulate, develop, and present their final work.

Assessment:

The course assessment will include:

  • Written proposal of the project or research.
  • Development of the theoretical framework and methodology.
  • Analysis of results and conclusions.
  • Oral presentation before an academic committee.
  • Final report outlining the contributions to the field of clinical counseling.

Importance of the Course:

This course represents the culmination of the training process, allowing students to demonstrate their academic and professional readiness to contribute to the advancement of clinical counseling through research or practical intervention.
