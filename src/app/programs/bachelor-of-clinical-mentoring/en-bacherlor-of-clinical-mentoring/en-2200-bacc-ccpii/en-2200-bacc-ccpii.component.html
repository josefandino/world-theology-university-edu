BACC-CCPII-2200 - Clinical Counseling Practices II

The course Practical Training in Clinical Counseling II offers a supervised experience in real clinical settings, allowing students to work with complex cases and severe disorders. This advanced stage of practice strengthens the ability to evaluate, intervene, and treat clinically in professional contexts.

Course Objectives:

  • Apply clinical interventions with individuals presenting severe psychological disorders.
  • Deepen case management from an ethical and professional perspective.
  • Develop personalized treatment plans.
  • Work in a multidisciplinary team within a real clinical environment.
  • Consolidate the necessary experience for the professional practice of clinical counseling.

Main Topics:

  • Intervention in cases of severe and chronic disorders.
  • Application of advanced therapeutic models.
  • Preparation of detailed clinical reports.
  • Individual and group supervision of handled cases.
  • Ethics and self-care for counselors in professional practice.

Methodology:

The course is conducted in real clinical settings where students actively participate in evaluation, counseling, and therapeutic intervention processes, under the guidance and supervision of accredited professionals.

Assessment:

The course assessment will include:

  • Evaluation of clinical performance in real scenarios.
  • Continuous supervision and professional feedback.
  • Submission of clinical reports and treatment plans.
  • Participation in supervision sessions and case discussions.
  • Self-assessment of professional development and clinical skills.

Importance of the Course:

This advanced clinical practice is key in the training of professional counselors. It allows the consolidation of essential competencies in addressing complex disorders, developing experience, clinical judgment, and ethical responsibility in the field of mental health.
