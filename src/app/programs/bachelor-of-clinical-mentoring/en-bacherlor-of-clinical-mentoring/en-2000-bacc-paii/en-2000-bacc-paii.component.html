BACC-PAII-2000 - Psychological Assessment II

The course Psychological Assessment II delves into the use of advanced psychological assessment techniques, with a special emphasis on managing complex clinical cases. Specialized instruments are analyzed, and skills are developed to interpret and communicate findings in therapeutic contexts.

Course Objectives:

  • Apply advanced psychological assessment techniques.
  • Interpret test results in cases with multiple clinical variables.
  • Select appropriate instruments for specific populations.
  • Develop detailed and professional psychological reports.
  • Evaluate therapeutic progress using psychological tools.

Main Topics:

  • Assessment of complex personality disorders.
  • Advanced use of projective and psychometric tests.
  • Basic neuropsychological assessment.
  • Integration of clinical data and patient context.
  • Writing comprehensive reports.

Methodology:

The course employs a combination of theoretical classes, practical work with real assessment instruments, analysis of complex clinical cases, and supervision of assessment processes. Clinical and ethical reasoning in decision-making is emphasized.

Assessment:

The course assessment will include:

  • Case studies with application and interpretation of tests.
  • Written reports with comprehensive patient analysis.
  • Presentations of complex clinical cases.
  • Participation in supervised practices.
  • Exams on advanced techniques and their application.

Importance of the Course:

The ability to conduct accurate assessments in complex cases is essential in clinical practice. This course provides students with advanced tools to deeply understand the psychological condition of patients and support effective therapeutic decisions.
