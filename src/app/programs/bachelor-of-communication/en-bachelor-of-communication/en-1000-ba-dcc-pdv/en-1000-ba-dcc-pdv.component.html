Introduction to visual production for communication, including photography and video production.

The course Photography and Digital Video introduces students to the fundamental principles of visual production in communication. Through the study of image theory, photographic and audiovisual techniques, and creative practice, students will learn to conceptualize, capture, and edit effective visual content for various formats and platforms. The course fosters the development of a critical and aesthetic perspective in constructing strategic and ethically responsible visual messages.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the basic principles of photography and digital video.
  • Apply techniques of composition, lighting, and visual storytelling.
  • Develop skills in operating cameras, equipment, and editing software.
  • Create visual content aligned with specific communication objectives.
  • Analyze the visual and communicative impact of images and videos in different contexts.
  • Promote ethical, critical, and creative visual production practices.

Main Topics:

  • Introduction to visual communication: image theory and analysis.
  • Technical foundations of digital photography: camera, light, composition, and exposure.
  • Basic principles of video production: pre-production, shooting, and post-production.
  • Audiovisual language: framing, camera movement, and editing.
  • Basic photo and video editing with digital tools.
  • Content production for social media and digital platforms.
  • Ethics in the production and dissemination of visual content.
  • Capstone project: photography and video applied to communication.

Methodology:

The course may be delivered in virtual, online, or hybrid format, promoting hands-on and creative learning through:

  • Virtual workshops on photography and audiovisual production.
  • Practice in image and video capture, editing, and montage.
  • Critical analysis of contemporary visual productions.
  • Guided readings on image theory and visual communication.
  • Discussion forums on ethics and visual aesthetics in communication.
  • Final project in visual production focused on a communication objective.

Assessment:

  • Production of photographic and audiovisual projects.
  • Active participation in workshops and practical activities.
  • Critical analysis of personal and third-party visual works.
  • Final project integrating photography and video with a defined communication purpose.

Course Significance:

Photography and Digital Video is essential for training visual communicators capable of creating engaging, strategic, and culturally relevant content in an increasingly image-driven world. Mastery of visual tools and languages enhances the ability to tell stories, influence audiences, and strengthen the presence of brands, organizations, and projects in the global audiovisual and digital landscape.
