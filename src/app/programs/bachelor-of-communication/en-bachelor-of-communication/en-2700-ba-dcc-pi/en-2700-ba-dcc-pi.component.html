BA-DCC-PI-2700 - Professional Internship

Practical experience in companies, media outlets, or public relations agencies, working on real-world projects.

The Professional Internship course offers students the opportunity to apply the knowledge acquired during their academic training in real work environments. Through participation in projects within companies, media organizations, advertising agencies, public relations agencies, or non-profit institutions, students will strengthen their technical, strategic, and ethical competencies, preparing for professional integration in the communication field.

Course Objectives

  • Apply theoretical and practical knowledge in real professional contexts.
  • Develop communication, management, analytical, and content creation skills.
  • Integrate into multidisciplinary teams, contributing proactively and collaboratively.
  • Reflect critically on professional practice and its social and ethical impact.
  • Consolidate competencies in adaptability, leadership, responsibility, and decision-making.
  • Strengthen employability and build professional networks in the communication field.

Main Topics

  • Application of communication strategies in real-world contexts.
  • Development of communication projects in media, companies, or agencies.
  • Content production for various formats and platforms.
  • Management of social media, events, campaigns, and public relations.
  • Adaptation to organizational cultures and professional work dynamics.
  • Evaluation of professional practice from critical and ethical perspectives.
  • Reflection on trends, challenges, and opportunities in the communication field.

Methodology

The course may be delivered in virtual, online, or hybrid (virtual-online) mode, and will be mainly developed through supervised practical experience via:

  • Student placement in partner companies, media outlets, or agencies.
  • Execution of tasks and projects assigned by the host institution.
  • Academic supervision through tutorials and progress reports.
  • Critical reflection through reports and final presentations.
  • Forums for sharing professional experiences among students.

Assessment

Assessment will be based on professional performance and critical reflection through:

  • Final internship report analyzing activities and learnings.
  • Performance evaluation by both the academic tutor and the hosting supervisor.
  • Participation in academic tutorials and follow-up activities.
  • Reflections on ethical, strategic, and professional challenges faced.

Course Significance

Professional Internship is an essential component in the student's academic journey, allowing them to integrate theory and practice in real communication settings. This experience strengthens technical, ethical, and professional skills, facilitates the transition into the workforce, and fosters a critical, strategic, and socially committed outlook through communication.
