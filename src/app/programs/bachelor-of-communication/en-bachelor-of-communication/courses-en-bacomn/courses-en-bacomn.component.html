The Bachelor's Degree in Communication at World Theology University offers a comprehensive education that covers all key aspects of communication, from theory to practice, in various professional contexts. Below is the list of courses that students will take throughout the program:

BA-DCC-IT-100 - Introduction to Communication - History and theory of communication, basic principles, and the importance of communication in society.

BA-DCC-PS-200 - Psychology of Communication - Study of how psychology influences the communication process, behavior, and human perceptions.

BA-DCC-CT-300 - Communication Theory I - Major communication theories: structuralism, functionalism, and critical theories.

BA-DCC-WCW-400 - Writing and Creative Writing - Development of writing skills, both creative and technical, for various formats and platforms.

BA-DCC-MT-500 - Media Theory - Analysis of mass media and its impact on culture and society.

BA-DCC-PPST-600 - Presentation and Public Speaking Techniques - Verbal and non-verbal communication strategies for effective presentations.

BA-DCC-OC-700 - Organizational Communication - Internal and external communication strategies in organizations, businesses, and governments.

BA-DCC-DM-800 - Digital Marketing - Principles and tools of digital marketing, social media, SEO, SEM, and data analysis.

BA-DCC-JW-900 - Journalistic Writing - Techniques for writing news, articles, reports, and other types of journalistic content.

BA-DCC-PDV-1000 - Photography and Digital Video - Introduction to visual production for communication, including photography and video production.

BA-DCC-IC-1100 - Intercultural Communication - Study of communication between different cultures and its importance in a globalized world.

BA-DCC-PRIM-1200 - Public Relations and Image Management - Strategies to create and maintain a positive image for companies and public figures.

BA-DCC-RTP-1300 - Radio and Television Production - Introduction to production and broadcasting in traditional media such as radio and television.

BA-DCC-DCP-1400 - Digital Content Production - Creation of content for blogs, podcasts, YouTube channels, and streaming platforms.

BA-DCC-ACA-1500 - Advertising and Creative Advertising - Advertising strategies and the creation of effective campaigns across various media.

BA-DCC-CPM-1600 - Communication Project Management - Techniques and tools for planning, executing, and evaluating communication projects.

BA-DCC-CSC-1700 - Communication for Social Change - Communication strategies used in social movements and change campaigns.

BA-DCC-CCM-1800 - Crisis Communication Management - Planning and executing strategies to handle crisis situations in communication.

BA-DCC-PC-1900 - Political Communication - Communication strategies applied to political campaigns, message management, and political media.

BA-DCC-EMCC-2000 - Event Management and Corporate Communication - Design and execution of events, and communication in the corporate and business context.

BA-DCC-ABS-2100 - Audience and Behavior Studies - Analysis of audience behavior and how to adapt communication to different demographic groups.

BA-DCC-ADA-2200 - Advanced Digital Advertising - Creation of advertising campaigns on advanced digital platforms such as Google Ads, Facebook Ads, etc.

BA-DCC-MLA-2300 - Media Law and Ethics - Study of laws and regulations affecting the media, and ethical principles in professional practice.

BA-DCC-SMMS-2400 - Social Media Marketing Strategies - Techniques for managing and promoting brands and products through social media.

BA-DCC-CR-2500 - Communication Research - Research methods and techniques applied to communication, including qualitative and quantitative studies.

BA-DCC-CMA-2600 - Content and Media Analysis - Critical study of the media and its effects on society.

BA-DCC-PI-2700 - Professional Internships - Practical experience in companies, media outlets, or public relations agencies, working on real projects.

BA-DCC-BT-2800 - Bachelor's Thesis - Development of a thesis in a communication area selected by the student, with guidance from an academic tutor.

BA-DCC-CFE-2900 - Comprehensive Final Exam - Final evaluation of all knowledge acquired throughout the degree program, covering theories, practices, and communication skills.
