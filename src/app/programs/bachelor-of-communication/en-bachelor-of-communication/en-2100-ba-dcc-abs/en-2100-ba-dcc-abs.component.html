BA-DCC-ABS-2100 – Audience and Behavioral Studies

Analysis of audience behavior and the adaptation of communication strategies to different demographic groups.

The course Audience and Behavioral Studies offers an in-depth understanding of audience research, analysis, and interpretation across various communication contexts. Through theoretical and methodological approaches, students will learn to segment target groups, identify media consumption patterns, and adapt communication strategies to diverse demographic, cultural, and social audiences.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the theoretical foundations of audience studies.
  • Analyze audience behavior and dynamics across different media and platforms.
  • Apply segmentation techniques and demographic analysis in strategic communication.
  • Conduct research on audience preferences, habits, and attitudes.
  • Adapt messages and communication strategies to various audience characteristics.
  • Evaluate the impact of communication on audience behavior.

Main Topics:

  • History and evolution of audience studies.
  • Theories on audience behavior: uses and gratifications, active reception.
  • Audience research methods: surveys, focus groups, digital analytics.
  • Audience segmentation: demographic, psychographic, and behavioral criteria.
  • Media consumption in the digital age: trends and emerging practices.
  • Content and strategy adaptation for different audiences.
  • Audience data analysis and strategic decision-making.
  • Ethical considerations in the study and use of audience data.

Methodology:

The course may be delivered in virtual, online, or hybrid (virtual-online) format, combining theory, analysis, and practice through:

  • Virtual lectures on audience study theories and techniques.
  • Workshops on designing audience research projects.
  • Application of digital and traditional media analytics tools.
  • Case studies based on campaigns informed by audience analysis.
  • Discussion forums on media behavior and consumption trends.
  • Final project: audience-based research and strategy proposal.

Assessment:

  • Design and implementation of audience research projects.
  • Interpretation and analysis of audience behavior data.
  • Active participation in workshops, case analyses, and discussion forums.
  • Final integrative project: communication strategy based on audience research.

Course Significance:

Audience and Behavioral Studies is essential for training professionals who can understand and anticipate the needs, interests, and behaviors of their audiences. Through strategic audience analysis, communicators can design more effective, personalized, and culturally relevant messages—enhancing the impact of their actions in a globalized and highly competitive environment.
