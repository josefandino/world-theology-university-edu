Communication strategies used in social movements and change-oriented campaigns.

The course Communication for Social Change explores the essential role of communication as a tool for social transformation. It examines strategies used in social movements, awareness campaigns, and community development projects. Through a theoretical and practical approach, students will learn to design, implement, and evaluate communication initiatives aimed at promoting social change, justice, and civic engagement.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the foundations of communication for social change.
  • Analyze communication strategies used in social movements and advocacy campaigns.
  • Design communication projects focused on social transformation.
  • Apply participatory and community-based communication tools.
  • Evaluate the impact of social communication initiatives.
  • Promote ethical, inclusive, and culturally respectful communication practices.

Main Topics:

  • Theories of communication for social change.
  • Social movements and strategic communication.
  • Designing awareness and mobilization campaigns.
  • Participatory and community-based communication.
  • Alternative media and social networks as tools for change.
  • Impact evaluation in social communication projects.
  • Case studies: successful experiences in communication for social change.
  • Ethics, human rights, and socially responsible communication.

Methodology:

The course may be delivered in virtual, online, or hybrid format, combining theory, critical analysis, and practical application through:

  • Virtual lectures on social communication theories and strategies.
  • Analysis of contemporary campaigns and social movements.
  • Design and simulation of communication projects for social change.
  • Discussion forums on communication, citizenship, and human rights.
  • Guided readings on participatory communication and social transformation.
  • Final project: design of a campaign for social change.

Assessment:

  • Development of social communication projects.
  • Active participation in case analysis and discussion forums.
  • Simulation of campaigns and participatory communication strategies.
  • Final integrative project addressing a real or simulated social issue.

Course Significance:

Communication for Social Change is essential for training communicators who are aware of their role as agents of transformation in their communities and in the world. This course equips students with the skills to design and implement communication strategies that promote social justice, equity, democratic participation, and respect for human rights in both local and global contexts.
