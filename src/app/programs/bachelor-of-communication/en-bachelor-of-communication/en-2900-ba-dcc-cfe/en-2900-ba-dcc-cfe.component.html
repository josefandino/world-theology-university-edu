BA-DCC-CR-2500 - Communication Research

Methods and techniques of research applied to communication, including qualitative and quantitative studies.

The Communication Research course introduces students to the main methods and techniques used in communication research. Through a theoretical and practical approach, students will learn to design research projects, apply qualitative and quantitative methodologies, collect and analyze data, and present results in a rigorous, ethical, and relevant manner to understand communication phenomena.

Course Objectives

  • Understand the epistemological and methodological foundations of communication research.
  • Design research projects aligned with communication questions and problems.
  • Apply qualitative and quantitative methods to communication studies.
  • Use data collection and analysis techniques rigorously.
  • Interpret research findings to inform communication strategies.
  • Promote ethical, critical, and culturally sensitive research practices.

Main Topics

  • Introduction to communication research: concepts and approaches.
  • Designing research projects: questions, objectives, and hypotheses.
  • Qualitative methods: interviews, focus groups, content analysis.
  • Quantitative methods: surveys, experiments, statistical analysis.
  • Data collection instruments and validation.
  • Analysis of qualitative and quantitative data.
  • Ethics in communication research.
  • Writing research reports and presenting results.

Methodology

The course may be offered in virtual, online, or hybrid (virtual-online) format, combining theory, practice, and applied projects through:

  • Virtual classes on communication research methods and techniques.
  • Workshops on designing and conducting research projects.
  • Practical application of data collection and analysis techniques.
  • Discussion forums on ethics and good research practices.
  • Case studies of relevant research in the field of communication.
  • Final project involving research on a communication phenomenon.

Assessment

Assessment will be based on the rigorous and critical application of course content through:

  • Design of communication research projects.
  • Application of qualitative and quantitative techniques.
  • Analysis and interpretation of collected data.
  • Active participation in workshops, exercises, and discussion forums.
  • Final research project and presentation of results.

Course Significance

Communication Research is fundamental for training professionals capable of producing rigorous and relevant knowledge about communication phenomena. This course equips students with methodological tools to understand social, cultural, and media dynamics and to support their professional actions with critical and ethical reasoning in the communication field.
