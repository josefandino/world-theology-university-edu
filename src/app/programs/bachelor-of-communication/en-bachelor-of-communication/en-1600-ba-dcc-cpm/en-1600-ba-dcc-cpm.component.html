Techniques and tools for planning, executing, and evaluating communication projects.

The course Communication Project Management provides practical and strategic training in planning, executing, and evaluating communication projects across various organizational contexts. Using modern project management methodologies, students will learn how to structure campaigns, coordinate interdisciplinary teams, and measure the impact of their communication initiatives, ensuring that objectives are met within the defined timeline and resources.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the fundamentals of project management as applied to communication.
  • Plan communication projects aligned with strategic goals.
  • Apply agile and traditional methodologies to manage communication projects.
  • Efficiently manage teams and resources.
  • Monitor, evaluate, and adjust projects to ensure effectiveness.
  • Develop an ethical, critical, and strategic approach to communication project management.

Main Topics:

  • Introduction to communication project management.
  • Defining objectives, scope, schedules, and resources.
  • Project management methodologies: traditional and agile (Scrum, Kanban).
  • Development of strategic communication plans.
  • Risk management and problem-solving in projects.
  • Teamwork and leadership in communication projects.
  • Monitoring, evaluation, and success metrics for communication projects.
  • Case studies on successful communication project management.

Methodology:

The course may be delivered in virtual, online, or hybrid format, combining theory and practice through:

  • Virtual lectures on project management techniques and tools.
  • Workshops for planning and simulating communication projects.
  • Analysis of real-world successes and failures in communication project execution.
  • Discussion forums on trends in communication project management.
  • Practical application projects developed in collaborative teams.
  • Guided readings on strategic management and project evaluation.

Assessment:

  • Development of communication project plans.
  • Simulated project management in team settings.
  • Active participation in workshops, case analysis, and forums.
  • Final integrative project: planning and evaluation of a real or simulated communication project.

Course Significance:

Communication Project Management is essential for training professionals capable of leading communication processes in a strategic, organized, and efficient way within public, private, and nonprofit organizations. This course develops key competencies for planning, executing, and evaluating projects that respond to the demands of the contemporary world, with a global, ethical, and results-oriented vision.
