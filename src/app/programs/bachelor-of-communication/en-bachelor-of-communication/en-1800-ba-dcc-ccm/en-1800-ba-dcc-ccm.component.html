Planning and execution of communication strategies to manage crisis situations.

The course Crisis Communication Management provides specialized training in the design and implementation of communication strategies to address crisis situations in organizations, businesses, and public environments. Students will learn to identify risks, plan strategic responses, manage information under pressure, and protect institutional reputation in high-uncertainty contexts, always from an ethical and responsible perspective.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the principles and dynamics of communication during crises.
  • Identify risk factors and design crisis communication management plans.
  • Apply effective strategies for prevention, response, and recovery.
  • Manage relations with the media, internal, and external stakeholders during a crisis.
  • Evaluate the impact of communication actions during and after the crisis.
  • Promote ethical, transparent, and empathetic communication in crisis scenarios.

Main Topics:

  • Fundamentals of crisis communication: concepts and typologies.
  • Risk identification and crisis scenario planning.
  • Design of crisis communication plans: protocols and information flows.
  • Managing media relations during a crisis.
  • Internal communication in crisis contexts.
  • Use of digital media and social networks in crisis management.
  • Reputation recovery and post-crisis evaluation.
  • Case studies of successful and failed crisis communication management.

Methodology:

The course may be delivered in virtual, online, or hybrid format, combining theory, practice, and simulation through:

  • Virtual lectures on crisis communication principles and practices.
  • Analysis of real institutional crisis management cases.
  • Simulations of crisis communication scenarios.
  • Design of response plans and communication protocols.
  • Discussion forums on ethics, leadership, and communication in crisis.
  • Final project: simulation of a crisis communication management scenario.

Assessment:

  • Development of crisis communication plans.
  • Active participation in crisis management simulations.
  • Critical analysis of real crisis communication cases.
  • Final project: simulation of a response to a real or simulated crisis.

Course Significance:

Crisis Communication Management is essential for training professionals who are prepared to lead communication strategies under pressure, protect reputational assets, and respond ethically and strategically in adverse situations. This course develops key competencies for acting effectively in uncertain environments, strengthening the communicative resilience of organizations in an increasingly interconnected and demanding world.
