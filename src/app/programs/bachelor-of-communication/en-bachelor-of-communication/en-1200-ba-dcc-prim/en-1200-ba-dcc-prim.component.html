Strategies for building and maintaining a positive image for organizations and public figures.

The course Public Relations and Image Management provides a strategic overview of how to build, position, and protect the image of organizations, businesses, and public figures. Through case analysis and hands-on practice of specific techniques, students will learn to design public relations campaigns, manage communication during crises, and develop action plans aimed at strengthening reputation and public recognition.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the foundations and principles of public relations and image management.
  • Analyze communication strategies to strengthen institutional or personal reputation.
  • Design public relations campaigns targeting various audiences.
  • Manage communication crises and protect organizational or individual image.
  • Apply planning, implementation, and evaluation techniques for public relations actions.
  • Promote ethical and responsible practices in image and reputation management.

Main Topics:

  • Public relations: definition, history, and evolution.
  • Public image and reputation: strategic construction and key concepts.
  • Audiences, media, and relationship management.
  • Strategic communication: personal and institutional branding.
  • Planning and execution of public relations campaigns.
  • Crisis management and emergency communication.
  • Corporate social responsibility and its link to public image.
  • Case studies on successful and failed image management.

Methodology:

The course may be delivered in virtual, online, or hybrid format, combining theory and practice through:

  • Interactive virtual classes on public relations concepts and strategies.
  • Critical analysis of real-life image management campaigns.
  • Development of public relations projects tailored to various contexts.
  • Discussion forums on best practices and ethical challenges.
  • Guided readings on emblematic cases in reputation and crisis management.
  • Simulations of public image crisis management scenarios.

Assessment:

  • Development of public relations and image management projects.
  • Participation in discussion forums and crisis simulations.
  • Critical analysis of real-world image management cases.
  • Final integrative project: strategic public relations planning.

Course Significance:

Public Relations and Image Management is essential for training professionals capable of designing and executing communication strategies that foster trust, strengthen reputation, and enhance the public profile of individuals and organizations. This course provides the theoretical and practical tools necessary to effectively manage image in a world where public perception is a fundamental strategic asset.
