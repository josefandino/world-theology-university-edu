Study of laws and regulations affecting the media, and ethical principles guiding professional practice.

The course Media Law and Ethics examines key laws, regulations, and legal frameworks governing media activity, as well as the ethical principles that guide responsible professional conduct. Students will analyze fundamental rights such as freedom of expression, data protection, and the right to information, and will reflect on the ethical dilemmas communicators face in a globalized and digital context.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand national and international legal frameworks applicable to media practices.
  • Analyze fundamental ethical principles in communication practice.
  • Evaluate legal and ethical conflicts in media environments.
  • Apply legal and ethical criteria in the production and dissemination of content.
  • Promote professional conduct based on human rights and social responsibility.
  • Develop a critical stance on contemporary legal and ethical challenges in traditional and digital media.

Main Topics:

  • Freedom of expression and the right to information.
  • Media regulation: agencies, laws, and international treaties.
  • Personal data protection and privacy in digital media.
  • Defamation, libel, and civil and criminal liability of media entities.
  • Journalistic ethics: principles of truthfulness, impartiality, and accountability.
  • Advertising, commercial communication, and content regulation.
  • Ethical dilemmas on social networks and digital platforms.
  • Case studies on legal and ethical conflicts in media communication.

Methodology:

The course may be delivered in virtual, online, or hybrid (virtual-online) format, combining theory, critical analysis, and practical application through:

  • Virtual lectures on media legislation and ethics.
  • Analysis of real-world cases involving legal and ethical media conflicts.
  • Academic debates on ethical principles and legal frameworks.
  • Guided readings on legislation, codes of ethics, and best practices.
  • Simulations of professional scenarios involving ethical and legal dilemmas.
  • Final project: analysis and proposal of best practices for responsible communication.

Assessment:

  • Critical analysis of legal and ethical cases in media contexts.
  • Active participation in debates, workshops, and simulations.
  • Written reflections on contemporary ethical challenges.
  • Final project: development of a code of best practices for media or communication projects.

Course Significance:

Media Law and Ethics is essential for training professionals who are aware of their rights and responsibilities in communication practice. This course strengthens critical thinking, professional integrity, and commitment to democratic principles, preparing students to act ethically, legally, and responsibly in complex and ever-evolving media environments.
