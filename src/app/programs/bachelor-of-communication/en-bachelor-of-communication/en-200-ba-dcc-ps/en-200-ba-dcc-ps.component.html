BA-DCC-PS-200 – Psychology of Communication

Study of how psychology influences the communication process, human behavior, and perception.

The course Psychology of Communication explores the interplay between psychological processes and human communicative dynamics. Through an interdisciplinary approach, it examines the cognitive, emotional, and social factors that shape how individuals interpret, transmit, and receive messages. This course provides essential tools for understanding communicative behavior across personal, group, and media contexts, strengthening students’ critical analysis and intervention skills in professional and multicultural settings.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the psychological principles underlying human communication processes.
  • Analyze how emotions, perceptions, and cognition affect interpersonal and mediated communication.
  • Identify psychological barriers to communication and strategies to overcome them.
  • Evaluate the influence of communication on the formation of attitudes, beliefs, and social behaviors.
  • Apply psychological concepts to design effective and ethically responsible messages.
  • Develop skills to interpret and enhance communicative interactions in diverse cultural contexts.

Main Topics:

  • Foundations of communication psychology: key concepts and basic models.
  • Perception and attention: their impact on message encoding and decoding.
  • Emotional processes and their influence on effective communication.
  • Persuasive communication: theories and psychological applications.
  • Interpersonal communication dynamics: empathy, assertiveness, and conflict management.
  • Communication in group and organizational contexts: leadership and cohesion.
  • Mass media and the construction of social reality.
  • Cultural factors and diversity in human communication.

Methodology:

The course will be delivered online using dynamic and participatory methods aimed at active learning:

  • Virtual lectures with real-time interaction.
  • Case studies applied to real-world communication scenarios.
  • Critical analysis of relevant texts and audiovisual materials.
  • Participation in academic forums for idea and experience exchange.
  • Practical exercises in designing psychologically effective messages.
  • Integrative final project applying psychological theories to a current communication issue.

Assessment:

Course evaluation will include:

  • Analytical essay (25%): Connection between psychological theories and communication processes.
  • Case study (20%): Analysis of a communication phenomenon from a psychological perspective.
  • Active forum participation (15%): Critically grounded contributions based on theory and practice.
  • Written exam (20%): Evaluation of essential theoretical knowledge.
  • Final project (20%): Communication intervention proposal based on psychological principles.

Course Significance:

Psychology of Communication is a foundational course for understanding the internal processes that impact the success and effectiveness of communication at all levels. Its analytical and practical approach enables future communication professionals to design strategic messages, interpret communicative behavior, and adapt to diverse cultural and social contexts. This knowledge strengthens the student’s comprehensive training, preparing them to face communicative challenges in a globalized environment with ethical sensitivity, technical competence, and multicultural awareness.
