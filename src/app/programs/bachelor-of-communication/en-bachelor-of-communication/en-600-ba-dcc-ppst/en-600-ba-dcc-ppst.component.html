BA-DCC-PPST-600 – Presentation and Public Speaking Techniques

Strategies for verbal and nonverbal communication in effective presentations.

The course Presentation and Public Speaking Techniques focuses on developing advanced verbal and nonverbal communication skills for delivering effective presentations across academic and professional contexts. Students will learn to structure clear messages, apply oral and physical expression techniques, and manage emotional and persuasive language in public speaking. The course emphasizes continuous practice, self-confidence, and the adaptation of speech to diverse and multicultural audiences.

Course Objectives:

  • Master verbal and nonverbal communication techniques for effective presentations.
  • Develop skills in planning, structuring, and delivering speeches.
  • Apply strategies for persuasion, motivation, and audience connection.
  • Manage emotional control, voice, posture, and gestures during a presentation.
  • Adapt presentations to different audiences, cultural contexts, and platforms.
  • Critically and constructively evaluate one’s own and others’ presentations.

Main Topics:

  • Foundations of effective oral communication.
  • Presentation structure: impactful openings, development, and conclusions.
  • Use of body language, voice, and facial expression.
  • Persuasion techniques and audience attention management.
  • Stage anxiety management and emotional regulation.
  • Speech adaptation for multicultural and digital contexts.
  • Use of visual and technological aids in presentations.
  • Critical evaluation and constructive feedback in presentations.

Methodology:

The course will be conducted virtually, promoting continuous practice and personalized feedback through:

  • Virtual public speaking workshops and recorded presentations.
  • Simulated speeches in various professional settings.
  • Voice control, posture, and body language exercises.
  • Forum discussions on communication strategies and experiences.
  • Analysis of outstanding speeches and presentations.
  • Final project: preparation and delivery of a comprehensive presentation.

Assessment:

  • Recorded presentations (30%): Assessment of speech and presentation structure.
  • Speech analysis (20%): Critique of a professional presentation.
  • Workshop and forum participation (20%): Active and reflective interaction.
  • Emotional control and body language exercises (15%): Specific practical tasks.
  • Final project (15%): Live or recorded presentation tailored to a specific audience.

Course Significance:

Presentation and Public Speaking Techniques is essential in training strategic communicators capable of delivering persuasive, effective, and ethical messages in any setting. Mastering public speaking strengthens students’ leadership, influence, and professional performance in a globalized world where communication is a key competence for personal and organizational success.
