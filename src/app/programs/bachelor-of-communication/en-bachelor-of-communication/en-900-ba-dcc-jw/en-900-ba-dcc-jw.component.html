BA-DCC-JW-900 – Journalism Writing

Techniques for writing news, articles, reports, and other forms of journalistic content.

The course Journalism Writing offers solid training in the techniques and core principles of journalistic writing. Through continuous practice, students will learn to write news articles, features, interviews, reports, and opinion pieces with a clear, accurate, and ethical style. Emphasis is placed on journalistic rigor, narrative structure, and adapting content for traditional and digital media platforms.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the fundamentals of print journalism and its ethical principles.
  • Apply writing techniques appropriate to various journalistic genres.
  • Develop skills to research, structure, and write high-quality journalistic content.
  • Adapt journalistic writing to print, digital, and multimedia platforms.
  • Critically evaluate the quality, accuracy, and impact of journalistic texts.
  • Promote journalistic practice committed to truth, social responsibility, and cultural diversity.

Main Topics:

  • Core principles of journalism: truthfulness, objectivity, and clarity.
  • News structure: inverted pyramid and lead writing.
  • Journalistic genres: news, feature, interview, report, and opinion piece.
  • Research and source verification techniques.
  • Adapting journalistic style to digital and cross-platform formats.
  • Ethics and responsibility in journalistic writing.
  • Use of emerging technologies in news production.
  • Writing for global and diverse audiences.

Methodology:

The course may be delivered in virtual, online, or hybrid formats, combining theory and practice through:

  • Writing workshops with weekly practical exercises.
  • Critical analysis of reference journalistic texts.
  • Simulated news coverage and writing under deadline pressure.
  • Forum debates on ethics, accuracy, and press freedom.
  • Content production projects for digital and traditional media.
  • Guided readings of style manuals and international journalism guides.

Assessment:

  • Writing of news articles, interviews, features, and reports.
  • Active participation in workshops, simulations, and forums.
  • Critical analysis of journalistic texts.
  • Final project: full editorial portfolio writing.

Course Significance:

Journalism Writing is fundamental for training communicators capable of informing ethically, clearly, and effectively in a world where information quality is essential for democratic strength. This course develops core competencies for working across traditional, digital, and emerging platforms, enhancing the student’s critical, investigative, and narrative abilities in both local and international contexts.
