BA-DCC-IC-1100 – Intercultural Communication

Study of communication across cultures and its significance in a globalized world.

The course Intercultural Communication explores the processes of communication between people from different cultures, analyzing how cultural differences influence perception, interpretation, and interaction. Through both theoretical and practical approaches, students will develop the skills to recognize, respect, and manage cultural diversity in personal, professional, and global contexts. The course promotes intercultural sensitivity as a fundamental competence in today’s world.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the theoretical foundations of intercultural communication.
  • Analyze the impact of cultural factors on communication processes.
  • Identify barriers and opportunities in cross-cultural communication.
  • Develop skills for the effective management of cultural diversity.
  • Apply intercultural communication strategies in professional and academic settings.
  • Promote a critical, respectful, and ethical attitude toward cultural diversity.

Main Topics:

  • Introduction to intercultural communication: concepts and theoretical models.
  • Culture, identity, and perception: their influence on communication.
  • Intercultural barriers: stereotypes, prejudice, and misunderstandings.
  • Intercultural competence: essential skills, attitudes, and knowledge.
  • Verbal and nonverbal communication in intercultural contexts.
  • Conflict resolution and negotiation in intercultural settings.
  • Intercultural communication in global and digital environments.
  • Ethics and responsibility in cross-cultural communication.

Methodology:

The course may be delivered in virtual, online, or hybrid format, combining theory, critical reflection, and practical application through:

  • Virtual lectures and participatory sessions on intercultural communication.
  • Case studies on successful and challenging intercultural interactions.
  • Debates and forums on cultural diversity and effective communication.
  • Guided readings on intercultural communication theory and practice.
  • Simulations and dynamics for resolving intercultural conflicts.
  • Final project applying intercultural strategies to a real or simulated case.

Assessment:

  • Essays and critical reflections on intercultural experiences.
  • Participation in debates, forums, and simulation activities.
  • Case analysis of intercultural communication scenarios.
  • Final project: applied strategy for intercultural communication.

Course Significance:

Intercultural Communication is fundamental for training professionals who can interact and lead effectively in diverse and globalized environments. This course develops essential competencies for building bridges of understanding between cultures, fostering inclusion, preventing conflict, and enriching communication processes in international organizations, media, and communities.
