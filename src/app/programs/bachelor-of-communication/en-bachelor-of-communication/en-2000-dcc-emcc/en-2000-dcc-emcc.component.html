Design and execution of events, and communication in corporate and business contexts.

The course Event Management and Corporate Communication trains students in the design, organization, and execution of strategic events within the corporate environment, as well as in the management of internal and external organizational communication. Through a practical and strategic approach, students will learn to align events and communication actions with business objectives, strengthening institutional image, reputation, and stakeholder relations.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the strategic role of events and communication in the corporate environment.
  • Design events aligned with business communication objectives.
  • Apply planning, coordination, and evaluation techniques for corporate events.
  • Manage internal and external communication in business contexts.
  • Develop skills to lead communication projects and event organization.
  • Promote ethical and socially responsible practices in corporate event management.

Main Topics:

  • Corporate communication: concepts, areas, and strategies.
  • Planning and managing corporate events: stages and processes.
  • Experience design: creativity and innovation in business events.
  • Public relations and audience engagement in events.
  • Internal communication: strengthening organizational culture through events.
  • Measurement and evaluation of events and communication actions.
  • Corporate social responsibility and sustainable events.
  • Case studies of successful events and corporate strategies.

Methodology:

The course may be delivered in virtual, online, or hybrid (virtual-online) format, combining theory, practice, and simulation through:

  • Virtual lectures on event management and corporate communication.
  • Simulations for the design and execution of business events.
  • Case studies of real corporate campaigns and events.
  • Discussion forums on innovation, responsibility, and corporate ethics.
  • Workshops on event planning and internal communication projects.
  • Final project: design of a strategic corporate event.

Assessment:

  • Design and presentation of corporate event proposals.
  • Active participation in simulations, workshops, and discussion forums.
  • Critical analysis of corporate events and communication strategies.
  • Final project: planning and simulated execution of an event.

Course Significance:

Event Management and Corporate Communication is essential for training professionals capable of designing strategic experiences that strengthen organizational identity, reputation, and stakeholder engagement. This course provides the necessary tools to lead communication and event management processes efficiently, creatively, and aligned with organizational values and goals in a globalized environment.
