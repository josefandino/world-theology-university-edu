BA-DCC-BT-2800 - Bachelor's Thesis

Development of a thesis in a communication-related area selected by the student, with guidance from an academic advisor.

The Bachelor's Thesis course guides students through the development of an original research project on a communication-related topic of their choice, under the supervision of an academic advisor. Throughout the process, students will apply the theoretical, methodological, and practical knowledge acquired during the program to conduct rigorous, critical, and ethical research that contributes to the field of communication.

Course Objectives

  • Formulate a relevant research question in the field of communication.
  • Design a solid research project with appropriate theoretical and methodological frameworks.
  • Apply data collection and analysis techniques rigorously and ethically.
  • Develop academic writing and scientific argumentation skills.
  • Critically reflect on the research process and its social implications.
  • Effectively present and defend research findings.

Main Topics

  • Formulation of research problems in communication.
  • Critical literature review and theoretical framework construction.
  • Qualitative, quantitative, or mixed research methodologies.
  • Design and implementation of data collection instruments.
  • Analysis and interpretation of research findings.
  • Academic writing and thesis structure.
  • Ethics in academic research.
  • Presentation and oral defense of the thesis.

Methodology

The course may be developed in virtual, online, or hybrid (virtual-online) format through:

  • Personalized guidance from an academic advisor specializing in the chosen area.
  • Virtual sessions to monitor individual progress.
  • Progressive development of thesis chapters.
  • Ongoing review of theoretical, methodological, and writing components.
  • Partial presentations of research progress for feedback.

Assessment

Assessment will be based on academic quality, methodological rigor, and argumentative coherence through:

  • Submission of partial drafts for review and feedback.
  • Final submission of the complete written thesis.
  • Oral defense before an academic panel.
