BA-DCC-MT-500 – Media Theory

Analysis of mass media and its impact on culture and society.

The course Media Theory offers a critical analysis of mass communication and its influence on the shaping of contemporary culture, politics, and society. Drawing on both classical and contemporary theoretical frameworks, it examines the role of media in the production of meaning, the construction of identities, and the dynamics of power in the public sphere. The course provides essential analytical tools to interpret and question media discourse in both local and global contexts.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand major theoretical approaches to mass media.
  • Analyze the media's impact on culture, society, and politics.
  • Identify power dynamics, representation, and hegemony in media content.
  • Critically evaluate the media’s role in shaping public opinion and social identities.
  • Apply media theory to the analysis of current mass communication cases.
  • Develop a critical and ethical stance toward contemporary media phenomena.

Main Topics:

  • Introduction to media theory: key concepts.
  • Mass communication and mass society: origins and evolution.
  • Frankfurt School and cultural critique: Adorno, Horkheimer, Marcuse.
  • Agenda-setting theory and the construction of reality.
  • Cultural industry, consumption, and audiences: Cultural Studies and Stuart Hall.
  • Digital media, social networks, and the transformation of the public sphere.
  • Media, globalization, and cultural diversity.
  • Critical analysis of contemporary media messages.

Methodology:

This course will be delivered virtually, fostering a critical and participatory approach through:

  • Live virtual classes focused on theoretical and applied analysis.
  • Critical reading of foundational media theory texts.
  • Case studies on current media products.
  • Forum debates on relevant and controversial topics.
  • Practical content analysis exercises.
  • Research project on the impact of a specific medium or media phenomenon.

Assessment:

  • Critical essay (30%): Analysis of a media phenomenon using media theory.
  • Case study (20%): Evaluation of the social and cultural impact of a specific medium or content.
  • Forum participation (15%): Critical and well-supported reflections.
  • Written test (15%): Mastery of essential theoretical concepts.
  • Final project (20%): Applied research on media and culture.

Course Significance:

Media Theory is a core course in the training of critical communicators capable of analyzing the role of media in the production of meaning and power. Through a solid theoretical framework and contemporary analytical tools, this course prepares students to critically interpret media phenomena, engage ethically in public debate, and act responsibly in globalized and multicultural professional environments.
