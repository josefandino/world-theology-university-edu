BA-DCC-CMA-2600 - Content and Media Analysis

Critical study of mass media and their effects on society.

The Content and Media Analysis course trains students in the critical and systematic study of messages disseminated through mass media. It explores content analysis methods, the media’s impact on culture and society, and the construction of mediated representations. Through an analytical and reflective approach, students will learn to interpret and evaluate media content in its symbolic, social, and political dimensions.

Course Objectives

  • Understand the theoretical foundations of media content analysis.
  • Apply qualitative and quantitative methods for media studies.
  • Critically interpret messages and representations disseminated in media.
  • Analyze the social, cultural, and political effects of media content.
  • Develop the ability to assess the ethical and social responsibility of media.
  • Foster a critical and reflective attitude toward media discourse.

Main Topics

  • Theories of content analysis and mass communication.
  • Content analysis methods: quantitative and qualitative.
  • Media representations of gender, race, class, and culture.
  • Agenda setting, framing, and priming in the media.
  • Media and the construction of social realities.
  • Critical analysis of news, entertainment, and advertising.
  • Audience studies and media reception.
  • Ethics and social responsibility in media content production and distribution.

Methodology

The course may be delivered in virtual, online, or hybrid (virtual-online) mode, combining theory, practice, and critical analysis through:

  • Virtual lectures on media analysis theories and methods.
  • Practical application of content analysis techniques.
  • Case studies of real media examples.
  • Discussion forums on media representations and their social impact.
  • Directed readings of relevant studies in media and communication.
  • Final project involving critical analysis of a selected media corpus.

Assessment

Assessment will focus on the critical application of learned theories and methods through:

  • Application of content analysis techniques to real media.
  • Critical interpretation of media representations.
  • Active participation in workshops, debates, and forums.
  • Final project involving comprehensive media content analysis.

Course Significance

Content and Media Analysis is essential for training communicators capable of interpreting public discourse critically, recognizing its impact on the construction of values, identities, and social realities. This course strengthens students’ analytical and ethical perspectives, preparing them to act consciously and responsibly in the complex global media landscape.
