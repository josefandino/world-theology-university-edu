BA-DCC-PC-1900 – Political Communication

Communication strategies applied to political campaigns, message management, and political media.

The course Political Communication analyzes the communication strategies used in political contexts, including electoral campaigns, government communication, and social movements. Students will learn to design effective political messages, manage media relations, use traditional and digital platforms, and understand the impact of communication on public opinion and the construction of political power—all from a critical and ethical perspective.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the theoretical and practical foundations of political communication.
  • Analyze the dynamics of message construction and political image management.
  • Design political communication strategies for campaigns and institutional communication.
  • Apply digital and traditional communication techniques in political scenarios.
  • Evaluate the influence of media on public opinion formation.
  • Promote ethical, democratic, and inclusive communication practices.

Main Topics:

  • Theory and evolution of political communication.
  • Electoral campaigns: strategic planning and message design.
  • Government and political organization communication management.
  • Media relations: strategies and challenges.
  • Political communication in social media and digital environments.
  • Construction of political image and discourse.
  • Poll analysis, public opinion, and crisis communication in politics.
  • Case studies of successful and failed political campaigns and administrations.

Methodology:

The course may be delivered in virtual, online, or hybrid format, combining theory, critical analysis, and practice through:

  • Virtual lectures on political communication strategies.
  • Case studies on contemporary political campaigns and movements.
  • Simulations of campaign design and political message management.
  • Discussion forums on ethics and responsibility in political communication.
  • Workshops on speechwriting, debates, and message crafting.
  • Final project: design of a comprehensive political communication strategy.

Assessment:

  • Design and presentation of political communication strategies.
  • Active participation in simulations, workshops, and forums.
  • Critical analysis of real political campaigns.
  • Final integrative project in applied political communication.

Course Significance:

Political Communication is essential for preparing professionals capable of designing and managing communication strategies in complex political settings, strengthening democratic processes and fostering informed civic participation. This course equips students to act effectively, responsibly, and ethically in campaigns, governments, political organizations, and media.
