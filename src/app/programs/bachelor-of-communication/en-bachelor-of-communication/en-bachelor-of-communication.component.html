Admission Requirements

To apply for the Bachelor's Degree in Communication, applicants must meet the following requirements:

  • Academic Transcripts: Official transcripts of secondary or higher education must be provided.
  • Resume or CV: An updated CV is required, reflecting professional, educational, or volunteer experience related to the field of communication, media, or marketing.
  • Personal Statement: Applicants must write a personal statement explaining their motivations for studying Communication, how they plan to apply what they learn in their professional career, and their interests within the field of communication and media.
  • Letters of Recommendation: Two letters of recommendation are required from professors, employers, or professionals who can attest to the applicant's skills and potential for studying and working in the communication field.
  • Application Fee: The application process includes a non-refundable fee.

Selection Criteria

Applicants must have completed secondary education with outstanding academic performance. Prior studies in communication are not required, but experience in media, marketing, or community work will be positively valued. Those with previous studies in fields such as social sciences, humanities, or related areas will have an advantage in the selection process.

Credits and Courses

The program consists of a total of 120 credits, distributed among core courses, electives, and optional courses, allowing students to personalize their learning according to their interests and specializations. The core courses include:

  • Communication Theories
  • Digital Marketing Strategies
  • Content Writing and Production
  • Crisis Management and Public Relations
  • Organizational Communication

The elective courses offer the opportunity to explore specialized areas of interest, such as communication in the entertainment media, creative advertising, or political campaign design.

Thesis or Comprehensive Exam

Upon completing the program, students must choose to complete a thesis or a comprehensive exam, which will allow them to demonstrate their mastery of the concepts learned. The program is designed to be completed within a flexible period, with the option for part-time or full-time study, and a maximum time frame of five years for completion.

Applicants for the Bachelor's Degree in Communication must meet a series of requirements to ensure their preparedness and ability to successfully complete the program. Below are the necessary requirements:

Official Academic Transcripts

Official academic transcripts from all previous studies, whether at the secondary or higher education level, must be submitted. The applicant must have completed secondary education and, preferably, a program of studies related to social sciences, communication, or related fields, with outstanding academic performance.

Resume or CV

The applicant must provide an updated resume that includes their professional experience, volunteering, or any relevant training in the field of communication, media, marketing, or advertising. The applicant must write a motivation letter explaining their reasons for applying to the Bachelor's Degree in Communication. Letters of Recommendation

At least two letters of recommendation are required from professors, employers, or mentors who can vouch for the applicant's leadership abilities, community work, or experience in communication. The letters should address the candidate's suitability for the program and their potential to apply what they have learned in a practical context.

Relevant Experience

Although not a formal requirement, it is recommended that applicants have prior experience in roles related to communication, media, or marketing. This experience may include volunteering with organizations, supporting marketing campaigns, or participating in digital or traditional communication projects.

Personal Interview

In some cases, the admissions committee may request a personal or online interview with the applicant. The purpose of this interview is to assess the applicant's commitment to the program, their academic and personal preparation, and their motivation to contribute to the field of communication.

Application Fee

All applicants must pay a non-refundable application fee at the time of submission. This fee covers the administrative costs related to the review of the application.

Recommended Prior Knowledge

Although not mandatory, it is recommended that applicants have completed previous courses in topics related to communication, media, marketing, or social studies. This prior knowledge will facilitate the understanding of advanced topics covered in the program.

Academic Requirements During the Program

Once admitted to the program, students must meet the following academic requirements to complete the Bachelor's Degree in Communication:

  • Total Credits: The student must complete a total of 120 credits, distributed among core courses, electives, and optional courses.
  • Minimum Grade Point Average: A minimum GPA of B or higher must be maintained in all courses to advance in the program and complete the final thesis or comprehensive exam.
  • Bachelor's Thesis or Comprehensive Exam: At the end of the program, students must choose between developing a research thesis (6 credits) focused on an area of communication or presenting a comprehensive exam that assesses their mastery of the program's key areas.
  • Study Mode: The program is 100% online and can be completed part-time or full-time, according to the student's needs. However, all academic work and the final project (thesis or exam) must be completed within a maximum of five years.

These requirements ensure that students are adequately prepared both academically and professionally to make effective interventions in the field of communication, combining scientific approaches with solid ethical principles.
