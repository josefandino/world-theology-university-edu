Introduction to production and broadcasting in traditional media such as radio and television.

The course Radio and Television Production introduces students to the technical and creative fundamentals of producing and broadcasting content in traditional media. It covers the stages of production, production techniques, scripting principles, and the handling of radio and television equipment. This course provides comprehensive training to develop effective content tailored to the specific dynamics of each medium.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the basic principles of radio and television production.
  • Become familiar with planning, production, and postproduction processes in both media.
  • Apply scripting and production techniques suited for radio and television.
  • Operate basic recording and broadcasting equipment.
  • Create creative content aligned with the characteristics of each medium.
  • Critically analyze the impact of media production on diverse audiences.

Main Topics:

  • History and evolution of radio and television.
  • Elements and formats of radio and television production.
  • Preproduction, production, and postproduction processes.
  • Scripting principles for radio and television.
  • Technical aspects: sound, lighting, cameras, and editing.
  • Direction and hosting of radio and television programs.
  • Live production and broadcasting techniques.
  • Current challenges and trends in radio and television.

Methodology:

The course may be delivered in virtual, online, or hybrid format, emphasizing practical and creative learning through:

  • Virtual lectures on production theory and techniques.
  • Hands-on workshops on scripting, production, and editing.
  • Simulations of radio and television programming.
  • Analysis of successful and failed media productions.
  • Discussion forums on technological and narrative trends in traditional media.
  • Final project: production of a radio or television program.

Assessment:

  • Production of radio and television content.
  • Development of scripts for radio and television programs.
  • Participation in practical workshops and transmission simulations.
  • Critical analysis of media productions.
  • Final integrative project in one of the two media formats.

Course Significance:

Radio and Television Production is a fundamental course for training versatile communicators capable of creating quality content in traditional media. Mastery of production techniques in radio and television remains crucial in a media landscape where convergence, audiovisual storytelling, and real-time communication continue to hold strategic relevance at both local and global levels.
