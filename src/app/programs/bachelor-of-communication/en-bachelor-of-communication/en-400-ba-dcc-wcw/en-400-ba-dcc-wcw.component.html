BA-DCC-WCW-400 – Writing and Creative Writing

Development of writing skills, both creative and technical, for various formats and platforms.

The course Writing and Creative Writing is designed to enhance students’ writing skills by combining creative and technical approaches across different formats and platforms. Through practical exercises and the analysis of representative works, students will develop competencies to produce clear, engaging, and strategic content adapted to narrative, academic, professional, and digital contexts. The course emphasizes the power of language as a tool for expression, communication, and social transformation.

Course Objectives:

  • Develop creative writing skills across various genres and styles.
  • Apply principles of technical writing to produce clear, precise, and coherent texts.
  • Adapt writing to different audiences, formats, and media.
  • Encourage creativity and originality in text production.
  • Analyze and improve the structure, style, and cohesion of one’s own and others’ texts.
  • Incorporate revision and editing strategies to optimize text quality.

Main Topics:

  • Foundations of writing: coherence, cohesion, clarity, and style.
  • Introduction to creative writing: narrative, poetry, and essay.
  • Techniques for creating characters, dialogue, and settings.
  • Principles of technical and academic writing.
  • Content adaptation for digital media: blogs, social networks, and web content.
  • Processes of text revision, proofreading, and editing.
  • Writing ethics: originality, plagiarism, and copyright.
  • Comprehensive writing project: creation of a textual portfolio.

Methodology:

The course will be delivered virtually, using didactic strategies that promote continuous practice and creative thinking:

  • Virtual writing workshops with individual and group feedback.
  • Critical analysis of creative and technical texts.
  • Guided readings of literary works and professional writing examples.
  • Weekly writing exercises in different genres and formats.
  • Discussion forums for exchanging creative experiences and perspectives.
  • Development of a final writing project in an open format.

Assessment:

Student assessment will consider progress and development through:

  • Writing portfolio (30%): Compilation of creative and technical exercises.
  • Analytical essay (20%): Critical reflection on a personal writing process.
  • Active participation in workshops and forums (20%): Constructive and well-founded contributions.
  • Text revision and improvement (15%): Application of editing and proofreading techniques.
  • Final project (15%): Development of a medium-length creative or technical text.

Course Significance:

Writing and Creative Writing is essential in the training of communication professionals, as the ability to express oneself with clarity, creativity, and effectiveness is indispensable across all professional fields. This course not only strengthens technical writing skills but also cultivates artistic sensitivity and communicative adaptability necessary for engaging with diverse audiences in a globalized, multicultural, and digital environment.
