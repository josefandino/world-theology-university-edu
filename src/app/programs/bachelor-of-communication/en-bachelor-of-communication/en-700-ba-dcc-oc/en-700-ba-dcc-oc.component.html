Strategies for internal and external communication in organizations, businesses, and government.

The course Organizational Communication analyzes communication strategies and processes that enable organizations, businesses, and governments to achieve their internal and external goals. It explores communication flows, organizational culture, public relations, and crisis management, offering tools for designing and implementing effective communication plans. The course emphasizes an ethical, strategic, and adaptive perspective suited for both local and global organizational contexts.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the foundations and strategic importance of organizational communication.
  • Analyze internal and external communication processes in various types of organizations.
  • Design communication strategies aligned with institutional goals.
  • Manage communication in organizational crisis scenarios.
  • Apply principles of ethical and culturally competent communication.
  • Evaluate the impact of communication practices on image, reputation, and organizational performance.

Main Topics:

  • Introduction to organizational communication: concepts and evolution.
  • Internal communication: channels, flows, and organizational culture.
  • External communication: public relations, media, and institutional image strategies.
  • Crisis communication and risk management in organizations.
  • Corporate communication in globalized and digital environments.
  • Corporate social responsibility and ethical communication.
  • Strategic planning in organizational communication.
  • Measurement and evaluation of communication effectiveness in organizations.

Methodology:

The course will be delivered virtually, combining theory and practice to promote active learning through:

  • Virtual lectures with active participation.
  • Analysis of real cases of organizational communication.
  • Design of internal and external communication proposals.
  • Guided reading of strategic and academic documents.
  • Forum discussions and critical reflection on organizational practices.
  • Capstone project: analysis and communication plan for a real or simulated organization.

Assessment:

  • Case analyses of organizational communication.
  • Design of communication strategies for specific scenarios.
  • Active participation in forums and collaborative activities.
  • Written exams on theoretical and practical content.
  • Final integrative project in organizational communication.

Course Significance:

Organizational Communication is fundamental for training professionals capable of strategically managing communication processes across all types of organizations. Mastering these competencies supports the creation of strong organizational cultures, effective public relations, and ethical responses to communication crises. This course prepares students to thrive in private companies, public institutions, and third-sector organizations, both locally and internationally.
