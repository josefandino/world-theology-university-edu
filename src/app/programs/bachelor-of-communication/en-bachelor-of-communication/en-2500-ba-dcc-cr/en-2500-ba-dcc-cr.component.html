BA-DCC-CR-2500 - Communication Research

Methods and techniques of research applied to communication, including qualitative and quantitative studies.

The course Communication Research introduces students to the main research methods and techniques applied to the communication field. Through a theoretical and practical approach, students will learn to design research projects, apply qualitative and quantitative methodologies, collect and analyze data, and present results in a rigorous, ethical, and relevant manner to better understand communication phenomena.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the epistemological and methodological foundations of communication research.
  • Design research projects tailored to communication-related questions and problems.
  • Apply qualitative and quantitative methods in communication studies.
  • Use data collection and analysis techniques rigorously.
  • Interpret research findings to inform communication strategies.
  • Promote ethical, critical, and culturally sensitive research practices.

Main Topics:

  • Introduction to communication research: concepts and approaches.
  • Research project design: questions, objectives, and hypotheses.
  • Qualitative methodology: interviews, focus groups, content analysis.
  • Quantitative methodology: surveys, experiments, and statistical analysis.
  • Data collection instruments and validation.
  • Qualitative and quantitative data analysis.
  • Ethics in communication research.
  • Research report writing and presentation of results.

Methodology:

The course may be delivered in virtual, online, or hybrid (virtual-online) mode, combining theory, practice, and applied projects through:

  • Virtual classes on research methods and techniques in communication.
  • Workshops on research project design and execution.
  • Practical application of data collection and analysis techniques.
  • Discussion forums on ethics and best practices in research.
  • Case studies of relevant communication research.
  • Final research project on a communication phenomenon.

Assessment:

  • Design of communication research projects.
  • Application of qualitative and quantitative techniques.
  • Analysis and interpretation of collected data.
  • Active participation in workshops, practices, and discussion forums.
  • Final research project and presentation of results.

Course Significance:

Communication Research is fundamental to training professionals capable of generating rigorous and relevant knowledge about communication phenomena. This course equips students with methodological tools to understand social, cultural, and media dynamics, and to critically and ethically support their professional actions in the communication field.
