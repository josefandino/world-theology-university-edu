The Doctor of Philosophy in Biblical Studies at World Theology University offers a carefully designed curriculum to equip students with the skills necessary for advanced research and in-depth analysis of the Scriptures. Throughout the program, students will engage in courses ranging from methodological issues to developing competencies in biblical and research languages, preparing them to write a high-level academic dissertation.

  • DPHBIWU 4100 Issues and Methods in Biblical Studies (3.00 credit hours): Explore contemporary topics in Old and New Testament studies, reviewing advancements in Hebrew and Greek, as well as key methods of biblical interpretation. Students will identify a dissertation-related topic and research it in depth.
  • DPHBIWU 4200 Principles of Teaching, Learning, and Course Design (3.00 credit hours): Review biblical perspectives on teaching and learning, focusing on adult education. Students will learn to design courses that facilitate learning and will apply these skills to creating a course related to their dissertation.
  • DPHBIWU 4400 Dissertation Proposal in Biblical Studies (3.00 credit hours): This course prepares students to draft their dissertation proposal. Through guided bibliographic research, they will learn to develop a research problem, identify appropriate methods, and write a doctoral proposal to be evaluated by a committee.
  • BLG 4340 Competence in Biblical Languages (0.00 credit hours): Students must demonstrate competence in Hebrew or Greek, which will be assessed by the biblical studies faculty.
  • DPHBIWU 4401-4405 Dissertation Research and Writing I-V in Biblical Studies (3-6 credit hours each, repeatable): These courses represent the dissertation research and writing phase. Students will work under the supervision of a professor to write a dissertation ranging from 40,000 to 110,000 words. As they progress, they will continue refining and completing their research work.
  • DPHBIWU 4406 Dissertation Review and Defense (3.00 credit hours): Once the dissertation is completed, students will defend their work before an evaluation committee. This process includes a thorough review of the content and an oral defense before a panel of experts.
  • RES 4340 Research Language Competency I (0.00 credit hours): Students must demonstrate competence in a research language related to their dissertation, enabling them to access the most relevant academic literature for their topic.
  • RES 1321 Research Language Competency II (0.00 credit hours): In addition to the first research language, students must demonstrate competence in a second language, providing even greater understanding of texts relevant to their dissertation.

Through these courses, students will not only develop advanced technical and methodological skills but also receive a comprehensive education that enables them to make a significant contribution to the field of biblical studies.

Admission Requirements

To be admitted into the Doctor of Science in Biblical Studies program at World Theology University, you must meet the following requirements:

  • Online Application: Complete an online application form providing your personal and academic information.
  • Official Transcripts: Submit official transcripts from all previous academic institutions, demonstrating a strong academic record in graduate studies.
  • Completed PDrTh Degree: A completed PDrTh (PhD in Theology) degree with a minimum GPA of 3.5 is required.
  • Research Paper: Submit a relevant research paper between 20 and 24 pages in length, showcasing your ability to conduct high-level academic research.
  • Proposed Research Topic: Provide a one-page anticipated research topic accompanied by a preliminary bibliography.
  • Academic References: Two letters of recommendation from academics who can attest to your research and teaching abilities.
  • Interview: Selected candidates will be interviewed by the admissions committee to assess their suitability for the program.
  • International Requirements: International students must meet additional requirements, such as language proficiency tests for the language of instruction, if necessary.
