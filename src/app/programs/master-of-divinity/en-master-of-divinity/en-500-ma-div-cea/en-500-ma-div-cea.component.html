MA-DIV-CEA-500 - Christian Ethics and Social Responsibility

This course examines the fundamental ethical principles of Christianity and their application to the moral dilemmas of the contemporary world in multicultural contexts. Through a theological, philosophical, and practical approach, students will analyze complex ethical issues such as social justice, human rights, poverty, bioethics, and environmental concerns. The course fosters the development of a critical ethical awareness grounded in Christian values and equips future leaders to act responsibly and compassionately in diverse local and global settings.

Course Objectives

  • Understand the biblical and theological foundations of Christian ethics.
  • Critically analyze major contemporary ethical dilemmas from a Christian perspective.
  • Apply Christian ethical principles to decision-making in professional, ministerial, and social contexts.
  • Evaluate various ethical positions in current debates, considering their biblical coherence and cultural relevance.
  • Develop comprehensive ethical proposals that promote justice, human dignity, and the common good.
  • Foster active social responsibility that is informed and respectful of cultural and religious diversity.

Main Topics

  • Biblical and theological foundations of Christian ethics.
  • Personal, social, and ecological ethics within the Christian tradition.
  • Human rights, social justice, and Christian engagement.
  • Contemporary dilemmas: bioethics, economics, politics, and the environment.
  • Social responsibility and civic engagement from a Christian perspective.
  • Intercultural ethics: challenges and opportunities in diverse contexts.
  • Christian ethical models in the face of contemporary moral pluralism.
  • Ethical leadership within the Church and the global society.

Methodology

The course will be delivered through expository virtual lectures, critical analysis of real ethical cases, discussion forums on contemporary dilemmas, readings of classical and current texts on Christian ethics, and practical social responsibility projects. Autonomous learning, respectful debate, and the application of course content to real-life situations will be encouraged.

Assessment

  • Analytical essays on ethical principles and their contemporary application.
  • Case studies on current ethical dilemmas.
  • Written exams on the foundations of Christian ethics.
  • Active participation in critical and collaborative discussion forums.
  • Final project: ethical proposal applied to a contemporary social or cultural issue.

Course Significance

The Christian Ethics and Social Responsibility course is crucial for the formation of professionals and leaders committed to a solid ethical practice responsive to the challenges of today's world. By integrating Christian principles into ethical reflection and action, students will be prepared to promote justice, equity, and peace in both local and global contexts. This course strengthens the capacity for ethical responsiveness to contemporary dilemmas, preparing students for integral leadership that positively impacts both the Church and society.
