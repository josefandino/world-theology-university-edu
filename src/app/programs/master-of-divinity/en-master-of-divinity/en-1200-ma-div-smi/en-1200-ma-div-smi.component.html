MA-DIV-SMI-1200 - Supervised Ministerial Internship

This course constitutes the practical culmination of the Master of Divinity program, offering students the opportunity to apply, under supervision, the theoretical knowledge, ministerial skills, and theological competencies acquired throughout their training. Under the guidance of an academic supervisor and a ministerial mentor, students will engage in real contexts of pastoral, educational, or social service, developing concrete projects, participating in ministerial activities, and critically reflecting on their practice in light of the ethical, theological, and professional principles learned.

Course Objectives

  • Apply theological knowledge and ministerial skills acquired in real ministry settings.
  • Develop practical competencies in pastoral leadership, teaching, counseling, or community service.
  • Foster critical reflection on ministerial practice in relation to theological and ethical principles.
  • Integrate academic learning and practical experience for the development of a contextualized and relevant ministry.
  • Strengthen communication, teamwork, conflict resolution, and transformational leadership skills.
  • Receive formative feedback and continuous evaluation from academic and ministerial supervisors.

Main Topics

  • Planning and execution of supervised ministerial projects.
  • Practical application of pastoral theology, counseling, teaching, and leadership.
  • Ministerial ethics in daily practice.
  • Management of community dynamics, conflict resolution, and promotion of spiritual growth.
  • Critical evaluation and self-assessment of ministerial performance.
  • Integration of theory and practice within the context of Christian service.
  • Development of a documented and reflective ministerial portfolio.
  • Preparation for long-term ministry in local and global contexts.

Methodology

The internship will be carried out through active participation in an assigned ministry, under the supervision of a ministerial mentor and an academic supervisor. Students must complete service hours, implement specific projects, attend supervision and reflection sessions, participate in discussion forums, and document their experiences in a final portfolio. The methodology will emphasize practical learning, critical reflection, and comprehensive professional development.

Assessment

  • Continuous evaluation of performance at the internship site by the ministerial mentor.
  • Periodic progress reports submitted to the academic supervisor.
  • Participation in reflection sessions and critical analysis of experiences.
  • Presentation of a final portfolio documenting activities, reflections, and learning outcomes.
  • Final project demonstrating ministerial impact, evidencing the integration of theory and practice.

Course Significance

The Supervised Ministerial Internship course is essential for consolidating the formation of Master of Divinity students, enabling them to demonstrate competence, ethical standards, and contextualization in ministry. Through the practical application of theological and ministerial knowledge in real-world settings, students will strengthen their vocation, leadership skills, and commitment to Christian service. This supervised experience ensures that World Theology University graduates are fully prepared to face the challenges and opportunities of twenty-first-century ministry with a global, sensitive, and transformative perspective.
