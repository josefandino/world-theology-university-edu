MA-DIV-TMGE-700 - Theology of Mission and Global Evangelism

This course explores the biblical, theological, and contemporary perspectives on the Church's mission in a globalized and pluralistic world. Students will examine the biblical foundations of mission, the historical development of missional thought, and the current challenges of evangelism in multicultural contexts. Emphasis is placed on the need for sensitive, contextualized, and ethically responsible missionary strategies, preparing future leaders for transformative ministry that respects cultural diversity and promotes justice, reconciliation, and Christian hope among all nations.

Course Objectives

  • Understand the biblical and theological foundations of mission and evangelism.
  • Analyze the historical evolution of missional thought and its impact on the global Church.
  • Identify the challenges and opportunities of evangelism in diverse and globalized cultural contexts.
  • Apply principles of contextualization and cultural sensitivity in missionary practice.
  • Develop innovative mission and evangelism strategies for the contemporary world.
  • Promote a holistic vision of Christian mission that includes spiritual, social, and ethical dimensions.

Main Topics

  • Biblical foundations of mission and evangelism.
  • History of Christian mission: from the early Church to the contemporary era.
  • Theologies of mission: evangelical, ecumenical, and contextual perspectives.
  • Contextualization of the gospel in diverse cultures.
  • Urban, digital, and rural/remote evangelism.
  • Holistic mission: evangelism, social justice, and community transformation.
  • Ethical and cultural challenges in global mission.
  • Designing missionary strategies in pluralistic and postmodern contexts.

Methodology

The course will be delivered through interactive virtual classes, analysis of biblical texts and key missional documents, case studies of contemporary missionary experiences, online debates, development of strategic projects, and simulations of missionary scenarios. Active participation, critical thinking, and practical engagement with global mission will be encouraged.

Assessment

  • Theological essays on the foundations and challenges of contemporary mission.
  • Case studies of successful missionary projects and their critical analysis.
  • Active participation in discussion forums on current missional issues.
  • Written exams on biblical and theological foundations of mission.
  • Final project: design of a contextualized missionary strategy for a specific cultural setting.

Course Significance

The Theology of Mission and Global Evangelism course is essential for forming leaders capable of engaging in God's mission with integrity, wisdom, and cultural sensitivity in a dynamic and diverse world. By equipping students with a profound understanding and a contextualized praxis of mission, this course strengthens the calling to be agents of transformation, reconciliation, and hope in all cultures and nations.
