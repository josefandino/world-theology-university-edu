MA-DIV-PTSE-900 - Public Theology and Sociopolitical Engagement

This course examines the role of Christian faith in the public sphere and its implications for promoting social justice and transforming social structures. Through a critical and theological approach, students will analyze the interaction between theology, public ethics, and contemporary sociopolitical processes. The course fosters a responsible understanding of Christian engagement with social, political, and economic issues, preparing students to act as agents of ethical and transformative change in their local communities and the global context.

Course Objectives

  • Understand the foundations of public theology and its relevance in today's context.
  • Critically analyze the historical and contemporary role of Christian faith in the public sphere.
  • Evaluate the impact of faith on the promotion of social justice, human rights, and social transformation.
  • Develop skills for intercultural and interreligious dialogue on sociopolitical issues.
  • Apply theological and ethical principles to responsible social and political action proposals.
  • Foster a vision of Christian leadership committed to peace, equity, and the common good.

Main Topics

  • Introduction to Public Theology: definition, history, and contemporary approaches.
  • The relationship between faith, politics, and the public sphere in the Christian tradition.
  • Social Justice Theology: biblical and ethical foundations.
  • Human rights, gender equity, and human dignity from a Christian perspective.
  • The role of the Church in social transformation and political advocacy.
  • Interreligious and intercultural dialogue for peace and global justice.
  • Public ethics and Christian discernment in times of sociopolitical crisis.
  • Designing social impact projects based on theological principles.

Methodology

The course will be delivered through virtual lectures, critical analysis of public theology documents and declarations, case studies of social transformation movements, online forum debates, preparation of applied theological essays, and the design of social action projects. Autonomous learning, ethical engagement, and critical thinking will be strongly encouraged.

Assessment

  • Analytical essays on the role of Christian faith in the public sphere.
  • Case studies on sociopolitical transformation experiences driven by Christian communities.
  • Active participation in critical discussion forums and analysis of contemporary ethical dilemmas.
  • Written exams on the foundations of public theology and Christian social ethics.
  • Final project: proposal for sociopolitical action based on principles of public theology.

Course Significance

The Public Theology and Sociopolitical Engagement course is vital for the formation of Christian leaders who understand their faith as a calling to ethical and social transformation within their contexts. In a world marked by inequalities, conflicts, and crises of values, this course equips students to act with responsibility, wisdom, and courage, promoting a more just, compassionate society grounded in the principles of the Kingdom of God. It strengthens World Theology University's commitment to relevant, global, and impactful theological education.
