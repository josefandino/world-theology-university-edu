MA-DIV-CAP-1300 - Integrative Final Project

This course represents the academic culmination of the Master of Divinity program, through the development of a theological or ministerial project that demonstrates the integration of knowledge, skills, and competencies acquired throughout the program. Under academic supervision, students will undertake a research project, theological reflection, or innovative ministerial proposal, applying rigorous academic methodologies and focusing on relevant problems or challenges in ministry and contemporary theological thought.

  • Develop a research or ministerial intervention project with academic rigor and contextual relevance.
  • Apply critical analysis skills, theological reflection, and advanced academic communication.
  • Demonstrate competence in the use of theological research methodologies and ethical principles in academic work.
  • Contribute to the advancement of theological knowledge or the strengthening of innovative ministerial practices.
  • Strengthen academic and ministerial leadership capacity to make an impact in diverse local and global contexts.

Main Topics

  • Formulation of the topic and objectives for the final project.
  • Methodological design of the research or ministerial proposal.
  • Critical review of relevant theological literature.
  • Analysis, interpretation, and application of primary and secondary sources.
  • Academic writing: structure, argumentation, and style.
  • Academic ethics: integrity, originality, and respect for sources.
  • Presentation and defense of the final project before an academic committee.

Methodology

The course will be conducted through personalized tutoring with an academic advisor, virtual sessions on methodological guidance, project development workshops, peer review processes, drafting phases, and the final project presentation. Autonomous work, critical thinking, and academic excellence in both written and oral production will be strongly encouraged.

Assessment

  • Submission of project proposals and progress according to established deadlines.
  • Continuous evaluation by the academic advisor.
  • Peer review and feedback from fellow students.
  • Grading of the final written project based on academic criteria of quality, originality, and relevance.
  • Oral defense of the project before an evaluation committee.

Course Significance

The Integrative Final Project is the culminating point of the Master of Divinity program, allowing students to demonstrate their academic maturity, ministerial competence, and ability to contribute significantly to theological reflection and Christian service in the contemporary world. Through this project, graduates consolidate their identity as leaders, researchers, and agents of transformation, prepared to make a positive impact in ecclesial, academic, and social contexts globally.
