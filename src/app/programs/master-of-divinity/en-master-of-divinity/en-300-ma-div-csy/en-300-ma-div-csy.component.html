MA-DIV-CSY-300 - Contemporary Systematic Theology

This course offers an in-depth study of the major currents in contemporary systematic theology, exploring their emergence, doctrinal foundations, and impact on the thought and practice of the present-day Church. Students will analyze how modern theological trends respond to social, cultural, and scientific challenges, and will critically evaluate their biblical coherence and ministerial relevance. Through a comparative and analytical perspective, the course fosters mature theological reflection and engagement with an informed, ethical, and contextualized Christian witness.

Course Objectives

  • Understand the major currents of contemporary systematic theology and their historical foundations.
  • Critically analyze the contributions and limitations of various modern theological schools.
  • Identify the influence of cultural, philosophical, and scientific factors on current theological development.
  • Evaluate the impact of contemporary theologies on the life and mission of the global Church.
  • Apply contemporary theological principles to pastoral reflection and the development of relevant ministerial proposals.
  • Develop a well-founded, critical, and contextually engaged personal theological position.

Main Topics

  • Introduction to Contemporary Systematic Theology: historical context and current challenges.
  • Liberation theology, feminist theology, and postcolonial theologies.
  • Contemporary evangelical theology: trends and current debates.
  • Narrative theology, postliberal theology, and public theology.
  • The impact of modernity and postmodernity on theological reflection.
  • Relationship between science, technology, and theology in the twenty-first century.
  • The mission of the Church in response to contemporary ethical, social, and ecological challenges.
  • Toward a global systematic theology: intercultural and interreligious perspectives.

Methodology

The course will be conducted through virtual lectures, analytical readings of contemporary theological texts, case studies of modern ecclesial movements, online debates, critical essays, and practical projects for ministerial application. Active participation, critical analysis, and collaborative work will be encouraged, fostering a profound and practical reflection on the course content.

Assessment

  • Analytical essays on specific theological movements.
  • Written exams on theological comprehension and application.
  • Virtual presentations on the impact of contemporary trends in real-world contexts.
  • Participation in critical and collaborative discussion forums.
  • Final project: theological proposal applied to a current ministerial or social challenge.

Course Significance

The Contemporary Systematic Theology course is fundamental for the formation of theological leaders competent in the critical and contextualized analysis of the Christian faith in today's world. It provides students with the intellectual and spiritual tools necessary to engage responsibly with contemporary realities, offering theologically relevant and ethically committed solutions. This course strengthens students' ability to contribute to academic, pastoral, and social dialogue, shaping leaders who understand and apply the Gospel in a dynamic, pluralistic, and globalized world.
