MA-DIV-ITID-1000 - Intercultural Theology and Interreligious Dialogue

This course offers an in-depth study of the relationships between cultures and religions from a theological perspective, promoting respect, mutual understanding, and interreligious cooperation. Students will explore the theological and ethical foundations of intercultural and interreligious dialogue, analyze models of interaction, and examine the opportunities and challenges presented by a world characterized by diversity. The course aims to prepare leaders capable of building bridges of understanding and collaboration, based on the recognition of the dignity and richness of diverse religious and cultural traditions.

Course Objectives

  • Understand the theological and ethical foundations of intercultural and interreligious dialogue.
  • Analyze the historical and contemporary dynamics of relationships between cultures and religions.
  • Identify the challenges and opportunities of interreligious dialogue in local and global contexts.
  • Develop intercultural communication skills and the ability to engage in respectful and constructive dialogue.
  • Apply principles of coexistence, justice, and interreligious cooperation in concrete projects.
  • Foster an inclusive theological vision that values diversity as a space for mutual enrichment.

Main Topics

  • Biblical and theological foundations of intercultural and interreligious dialogue.
  • Theology of hospitality, respect, and reconciliation.
  • Historical models of encounter and dialogue among religions.
  • Dynamics of intercultural communication: sensitivity, active listening, and conflict mediation.
  • Major religious traditions of the world: beliefs, practices, and ethical visions.
  • Contemporary challenges: religious pluralism, secularization, and fundamentalisms.
  • Interreligious dialogue for peace, justice, and sustainable development.
  • Designing interreligious cooperation initiatives in local and global contexts.

Methodology

The course will be conducted through virtual classes, analysis of theological texts and interreligious dialogue documents, case studies of successful intercultural initiatives, communication skills workshops, online debates, dialogue simulations, and the design of interreligious initiatives. Collaborative learning, critical reflection, and active participation in dialogue scenarios will be strongly encouraged.

Assessment

  • Analytical essays on theological and ethical foundations of interreligious dialogue.
  • Case studies on experiences of interreligious cooperation.
  • Active participation in discussion forums and intercultural dialogue simulations.
  • Written exams on religious traditions and principles of intercultural coexistence.
  • Final project: design of an interreligious cooperation proposal adapted to a specific context.

Course Significance

The Intercultural Theology and Interreligious Dialogue course is key to preparing leaders capable of promoting peace, understanding, and cooperation in an increasingly pluralistic and diverse world. By integrating theological principles with intercultural skills, students will be equipped to contribute to the building of more just, inclusive, and compassionate societies. This course reflects World Theology University's commitment to a global theological education that is sensitive to contemporary challenges and oriented toward positive impact in human and social relationships.
