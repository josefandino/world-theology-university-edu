MA-DIV-BEH-200 - Biblical Exegesis and Hermeneutics

This course offers rigorous training in the methods of biblical exegesis and hermeneutics, fundamental for a critical, historical, and theological interpretation of Scripture. Students will be introduced to the main theories and interpretive approaches applied to the Old and New Testaments, learning to analyze biblical texts in their original context and to translate their teachings into contemporary situations. Through the study of historical, linguistic, and theological tools, the course promotes a profound and responsible understanding of the Word of God for its application in teaching, preaching, and academic research.

Course Objectives

  • Understand the fundamental principles of biblical exegesis and theological hermeneutics.
  • Analyze the historical, cultural, and literary contexts of biblical texts.
  • Apply critical interpretive methods to selected passages from the Old and New Testaments.
  • Evaluate different hermeneutical approaches and their theological and ministerial implications.
  • Develop skills to interpret and communicate the biblical message clearly, accurately, and contextually.
  • Foster an attitude of respect and responsibility in interpreting sacred texts in the face of cultural and religious diversity.

Main Topics

  • Introduction to exegesis and hermeneutics: definitions, history, and contemporary relevance.
  • Methods of exegesis: grammatical, historical, literary, and canonical.
  • Principles of contextual interpretation: literary genre, historical and cultural background.
  • Hermeneutics of the Old Testament: law, prophets, wisdom literature, and poetry.
  • Hermeneutics of the New Testament: gospels, epistles, and apocalyptic literature.
  • Contemporary models of interpretation: liberationist, feminist, postcolonial, and other global approaches.
  • Homiletical and pastoral application of biblical exegesis.
  • Ethics of interpretation: responsibility, truth, and intercultural respect.

Methodology

The course will be conducted through interactive virtual classes, practical textual analysis workshops, online discussions of interpretation cases, guided readings of key works on exegesis and hermeneutics, the preparation of interpretative essays, and exercises in pastoral application. Active participation, autonomous learning, and the development of critical and reflective thinking will be promoted.

Assessment

  • Critical interpretation essays of selected biblical texts.
  • Comparative analysis of different hermeneutical approaches.
  • Written exams on fundamental concepts of exegesis and hermeneutics.
  • Active participation in discussion forums and academic debates.
  • Final project involving applied interpretation in a contemporary ministerial or social context.

Course Significance

The Biblical Exegesis and Hermeneutics course is essential for advanced theological education, as it equips students with the necessary tools to interpret Scripture critically, profoundly, and contextually. Through a respectful and academic approach, students will be able to address the hermeneutical challenges of a diverse and globalized society, contributing to the formation of informed, ethical, and committed faith communities aligned with the transformative message of the Word of God. This course strengthens the intellectual, spiritual, and practical foundation of future leaders, scholars, and professionals of faith in the twenty-first century.
