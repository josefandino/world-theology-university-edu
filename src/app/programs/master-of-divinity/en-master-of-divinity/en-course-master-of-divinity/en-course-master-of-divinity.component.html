The Master of Divinity program at World Theology University offers a comprehensive and deeply specialized curriculum, designed to provide students with rigorous theological training, a critical understanding of Scripture, and practical preparation for ministerial leadership in diverse and globalized contexts. Each course is structured to foster analytical thinking, ethical commitment, and excellence in pastoral service, equipping graduates to competently address the contemporary challenges of Christian ministry.

MA-DIV-ATF-100 - Advanced Theological Foundations: Systematic study of the fundamental doctrines of the Christian faith, their historical development, and contemporary application.

MA-DIV-BEH-200 - Biblical Exegesis and Hermeneutics: Critical, historical, and theological methods of biblical interpretation applied to texts from the Old and New Testaments.

MA-DIV-CSY-300 - Contemporary Systematic Theology: Analysis of major current theological movements and their impact on ecclesial thought and practice.

MA-DIV-HCT-400 - History of Christianity and Theological Thought: Exploration of the historical development of the Church and the major theological traditions throughout the centuries.

MA-DIV-CEA-500 - Christian Ethics and Social Responsibility: Christian ethical principles applied to contemporary moral dilemmas in multicultural contexts.

MA-DIV-PLM-600 - Pastoral Leadership and Ministry Management: Development of ethical leadership skills, management of religious organizations, and strategies for community growth.

MA-DIV-TMGE-700 - Theology of Mission and Global Evangelism: Biblical and contemporary perspectives on the Church’s mission in a globalized and pluralistic world.

MA-DIV-PCSC-800 - Pastoral Counseling and Spiritual Care: Techniques for spiritual accompaniment, emotional support, and pastoral guidance from a comprehensive theological perspective.

MA-DIV-PTSE-900 - Public Theology and Sociopolitical Engagement: Analysis of the role of Christian faith in the public sphere, social justice, and the transformation of social structures.

MA-DIV-ITID-1000 - Intercultural Theology and Interreligious Dialogue: Study of relationships between cultures and religions, promoting respect, mutual understanding, and interfaith cooperation.

MA-DIV-MTTR-1100 - Methodology for Theological Research: Academic research tools applied to theological study and the production of high-quality scholarly work.

MA-DIV-SMI-1200 - Supervised Ministerial Internship: Practical application of acquired knowledge in real ministry contexts under academic supervision.

MA-DIV-CAP-1300 - Capstone Project: Development of a theological or ministerial project that demonstrates the integration of knowledge, skills, and competencies acquired throughout the Master’s program.
