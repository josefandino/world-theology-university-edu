MA-DIV-MTTR-1100 - Methodology of Theological Research

This course provides comprehensive training in academic research methods applied to theological studies. Students will acquire conceptual and practical tools to develop rigorous, ethical, and contextually relevant theological research. The course addresses strategies for formulating research problems, conducting critical literature reviews, designing projects, analyzing primary and secondary sources, and writing high-quality academic papers. It equips students to contribute meaningfully to contemporary theological production and the global academic dialogue.

Course Objectives

  • Understand the epistemological and methodological foundations of theological research.
  • Develop skills to formulate pertinent and well-defined research questions.
  • Apply techniques for searching, analyzing, and synthesizing specialized academic literature.
  • Design theological research projects with academic rigor and contextual relevance.
  • Master citation standards, referencing styles, and academic writing norms in theology.
  • Foster research ethics, including originality, integrity, and respect for sources.

Main Topics

  • Theological epistemology and foundations of academic research.
  • Formulation of research problems and objectives in theology.
  • Critical literature review: strategies and digital tools.
  • Research project design: qualitative and quantitative methods in theological studies.
  • Use and analysis of primary sources (biblical, patristic, conciliar texts) and secondary sources (academic literature).
  • Academic style norms (APA, Turabian, Chicago) applied to theological writing.
  • Research ethics: academic honesty, proper citation, and plagiarism prevention.
  • Preparation of academic papers, theses, and articles for theological journals.

Methodology

The course will be delivered through expository virtual lectures, practical workshops on project formulation, bibliographic search exercises, critical analysis of theological articles, proposal defense simulations, participation in academic discussion forums, and the production of written assignments. Critical thinking, research autonomy, and academic excellence will be strongly promoted.

Assessment

  • Reflective essays on the foundations of theological research.
  • Development of a critical literature review on a specific theological topic.
  • Active participation in research workshops and simulation exercises.
  • Written exams on research methodologies and academic standards.
  • Final project: complete design of an original theological research proposal.

Course Significance

The Methodology of Theological Research course is fundamental for preparing researchers and theological leaders capable of producing original, relevant, and high-quality knowledge in academic and ministerial contexts. Through solid methodological training, students will be equipped to contribute to the advancement of theological thought, interreligious dialogue, and the strengthening of critical and reflective faith communities.
