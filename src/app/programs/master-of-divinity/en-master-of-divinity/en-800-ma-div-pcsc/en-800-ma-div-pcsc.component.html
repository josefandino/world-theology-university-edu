MA-DIV-PCSC-800 - Pastoral Counseling and Spiritual Care

This course provides comprehensive training in techniques of spiritual accompaniment, emotional support, and pastoral guidance, grounded in theological, psychological, and ethical principles. Students will explore models of pastoral counseling, dynamics of spiritual care, and crisis intervention strategies, developing practical skills to accompany individuals and communities through various life situations. The course takes a holistic approach, promoting a deep understanding of the human person, pastoral compassion, and intercultural sensitivity in the ministry of care.

Course Objectives

  • Understand the theological and psychological foundations of pastoral counseling and spiritual care.
  • Develop practical skills in active listening, accompaniment, and pastoral guidance.
  • Analyze emotional, spiritual, and relational dynamics in situations of need or crisis.
  • Apply ethical principles in the practice of pastoral counseling and spiritual care.
  • Design accompaniment strategies for multicultural and diverse contexts.
  • Foster a holistic ministry of care, sensitive to the spiritual and emotional needs of individuals.

Main Topics

  • Biblical and theological foundations of pastoral and spiritual care.
  • Models of pastoral counseling: classical and contemporary approaches.
  • Skills for active listening, empathy, and therapeutic communication.
  • Pastoral care in situations of grief, crisis, trauma, and life transitions.
  • Ethics and boundaries in pastoral counseling practice.
  • Intercultural dimensions of spiritual care.
  • Designing emotional and spiritual support programs within the community.
  • Pastoral self-care: emotional and spiritual health of the counselor.

Methodology

The course will be delivered through dynamic virtual classes, analysis of clinical and pastoral case studies, counseling session simulations, critical reading of specialized texts, participation in discussion forums, and development of practical spiritual care projects. Experiential learning, personal reflection, and collaborative work will be strongly encouraged.

Assessment

  • Reflective essays on foundations and models of pastoral counseling.
  • Case studies and simulations of spiritual care interventions.
  • Active participation in workshops and discussion forums.
  • Written exams on key concepts of pastoral counseling.
  • Final project: design of a pastoral counseling and spiritual care program for a specific community.

Course Significance

The Pastoral Counseling and Spiritual Care course is fundamental for equipping pastoral leaders to offer spiritual and emotional support in an ethical, sensitive, and effective manner. In a world marked by emotional complexity and cultural diversity, pastoral care becomes an essential ministry of accompaniment, comfort, and guidance. This course equips students to exercise compassionate and holistic leadership, promoting spiritual and emotional well-being within their communities and in global society.
