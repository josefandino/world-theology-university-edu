MA-DIV-PLM-600 - Pastoral Leadership and Ministerial Management

This course is designed to develop ethical and effective leadership skills within pastoral and ministerial contexts. Through a comprehensive approach, students will learn principles of religious organization management, transformational leadership, resource management, and community growth strategies. The course combines theological reflection with practical leadership tools, equipping students to lead faith communities strategically, sensitively, and sustainably, while respecting the cultural and social diversity of their contexts.

Course Objectives

  • Understand the biblical and theological principles of pastoral leadership.
  • Develop skills for ethical, transformational, and collaborative leadership.
  • Apply organizational management techniques in ministerial settings.
  • Design strategies for the growth, strengthening, and sustainability of faith communities.
  • Analyze contemporary challenges in pastoral administration and propose creative and contextualized solutions.
  • Foster a culture of service, accountability, and ministerial excellence.

Main Topics

  • Theological foundations of pastoral leadership and ministerial management.
  • Christian leadership models: transformational, servant, and contextualized leadership.
  • Management of religious organizations: planning, resource administration, and decision-making.
  • Team dynamics, effective delegation, and conflict resolution.
  • Pastoral communication: preaching, teaching, and organizational communication.
  • Community growth strategies and the development of local leadership.
  • Ethics in ministerial management: transparency, integrity, and social responsibility.
  • Innovation and pastoral adaptation in multicultural and globalized contexts.

Methodology

The course will be delivered through virtual classes, case studies on ministerial management, practical leadership workshops, development of community action plans, critical discussion forums, and simulations of organizational problem-solving. Personal reflection, teamwork, and the design of projects applied to real ministerial contexts will be encouraged.

Assessment

  • Reflection essays on leadership and ministerial management principles.
  • Case studies and analysis of organizational best practices.
  • Active participation in debates and collaborative workshops.
  • Written evaluations on the fundamentals of pastoral leadership.
  • Final project: design of a strategic plan for a ministerial organization or faith community.

Course Significance

The Pastoral Leadership and Ministerial Management course is crucial for preparing leaders capable of effectively, ethically, and sensitively leading faith communities in the face of contemporary challenges. Through a solid theological foundation combined with practical leadership and management tools, students will be equipped to promote the spiritual, organizational, and social growth of the churches and ministries they serve. This course strengthens the commitment to leadership centered on service, excellence, and positive impact in both local and global communities.
