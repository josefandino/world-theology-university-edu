MA-DIV-ATF-100 - Advanced Theological Foundations

This course offers a systematic and in-depth study of the fundamental doctrines of the Christian faith, examining their historical development, biblical foundations, and contemporary relevance. Through a critical and reflective approach, students will explore major theological currents, historical controversies, and modern challenges in the interpretation and application of doctrine. This knowledge will enable future spiritual and academic leaders to communicate and apply the foundations of the faith with precision and relevance in diverse and dynamic cultural contexts.

Course Objectives

  • Understand the fundamental doctrines of Christianity from a biblical, historical, and systematic perspective.
  • Analyze the historical development of major doctrines through councils, movements, and key theologians.
  • Identify various schools of theological thought and their impact on the contemporary church.
  • Apply theological principles to the analysis of current cultural contexts and the resolution of contemporary ethical dilemmas.
  • Develop critical skills to evaluate doctrinal positions in academic and ecclesial debates.
  • Integrate theological foundations into ministerial practice, teaching, and academic research.

Main Topics

  • Introduction to Systematic Theology: concept, purpose, and methods.
  • Doctrine of God: attributes, Trinity, and divine revelation.
  • Christology: the person and work of Jesus Christ.
  • Pneumatology: the study of the Holy Spirit and His work in the life of the believer and the Church.
  • Theological Anthropology: human nature, sin, and redemption.
  • Ecclesiology: nature, mission, and governance of the Church.
  • Eschatology: theological views on the end times.
  • Contemporary Applications: current challenges to orthodox theology in a globalized world.

Methodology

The course will be delivered through interactive virtual lectures, analysis of relevant theological case studies, moderated online discussions, critical readings of classical and contemporary texts, practical doctrinal analysis exercises, and the development of applied research projects. Active participation and autonomous learning will be encouraged, fostering an environment of respectful dialogue and critical thinking.

Assessment

  • Critical theological reflection essays based on assigned readings.
  • Analysis of contemporary cases requiring the application of theological principles.
  • Written exams to assess knowledge and understanding of key concepts.
  • Active participation in academic discussion forums and collaborative activities.
  • Final project involving applied theological research addressing a current context or a relevant ministerial issue.

Course Significance

The Advanced Theological Foundations course constitutes a cornerstone in the formation of Master of Divinity students, providing a rigorous and profound understanding of the doctrinal bases of Christianity. This formation equips future spiritual leaders, educators, and professionals to face the challenges of a multicultural and changing world, strengthening their critical thinking, ethical commitment, and communicative competence. By mastering theological foundations, students will be able to wisely and sensitively apply the principles of faith in their professional, ministerial, and academic contexts, making a significant contribution to global dialogue, the development of strong faith communities, and the advancement of relevant twenty-first-century theology.
