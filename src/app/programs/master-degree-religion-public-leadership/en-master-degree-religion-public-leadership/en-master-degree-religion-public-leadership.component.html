Since the dawn of humanity, religions have played a fundamental role in shaping the thoughts, cultures, and actions of societies. Throughout history, religion has served both as a source of inspiration for acts of heroism and justice and as a justification for some of the darkest events. Despite technological advancements and the growing influence of secular democracies, religious beliefs remain a powerful force in public life and in the daily lives of individuals.

Today, religion continues to hold a central position in debates on ethics, politics, peace, and conflict, with an influence that extends across various professional fields and sectors of society. Understanding this influence is more critical than ever, particularly for those working in areas where decisions and actions have a direct impact on communities and social well-being.

The Master’s Program in Religion and Public Leadership (MRPL) at World Theology University is designed to address this need. This one-year program is tailored for professionals with experience in primarily "secular" fields who are committed to creating positive societal change and view religion as a source of inspiration for peace and justice. Ideal candidates include journalists, government officials, humanitarian workers, educators, artists, healthcare professionals, business leaders, and lawyers, among others. These individuals can leverage this program to develop a profound understanding of the role of religion in the public sphere and apply it effectively in their professions.

The MRPL offers a unique opportunity to explore the complexities of religion in social, political, and cultural contexts, enabling students to tailor their learning experience to their specific interests. By the end of the program, graduates will be equipped to be informed leaders in their fields, capable of articulating and applying a deep and nuanced understanding of how religion influences public life, while contributing to a more just and peaceful world.

The Master’s Program in Religion and Public Leadership (MRPL) at World Theology University offers a unique educational experience characterized by its non-confessional and academic approach. This means that the program does not adhere to any particular religious tradition, allowing students to study religion objectively and with a critical spirit. The program is deeply committed to promoting just peace, helping students understand the dynamics of power, peace, and conflict surrounding religious beliefs and practices in the contemporary world.

One of the most significant features of the MRPL is its flexibility. Students have the opportunity to tailor their curriculum to align with their professional and academic interests. This interdisciplinary approach allows students to take courses from both the World Theology University School of Divinity and other university faculties. In this way, students can explore the relationship between religion and topics such as politics, law, public health, activism, and more, adapting their training to the needs and challenges of their specific professional contexts.

Why Study Religion and Public Leadership?

Throughout history, religion has played a central role in shaping societies. However, in today’s world, where global interactions are increasingly complex, there is a growing need for leaders and professionals who understand the profound influence that religion continues to exert on politics, culture, and human relationships. The power of religion remains immense, yet few academic programs prepare professionals to comprehend its complexity and its potential for both conflict and peacebuilding.

The MRPL is designed to fill that gap. It provides students with the tools necessary to critically understand and analyze the roles religions play in their respective contexts. This includes a special focus on how religious beliefs influence debates on social justice, human rights, and public policy creation. By the end of the program, graduates will be equipped to think innovatively about the interaction between religion and society and to lead with a clear vision that promotes peace and justice in their professional fields.

The Master’s Program in Religion and Public Leadership (MRPL) offers a series of courses designed to provide students with a comprehensive and critical understanding of religion in public contexts. The courses are carefully selected to combine theory, practice, and interdisciplinary application, enabling students to tailor their learning experience to their professional interests. Below are some of the key courses in the program:

  • Theories and Methods in the Study of Religion (4 credits): This foundational course introduces students to the major theories and methodological approaches used in the study of religion. Students will learn to analyze religious phenomena from an academic perspective, with an emphasis on critically understanding religious practices in the public sphere.
  • Religion and Public Leadership I and II (4 credits each): This two-part course provides an in-depth analysis of how religion influences leadership and decision-making in public contexts. Through case studies and discussions, students will explore how religious beliefs impact social, political, and cultural issues, as well as conflict resolution.
  • Power, Peace, and Conflict in Religion (4 credits): In this course, students examine the role of religion in social and political conflicts, as well as its potential to promote peace. Historical and contemporary cases are explored, with an analysis of strategies used by religious and secular leaders to address religion-related conflicts.
  • Ethics, Human Rights, and Religion (4 credits): This course focuses on the relationship between religion and human rights. Students analyze how religious traditions influence contemporary ethical debates, addressing topics such as social justice, human dignity, and the role of religion in promoting or violating human rights.
  • Religion, Politics, and Society (4 credits): In this course, students study the interaction between religion and political institutions. The course explores how religious beliefs and organizations have shaped public policy development and their impact on social movements and political change on a global scale.
  • Religion and Media (4 credits): This course examines how media represent and communicate issues related to religion. Students will investigate the role of media in shaping public perceptions of religion and analyze how religion is portrayed in contemporary media platforms, from journalism to social networks.
  • Final Project Seminar (4 credits): During this seminar, students develop their final project under the supervision of an academic advisor. The seminar includes collaborative sessions where students share progress on their projects and receive feedback from peers and the professor, ensuring the final project is high-quality and relevant to their professional field.

In addition to the key courses, students can select elective courses from the World Theology University School of Divinity and other university faculties, allowing them to tailor their curriculum to their specific professional interests. Elective courses may cover topics such as social activism, international development, public health, and education.

To successfully complete the Master’s Program in Religion and Public Leadership (MRPL) at World Theology University, students must fulfill the following academic and evaluation requirements:

  • Complete eight semester-long courses, each worth 4 credits. Students are required to complete a total of 32 credits distributed over two semesters to meet the program requirements. These courses are designed to provide a strong theoretical and practical foundation in the study of religion and its relationship to the public sphere.
  • Take at least four courses within the World Theology University School of Divinity. Half of the courses must be selected from the offerings of the World Theology University School of Divinity, ensuring that students acquire specialized knowledge in religion and public leadership. The other four courses can be chosen from other university faculties based on the student’s professional interests.
  • Complete two core courses: "Theories and Methods in the Study of Religion" and "Religion and Public Leadership." These two courses are essential to the program, providing the theoretical and methodological framework necessary to understand the complex interactions between religion and the public sphere. Students will learn to apply these theories to their professional fields.
  • Develop a final project related to the student’s profession. At the end of the program, students must create a final project that explores the intersection between religion and their specific professional field. This project is conducted under the supervision of an academic advisor and must be approved prior to submission. The final project may take various forms, such as a portfolio, a comprehensive paper, or a series of case studies.
  • Complete an oral examination. Students will present their final project before an evaluation committee composed of faculty members. This oral examination aims to assess the student’s in-depth understanding of their project and their ability to articulate their ideas coherently and critically.
  • Deliver a public presentation of the final project. At the end of the academic year, students must publicly share the findings and conclusions of their final project. This public presentation allows students to demonstrate their ability to communicate the results of their work to a broader audience, contributing to the discussion on the role of religion in the public sphere.
