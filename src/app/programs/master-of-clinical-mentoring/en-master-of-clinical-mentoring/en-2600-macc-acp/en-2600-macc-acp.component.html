MACC-ACP-2600 - Advanced Clinical Practice

Advanced Clinical Practice offers students the opportunity to engage in supervised clinical experiences involving complex and challenging cases. This course is designed to refine therapeutic skills, deepen clinical judgment, and enhance confidence in diverse counseling scenarios.

Course Objectives:

  • Strengthen advanced counseling techniques through direct client work.
  • Manage complex psychological and emotional cases with professional competence.
  • Demonstrate clinical reasoning and case formulation skills.
  • Integrate theory and practice in real-life counseling contexts.
  • Receive and apply constructive supervision and feedback.

Main Topics:

  • Advanced case management and intervention strategies
  • Clinical supervision and ethical decision-making
  • Multicultural and trauma-informed approaches
  • Documentation and professional communication
  • Reflective practice and self-assessment

Methodology:

This course involves on-site clinical practice under professional supervision, individual and group supervision sessions, reflective journaling, and case presentations. Students will work directly with clients, applying interventions learned throughout the program.

Assessment:

  • Evaluation by clinical supervisors
  • Documentation of case notes and treatment plans
  • Participation in supervision and reflective discussions
  • Final case study presentation

Importance of the Course:

This course marks the culmination of clinical training, preparing students to enter the field as competent, ethical, and confident professionals capable of addressing complex mental health challenges.
