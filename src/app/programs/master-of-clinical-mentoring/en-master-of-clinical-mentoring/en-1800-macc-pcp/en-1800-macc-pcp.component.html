MACC-PCP-1800 - Psychopathology in Clinical Practice

Psychopathology in Clinical Practice offers an in-depth study of mental disorders from a clinical and diagnostic perspective. Students will examine classification systems such as the DSM and ICD, explore symptomatology, and develop strategies for assessment and treatment planning in real-world clinical environments.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the major categories of mental disorders and their diagnostic criteria.
  • Identify symptoms, causes, and progression of common and complex psychological conditions.
  • Learn how to conduct clinical interviews and assessments.
  • Integrate diagnostic knowledge with appropriate treatment approaches.
  • Explore ethical and cultural considerations in diagnosis and intervention.

Main Topics:

  • Diagnostic frameworks: DSM-5 and ICD-11
  • Anxiety, mood, psychotic, and personality disorders
  • Neurodevelopmental and neurocognitive disorders
  • Trauma- and stressor-related disorders
  • Assessment tools and diagnostic interviews
  • Case formulation and treatment planning

Methodology:

The course uses clinical case studies, diagnostic simulations, lectures, role-playing, and group discussions to reinforce learning and application of psychopathological principles.

Assessment:

  • Written exams and diagnostic quizzes
  • Case study presentations
  • Clinical observation and reporting assignments
  • Participation in group diagnostic exercises

Importance of the Course:

This course equips students with the clinical insight and diagnostic skills necessary to recognize and manage mental disorders in professional counseling and mental health settings.
