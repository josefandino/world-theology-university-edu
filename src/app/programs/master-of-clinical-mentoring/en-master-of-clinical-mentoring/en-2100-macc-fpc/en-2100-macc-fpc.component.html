MACC-FPC-2100 - Family Psychology and Counseling

Family Psychology and Counseling explores the dynamics of family systems and their impact on individual and group behavior. The course emphasizes practical counseling strategies aimed at restoring and strengthening family relationships. Students will learn how to assess family structures, identify patterns of dysfunction, and apply therapeutic techniques that promote healing and communication within the family unit.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand key theories and concepts in family psychology.
  • Identify common family conflicts and their psychological roots.
  • Learn effective counseling techniques for families and couples.
  • Develop skills to create safe, supportive environments for family dialogue.
  • Explore the role of culture, values, and spirituality in family functioning.

Main Topics:

  • Family systems theory and dynamics
  • Attachment patterns and parenting styles
  • Communication strategies within the family
  • Conflict resolution and reconciliation processes
  • Blended families, divorce, and remarriage issues
  • Therapeutic approaches to family counseling

Methodology:

This course integrates lectures, real-life case studies, group discussions, family counseling simulations, and reflective assignments to enhance practical understanding.

Assessment:

  • Family assessment project
  • Quizzes on theories and counseling strategies
  • Role-play counseling sessions
  • Final paper analyzing a specific family dynamic

Importance of the Course:

This course prepares students to effectively counsel families facing emotional, relational, and structural challenges, enabling healthier and more resilient relationships.
