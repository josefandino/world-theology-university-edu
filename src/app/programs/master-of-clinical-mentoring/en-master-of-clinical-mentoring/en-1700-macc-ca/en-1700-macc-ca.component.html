MACC-CA-1700 - Counseling for Addictions

Counseling for Addictions provides students with the theoretical knowledge and practical strategies necessary to support individuals struggling with substance abuse and behavioral addictions. The course emphasizes evidence-based approaches to treatment and recovery in both individual and group counseling settings.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the psychological, emotional, and physiological aspects of addiction.
  • Identify the stages of addiction and recovery.
  • Learn intervention and counseling techniques for addiction treatment.
  • Explore the role of family, community, and support networks in recovery.
  • Develop cultural competence when working with diverse populations affected by addiction.

Main Topics:

  • Types of addictions: alcohol, drugs, gambling, internet, and more
  • The cycle of addiction and relapse prevention
  • Motivational interviewing and cognitive-behavioral approaches
  • 12-step programs and alternative recovery models
  • Co-occurring disorders and dual diagnosis
  • Ethical considerations in addiction counseling

Methodology:

The course incorporates lectures, video case reviews, group discussions, role-play scenarios, and guest speakers from rehabilitation and support centers.

Assessment:

  • Reflection papers and quizzes
  • Case study analysis and intervention plan
  • Participation in simulated counseling sessions
  • Final project or exam

Importance of the Course:

This course prepares students to offer effective and compassionate support to individuals battling addiction, equipping future counselors with tools that promote healing, accountability, and long-term recovery.
