MACC-DCP-2300 - Development of Counseling Programs

Development of Counseling Programs introduces students to the process of creating, implementing, and managing effective counseling programs tailored to diverse organizational and community settings. The course emphasizes practical planning, needs assessment, resource allocation, and outcome evaluation to ensure program success and sustainability.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the stages of developing counseling programs from concept to execution.
  • Conduct community and organizational needs assessments.
  • Create strategic plans aligned with counseling goals and populations served.
  • Identify key resources, partnerships, and funding opportunities.
  • Design evaluation methods to measure program effectiveness.

Main Topics:

  • Principles of program development
  • Needs assessment tools and techniques
  • Strategic planning in counseling
  • Program implementation and management
  • Monitoring, evaluation, and reporting
  • Ethical and cultural considerations in program development

Methodology:

This course combines lectures, case studies, group workshops, and real-life project simulations to prepare students for practical application.

Assessment:

  • Program proposal based on a specific community need
  • Group project: counseling program design and presentation
  • Case study analysis
  • Final written report: evaluation plan for a counseling initiative

Importance of the Course:

This course equips students with the skills necessary to lead and shape impactful counseling initiatives, meeting the emotional and psychological needs of individuals in various settings with professionalism and vision.
