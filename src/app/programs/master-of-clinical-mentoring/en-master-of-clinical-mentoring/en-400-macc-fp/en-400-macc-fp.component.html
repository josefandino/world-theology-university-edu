MACC-FP-400 - Fundamentals of Psychotherapy

Fundamentals of Psychotherapy provides an overview of the primary therapeutic techniques and approaches used in clinical settings. The course introduces students to the theoretical foundations and practical applications of various psychotherapeutic models.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the key principles behind major psychotherapy approaches.
  • Learn the goals, strategies, and techniques of common therapeutic models.
  • Develop basic skills for conducting therapeutic conversations.
  • Identify appropriate therapeutic methods for different clinical contexts.
  • Explore ethical and cultural considerations in psychotherapy.

Main Topics:

  • Overview of psychotherapy: definition and history.
  • Psychoanalytic and psychodynamic therapy.
  • Cognitive-behavioral therapy (CBT).
  • Humanistic and person-centered therapy.
  • Integrative and eclectic approaches.
  • Therapeutic relationship and communication skills.

Methodology:

The course includes lectures, role-playing, video demonstrations, case study discussions, and guided practice in therapeutic techniques.

Assessment:

Evaluation will be based on:

  • Written exams on theoretical models and techniques.
  • Participation in role-plays and practical exercises.
  • Case analysis reports applying therapeutic concepts.
  • Reflection papers on personal learning and application.

Importance of the Course:

This course lays the groundwork for clinical practice by introducing students to the core methods and principles of psychotherapy, essential for any future mental health professional.
