MACC-RCC-2500 - Research in Clinical Counseling

Research in Clinical Counseling guides students in the development of research projects that aim to contribute to the growth and innovation of clinical counseling and applied psychology. Emphasis is placed on ethical research practices, evidence-based methodology, and the integration of findings into real-world clinical settings.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the role of research in clinical counseling and psychological practice.
  • Develop skills to design, conduct, and evaluate qualitative and quantitative research studies.
  • Apply appropriate research methodologies to investigate clinical issues.
  • Interpret and present research findings effectively.
  • Promote ethical standards in psychological research.

Main Topics:

  • Research design and methodology
  • Literature review and hypothesis formation
  • Data collection techniques (surveys, interviews, case studies)
  • Data analysis and interpretation
  • Writing research reports and academic articles
  • Ethical issues in clinical research

Methodology:

The course combines theoretical instruction with practical application, including the development of an individual or group research project. Students will participate in peer reviews, guided workshops, and presentations of research proposals and findings.

Assessment:

  • Submission of a complete research proposal
  • Development of a clinical counseling research project
  • Oral presentation of research findings
  • Final paper or poster summarizing the study

Importance of the Course:

This course equips students with the tools to critically engage with scientific literature, contribute to the body of knowledge in clinical counseling, and apply research to improve mental health interventions and outcomes.
