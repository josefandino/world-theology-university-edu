MACC-CE-2900 - Comprehensive Examination

Comprehensive Examination is a culminating evaluation designed to assess the student’s mastery of the core concepts, theories, and practices taught throughout the clinical counseling program. It ensures readiness for professional practice or further academic study.

Course Objectives:

  • Review and integrate knowledge from all major courses.
  • Demonstrate critical thinking and application of theory.
  • Evaluate counseling scenarios and make informed decisions.
  • Articulate ethical responses to complex clinical situations.
  • Pass a comprehensive written and/or oral exam.

Main Topics:

  • Foundations of counseling and psychotherapy
  • Psychopathology and psychological assessment
  • Crisis and trauma intervention
  • Ethics in clinical practice
  • Family and group therapy techniques

Methodology:

Students will participate in structured review sessions, take mock exams, and receive feedback to identify areas needing improvement. The comprehensive exam may include multiple-choice questions, case analyses, and essay responses.

Assessment:

  • Completion of review modules and mock exams
  • Final written and/or oral comprehensive examination
  • Minimum passing score established by the academic committee

Importance of the Course:

The comprehensive examination validates the student’s academic and clinical competence. It is a required component for graduation and often serves as a prerequisite for licensure or further professional advancement.
