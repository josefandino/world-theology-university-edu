MACC-IP-700 - Introduction to Psychopathology

Introduction to Psychopathology provides an overview of the most common psychological disorders, exploring their symptoms, diagnostic criteria, and implications for treatment in clinical practice. Students will learn the foundations of mental illness and how it is classified and identified.

Course Objectives:

  • Identify and describe major psychological disorders based on standard diagnostic criteria (e.g., DSM-5).
  • Understand the clinical characteristics and presentations of each disorder.
  • Explore biological, psychological, and social factors involved in mental illness.
  • Recognize the impact of disorders on individuals, families, and communities.
  • Introduce basic methods of assessment and intervention.

Main Topics:

  • Foundations of psychopathology.
  • Anxiety, mood, and depressive disorders.
  • Psychotic and personality disorders.
  • Neurodevelopmental and trauma-related disorders.
  • Diagnostic tools and classification systems.

Methodology:

This course includes lectures, case analysis, video observations, diagnostic exercises, and group discussions to support a comprehensive understanding of mental disorders.

Assessment:

Evaluation will be based on:

  • Quizzes and exams on diagnostic criteria and terminology.
  • Written case analysis and symptom identification.
  • Class participation and discussion of clinical examples.

Importance of the Course:

This course lays the groundwork for advanced clinical training by equipping students with the tools to recognize and understand mental health disorders in a professional setting.
