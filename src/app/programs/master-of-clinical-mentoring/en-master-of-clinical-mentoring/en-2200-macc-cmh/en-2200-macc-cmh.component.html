MACC-CMH-2200 - Counseling for Mental Health

Counseling for Mental Health focuses on the integration of mental health principles into community settings, especially within congregational contexts. The course equips students with strategies to recognize, address, and support emotional and psychological well-being, while reducing stigma and promoting a culture of care.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand foundational concepts of mental health and emotional well-being.
  • Recognize common mental health challenges in community and faith-based environments.
  • Learn intervention techniques that support mental health within a congregational setting.
  • Develop skills to foster safe spaces for emotional expression and recovery.
  • Promote collaboration between spiritual and mental health care resources.

Main Topics:

  • Understanding mental health and its impact on daily life
  • Mental health stigma in faith-based communities
  • Identifying signs of emotional distress
  • Community-based approaches to mental health care
  • Integrating psychological and spiritual support
  • Referral practices and when to involve clinical professionals

Methodology:

This course uses a mix of theoretical instruction, role-playing scenarios, real-life case discussions, group projects, and reflection journals to develop practical skills.

Assessment:

  • Weekly reflective journals on mental health topics
  • Group presentation on a community mental health strategy
  • Case study analysis and response plan
  • Final project: mental health integration plan for a local community

Importance of the Course:

This course empowers future counselors and community leaders to address mental health needs compassionately and effectively, bridging the gap between spiritual guidance and emotional care.
