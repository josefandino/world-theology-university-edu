MACC-IC-800 - Integrated Counseling

Integrated Counseling explores the use of multiple psychological theories and intervention models in a unified approach to counseling. Students will learn how to apply techniques from different frameworks to meet the diverse needs of clients in a flexible and effective way.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the foundations and principles of integrative counseling models.
  • Evaluate and compare major psychological theories and their clinical applications.
  • Learn to develop individualized treatment plans based on client needs.
  • Practice the integration of cognitive, behavioral, humanistic, and systemic approaches.
  • Apply ethical and culturally competent practices in integrative counseling.

Main Topics:

  • Overview of counseling theories (CBT, humanistic, psychodynamic, systemic, etc.).
  • Principles of integration and eclecticism.
  • Case formulation and intervention planning.
  • Cultural sensitivity and ethical considerations in integrated practice.
  • Techniques for blending methods in real-life scenarios.

Methodology:

The course includes theory discussions, practical demonstrations, clinical simulations, and critical analysis of case studies to foster a deep understanding of integrative practice.

Assessment:

Students will be assessed through:

  • Written assignments and theoretical reflections.
  • Development of integrated treatment plans.
  • Case study presentations.
  • Participation in discussions and practical exercises.

Importance of the Course:

This course prepares students to be flexible and adaptive counselors, capable of drawing from multiple therapeutic traditions to effectively support diverse clients in a range of settings.
