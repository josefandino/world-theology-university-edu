MACC-ACP-900 - Applied Clinical Psychology

Applied Clinical Psychology focuses on the practical use of clinical psychology principles in professional counseling environments. Students will gain hands-on experience in assessment, diagnosis, and intervention planning across diverse client populations.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand core concepts and methods used in clinical psychology.
  • Apply psychological theories to real-world counseling situations.
  • Develop clinical skills for assessment, diagnosis, and intervention.
  • Gain knowledge of ethical and legal responsibilities in clinical practice.
  • Enhance professional competence through evidence-based practices.

Main Topics:

  • Introduction to clinical assessment tools and techniques.
  • Case conceptualization and diagnostic processes.
  • Evidence-based intervention models.
  • Ethical issues and professional boundaries in clinical settings.
  • Multicultural considerations in applied clinical practice.

Methodology:

The course includes lectures, clinical simulations, case studies, group discussions, and supervised practical exercises to enhance clinical competence.

Assessment:

Students will be evaluated through:

  • Clinical case reports and treatment plans.
  • Simulated counseling sessions and peer reviews.
  • Quizzes and written exams on clinical concepts.
  • Participation in discussions and role-plays.

Importance of the Course:

This course equips students with essential clinical skills needed for effective counseling practice, preparing them for work in mental health clinics, hospitals, and private practice environments.
