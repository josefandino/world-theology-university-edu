MACC-MC-1400 - Marriage Counseling

Marriage Counseling focuses on principles and therapeutic techniques to guide couples through relational challenges. The course emphasizes conflict resolution, communication improvement, and the restoration of emotional and relational bonds.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the foundations of marital relationships from psychological and emotional perspectives.
  • Identify common issues that affect couples, such as communication breakdowns, infidelity, and unmet expectations.
  • Apply counseling techniques tailored to couples in distress.
  • Facilitate conflict resolution and mutual understanding.
  • Promote healing, forgiveness, and relational growth.

Main Topics:

  • Stages of marital development and challenges.
  • Communication and active listening in marriage.
  • Conflict resolution strategies.
  • Therapeutic models for couples (e.g., Emotionally Focused Therapy, Gottman Method).
  • Sexual intimacy and emotional connection.
  • Crisis situations in marriage (e.g., affairs, separation).

Methodology:

This course integrates theoretical instruction with practical exercises, including role-playing, case studies, group discussions, and counseling simulations with peer feedback.

Assessment:

Students will be assessed through:

  • Quizzes and written exams on theories and techniques.
  • Couple counseling session simulations.
  • Reflection essays on personal views of marriage and counseling.
  • Development of a counseling plan for a case study couple.

Importance of the Course:

This course equips students with the tools to support couples in crisis, enhance relationship satisfaction, and contribute to the strengthening of marriages through therapeutic guidance.
