MACC-PMD-1500 - Psychology of Mood Disorders

Psychology of Mood Disorders explores the clinical characteristics, diagnosis, and treatment of mood disorders such as depression, bipolar disorder, and related affective conditions. The course provides an in-depth understanding of the biological, psychological, and social factors that influence mood regulation and emotional well-being.

Course Objectives:

  • Identify and differentiate the various types of mood disorders.
  • Understand the diagnostic criteria according to DSM-5 and other diagnostic tools.
  • Analyze the causes and risk factors associated with mood disorders.
  • Examine evidence-based treatment options including psychotherapy and pharmacological interventions.
  • Evaluate case studies to apply diagnostic and therapeutic approaches in clinical settings.

Main Topics:

  • Major Depressive Disorder (MDD)
  • Bipolar I and II Disorders
  • Cyclothymia and Persistent Depressive Disorder
  • Suicidality and crisis management
  • Cognitive, behavioral, and psychodynamic treatment approaches
  • The role of medication and interdisciplinary treatment plans

Methodology:

This course includes lectures, interactive discussions, case study analysis, guest speakers from clinical practice, and experiential learning through role-play and diagnostic simulations.

Assessment:

Students will be assessed through:

  • Written exams covering diagnostic criteria and treatment models
  • Analysis of clinical case studies
  • Participation in classroom discussions and presentations
  • Final project focused on designing a treatment plan for a specific mood disorder

Importance of the Course:

This course prepares students to effectively recognize and treat mood disorders, which are among the most common and debilitating mental health conditions in clinical practice. It enhances their competence in providing compassionate, evidence-based care.
