MACC-PP-1200 - Personality Psychology

Personality Psychology explores major theories and research related to human personality. The course examines how individual personality traits influence behavior, relationships, and overall emotional well-being.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand key theories of personality development and structure.
  • Analyze various personality assessment tools and their applications.
  • Examine the role of personality in interpersonal relationships.
  • Evaluate how personality impacts emotional and psychological health.
  • Explore cultural, biological, and environmental factors that shape personality.

Main Topics:

  • Psychoanalytic, trait, humanistic, and cognitive-behavioral theories.
  • Personality assessment methods (e.g., MMPI, Big Five).
  • Personality and mental health.
  • Personality in the workplace and social settings.
  • Developmental and cross-cultural perspectives on personality.

Methodology:

The course includes lectures, personality inventories, case studies, reflective essays, and class discussions.

Assessment:

Students will be evaluated through:

  • Quizzes and written exams on personality theories.
  • Analysis and interpretation of personality assessments.
  • Research projects on personality-related topics.
  • Participation in group discussions and presentations.

Importance of the Course:

This course equips students with insights into personality structure and development, helping them to understand themselves and others better and apply this knowledge in counseling, education, and workplace environments.
