MACC-FC-1000 - Family Counseling

Family Counseling introduces students to theories and practical techniques used in counseling families. The course emphasizes understanding family dynamics, communication patterns, and conflict resolution strategies to promote healthier family relationships.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand foundational theories of family systems and therapy.
  • Identify and analyze family dynamics and roles.
  • Apply counseling techniques to address family conflicts.
  • Develop skills for facilitating communication and problem-solving within families.
  • Learn how to work with diverse family structures and cultural backgrounds.

Main Topics:

  • Family systems theory and models of therapy.
  • Assessment of family relationships and communication styles.
  • Strategies for managing conflict and building cohesion.
  • Working with blended families and multicultural considerations.
  • Ethical issues in family counseling.

Methodology:

The course includes lectures, family case studies, role-playing exercises, group projects, and simulated counseling sessions.

Assessment:

Students will be evaluated through:

  • Analysis of family case scenarios.
  • Reflection papers and counseling plans.
  • Role-play evaluations and class participation.
  • Midterm and final written exams.

Importance of the Course:

This course prepares students to effectively support and guide families through challenges, contributing to improved mental health and relational well-being in diverse contexts.
