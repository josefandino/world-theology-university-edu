MACC-TP-1900 - Trauma Psychology

Trauma Psychology explores the psychological impact of traumatic experiences and the therapeutic strategies used for intervention and recovery. The course covers the theoretical foundations of trauma, including acute and chronic trauma, and emphasizes evidence-based practices for helping individuals who have experienced abuse, domestic violence, accidents, and other life-altering events.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the psychological and physiological effects of trauma on individuals across the lifespan.
  • Identify types of trauma and trauma-related disorders such as PTSD.
  • Learn intervention models and therapeutic approaches for trauma recovery.
  • Develop skills for assessing trauma impact and planning appropriate treatment.
  • Examine ethical, cultural, and relational dynamics in trauma-informed care.

Main Topics:

  • Definition and classification of trauma
  • Neurobiology of trauma and memory
  • Trauma in childhood, adolescence, and adulthood
  • Domestic violence and sexual abuse
  • Trauma-focused cognitive-behavioral therapy (TF-CBT)
  • Resilience, post-traumatic growth, and recovery models

Methodology:

This course uses a combination of lectures, clinical case analysis, videos, trauma-informed role-plays, group discussions, and reflective assignments.

Assessment:

  • Research papers and trauma case analysis
  • Participation in trauma intervention simulations
  • Quizzes and exams on theoretical content
  • Reflection essays on trauma-related topics

Importance of the Course:

Students will gain critical knowledge and skills for working effectively with individuals affected by trauma, preparing them to offer competent and compassionate care in clinical and community settings.
