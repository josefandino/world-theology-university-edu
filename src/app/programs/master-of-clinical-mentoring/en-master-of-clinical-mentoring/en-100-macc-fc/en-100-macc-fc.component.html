MACC-FC-100 - Foundations of Counseling

Foundations of Counseling introduces students to the basic principles of counseling, the ethics and responsibilities of professional practice, and an overview of different counseling approaches used across a variety of settings.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the core principles and purpose of counseling.
  • Identify the roles and ethical responsibilities of professional counselors.
  • Explore the main theoretical approaches in counseling.
  • Recognize how counseling is applied in diverse cultural and clinical contexts.
  • Develop foundational skills for effective counselor-client interactions.

Main Topics:

  • Definition and scope of counseling.
  • Ethical standards and legal considerations.
  • Counseling theories (person-centered, cognitive-behavioral, etc.).
  • Counselor-client relationship and communication skills.
  • Cultural competence and counseling in different settings.

Methodology:

The course combines lectures, readings, case study analysis, discussions, and role-play activities to promote understanding and application of foundational counseling knowledge and skills.

Assessment:

Student performance will be evaluated through:

  • Written reflections and response papers.
  • Quizzes on theoretical content and terminology.
  • Participation in class discussions and role-plays.
  • A final project or exam evaluating comprehension of course materials.

Importance of the Course:

This course is essential for anyone entering the field of counseling, as it lays the groundwork for further study and practice by providing an integrated understanding of the profession's ethical, theoretical, and practical components.
