MACC-CYA-1600 - Counseling for Youth and Adolescents

Course Description:

Counseling for Youth and Adolescents focuses on the emotional, psychological, and social development of children and teenagers. The course equips students with the knowledge and skills needed to provide effective support, guidance, and therapeutic intervention tailored to the unique challenges of this age group.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the developmental stages of youth and adolescents.
  • Identify common emotional and behavioral issues during adolescence.
  • Apply age-appropriate counseling techniques and strategies.
  • Explore the role of family, school, and social environment in adolescent development.
  • Develop practical skills for building rapport and trust with young clients.

Main Topics:

  • Developmental psychology of youth and adolescents
  • Anxiety, depression, and behavioral disorders in teens
  • Bullying, peer pressure, and self-esteem issues
  • Family dynamics and communication
  • School-related stress and academic performance
  • Therapeutic techniques: CBT, play therapy, and narrative therapy

Methodology:

The course uses lectures, group discussions, role-play, case studies, and guest speakers from youth-focused organizations and clinics.

Assessment:

  • Written assignments and quizzes
  • Case study evaluations
  • Interactive group projects
  • Final counseling simulation or research paper

Importance of the Course:

This course provides essential tools for those who work with young people, preparing counselors to respond effectively to the emotional and mental health needs of youth and adolescents in various settings.
