MACC-CGL-2000 - Counseling in Grief and Loss

Counseling in Grief and Loss provides students with the knowledge and tools to support individuals experiencing grief resulting from death, separation, or other significant life losses. The course explores the emotional, psychological, spiritual, and cultural aspects of grief, while teaching evidence-based strategies to help individuals navigate the grieving process and find meaning and healing.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the stages and expressions of grief across different populations.
  • Identify the impact of grief on mental, emotional, and physical well-being.
  • Learn counseling techniques for working with clients in mourning.
  • Explore cultural and spiritual perspectives on loss and bereavement.
  • Recognize complicated grief and when to refer for specialized support.

Main Topics:

  • Theories and models of grief (e.g., KÃ¼bler-Ross, Worden)
  • Grief across the lifespan: children, adolescents, adults, and elders
  • Loss through death, divorce, illness, and life transitions
  • Cultural and faith-based approaches to mourning
  • Complicated and disenfranchised grief
  • Therapeutic interventions and healing practices

Methodology:

This course uses a mix of lectures, personal reflection, group discussion, grief case studies, and simulated counseling sessions.

Assessment:

  • Case study analysis of grief situations
  • Participation in peer role-play counseling exercises
  • Quizzes on grief theories and intervention strategies
  • Final project or reflection paper on personal or observed experiences of loss

Importance of the Course:

Students will be equipped to provide compassionate, culturally sensitive, and informed counseling to those facing loss, enabling them to support healing and resilience in their communities.
