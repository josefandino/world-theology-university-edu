MACC-PC-1100 - Psychological Counseling

Psychological Counseling introduces students to the foundational principles, theories, and techniques of psychological counseling. It explores the counselor-client relationship, counseling models, and the practical application of interventions in various clinical contexts.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the role and responsibilities of a psychological counselor.
  • Identify and apply key counseling theories and models.
  • Develop basic counseling skills, including empathy, active listening, and goal setting.
  • Explore multicultural and ethical considerations in counseling practice.
  • Analyze case studies to apply theoretical knowledge to real-life scenarios.

Main Topics:

  • The helping relationship and the therapeutic process
  • Major counseling theories: humanistic, cognitive-behavioral, psychodynamic
  • Stages of the counseling process
  • Ethical and multicultural considerations
  • Assessment and goal setting in counseling

Methodology:

The course includes lectures, role-plays, group discussions, case study analysis, and practical exercises to build counseling skills in a safe and supervised environment.

Assessment:

  • Participation in counseling simulations
  • Written assignments and reflection papers
  • Case analysis presentations
  • Final exam or practical demonstration

Importance of the Course:

This course lays the foundation for effective psychological counseling practice. It equips students with essential skills and ethical understanding to support clients in achieving emotional and mental well-being.
