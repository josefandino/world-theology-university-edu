MACC-EMC-600 - Ethics and Morality in Counseling

Ethics and Morality in Counseling examines the ethical principles and moral responsibilities involved in clinical counseling practice. The course emphasizes professional standards, legal considerations, and ethical decision-making in complex or challenging situations.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand core ethical principles in clinical and pastoral counseling.
  • Develop skills for ethical decision-making in difficult or ambiguous situations.
  • Identify legal and moral responsibilities of counselors.
  • Recognize common ethical dilemmas and how to resolve them.
  • Promote integrity and accountability in counseling practices.

Main Topics:

  • Foundations of ethics in counseling.
  • Codes of ethics and professional conduct.
  • Confidentiality, informed consent, and boundaries.
  • Handling dual relationships and cultural sensitivity.
  • Ethical challenges in faith-based and clinical contexts.

Methodology:

The course uses case studies, group discussion, role-plays, readings, and reflective exercises to help students internalize ethical principles and apply them in real-world scenarios.

Assessment:

Evaluation will be based on:

  • Analysis of ethical case studies.
  • Class participation and group discussions.
  • Written reflections and ethical position papers.
  • Final exam or applied ethics project.

Importance of the Course:

This course equips future counselors with the ethical grounding and decision-making tools needed to serve others with professionalism, integrity, and compassion.
