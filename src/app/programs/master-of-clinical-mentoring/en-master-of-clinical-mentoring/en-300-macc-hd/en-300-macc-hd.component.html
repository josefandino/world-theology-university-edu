MACC-HD-300 - Human Development

Human Development explores the physical, cognitive, emotional, and social stages of human growth from childhood through old age. Students will gain insights into how individuals evolve across the lifespan and the factors that influence development.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the key stages of human development from infancy to late adulthood.
  • Identify the biological, psychological, and social influences on development.
  • Examine major developmental theories and their application.
  • Analyze developmental challenges and milestones at different life stages.
  • Explore the impact of culture, environment, and family on human growth.

Main Topics:

  • Theories of human development (e.g., Piaget, Erikson, Vygotsky).
  • Physical and cognitive development in childhood and adolescence.
  • Emotional and social development across the lifespan.
  • Adult development and aging.
  • Death, dying, and bereavement.

Methodology:

The course includes lectures, video materials, group discussions, case studies, and reflection activities. Students will apply theoretical knowledge to real-life scenarios and developmental case reviews.

Assessment:

Evaluation will be based on:

  • Exams covering developmental stages and theories.
  • Research projects on specific age groups or developmental challenges.
  • Class presentations and participation in discussions.
  • Written reflections on personal or observed developmental experiences.

Importance of the Course:

This course equips students with essential knowledge for careers in education, counseling, psychology, social work, and healthcare, helping them understand and support people at every stage of life.
