MACC-DPS-500 - Developmental Psychology and Spirituality

Developmental Psychology and Spirituality explores how psychological development across the lifespan shapes an individual's spiritual beliefs, values, and experiences. The course integrates developmental theory with spiritual formation and examines how these areas interact in personal growth and counseling settings.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand key developmental stages and their influence on spirituality.
  • Examine the relationship between emotional, cognitive, and spiritual growth.
  • Identify how spiritual needs and expressions evolve through life.
  • Explore how spirituality can be integrated into psychological support and counseling.
  • Develop sensitivity to diverse spiritual expressions across developmental phases.

Main Topics:

  • Theories of human development and their spiritual implications.
  • Spiritual formation in childhood, adolescence, and adulthood.
  • Crisis, identity, and spiritual transitions.
  • Spirituality in aging and end-of-life stages.
  • The role of faith communities and cultural context in spiritual development.

Methodology:

The course combines lectures, reflective writing, case studies, group discussions, and experiential learning activities focused on integrating developmental psychology with spirituality.

Assessment:

Evaluation will be based on:

  • Written assignments and reflection papers.
  • Case study analysis.
  • Class participation and group work.
  • Final integrative project or presentation.

Importance of the Course:

This course provides a unique framework for understanding the spiritual dimension of human development, which is crucial for professionals working in counseling, pastoral care, and related fields.
