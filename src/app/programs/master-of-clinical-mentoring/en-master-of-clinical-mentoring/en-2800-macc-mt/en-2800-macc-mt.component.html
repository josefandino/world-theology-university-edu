MACC-MT-2800 - Master’s Thesis

Master’s Thesis requires students to design and conduct original research on a relevant topic within the field of clinical counseling. This capstone project demonstrates their ability to integrate theoretical knowledge with research methodology, contributing meaningfully to the discipline.

Course Objectives:

  • Identify and refine a research question in clinical counseling.
  • Conduct a comprehensive literature review.
  • Design and implement a research methodology.
  • Analyze data and interpret findings in context.
  • Present and defend the thesis before a review committee.

Main Topics:

  • Research design and planning
  • Ethical considerations in human subjects research
  • Data collection and analysis techniques
  • Thesis writing and academic formatting
  • Preparation for thesis defense

Methodology:

Students will work under the supervision of a faculty advisor, attending individual and group research seminars. They will follow a structured timeline for proposal submission, data collection, analysis, and final thesis presentation.

Assessment:

  • Research proposal approval
  • Progress reports and advisor evaluations
  • Completed written thesis
  • Oral defense before an academic panel

Importance of the Course:

This thesis serves as a culmination of the student’s academic training, showcasing their ability to contribute scholarly research to the field of clinical counseling. It also prepares them for doctoral-level studies or advanced clinical roles.
