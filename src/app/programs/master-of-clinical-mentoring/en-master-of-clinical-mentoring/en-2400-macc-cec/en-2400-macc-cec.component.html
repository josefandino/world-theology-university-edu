MACC-CEC-2400 - Counseling in Extreme Crisis

Counseling in Extreme Crisis focuses on advanced intervention techniques for dealing with high-intensity situations, including natural disasters, suicides, mass trauma, and other catastrophic events. Students will explore both immediate response strategies and long-term recovery approaches grounded in psychological and spiritual care.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the psychological impact of extreme crises on individuals and communities.
  • Develop skills to provide immediate and effective emotional support during disasters and traumatic events.
  • Apply trauma-informed and evidence-based practices in crisis settings.
  • Integrate spiritual and pastoral support into extreme crisis counseling.
  • Collaborate with emergency response teams and community organizations.

Main Topics:

  • Types and phases of extreme crises
  • Psychological first aid (PFA)
  • Suicide prevention and postvention counseling
  • Support for survivors of mass trauma
  • Care for caregivers and counselors
  • Community resilience and recovery

Methodology:

The course includes lectures, role-playing, real case study analysis, simulations, and guest speakers with field experience in disaster response and trauma recovery.

Assessment:

  • Response plan for a simulated crisis scenario
  • Research paper on effective trauma interventions
  • Participation in practical simulations and debriefs
  • Final exam covering theory and applied skills

Importance of the Course:

This course prepares students to be competent and compassionate responders in the face of the most challenging human experiences, providing holistic support that addresses both emotional wounds and spiritual needs.
