Master's Degree in Clinical Counseling

The Master's in Pastoral Clinical Counseling is designed for pastors and Christian leaders who wish to gain advanced knowledge in spiritual and emotional care, using psychological principles applied to the pastoral context. This program offers a comprehensive approach, combining biblical wisdom with clinical counseling techniques, aiming to equip leaders to effectively address the emotional and spiritual needs of their congregations.

100% Online: Study from Anywhere

This program is entirely online, allowing you to study from the comfort of your home, no matter where you are. You can tailor your study schedule according to your ministerial and family responsibilities while connecting with a global community of pastors and leaders who share your values.

Professional Opportunities

The master's program offers various professional opportunities in the ecclesiastical and community spheres. Graduates will be prepared to serve as pastoral clinical counselors in churches, Christian organizations, ministries, and spiritual support centers, providing emotional and spiritual help to individuals in crisis or with specific needs.

Key Skills for Pastoral Counseling

Throughout the program, you will develop critical skills that will allow you to make a difference in your ministry and the lives of those you serve. Among the most prominent skills are:

  • Problem Solving and Critical Judgment: You will apply biblical and psychological principles to address complex situations.
  • Effective Communication: You will learn to communicate clearly and compassionately, both verbally and in writing.
  • Project Management: You will be able to develop and implement pastoral support and counseling programs within your church or ministry.

World-Class Training

Our academic body includes experts in clinical psychology and pastoral counseling with international experience. You will benefit from a program that combines over 50 years of world-leading research in the fields of psychology and applied theology.

Global Support Network

You will study alongside Christian leaders from around the world, creating connections that will help you grow personally and professionally. Through our online learning platform, you will be able to engage in real-time discussions and collaborate on projects with colleagues from different cultures and ministerial contexts.

A Transformative Approach

The Master's in Pastoral Clinical Counseling will enable you to acquire a holistic perspective on mental and spiritual health. You will learn to develop support programs that positively impact your community, promoting emotional well-being and spiritual restoration. You will be equipped to address challenges such as stress, anxiety, depression, and family issues within a Christian framework.

This program is designed to offer both flexibility and depth, helping you combine your pastoral calling with the necessary skills to provide effective and transformative support in your ministry.

Admission Requirements

To apply for the Master's in Pastoral Clinical Counseling, applicants must meet the following requirements:

  • Academic Transcripts: Official transcripts of previous university studies must be submitted.
  • Curriculum Vitae (CV): An updated CV is required, reflecting ministerial or professional experience.
  • Applicant Statement: Applicants must submit a statement explaining their reasons for pursuing this master's degree, how they plan to apply what they learn in their ministry, and what motivates them to pursue training in pastoral clinical counseling.
  • Letters of Recommendation: Two letters of recommendation are required from mentors, pastors, or leaders who can attest to the applicant's ministerial capabilities and suitability for the program.
  • Application Fee: The application process includes a non-refundable fee.

Selection Criteria

Applicants must have completed a bachelor's degree with a minimum GPA of B or higher. Although prior studies in counseling or psychology are not required, applicants are encouraged to have experience in Christian leadership or pastoral work. Those with a background in theological or ministerial studies will be given preference.

Application Process

The application process is fully online and can be completed from anywhere in the world. For more information about application deadlines and specific admission requirements, applicants are encouraged to review the instructions in the program's admissions section.

This approach ensures that candidates are adequately prepared for success in the Master's in Pastoral Clinical Counseling and to continue their development in ministry and Christian leadership.

Credits and Courses

The program consists of a total of 62 credits, distributed between core courses, electives, and optional courses, allowing students to personalize their learning. The core courses include topics such as:

  • Pastoral Counseling
  • Clinical Psychology
  • Crisis Intervention
  • Christian Family Therapy

The elective courses provide the opportunity to explore specialized areas of interest.

Thesis or Comprehensive Exam

At the end of the program, students must choose between completing a thesis or a comprehensive exam, which will allow them to demonstrate their mastery of the concepts learned. The program is designed to be completed either part-time or full-time, within a maximum period of five years.

Program Objectives

The main objective is to train ethically responsible pastors and leaders who are capable of integrating faith and science in counseling, promoting mental health and well-being within their communities. Additionally, it prepares students for leadership, teaching, and research roles in the field of pastoral counseling.

Applicants for the Master's in Pastoral Clinical Counseling must meet a series of requirements that ensure their preparation and ability to successfully complete the program. Below are the necessary requirements:

Official Academic Transcripts

Official transcripts of all undergraduate or higher education programs previously completed must be submitted. The applicant must have completed a bachelor's program or equivalent in any discipline with a minimum overall GPA of B or higher. A degree in counseling or psychology is not required, although prior studies in areas related to ministry, theology, or the humanities will be positively valued.

Curriculum Vitae (CV)

The applicant must provide an updated curriculum vitae that includes their relevant professional and ministerial experience, as well as any training or education in pastoral counseling, psychology, or leadership within the church or community. This document should reflect the applicant's professional trajectory and highlight the competencies acquired in the ministerial field.

Applicant Statement

This statement is a letter of motivation where the applicant must explain their reasons for applying to the Master's in Pastoral Clinical Counseling. It should include how the program will contribute to their ministerial and professional goals, and how they plan to apply the knowledge gained within their community or congregation. It should also demonstrate a clear understanding of the importance of integrating pastoral counseling with clinical psychology principles.

Letters of Recommendation

At least two letters of recommendation are required from ministerial leaders, pastors, or mentors who can attest to the applicant's leadership, pastoral service, and counseling abilities. The letters should address the applicant's suitability for the program and their potential to apply what they have learned in a practical context.

Ministerial or Leadership Experience

While not a formal requirement, it is recommended that applicants have at least two years of experience in a leadership role within a church or Christian community. This experience may include informal pastoral counseling, teaching, preaching, or involvement in spiritual support programs.

Personal Interview

In some cases, the admissions committee may request a personal or online interview with the applicant. This interview aims to assess the applicant's commitment to the program, their academic and personal preparation, as well as their ministerial vision.

Application Fee

All applicants must pay a non-refundable application fee at the time of submitting their application. This fee covers the administrative costs related to the review of the application.

Recommended Prior Knowledge

While not mandatory, it is recommended that applicants have completed prior courses in topics related to pastoral counseling, psychology, or biblical studies. This prior knowledge will facilitate the understanding of the advanced topics covered in the program.

Academic Requirements During the Program

Once admitted to the program, students must meet the following academic requirements to complete the Master's in Pastoral Clinical Counseling:

  • Total Credits: The student must complete a total of 62 credits, distributed between core courses, electives, and optional courses.
  • Minimum GPA: A minimum GPA of B or higher must be maintained in all courses in order to advance in the program and complete the final thesis or comprehensive exam.
  • Master's Thesis or Comprehensive Exam: At the end of the program, students must choose between completing a research thesis (6 credits) focused on pastoral counseling or taking a comprehensive exam to evaluate their mastery of the key areas of the program.
  • Study Mode: The program is 100% online and can be completed either part-time or full-time, depending on the student's needs. However, all academic work and the final project (thesis or exam) must be completed within a maximum period of five years.

These requirements ensure that students are adequately prepared both academically and spiritually to carry out pastoral clinical counseling work in their communities, combining a biblical approach with clinical psychology principles.
