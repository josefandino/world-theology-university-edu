MACC-APE-2700 - Advanced Professional Ethics

Advanced Professional Ethics explores the complex ethical dilemmas encountered in clinical counseling. The course equips students with advanced tools for ethical decision-making, focusing on real-world challenges and professional responsibilities in diverse counseling settings.

Course Objectives:

  • Analyze complex ethical issues in clinical practice.
  • Apply ethical frameworks and decision-making models.
  • Understand legal and professional responsibilities.
  • Recognize cultural and contextual factors in ethical situations.
  • Promote ethical leadership and accountability in counseling.

Main Topics:

  • Advanced principles of ethics in clinical practice
  • Confidentiality, informed consent, and client rights
  • Boundary issues and dual relationships
  • Legal considerations in mental health counseling
  • Multicultural competence and ethical practice

Methodology:

The course includes lectures, analysis of case studies, group discussions, role-playing ethical scenarios, and the development of personal ethical guidelines. Students will reflect on their values and ethical approaches in counseling.

Assessment:

  • Written analysis of ethical case studies
  • Participation in ethical debate sessions
  • Quizzes on professional codes of ethics
  • Final project: personal ethical decision-making model

Importance of the Course:

This course prepares students to navigate the ethical complexities of modern clinical practice with clarity, integrity, and professionalism, ensuring responsible care and leadership in the field.
