Program Courses

The Master's in Clinical Counseling offers a wide range of courses designed to provide comprehensive training in clinical counseling and psychology. Below is a list of the courses that students will take during the program:

MACC-FC-100 - Foundations of Counseling: Basic principles of counseling, professional practice, and the approaches used in various contexts.

MACC-GP-200 -General Psychology: Basic concepts of psychology, with an emphasis on human behavior and its relation to mental health.

MACC-HD-300 - Human Development: Study of the stages of human development from childhood to old age.

MACC-FP-400 - Fundamentals of Psychotherapy: Introduction to the major psychotherapeutic techniques used in clinical practice.

MACC-DPS-500 - Developmental Psychology and Spirituality: Study of how human development influences beliefs, values, and psychological processes.

MACC-EMC-600 - Ethics and Morality in Counseling: Ethical principles in the practice of clinical counseling, with an emphasis on decision-making in difficult situations.

MACC-IP-700 - Introduction to Psychopathology: Study of common psychological disorders and their diagnosis in the clinical setting.

MACC-IC-800 - Integrated Counseling: Integrated approaches to counseling by applying different psychological theories and intervention practices.

MACC-ACP-900 - Applied Clinical Psychology: Application of the principles of clinical psychology in various professional counseling environments.

MACC-FC-1000 - Family Counseling: Counseling techniques for working with families and resolving family conflicts.

MACC-PC-1100 - Psychological Counseling - Are you interested in supporting and transforming lives? Psychological Counseling provides key tools to intervene in clinical settings, developing empathy, active listening, and applying therapeutic models with a strong ethical and practical foundation.

MACC-PP-1200 - Personality Psychology: Study of personality psychology and its impact on interpersonal relationships and emotional well-being.

MACC-CC-1300 - Crisis Counseling: Techniques for intervening in severe emotional and spiritual crises, such as loss, anxiety disorders, and family conflicts.

MACC-MC-1400 - Marriage Counseling: Principles and techniques for offering counseling to couples, including conflict resolution and relationship restoration.

MACC-PMD-1500 - Psychology of Mood Disorders: Study of disorders such as depression and bipolar disorder, and their treatment in the clinical context.

MACC-CYA-1600 - Counseling for Youth and Adolescents: Strategies for supporting youth in their emotional and psychological development.

MACC-CA-1700 - Counseling for Addictions: Strategies for addressing addiction to alcohol, drugs, and other addictive behaviors.

MACC-PCP-1800 - Psychopathology in Clinical Practice: Study of mental disorders from a clinical perspective, with an emphasis on how to approach them in professional settings.

MACC-TP-1900 - Trauma Psychology: Theory and practice of intervention in cases of trauma, such as abuse, domestic violence, and other traumatic events.

MACC-CGL-2000 - Counseling in Grief and Loss: Techniques for supporting individuals going through grief processes, whether due to death, separation, or other significant losses.

MACC-FPC-2100 - Family Psychology and Counseling: Study of family psychology applied to counseling, with a focus on restoring family relationships.

MACC-CMH-2200 - Counseling for Mental Health: Strategies for integrating mental health into community life and promoting emotional well-being within the congregation.

MACC-DCP-2300 - Development of Counseling Programs: Students will learn how to design, implement, and manage counseling programs in various organizations and communities.

MACC-CEC-2400 - Counseling in Extreme Crisis: Advanced techniques for intervening in extreme crises, such as natural disasters, suicides, and mass trauma.

MACC-RCC-2500 - Research in Clinical Counseling: Students will develop research projects that contribute to the advancement of clinical counseling and applied psychology.

MACC-ACP-2600 - Advanced Clinical Practice: Completion of advanced supervised practice in clinical settings, addressing more complex and challenging cases.

MACC-APE-2700 - Advanced Professional Ethics: Study of ethical dilemmas in the professional practice of clinical counseling, with an emphasis on decision-making in complex situations.
