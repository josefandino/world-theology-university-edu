MACC-GP-200 - General Psychology

General Psychology introduces students to the basic concepts of psychology, focusing on understanding human behavior and its connection to mental health. The course provides an overview of major psychological theories, processes, and applications in everyday life.

Course Objectives:

  • Understand the foundational principles and history of psychology as a science.
  • Recognize key psychological theories and their application to behavior and mental processes.
  • Explore the biological, cognitive, and social bases of behavior.
  • Identify factors that influence mental health and emotional well-being.
  • Apply psychological knowledge to personal and social contexts.

Main Topics:

  • Introduction to psychology and research methods.
  • Biopsychology and the nervous system.
  • Sensation, perception, and consciousness.
  • Learning, memory, and cognition.
  • Personality, psychological disorders, and treatment.

Methodology:

The course uses a mix of lectures, multimedia resources, discussions, and interactive activities. Students will engage in individual and group work to reflect on psychological concepts and real-life applications.

Assessment:

Students will be evaluated through:

  • Quizzes and exams covering core psychological concepts.
  • Written assignments and reflections on mental health topics.
  • Group discussions and participation in classroom activities.
  • A final project analyzing a real-world psychological issue.

Importance of the Course:

This course provides a solid foundation in understanding human thought and behavior, which is essential for students pursuing careers in counseling, social work, education, healthcare, or any field involving interpersonal interaction and mental well-being.
