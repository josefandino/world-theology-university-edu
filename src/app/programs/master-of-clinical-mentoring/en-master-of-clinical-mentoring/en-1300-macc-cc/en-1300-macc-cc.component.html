MACC-CC-1300 - Crisis Counseling

Crisis Counseling provides training in effective techniques to intervene in emotional and spiritual crises. The course covers how to support individuals experiencing traumatic events such as grief, anxiety disorders, suicidal ideation, or family conflicts.

Course Objectives:

  • Identify the different types of psychological and spiritual crises.
  • Apply crisis intervention models in various scenarios.
  • Develop empathetic listening and immediate response skills.
  • Understand the impact of trauma on emotional health.
  • Provide short-term support and appropriate referrals when necessary.

Main Topics:

  • Crisis theory and intervention models (e.g., ABC Model of Crisis Intervention).
  • Handling grief, trauma, and sudden loss.
  • Anxiety and panic responses.
  • Spiritual dimensions of crisis counseling.
  • Ethical and cultural considerations in crisis situations.

Methodology:

This course includes lectures, role-playing simulations, case analysis, reflective journals, and peer discussions to develop applied skills in crisis counseling.

Assessment:

Students will be evaluated through:

  • Written exams on crisis theories and techniques.
  • Simulated intervention role plays and feedback.
  • Case study analysis and response plans.
  • Personal reflection papers on trauma and healing.

Importance of the Course:

This course prepares students to respond effectively to individuals in distress, helping to stabilize emotional and spiritual health during times of acute crisis, and laying the foundation for long-term recovery.
